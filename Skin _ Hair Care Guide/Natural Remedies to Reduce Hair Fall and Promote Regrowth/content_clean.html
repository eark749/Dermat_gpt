Various natural methods exist for reducing hair loss and promoting regeneration. In this blog, we'll examine several successful natural remedies for these purposes.

Understanding Hair Fall and Its Causes

Alopecia (hair loss) can affect the scalp or the entire body and can be transient or permanent. It can be caused by genes, hormonal changes, medical disorders, or just the natural aging process. Anyone can lose head hair, but men are more likely to do so. Baldness is commonly defined as significant hair loss from the scalp. The most prevalent cause of baldness is hereditary hair loss, which occurs with age. Some people prefer to let their hair loss go untreated and unhidden. Others may conceal it with haircuts, makeup, hats, or scarves. Others pick one of the available treatments to prevent additional hair loss or stimulate growth. Consult your physician about your concerns about hair loss and the available treatment options.

Common Causes of Hair Fall

Hair loss is usually caused by one or more factors: heredity, hormonal changes, medications and supplements, radiation therapy for the head, hairstyles, and treatments.

The Importance of Addressing Hair Fall Early

Beginning treatment as soon as possible is essential, but many people wait until their hair loss is severe before seeking treatment, which is much more challenging.

Effective Natural Remedies for Reducing Hair Fall

  1. Massage: Massaging the scalp with hair oils and masks stimulates it and may increase hair thickness. Stretching during the massage may promote hair growth and thickness in the dermal papilla cells found at the base of the hair follicles. These cells serve an essential role in hair repair and growth. The study found that scalp massage enhanced hair growth, blood flow, and scalp health in people.  Massage your scalp can also help relieve stress and tension, which can lead to hair loss. To massage your scalp, use your fingertips rather than your fingernails. Move in small circles across your scalp, using mild to medium pressure. There is no set amount of time to massage. However, in the trial above, each scalp massage lasted four minutes and was performed daily for 24 weeks.

However, the research shows only a few animal trials, and additional human research is needed. You can apply pure aloe vera gel to your scalp many times per week, as well as aloe vera-containing shampoo and conditioner.

  3. Coconut oil: Coconut oil includes lauric acid, a fatty acid that penetrates the hair shaft and reduces protein loss. Coconut oil on the scalp may improve the microbiota, which helps maintain healthier scalp and hair follicles. Depending on your hair type, coconut oil can be applied before or after shampooing. If your hair is greasy, use a leave-in treatment overnight or for a few hours before washing it—massage coconut oil into your scalp and hair. If your hair is dry, you can apply a leave-in treatment.

  4. Viviscal: Viviscal is a natural hair growth supplement that may stimulate growth in those with thinning hair. It is rich in minerals, including shark and mollusk powder, organic silica, vitamin C (produced from acerola cherry), biotin, and zinc.   5. Fish oil: Fish oil supplements contain nutrients, including protein and omega fatty acids. Omega-3 supplements and antioxidants improve hair density, diameter, and loss. Omega-3 fatty acids also help decrease inflammation and strengthen immunity. These could help accelerate your hair growth cycle and improve your overall health. Before adding supplements to your diet, consult a healthcare expert and adhere to the manufacturer's advised dosage.

  6. Ginseng: Ginseng supplements help with hair development by activating hair follicles. Ginsenosides are the active components of ginseng and are responsible for the herb's beneficial effects on hair.

  7. Onion juice: If you can tolerate the odor of onion juice, you might discover that the benefits are worthwhile. The researchers found that onion juice can help encourage hair growth and treat patchy alopecia areata. This is an autoimmune condition in which the body assaults the hair follicles, resulting in hair loss in various body areas. Onion juice also promotes circulation.

  8. Rosemary oil: Rosemary oil may help promote new hair development and prevent hair loss. It was also discovered that rosemary oil could be equally beneficial as minoxidil in treating androgenetic alopecia. Massage a few drops of rosemary oil into your hair and scalp before rinsing. You can repeat this several times in a week. You can also add a few drops of rosemary oil daily to your shampoo and conditioner.  Avoid applying essential oils directly to the skin and mix them with a carrier oil or shampoo.

  9. Geranium oil: Geranium, also known as Pelargonium graveolens, is an aromatic shrub in South Africa. Geranium oil is a thick oil obtained from the leaves. Geranium oil stimulated hair growth and improved circulation in mice. Add three drops of geranium essential oil to eight drops of carrier oil. Apply it immediately to your hair, and you can also mix 2 or 3 drops of it in shampoo and conditioner.

  10. Lemon oil: Lemon oil helps keep your scalp healthy and promotes hair development. Sinapic acid, a bioactive molecule in lemons, may also help encourage hair development. Apply freshly squeezed lemon juice to your scalp and hair for 15 minutes before shampooing. Lemon essential oil can also be used to make a hair mask by diluting it with a carrier oil.

Promoting Hair Regrowth Naturally

Dietary modifications, vitamins, and other methods can help regenerate your hair naturally. People employ several home remedies to try to regrow their hair. However, it is vital to recognize that the research supporting such procedures is not necessarily extensive.

Scalp Massage Techniques

There are different ways to incorporate scalp massages into your self-care routine. There are no  guidelines for how to perform a scalp massage, but here are some general steps: 

  • Use light to medium pressure when pressing your fingertips or palms against your scalp.

  • Move your hands in small circles over one area of your scalp for a few seconds. Then, move to another section until you have covered your entire head.

  • Go on for six minutes. Repeat 1 to 2 times every day.

Homemade Hair Masks

  1. Coconut Oil and Honey Hair Mask: This hair mask is used to repair hair. It consists of coconut oil ( 1 tablespoon) and honey (1 tablespoon). Combine all the ingredients in a bowl. Transfer the mixture to a pot and cook until melted. Apply it to your hair from top to bottom, then cover it with a shower cap. Allow it to sit for 15-20 minutes. Rinse.

  2. Coconut Oil and Cinnamon Hair Mask: This mask is used for hair growth. Mix coconut oil (1 teaspoon) and cinnamon (1 teaspoon) until thoroughly combined. Part your hair and start applying the mask. Use your fingers to massage the product into your scalp. Tie your hair up and protect it with a shower hat or towel. Allow this mask to stay for 30–45 minutes, then rinse with your regular shampoo and conditioner. This mask works best when worn once a week.

  3. Egg and Lemon Hair Mask: This mask is used for oily hair. The ingredients are one egg and ½ lemons. Crack an egg and use only the white. Squeeze half a lemon into a mixing dish. Mix the egg white and lemon juice until creamy. Apply it to moist hair from the top down. Tie your hair up and cover it with a shower hat. Allow this mask to stay for 30–45 minutes, then rinse with shampoo and conditioner.

  4. Brown Sugar and Olive Oil Hair Mask: It is used for dry hair. Combine brown sugar (2 teaspoons) and olive oil (1 teaspoon). Apply the mask to your hair from the top down. Allow the mask to sit for 15-20 minutes and rinse.

  5. Banana, Yogurt, and Honey Hair Mask: It is used for frizzy hair. Ingredients include 1 sliced banana, 2 tablespoons of plain yogurt, and 1 tablespoon of honey. Combine all ingredients in a blender or mash the bananas until smooth. Apply the mask to damp hair from the top down. Tie your hair up and cover it with a shower hat. Put on your hair for 30 to 45 minutes, and rinse with shampoo and conditioner.

  6. Coconut Oil, Avocado, and Honey Hair Mask: It is used for damaged hair. Ingredients include 1 avocado, 2 eggs, honey (1 teaspoon), and coconut oil (1 teaspoon).  Mash the avocado and mix it with the eggs, honey, and oil. Apply it to your hair,  wait 15-20 minutes, and rinse.

  7. Coconut Oil, Strawberry, and Honey Hair Mask: It is used for shiny hair. The ingredients include 6–8 strawberries, coconut oil (1 teaspoon), and honey (1 teaspoon). Blend all of the ingredients to make a puree. Apply it evenly to damp hair. Allow it to sit for 5-10 minutes. Rinse well with warm water.

Beneficial Vitamins and Supplements

Vitamins A, B complex ( such as B12, biotin, and niacin ), C, D, and E are essential for hair growth. Supplements for hair development include collagen and MSM (Methylsulfonylmethane).


Lifestyle Changes to Support Hair Health

A good diet can help keep your hair thick and lustrous. What you consume can also help prevent hair loss. You may notice the impact on your hair if you are not getting specific nutrients from your diet.

Essential fatty acids, primarily omega-3s, are important for maintaining healthy hair, skin, and nails. Eat some of these omega-3-rich foods daily: tuna, salmon, mackerel, and other fatty seafood; flaxseed oil; flax seeds; chia seeds; walnuts; canola oil; tofu; soybeans; and cruciferous vegetables (such as cauliflower, broccoli, and Brussels sprouts).

Vitamins B6, B12, and folic acid are also essential for hair. Vegetarians and vegans frequently consume insufficient quantities. B6-rich foods include bananas, potatoes (white and sweet), and spinach. Primary sources of B12 include meat, chicken, fish, and dairy products. Folic acid is found in different fruits and vegetables, mainly citrus fruits and tomatoes. It can also be found in beans, lentils, and whole and fortified grains.

Protein is also essential for hair health, although many people do not get enough. Lean meats, such as fish and poultry, eggs, and soy products, are excellent sources. Eat one serving every day.

Because trace nutrients such as iron, magnesium, zinc, and biotin affect hair, it is recommended that you take a multivitamin daily. Eating the proper meals can be beneficial, but hair loss can be caused by various factors. Sometimes, hereditary variables, for example, male or female pattern baldness, play a role. Thyroid disorders, autoimmune diseases, anemia, and hormonal factors can cause hair changes or loss.

Stress Management

If your doctor links excessive hair growth to stress, including anxiety-related stress, then stress can cause excessive dark and coarse hair growth. While most people lose hair due to stress, others report that their hair grows faster, darker, and on their faces.

Proper Hair Care Practices

You cannot adjust the number of hair follicles. The following suggestions may help you improve  hair growth naturally:

  • Follow a nutritious diet.

  • Supplements such as biotin, probiotics, iron, etc.

  • Avoid using hair dyes.

  • Acupuncture

  • Aromatherapy

  • Minimize heat from hair styling tools.

There is minimal evidence to support these natural remedies. If these do not work, consult a doctor or dermatologists about other possibilities. Topical creams and shampoos, oral drugs, and hair transplantation, such as follicular unit transplant and follicular unit extraction, are all possible options.

Adequate Hydration

Drinking at least two liters of water every day will increase the strength of the hair and promote growth. Dehydration immediately stops hair growth. Our hair needs hydration (ideally soft water). When the ends of your hair lack the necessary moisture, they break or become brittle.

Conclusion

Some components of hair growth, such as genetics, are beyond a person's control. Eating a healthy and nutritious diet can help increase a person's hair growth. Using home remedies and taking good care of your hair will help it thicken and grow. If you are concerned about hair loss or growth, consult a doctor.
