What is BHA (Salicylic Acid)? BHA is a form of chemical exfoliator from the beta hydroxy acid family.  The most prevalent type of BHA used in cosmetics is salicylic acid, which is obtained from willow bark. BHA is oil-soluble, allowing it to enter pores and exfoliate both the skin's surface and within them. BHA benefits include unclogging pores, exfoliating surface skin, reducing inflammation, controlling oil production, improving skin tone, and preventing breakouts.

The Science of BHA

The science behind BHA (beta hydroxy acid), specifically salicylic acid, demonstrates its skincare efficacy and distinct benefits for a variety of skin issues. This blog is a detailed look at the science behind BHA and how it affects the skin.

Structure and Properties of Salicylic Acid (BHA):

  • Salicylic acid is a beta hydroxy acid (BHA) with a lipophilic (oil-attracting) and hydrophilic (water-attracting) structure.

  • Unlike alpha hydroxy acids (AHAs), BHA is oil-soluble, allowing it to penetrate and dissolve sebum (oil) in pores.

  • The lipophilic nature of salicylic acid enables it to penetrate deeper into the pores, making it effective for exfoliating inside the follicles.

How BHA Differs from Other Exfoliants

Beta hydroxy acid (BHA), specifically salicylic acid, is distinct from other forms of exfoliants, such as alpha hydroxy acids (AHAs), in terms of chemical structure, characteristics, and skincare effects. Understanding these distinctions can help you select the best exfoliant for your skin type and issues. Here's a comparison between BHA and other exfoliants:

  • BHA (Beta Hydroxy Acid, Salicylic Acid): BHA is oil-soluble, therefore it can penetrate deep into pores. Salicylic acid comes from willow bark and other plant sources. It penetrates pores and exfoliates within the follicles. Effective for treating acne, blackheads, and clogged pores. Its anti-inflammatory characteristics make it ideal for sensitive and acne-prone skin. It helps control sebum production.

  • AHAs (Alpha Hydroxy Acids, e.g., Glycolic Acid, Lactic Acid): AHAs are water soluble and act on the skin's surface. It stimulates collagen production. Improves hyperpigmentation and aging spots. It may enhance solar sensitivity.

Best Practices for Using BHA

When using beta hydroxy acid (BHA), such as salicylic acid, in your skincare routine, you must follow specific best practices to maximize its efficiency while minimizing the risk of discomfort. Here are some best practices to use BHA effectively:

  1. Start with a lower Concentration: If you're new to utilizing BHA, begin with a lesser concentration to test your skin's tolerance. You can progressively increase the concentration of BHA as needed.

  2. Incorporate BHA into Your Routine Properly: Apply BHA after cleaning your face and before using any other serums or treatments. Allow the BHA product to seep into your skin for a few minutes before applying any further products.

  3. Use BHA Regularly: Use BHA regularly for the best benefits. Begin by using BHA a couple of times per week and progressively increasing the frequency based on how your skin responds.

  4. Targeted Areas: Apply BHA to areas of concern, such as an oily T-zone, clogged pores, or acne-prone skin. To avoid irritation, don't use BHA near the delicate eye area.

  5. Monitor skin response: Pay attention to how your skin reacts to BHA. If you suffer extreme dryness, redness, or irritation, reduce your BHA use or discontinue it temporarily.

  6. Follow with Moisturizer and Sunscreen: After using BHA, apply a light moisturizer to nourish and soothe the skin. Follow with sunscreen. BHA can enhance sun sensitivity, so use sunscreen during the day to protect your skin from UV damage.

  7. Avoid Over-Exfoliation: Using BHA excessively or in combination with other exfoliants can result in over-exfoliation. To avoid irritation, do not use other harsh exfoliants on the days you use BHA.

  8. Consider Your Skin Type and Goals: Adapt your BHA usage to your skin type (e.g., oily, combination, sensitive) and skincare objectives (e.g., acne therapy, pore refinement, skin texture improvement).

  9. Consult a Dermatologist if Needed: See a dermatologist before integrating BHA into your regimen if you have any special skin issues or conditions. A dermatologist can offer personalized advise on the best BHA products and applications for your skin.

Dermatologists can provide significant insights and advice on the usage of beta hydroxy acid (BHA), namely salicylic acid, in skincare. Dermatologists claim the following regarding BHA and its benefits:

  2. Treatment for Acne and Breakouts: Dermatologists prescribe BHA to treat acne and prevent outbreaks. BHA can treat acne by targeting excess oil, bacteria, and inflammation in the pores, thereby clearing and preventing blemishes.

  3. Oil Regulation: BHA is well-known for its oil-regulating abilities. It reduces excess sebum production, making it ideal for oily and mixed skin.

  4. Anti-inflammatory and Soothing Properties: Dermatologists stress BHA's anti-inflammatory properties.  BHA can reduce redness and irritation, which makes it ideal for sensitive and acne-prone skin.

  5. Increases Skincare Product Penetration: Dermatologists believe that BHA can improve the absorption of other skincare products. Using BHA before applying serums and moisturizers allows these products to permeate deeper into the skin, increasing potency.

  6. Treatment for Blackheads and Congestion: Dermatologists advocate using BHA to treat blackheads and congestion. BHA can remove oil and dead skin cells, which cause blackheads and visible pores.

  7. Anti-aging Benefits: Some dermatologists recommend BHA for its anti-aging qualities. Regular use of BHA can increase cell turnover, reduce fine lines and wrinkles, and improve overall skin tone.

  8. Usage Tips and Recommendations: Dermatologists recommend beginning with a lower concentration of BHA, particularly for sensitive skin. Gradually increase the frequency of BHA use based on skin tolerance, and always use sunscreen throughout the day when using BHA.
