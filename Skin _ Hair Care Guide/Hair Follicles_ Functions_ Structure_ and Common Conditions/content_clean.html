Hair follicles are microscopic, pocket-like pores in the skin. As the name implies, they develop hair. The average individual has over 100,000 hair follicles on the scalp alone. We'll look at what hair follicles are and how they produce hair.

Understanding Hair Follicles

A hair follicle is a tube-like structure (pore) that covers the root and strands of hair. Hair follicles are found in the upper two layers of your skin. You are born with over 5 million hair follicles in your body, including over 1 million on your scalp. Hair grows from your hair follicles as you age. Your hair follicle is one of the few structures in your body that can degenerate and regenerate. This technique promotes hair development in your body.

Anatomy of a Hair Follicle

A hair follicle is a tunnel-shaped structure located in the epidermis (the skin's outer layer). Hair grows from the bottom of a hair follicle. The hair root is made up of protein cells that receive blood from surrounding blood veins. As additional cells are produced, the hair emerges from the skin and reaches the surface. Sebaceous glands near hair follicles generate oil, which nourishes both hair and skin.

The Hair Growth Cycle

Hair grows from the follicles in cycles. This cycle consists of three distinct phases:

  • Anagen (growth) phase: The hair starts to grow from the root. This phase normally lasts three to seven years.

  • Catagen (transitional) phase: During this period, follicle growth slows and decreases. This lasts for two to four months.

  • Telogen (resting) phase: The old hair falls out, and new hair grows from the same hair follicles. This lasts for three to four months.

According to research, hair follicles are not simply "resting" during the telogen period. This phase is marked by a high level of cellular activity, which allows the tissues to renew and develop more hair. In other words, the telogen phase is critical for the development of healthy hair. Different follicles experience different stages of the cycle at the same time. Some follicles are growing, while others may be resting. Some of your hair may be growing, while others are falling out.

Functions of Hair Follicles

A hair follicle's function is to help your hair grow. In addition to stimulating hair growth, your hair follicles perform the following functions:

  • Help in the healing of wounds and injuries to your skin.

  • Develop new blood vessels through angiogenesis.

  • Generate new nervous system neuron cells (neurogenesis).

Hair Production and Growth

 

On average, your hair grows around half an inch per month. Your hair growth rate is influenced by your age, hair type, and overall health. Hair follicles impact not just how much your hair grows but also how it looks. The shape of your follicle impacts the curliness of your hair. Circular follicles make straight hair, while oval follicles produce curly hair. Hair follicles also help to determine the color of your hair. Hair, like skin, obtains its pigment from melanin. There are two types of melanin: eumelanin and pheomelanin. 

Your genes control whether you have eumelanin or pheomelanin, and how much of each you have. A high concentration of eumelanin causes hair to be black, a moderate amount causes hair to be brown, and a low concentration causes hair to be blonde. In contrast, pheomelanin causes hair to turn red. This melanin is stored in hair follicle cells and determines the hair color. As you get older, your follicles may lose their ability to create melanin, causing gray or white hair to grow instead. Hair can regenerate after being taken out of the follicle. A damaged follicle may stop generating hair. Certain disorders, including alopecia, can cause follicles to stop generating hair entirely.

Role in Scalp Health

  1. Hydrated Scalp: A well-hydrated scalp promotes a healthy environment for hair follicles, preventing dry scalp and flakiness, which can cause irritation and hair loss.

  2. Hygiene and cleanliness: Removes debris, extra oil, and product buildup from the scalp, which can clog hair follicles and prevent the growth of new hair.

  3. Blood Circulation: Regular scalp massages encourage blood flow, which makes sure that vital nutrients and oxygen reach hair follicles and support the growth of healthy hair.

  4. pH Balance: Keeping the scalp's natural pH (around 5.5) helps to prevent fungal and bacterial growth, which can cause scalp conditions that harm hair health.

  5. Reduced Inflammation: Anti-inflammatory products, such as those containing aloe vera or tea tree oil, can soothe the scalp and relieve irritation.

  6. Scalp Exfoliation: Removes dead skin cells and promotes the turnover of new skin cells, keeping the scalp healthy and free of buildup that can clog hair follicles.

  7. Stress Management: High-stress levels can lead to scalp issues such as increased sebum production or tension in the scalp muscles, which can negatively impact hair follicles. Practices such as meditation, yoga, and deep breathing exercises can improve overall scalp health by reducing stress.

  8. Diet and Nutrition: Consuming a diet rich in vitamins (A, C, D, E), minerals (zinc, iron), and proteins supports overall scalp health and hair growth. Drinking plenty of water keeps the scalp hydrated and helps flush out toxins that could otherwise accumulate and affect scalp health.

  9. Gentle Hair Handling: Avoiding tight hairstyles, harsh brushing, and excessive heat styling prevents physical damage to the scalp and hair follicles.

Common Hair Follicle Conditions

Several factors influence the health of your hair follicles. The most common conditions include the following:

  • Alopecia areata.

  • Baldness in women and people born as either female or male.

  • Folliculitis.

  • Hidradenitis suppurativa.

  • Stress.

  • Telogen effluvium.

  • Whiteheads, blackheads, or dilated Winer pores.

Folliculitis: Causes and Treatments

Folliculitis occurs when hair follicles become infected with germs, most commonly Staphylococcus aureus (staph). It could also be caused by viruses, fungus, parasites, medicines, or physical trauma. Sometimes the cause is unknown.

In the vast majority of cases of folliculitis, the only treatment required is time, as most cases resolve naturally. More severe instances can be treated medically with antibiotics, antifungals, or antiparasitic drugs.

Androgenetic Alopecia: Impact on Follicles

  1. Hair shafts become shorter and thinner as a result of the miniaturization of hair follicles, which occurs over time. Terminal (thick, pigmented) hairs are replaced by vellus hairs, which are thin and unpigmented.

  2. The anagen (growth) phase of the hair cycle has been shortened. Hair loses density and sheds more when it spends more time in the resting (telogen) phase rather than growing.

  3. A faster transition from the anagen to the telogen phases is observed in the hair cycle dynamics. Baldness and hair thinning appear when the entire hair growth cycle is disrupted.

  4. Hair follicles in people with AGA are more sensitive to androgens, particularly dihydrotestosterone. Follicle miniaturization and hair loss are brought on by DHT's binding to androgen receptors in the hair follicles.

  5. Research indicates that the inflammatory processes surrounding hair follicles might be a factor in the gradual shrinkage and eventual disappearance of these structures.

  3. Detangle: Detangling your hair will reduce knots and breakage. This will smooth out your hair and prepare it for detangling with a brush or wide-tooth comb. You can also apply a hair oil or serum before detangling.

  4. Protect, prime, and style: Always prepare your hair before using heated tools such as a blow dryer, curling iron, or straightener. This not only protects your hair from breakage, but it also improves styling results and ensures your hairstyle.

  5. Moisturize: After styling or air drying your hair, you may want to add some moisture back in. On days when you don't wash your hair, your strands may benefit from more hydration. 

  6. Treatment: There are three sorts of treatments you can incorporate into your weekly routine to offer your hair extra TLC: a mask treatment, an oil treatment, and a scalp treatment. Regular hair treatments will help address particular hair concerns such as damage, dryness, and an oily scalp, flaky, or dry scalp, as well as improve your hair's general appearance and health.
