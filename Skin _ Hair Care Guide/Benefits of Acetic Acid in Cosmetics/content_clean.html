Decoding Acetic Acid: Its Origins and Properties

Acetic acid is an organic chemical that has the formula CH3COOH. It is a carboxylic acid composed of a methyl group linked to a carboxyl functional group. The systematic IUPAC name for acetic acid is ethanoic acid, and its chemical formula is C2H4O2. Vinegar is a solution of acetic acid in water that includes 5% to 20% ethanoic acid by volume. The pungent smell and sour taste are due to the presence of acetic acid.

  • In the solid state of acetic acid, there is a chain of molecules joined to one another by hydrogen bonds.

  • Dimers of ethanoic acid in its vapour phase can be detected at temperatures of approximately 120o.

  • When ethanoic acid is present in a dilute solution, it can form dimers in the liquid phase. Solvents that increase hydrogen bonding have a detrimental effect on these dimers.

  • The structure of acetic acid is CH3(C=O)OH, or CH3CO2H.
