Dermaplaner Tools: Pros, Cons, and How to Use Them

Dermaplaning tools are specialized equipment that exfoliates the skin and removes fine vellus hairs (peach fuzz) off the face. Here's an overview of their advantages, disadvantages, and how to use them effectively:

Pros: 

  • Effectively removes dead skin cells from the skin's surface, promoting smoother and brighter skin.

  • Removes fine vellus hair (peach fuzz), which can enhance the smoothness and appearance of the skin.

  • Enhances the absorption of skincare products by removing the barrier of dead skin cells and 

  • Provides a smooth canvas for makeup application, allowing for a flawless finish.

  • It's a non-invasive procedure that doesn't require chemicals or downtime, making it suitable for most skin types.

Cons:

  • Improper technique or excessive pressure can lead to skin irritation, redness, or small cuts.

  • Dermaplaning may exacerbate active acne or inflamed skin areas, so it's generally avoided in these cases.

  • Requires regular cleaning and blade replacement to ensure hygiene and effectiveness.

  • Professional-grade dermaplaning tools may be more effective and safer when used by trained professionals.

How:

  • Start with clean, dry skin. Remove any makeup, oils, or debris using a gentle cleanser. Optionally, you can apply a toner to balance the skin's pH levels and prepare it for exfoliation.

  • Hold the dermaplaning tool at a 45-degree angle to the skin's surface. This angle allows for gentle and effective scraping.

  • Begin at the forehead or cheek area, and gently glide the dermaplaning tool in short, upward strokes against the direction of hair growth. Avoid applying too much pressure. Let the blade do the work without pressing too hard on the skin. Continue across the entire face, focusing on areas with peach fuzz or dry patches. Take care around sensitive areas like the nose and lips.

  • After dermaplaning, rinse your face with cool water to remove any remaining dead skin cells or hair. Apply a soothing moisturizer or serum to hydrate the skin and calm any potential irritation. Avoid direct sunlight and apply sunscreen (SPF 30 or higher) if going outside, as the skin may be more sensitive after exfoliation.

  • Clean the dermaplaning tool thoroughly after each use to prevent bacteria buildup. Replace blades as recommended by the manufacturer to ensure optimal performance and hygiene.

Using Razors for Facial Hair: Pros and Cons

Using razors to remove face hair has both advantages and downsides. Here's an overview of the pros and cons to consider.

Pros:

  • Razors are commonly accessible and reasonably priced in drugstores, supermarkets, and online. They are simple to operate at home and do not require any specific knowledge or expertise.

  • Shaving produces immediate hair removal results. It effectively trims hair near the skin's surface, leaving a smooth finish that lasts many days.

  • Razors can be used on several face areas, including the upper lip, chin, cheeks, and jawline. They are adaptable to targeting specific areas with fine or coarse hair.

  • Shaving with razors is non-invasive and suitable for most skin types, including sensitive skin. It uses no chemicals or heat, making it a simple option for many.

  • Razors are more affordable than other hair removal options such as waxing or laser treatments. Disposable razors are extremely affordable and easily replaced.

Cons:

  • Shaving can irritate, especially if done incorrectly. Razor burn, redness, and ingrown hairs are all possible, especially if you have sensitive skin or dull blades.

  • Shaving, as opposed to waxing or laser treatments, removes hair only temporarily. Hair regrows in a few days and requires frequent maintenance to stay smooth.

  • Improper technique or the use of a dull razor blade increases the risk of cuts, nicks, and abrasions to the skin. Careful handling and sharp blades are required to reduce this risk.

  • Razors require regular maintenance to ensure effectiveness and hygiene. Blades should be cleaned after each use and replaced frequently to prevent bacterial buildup and maintain sharpness.

  • Shaving can sometimes lead to ingrown hairs, where hair curls back into the skin instead of growing outward. This can cause bumps or irritation, especially in areas with coarse hair.

Myth: Dermaplaner Tools Cause Hair to Grow Back Thicker

Dermaplaner tools are both safe and effective for exfoliating the face and removing hair. They do not disrupt hair development patterns or cause hair to regrow thicker. This myth most likely stems from the transitory sensation of blunt hair tips following removal, which is not a permanent alteration in hair structure or development.

Myth: Shaving Leads to Faster Hair Regrowth

Shaving is a safe and effective way to get rid of hair on the skin's surface. It does not speed up hair renewal or encourage hair to grow thicker. Any perceived changes in hair structure or appearance after shaving are only transient and do not affect the underlying hair development process.

Myth: Dermaplaning and Shaving Are Only for Certain Skin Types

Dermaplaning and shaving are not limited to specific skin types.
