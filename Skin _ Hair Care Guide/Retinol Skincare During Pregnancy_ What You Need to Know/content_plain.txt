Safety Concerns of Retinol Use During Pregnancy
Although retinol is a powerful skincare ingredient, it can be harmful if taken orally in high doses, particularly when pregnant. Retinol and other high dosages of vitamin A have been linked to a higher risk of birth abnormalities. As a result, it is suggested that pregnant women stay away from skincare products that include retinol. Although topical retinol usage during pregnancy has not received much research, there is some evidence to suggest that it may be harmful to the foetus as it develops. An increase in the incidence of foetal abnormalities was shown to be connected with topical retinoid usage during pregnancy, according to a study that was published in the Journal of the American Academy of Dermatology.
It is typically advised that pregnant women avoid using any skincare products that contain retinol or other forms of vitamin A, even if the hazards of topical retinol use are not well documented. Additionally, it's critical to stay away from any supplements or drugs with significant amounts of vitamin A. It is advised that you consult a dermatologist or your healthcare practitioner if you are already using retinol-containing skincare products and are pregnant or intend to become pregnant. They can offer advice on safe skincare procedures to follow while pregnant and suggest substitutes that are also safe to use.
