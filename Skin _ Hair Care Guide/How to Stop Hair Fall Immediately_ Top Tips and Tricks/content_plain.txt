Temporary vs. Permanent Solutions
Hair loss is a very distressing experience. People experience an increase in the amount of hair in their pillow when they wake up, bathe, and use a hairbrush to comb it. Some factors within and beyond our control can cause temporary hair loss. Three factors are responsible for temporary hair loss:
Natural Cycle
: A person loses 50 to 100 hairs every day. This is a completely typical occurrence and is a natural component of the life cycle of your hair. There are three distinct phases of the hair cycle: the telogen phase (little shedding); the exogen phase (increased shedding); and the anagen phase (new hair strands replace the old ones). Research indicates that the exogen phase commonly occurs in most individuals during the autumn and winter. Nevertheless, there is reason to be worried when the act of casually brushing or combing the hair results in a significant number of strands consistently falling out.  This suggests the presence of an underlying factor contributing to hair loss.
External Factors
: Using various chemicals in hair straighten,
hair color
, or curling might result in temporary hair loss by increasing the shedding of hair strands. Hair loss can be induced by styling products such as gels, mousses, hair sprays, and styling waxes, mainly when used with heat-styling appliances like flat irons or curling irons. Traction alopecia is a condition in which tight hairstyles such as ponytails or cornrows put excessive pressure on the scalp, causing the hair to weaken and eventually fall out. Identify the specific chemical and hairstyling method responsible for hair loss and how to stop it temporarily, which may allow the hair to grow back. Changing your hairstyle may not necessarily prevent permanent hair loss.
Identifiable Causes
: Various factors are responsible for transient hair loss. Stress, hormone fluctuations due to pregnancy, menopause, or other changes in the body are some major factors. The hair typically regenerates after your body has acclimated through these stages. Genetic factors and health disorders, such as thyroid disease or alopecia, can lead to permanent loss. Consult with a
dermatologist
to accurately identify the underlying cause of your hair loss. It is essential to seek help when you notice your hair thinning.
