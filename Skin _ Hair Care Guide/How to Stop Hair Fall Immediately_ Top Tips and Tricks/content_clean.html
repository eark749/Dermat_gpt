It is impossible to stop hair fall, but there are some things you can do to reduce it efficiently. In this blog, we will look at some tips and tricks.

Understanding Immediate Hair Fall Prevention

The typical hair development cycle expects to experience a small amount of hair loss daily. In the majority of individuals, the hair that has been lost regrows, allowing them to maintain a complete hair loss. However, illness, hormonal imbalance, stress, aging, and inherited conditions can disrupt the hair growth cycle. 

Recognizing Sudden Hair Fall Triggers

Sudden hair loss can be distressing and may be caused by various factors. You can identify these triggers by taking the necessary measures to address and mitigate hair loss.  The most prevalent causes are tension, anxiety, nutritional deficiencies, hormonal imbalances, medical conditions, scalp conditions, lifestyle, environmental factors, and genetic factors.

Importance of Prompt Action

Hair loss is caused by transforming terminal hair into vellus hair, which shrinks and dries out. This process can be caused by the effect of dihydrotestosterone (DHT) on the scalp's hair follicles, resulting in their gradual reduction in size.

Temporary vs. Permanent Solutions

Hair loss is a very distressing experience. People experience an increase in the amount of hair in their pillow when they wake up, bathe, and use a hairbrush to comb it. Some factors within and beyond our control can cause temporary hair loss. Three factors are responsible for temporary hair loss:

  • Natural Cycle: A person loses 50 to 100 hairs every day. This is a completely typical occurrence and is a natural component of the life cycle of your hair. There are three distinct phases of the hair cycle: the telogen phase (little shedding); the exogen phase (increased shedding); and the anagen phase (new hair strands replace the old ones). Research indicates that the exogen phase commonly occurs in most individuals during the autumn and winter. Nevertheless, there is reason to be worried when the act of casually brushing or combing the hair results in a significant number of strands consistently falling out.  This suggests the presence of an underlying factor contributing to hair loss.

  • External Factors: Using various chemicals in hair straighten, hair color, or curling might result in temporary hair loss by increasing the shedding of hair strands. Hair loss can be induced by styling products such as gels, mousses, hair sprays, and styling waxes, mainly when used with heat-styling appliances like flat irons or curling irons. Traction alopecia is a condition in which tight hairstyles such as ponytails or cornrows put excessive pressure on the scalp, causing the hair to weaken and eventually fall out. Identify the specific chemical and hairstyling method responsible for hair loss and how to stop it temporarily, which may allow the hair to grow back. Changing your hairstyle may not necessarily prevent permanent hair loss. 

  • Identifiable Causes: Various factors are responsible for transient hair loss. Stress, hormone fluctuations due to pregnancy, menopause, or other changes in the body are some major factors. The hair typically regenerates after your body has acclimated through these stages. Genetic factors and health disorders, such as thyroid disease or alopecia, can lead to permanent loss. Consult with a dermatologist to accurately identify the underlying cause of your hair loss. It is essential to seek help when you notice your hair thinning.
