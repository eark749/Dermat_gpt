What Is Tranexamic Acid and How Does It Work on the Skin?
Tranexamic acid, sometimes known as TXA, is a medication that promotes blood clotting. While this is presently the only permitted use of TXA, some data suggests it may have off-label benefits in dermatology. People may use it to treat
melasma
, hereditary angioedema, and urticaria. As a result, TXA is gaining popularity in the area of skin care because it can improve skin look while causing fewer negative effects than comparable chemicals.

Incorporating Tranexamic Acid into Your Skincare Routine: Tips and Advice
If you're thinking about introducing tranexamic acid into your skincare routine, here are some suggestions:
Patch Test
: Before fully adopting tranexamic acid into your routine, conduct a patch test to ensure that you do not experience an unpleasant reaction.
Start Slowly
: Begin with a lower dose and gradually increase as your skin becomes more tolerant. Many products have tranexamic acid amounts of 2-5%.
Use at Night
: Tranexamic acid can be taken both day and night, but many people choose to use it at night for better skin regeneration and to avoid photosensitivity.
Layering
: Apply after cleaning but before moisturizing. Layering with other active components is generally safe, but contact the product instructions or a
dermatologist
beforehand.
Sun Protection
: Use

Targeting Melasma: How Tranexamic Acid Can Help Restore Skin Clarity
Oral TXA has been demonstrated to be an effective and quick-acting treatment for melasma, particularly in severe or refractory instances. Tranexamic acid reduces plasminogen activation, which reduces the generation of prostaglandins and other inflammatory mediators that stimulate melanocytes (pigment-producing cells). This leads to diminished melanin synthesis and pigmentation. Here are the detailed benefits of tranexamic acid for melasma.
