From Tropical Groves to Skincare Shelves: The Journey
Mango butter travels from tropical groves to skincare shelves via several stages of cultivation, extraction, processing, and formulation.  Here's a timeline of mango butter's development:
Cultivation
: Mangoes (Mangifera indica) are native to tropical and subtropical locations around the world, particularly in India, Thailand, the Philippines, and other portions of Africa and South America. The mango fruit matures and is harvested during its season, usually when it is ripe. Mango fruit seeds or kernels are collected for further processing.
Extraction and Processing
: The seeds, or kernels, of the mango fruit are removed. These seeds contain the fatty kernels that are used to make mango butter. There is a process used to remove the butter from the kernels. In general, the kernels are cold-pressed or expeller-pressed to extract the natural fat or oil content. To remove contaminants or undesired components, the extracted butter may be refined. Some manufacturers may choose unrefined mango butter in order to preserve more of its natural characteristics.
Skincare Formulation
: Mango butter is extracted and used as a significant ingredient in a variety of skincare products. It can be found in creams, lotions, balms, serums, masks, and other types of skincare products. Before mixing mango butter with other natural components or compounds to produce skincare formulations, skincare firms thoroughly analyse its quality and purity.
Packaging and Distribution
: Mango butter-containing skincare products are packaged in accordance with the manufacturer's specifications. These items can be packaged in jars, bottles, tubes, or other appropriate containers. After that, finished skincare products are distributed through a variety of channels, such as retail stores, online platforms, speciality skincare boutiques, and directly through the manufacturer's outlets.
Consumer Use
: End users buy mango butter-containing skincare products and incorporate them into their everyday skincare routines. These products moisturise, nourish, and provide other skincare advantages.

DIY Skincare Recipes with Mangifera Indica Seed Butter
Mangifera Indica Seed Butter, often known as mango seed butter, is a nutrient-dense substance that may be utilised in a variety of DIY skincare preparations. Here are a few quick and easy recipes that use mango seed butter:
Mango Seed Butter Body Butter
:
Ingredients
: 1/2 cup mango seed butter, 1/4 cup coconut oil, and 1/4 cup shea butter.
Optional
: a few drops of your favourite essential oil (lavender, chamomile, etc.).
Instructions
: Melt mango seed butter, coconut oil, and shea butter in a double boiler until completely melted and combined. Remove from the heat and set aside to cool slightly. Add essential oil if desired for fragrance. Fill a clean container or jar halfway with the mixture. Allow it to solidify at room temperature or in the refrigerator for a few hours. To moisturise dry skin, use this luxurious body butter. Apply as needed.
Nourishing Mango Seed Butter Lip Balm
:
Ingredients
: 2 tablespoons mango seed butter, 1 tablespoon beeswax, and 1 tablespoon coconut oil.
Optional
: a drop of vitamin E oil or a few drops of essential oil (for a refreshing scent, try peppermint).
Instructions
: In a heatproof container or jar, combine mango seed butter, beeswax, and coconut oil. Melt the ingredients in a double boiler until thoroughly combined. Remove from heat and, if using, add vitamin E oil or essential oil for additional benefits or fragrance. Fill lip balm containers or small jars halfway with the mixture. Before using, allow it to cool and solidify. Apply this homemade lip balm to your lips to moisturise and protect them.
Mango Seed Butter Face Mask
:
Ingredients
: 1 tablespoon raw honey, 2 tablespoons mango seed butter, and 1 teaspoon (optional) plain yoghurt.
Instructions
: In a bowl, soften mango seed butter by gently heating or allowing it to reach room temperature. Mix in the raw honey and yoghurt (if using) until well combined. Allow the mixture to sit on clean, dry skin for 15-20 minutes. Rinse your skin with lukewarm water and pat it dry. This homemade face mask has moisturising and soothing properties.
Before using any new homemade skincare product, always perform a patch test to ensure there are no allergic reactions. These DIY recipes can be adjusted based on personal preferences and skin needs, providing natural and nourishing care for your skin.
