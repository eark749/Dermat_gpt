Overview of Differin Gel
Differin Gel is an acne-treating topical medication. Adapalene, the active ingredient, is a retinoid that works by unclogging pores and preventing the formation of new acne. Here's a rundown of Differin Gel:
Adapalene in Differin Gel works by normalising skin cell growth and reducing inflammation. It also aids in the prevention of new acne by lowering the amount of oil produced by the skin.
Differin Gel should be applied once a day, preferably in the evening, to clean, dry skin. Apply a pea-sized amount of the gel to the entire face, avoiding the eyes and lips. It is critical to use sunscreen during the day because adapalene can increase sun sensitivity.
Differin Gel can reduce the number and severity of acne breakouts while also improving the appearance of existing acne lesions. The full benefits of Differin Gel may take up to 12 weeks to become apparent.
Differin Gel is appropriate for adults and adolescents aged 12 and up. It is not advised to use it during pregnancy or while breastfeeding.
The most common Differin Gel side effects are skin dryness, redness, itching, and scaling. These side effects are usually minor and resolve themselves after a few weeks of use. Stop using the product and seek medical attention if you experience severe irritation or an allergic reaction.
Differin Gel can make your skin more sensitive to sunlight, so use sunscreen and avoid tanning beds while using it. It may also interact with other topical medications or skin care products, so consult your doctor or chemist before combining them.

: Look for skincare items that complement one another, like a cleanser and moisturiser from the same brand. This can make sure the products are made to complement one another and are less likely to irritate users or have other unfavourable side effects.
Be consistent

: Avoid using multiple products that contain active ingredients, such as retinoids or AHAs, as this can increase the risk of irritation or other negative side effects.
