Dihydrotestosterone (DHT) is a testosterone-derived hormone that causes hair follicle shrinkage and contributes to hair loss in genetically predisposed individuals. Some studies suggest that caffeine may inhibit DHT's effects on hair follicles, potentially slowing or preventing hair loss.

Caffeine is a powerful antioxidant, so it can help protect hair follicles from free radical damage. By reducing oxidative stress in the scalp, caffeine may enhance the environment for hair regrowth.
According to some research, caffeine may stimulate the proliferation of cells in hair follicles, resulting in increased hair growth. This effect could be mediated by multiple signalling pathways involved in hair follicle development and regeneration.
While these mechanisms suggest that caffeine may have benefits for hair regrowth, more research is needed to fully understand its effectiveness and optimal use in hair care products. Furthermore, individual responses to caffeine can vary, so the results may not be consistent for everyone.

: Mix 2-3 tablespoons of finely ground coffee with an equal amount of your favorite hair conditioner. Add a teaspoon of coconut oil or olive oil to the mixture for added hydration. Apply the coffee mask to damp hair, focusing on the scalp and roots. Leave the mask on for 20-30 minutes, then rinse it out thoroughly with warm water. Shampoo and condition your hair as usual. Use this coffee hair mask once a week for best results.

: Brew a cup of green tea and allow it to cool completely. Pour the green tea into a spray bottle for easy application.
