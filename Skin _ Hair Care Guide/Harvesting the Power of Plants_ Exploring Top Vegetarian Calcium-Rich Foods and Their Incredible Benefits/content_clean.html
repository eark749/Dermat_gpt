Enhancing calcium absorption is essential to ensuring maximum benefit from this vital mineral. The following techniques will improve calcium's bioavailability and maximize its absorption:

It increases the synthesis of calcium-binding proteins, which aid in calcium transport across the intestinal lining. Make sure you are getting enough vitamin D by getting enough sun exposure or taking supplements, especially if you are at risk for vitamin D deficiency or have little exposure to the sun.

  2. Calcium intake spread out throughout the day: Spreading out your daily calcium intake into several smaller doses can help with absorption. There is a limit to how much calcium the body can absorb at once, so consuming a lot of calcium in one meal may make it harder for it to be absorbed.

  3. Combine calcium with magnesium and vitamin K: These nutrients are thought to be important for bone health and calcium metabolism. It may help calcium absorption if you eat foods high in these nutrients, such as leafy greens for vitamin K and nuts/seeds for magnesium.

  4. stomach acid: Since calcium is best absorbed in an acidic environment, having enough stomach acid (hydrochloric acid) is essential. Drugs or medical conditions that reduce stomach acid production may impair calcium absorption.  If you suspect that you have low stomach acid, see your doctor.

  5. Reduce oxalates and phytates: Oxalates, which are present in foods like spinach and rhubarb, and phytates, which are present in grains, legumes, and nuts, can bind to calcium and prevent it from being absorbed. Despite the fact that these foods are healthy and ought to be part of your diet, think about balancing them with other calcium sources.

  6. Avoid consuming too much alcohol and caffeine: Both substances can impair calcium absorption. It is important to drink these beverages in moderation because excessive consumption can lower calcium levels.

  7. Avoid consuming too much salt: A high-sodium diet can cause an increase in the excretion of calcium through the urine, potentially lowering the body's calcium levels.

  8. Exercise: Weight-bearing activities such as running, walking, and resistance training encourage bone remodeling and support bone density maintenance, promoting overall bone health.

A healthcare professional or registered dietitian can help you determine your specific calcium needs and develop a personalised plan to optimise calcium absorption and overall nutritional balance.
