The Science Behind Why Gum Sticks to Hair

Gum's stickiness is primarily due to its composition and interactions with hair. The science behind why gum sticks to hair is as follows:

Composition of Gum:

  • Chewing gum contains polymers such as synthetic rubbers and resins such as polyvinyl acetate or elastomers, which give it its elasticity and stickiness.

  • Softeners and plasticizers keep the gum pliable and sticky, allowing it to stick to surfaces.

Understanding the science behind why gum sticks to hair can help guide the selection of appropriate removal methods, such as using substances that can disrupt the adhesive properties or change the texture of the gum to facilitate removal without harming the hair.

Step-by-Step Guide to Removing Gum Without Scissors

Here's a step-by-step guide to removing gum from hair without using scissors:

  1. Ice Method: Place an ice pack or ice cubes wrapped in a cloth against the gum for a few minutes. This causes the gum to harden, making it less sticky and easier to remove. Once the gum has frozen, use your fingers to gently break it into small pieces. Take care not to pull or tug on your hair.  Comb through the hair with a fine-toothed comb, beginning at the tips and working your way up to the scalp. Comb out the gum remnants carefully.

  2. Oil or Peanut Butter Method: Coat the affected hair and gum with vegetable oil, peanut butter, or mayonnaise. These substances help break down the gum's stickiness. Gently massage the oil or peanut butter into the gum and surrounding hair. Let it sit for a few minutes to soften the gum. Use a fine-toothed comb to comb through the hair, starting at the ends and working your way up. The gum should slide out more easily.

  3. Vinegar Method (Optional): Soak the affected hair in vinegar for a few minutes. Vinegar can help dissolve the gum, making it easier to remove. After soaking, rinse the hair with water. Then, use a fine-toothed comb to gently comb out the softened gum.

By carefully following these steps and being patient, you can effectively remove gum from hair without using scissors while minimising hair damage.

The Ice Technique: A Cold Approach to Gum Removal

The ice technique is a simple and effective method for removing gum from hair by hardening the gum and making it easier to break off. Here's a step-by-step procedure:

Requirement: Shampoo and conditioner, an ice pack or ice cubes, and a fine-toothed comb are needed.

How to Use the Ice Technique to Remove Gum:

  • To prepare the ice, use an ice pack or wrap several ice cubes in a cloth.

  •  To apply ice, press an ice pack or a wrap of ice against the gum that has become lodged in your hair.  Ensure that the ice comes into contact with the gum and hold it there for a few minutes.

  • The ice's chilly temperature will make the gum harder and less sticky.  Be patient as this process could take several minutes.

  • After the gum has solidified, gently break it off with your fingers into small, manageable pieces.  Avoid tugging or pulling on the hair by being gentle.

  • Beginning at the tips of the hair and working your way up to the scalp, comb through the hair with a fine-toothed comb.  After the ice has loosened the gum remnants, gently brush them out.

  • Use warm water to thoroughly rinse the hair after the gum has been successfully removed.  To make sure all residue is gone and to keep the hair clean, use shampoo and conditioner afterwards. 

The icing technique uses cool temperatures to solidify the gum and make it easier to remove from the hair. It is a non-invasive, safe method. This method is especially useful when the gum is still fresh and has not been thoroughly worked into the hair.
