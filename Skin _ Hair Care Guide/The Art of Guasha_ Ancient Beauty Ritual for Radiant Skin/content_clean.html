The History and Tradition of Guasha in Chinese Skincare

Gua sha techniques date back to the Palaeolithic Age, when hands or stones were rubbed on various regions of the body to relieve pain and/or symptoms when individuals were ill or asleep. During the Ming Dynasty (1368-1644), the ancient Chinese employed it extensively as a healing method. Scrapers were made of a variety of materials, depending on the individual's financial situation and type of therapy. According to tales, empresses and concubines in China primarily employed jade to scrape their faces, while others used copper, animal horns, sharp stones, and different metals as gua sha.

The Guasha Ritual: Your Daily Journey to Radiant Skin

Here's how you could turn this theme into an educational and inspiring guide:

  1. Prepare your skin: Cleanse your skin and apply a lubricating oil, lotion, or Gua Sha-specific cream to create a smooth surface on which the tool can glide.

  2. Begin with gentle strokes: Hold the Gua Sha tool at a comfortable angle, usually between 15 and 30 degrees, with the flat side against your skin. Begin with light pressure and gently scrape the tool in one direction, following the natural contours of your body. Start in the centre and work your way outward. Whenever you scrape, always work your way down for the back and limbs, and towards the centre for the chest and abdomen.

  3. Sustain Moderate Pressure: Apply moderate pressure consistently; do not apply too much pressure as this may result in bruises or skin damage. Listen to your body and adjust the pressure accordingly.

  4. Repeat Strokes: Depending on the area, make a few strokes (usually 5-10) in each direction before proceeding to the next section.

  5. Be mindful of sensitive areas: Exercise caution when approaching sensitive areas such as the spine, joints, and bony prominences. Use a lighter pressure in these areas.

  6. Concentrate on problem areas: Spend extra time on areas of tension, knots, or discomfort. Gua Sha can help to relax tight muscles and fascia.

  7. Stay hydrated: Before and after a Gua Sha session, drink plenty of water to help the body detoxify.

  8. Clean and care for your Gua Sha tool: After each use, thoroughly clean it with mild soap and warm water. Pat it dry with a clean cloth and keep it in a secure location.

  9. Consult a professional: If you are new to Body Gua Sha or have any specific health concerns, consult a trained practitioner to ensure proper technique and safety.

Enhancing Beauty: Guasha for Facial Harmony

If you have taken the plunge and purchased a gua sha tool, try these simple steps for a gua sha facial routine:

  • Select the right tool: Make sure the tool fits the size and shape of your face.

  • Examine for any signs of damage: Look for chips or damage to your tool.

  • Cleanse: Begin by removing all makeup and washing your face with a gentle cleanser.

  • Apply facial oil:  Next, put a few drops of facial oil on your skin. This will allow the gua sha tool to glide smoothly across your skin.

  • Start with the neck: Begin your gua sha facial routine by starting with your neck. Hold the tool at a 45-degree angle and gently scrape up from the base of your neck to your chin. 

  • Move to the jawline: Next, move the tool to your jawline and repeat the upward scraping motion, beginning at the centre of your chin and moving out towards your ears.

  • Work around the nose: Gently press the tip of the gua sha into the small crease on the outside of each nostril. Massaging this area can help to reduce the appearance of nasolabial folds.

  • Work on your cheeks: Move the tool to your cheeks and repeat the upward motion, beginning in the centre of your face and moving outward towards your ears.

  • Work beneath the eyes: Use the tool to gently sweep under the eyes. Begin scraping from the inside of the eyes, close to the nose, to the corners, being especially gentle with the delicate under-eye skin.

  • Work on your forehead: Move the tool to your forehead and use upward and outward strokes, beginning in the centre and moving out to your temples.

  • Work on your brows: Using the curved end of the tool, gently scrape your brows from the inner corner outward.

  • Repeat on the opposite side: Repeat the steps on the opposite side of your face.

  • Finish with your neck:  Finish your gua sha facial routine by scraping from the base of your neck to your chin with the tool.

  • Apply moisturizer: Finally, use your favourite moisturiser to maximise the benefits of your gua sha facial routine.

Remember to be gentle and avoid applying too much pressure when using the tool. To avoid bacteria buildup, clean your tool after each use with soap and warm water, followed by 70% isopropyl alcohol.
