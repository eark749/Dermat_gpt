DIY foot soaks for healing cracked heels
DIY foot soaks can be very effective for healing cracked heels as they help soften the skin, remove dead skin cells, and provide nourishment. Here are a few simple foot soak recipes you can try at home:
Epsom Salt Soak
: Ingredients are 1/2 cup Epsom salt and Warm water. Fill a basin or foot spa with warm water. Add the Epsom salt and stir until it dissolves. Soak your feet in the solution for 15-20 minutes. Gently scrub your heels with a pumice stone to remove dead skin. Pat your feet dry and apply a moisturizing cream or oil.
Vinegar Soak
: Ingredients are 1 cup apple cider vinegar and 2 cups warm water. Mix the apple cider vinegar with warm water in a basin or foot spa. Soak your feet in the solution for 15-20 minutes. After soaking, gently scrub your heels with a soft brush or pumice stone. Rinse your feet with clean water and pat them dry. Apply a thick moisturizer or foot cream to lock in moisture.
Milk and Honey Soak
: Ingredients are 2 cups milk and 1/4 cup honey. Heat the milk until warm (but not boiling). Pour the warm milk into a basin or foot spa. Add honey and mix well until it dissolves. Soak your feet in the mixture for 15-20 minutes. Gently exfoliate your heels with a foot scrub or pumice stone. Rinse your feet with lukewarm water and pat them dry. Apply a moisturizing lotion or oil to your heels for extra hydration.
Oatmeal Soak
: Ingredients are 1/2 cup oatmeal and Warm water. Grind the oatmeal into a fine powder using a blender or food processor. Fill a basin or foot spa with warm water and add the oatmeal powder. Stir well to ensure the oatmeal is evenly distributed. Soak your feet in the mixture for 15-20 minutes. Gently massage your heels with the oatmeal paste to exfoliate and soften the skin. Rinse your feet with clean water and pat them dry. Apply a thick moisturizer or foot cream to lock in moisture.
These DIY foot soaks can be used regularly to heal cracked heels and keep your feet soft and smooth. However, if you have any open wounds or infections on your feet, you should seek medical attention before attempting these treatments.

Proper foot care and hygiene practices
Proper foot care and hygiene are essential for keeping your feet healthy and happy. Here are some important practices to include in your daily routine:
Keep Your Feet Clean
: Wash your feet every day with warm water and a mild soap. Scrub dirt and dead skin cells away with a soft cloth or brush, paying special attention to the area between the toes, where bacteria and fungi thrive. Make sure to completely dry your feet, paying special attention to the spaces between your toes.
Trim Toenails Regularly
: To avoid ingrown toenails, trim your toenails straight across rather than too short. Use clean, sanitised nail clippers to smooth the edges of the nails. Trim your nails after bathing, when they are softer and easier to cut.
Moisturise Daily
: Apply a moisturising cream or lotion to your feet daily, paying special attention to dry areas like the heels and soles. Moisturising protects against dry, cracked skin and keeps your feet soft and supple. Avoid using moisturiser between your toes, as this can create a damp environment that encourages fungal growth.
Wear Clean Socks and Shoes
: Change your socks every day, especially if your feet sweat excessively. Opt for socks made of moisture-wicking materials like cotton or bamboo to keep your feet dry. Rotate your shoes to allow them to air out and dry completely between wears. Consider using antifungal powders or sprays inside your shoes to prevent fungal infections.
Choose Proper Footwear
: Wear comfortable shoes and provide adequate support for your feet. Avoid shoes with too tight or narrow toe boxes, which can cause friction, pressure, and foot deformities. Choose breathable shoes made of natural materials that promote air circulation and reduce moisture buildup.
Protect Your Feet
: To reduce the risk of fungal infections like athlete's foot, wear protective footwear in public places such as swimming pools, gyms, and communal showers. Use flip-flops or shower shoes to protect your feet from potentially contaminated surfaces.
Inspect Your Feet Daily
: Examine your feet for signs of redness, swelling, cuts, blisters, or other abnormalities. Address any issues as soon as possible by cleaning and applying antiseptic ointment, or seek medical attention if necessary, especially if you have diabetes or other conditions that impair circulation or wound healing.
Practice Good Foot Hygiene in Public Places
: To reduce the risk of infection, avoid walking barefoot in public areas such as locker rooms, saunas or hotel rooms. Use your towel, and do not share personal items such as nail clippers or shoes with others.
By incorporating these foot care and hygiene practices into your daily routine, you can help keep your feet healthy and comfortable while avoiding common foot problems and infections. If you are concerned about your foot health or notice any persistent problems, see a healthcare professional for an evaluation and treatment.
Choosing the right footwear to prevent cracked heels
To avoid cracked heels, choosing the appropriate shoes is vital. The following advice will help you select shoes that will maintain the health and hydration of your heels:
Choose shoes that fit properly and give your feet enough support. Shoes that are excessively tight or loose can press and rub against the heels, causing dryness and cracking. Make sure the toe box has adequate room to avoid being crowded or rubbing against the heels.
Look for shoes that provide adequate cushioning and support, particularly in the heel area. Cushioned insoles and shock-absorbing materials can help reduce the impact on your heels and minimise the risk of developing cracks. Consider adding orthotic inserts if you need extra support or have specific foot conditions.
Choose shoes made of breathable materials such as leather or mesh, which promote air circulation and prevent moisture buildup. Breathable shoes help to keep your feet dry and reduce the likelihood of fungal infections and skin irritation.
While sandals and open-back shoes are fashionable, they typically provide little support and expose your heels to friction and dryness. Choose closed-back shoes or sandals with supportive straps to keep your feet securely in place.
High heels can put too much pressure on the heels, leading to dryness and cracking. Choose shoes with low or moderate heels to reduce foot strain and the risk of cracking.
Wear moisture-wicking socks made from cotton or bamboo, which absorb sweat and keep your feet dry. Moisture-wicking socks can help prevent excess moisture buildup, which can lead to cracked heels.
Avoid wearing the same shoes every day. Rotate your shoes regularly so they can air out and dry completely between wears. Moisture trapped inside shoes softens the skin, making it more susceptible to cracking.
Choose footwear that is appropriate for your planned activity. For exercise and activities that require prolonged standing or walking, wear supportive athletic shoes. Avoid going barefoot on hard or rough surfaces to prevent friction and injury to the heels.
By selecting footwear that fits well, provides adequate support and cushioning, and promotes proper airflow, you can help prevent cracked heels and maintain the overall health of your feet. The role of custom orthotics in managing foot health
Custom orthotics play an important role in foot health management by supporting, stabilising, and aligning the feet and lower limbs. Here are a few ways that custom orthotics help with overall foot health:
Custom orthotics are specifically designed to support the arches of the feet, thereby distributing body weight more evenly and reducing stress on the feet, ankles, knees, hips, and lower back. By supporting the arches, orthotics can help prevent overpronation (excessive inward rolling of the foot) or supination (excessive outward rolling), which can lead to various foot problems and injuries.
Custom orthotics assist in the proper alignment of the feet, ankles, and lower limbs while standing, walking, and running. Proper alignment lowers the risk of biomechanical issues like flat feet, high arches, and abnormal gait patterns, which can all lead to foot pain, instability, and injury.
Orthotics can redistribute pressure on the feet by cushioning and padding areas with high pressure or friction. This relieves the pain and discomfort caused by conditions such as plantar fasciitis, metatarsalgia, and heel spurs, allowing for greater comfort and mobility.
Custom orthotics can absorb shock and impact forces generated during weight-bearing activities like walking or running. By absorbing shock, orthotics help reduce strain on the muscles, tendons, and joints of the feet and lower limbs, which can help prevent overuse injuries and fatigue.
Orthotics can help correct or accommodate foot deformities like bunions, hammertoes, and claw toes by providing individualised support and realignment. By addressing underlying structural issues, orthotics can help improve foot function and reduce pain and discomfort associated with deformities.
Custom orthotics can help to prevent injuries by stabilising and supporting the feet and lower limbs during physical activities. By optimising biomechanics and reducing excessive motion, orthotics can help reduce the risk of common injuries such as stress fractures, tendonitis, and sprains.
Athletes and active individuals can benefit from custom orthotics by improving biomechanical efficiency, reducing fatigue, and enhancing performance during sports and exercise. Orthotics can improve foot function and mechanics, resulting in better balance, agility, and power transfer.
