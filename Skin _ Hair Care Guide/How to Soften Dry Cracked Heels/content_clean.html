Causes of cracked heels

Cracked heels can be caused by a variety of factors, including:

  • Dry skin: When the skin on your feet is dry, it can become brittle and crack.

  • Medical conditions: Certain medical conditions, such as eczema and diabetes, can lead to dry and cracked skin.

  • Footwear: Wearing shoes that do not fit properly or that do not provide enough support can put pressure on the feet, causing the skin to crack.

  • Standing for long periods of time: If you stand for long periods of time, your feet can become dry and cracked.

  • Age: As we age, our skin becomes thinner and more prone to cracking.

DIY foot soaks for healing cracked heels

DIY foot soaks can be very effective for healing cracked heels as they help soften the skin, remove dead skin cells, and provide nourishment. Here are a few simple foot soak recipes you can try at home:

  • Epsom Salt Soak: Ingredients are 1/2 cup Epsom salt and Warm water. Fill a basin or foot spa with warm water. Add the Epsom salt and stir until it dissolves. Soak your feet in the solution for 15-20 minutes. Gently scrub your heels with a pumice stone to remove dead skin. Pat your feet dry and apply a moisturizing cream or oil.

  • Vinegar Soak: Ingredients are 1 cup apple cider vinegar and 2 cups warm water. Mix the apple cider vinegar with warm water in a basin or foot spa. Soak your feet in the solution for 15-20 minutes. After soaking, gently scrub your heels with a soft brush or pumice stone. Rinse your feet with clean water and pat them dry. Apply a thick moisturizer or foot cream to lock in moisture.

  • Milk and Honey Soak: Ingredients are 2 cups milk and 1/4 cup honey. Heat the milk until warm (but not boiling). Pour the warm milk into a basin or foot spa. Add honey and mix well until it dissolves. Soak your feet in the mixture for 15-20 minutes. Gently exfoliate your heels with a foot scrub or pumice stone. Rinse your feet with lukewarm water and pat them dry. Apply a moisturizing lotion or oil to your heels for extra hydration.

  • Oatmeal Soak: Ingredients are 1/2 cup oatmeal and Warm water. Grind the oatmeal into a fine powder using a blender or food processor. Fill a basin or foot spa with warm water and add the oatmeal powder. Stir well to ensure the oatmeal is evenly distributed. Soak your feet in the mixture for 15-20 minutes. Gently massage your heels with the oatmeal paste to exfoliate and soften the skin. Rinse your feet with clean water and pat them dry. Apply a thick moisturizer or foot cream to lock in moisture.

These DIY foot soaks can be used regularly to heal cracked heels and keep your feet soft and smooth. However, if you have any open wounds or infections on your feet, you should seek medical attention before attempting these treatments.

Exfoliation techniques for smoother heels

Exfoliation is essential for removing dead skin cells and achieving smoother heels. Here are some effective exfoliation techniques to get smoother heels:

Wet the pumice stone and gently rub it across the heels in a circular motion. Continue exfoliating until you have removed enough dead skin. Rinse your feet with water and pat dry. Rinse thoroughly with water, pat dry, and apply moisturiser.

  • Foot Files and Pedicure Tools: These tools are specifically designed to remove calluses and dead skin from the feet. They usually have a rough surface that rubs away dry, rough patches. Use them on dry skin and follow the instructions that come with the tool.

  • Chemical Exfoliants: Exfoliants containing alpha hydroxy acids (AHAs) or beta hydroxy acids (BHAs) can aid in the removal of dead skin cells and promote smoother heels. Look for products with ingredients such as glycolic acid, lactic acid, or salicylic acid. Follow the product's instructions carefully and avoid excessive exfoliation, especially if you have sensitive skin.

  • Exfoliating Socks or Foot Masks: These products contain ingredients such as AHAs or enzymes, which exfoliate the skin over time. Simply wear the socks or foot masks as directed and let the exfoliating ingredients do their magic.

Regardless of the exfoliation technique you use, moisturise your heels afterwards to keep the skin hydrated and prevent further dryness. Consistency is key, so exfoliate your heels regularly, especially if they become dry and rough quickly.

Preventative Strategies to Avoid Cracked Heels

Preventing cracked heels requires a combination of regular foot care and lifestyle changes. Here are some preventative strategies to help you avoid cracked heels:

  1. Keeping your feet well-moisturized is key to preventing cracked heels. Apply a thick moisturizer or foot cream daily, especially after bathing or showering, to lock in moisture and keep the skin soft and supple.

  2. Regular exfoliation removes dead skin cells from the heels, preventing the buildup of dry, rough patches that can lead to cracks. Use a pumice stone, foot scrub, or foot file to gently exfoliate your heels at least once or twice a week.

  3. Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated from the inside out. Hydrated skin is less likely to become dry and cracked. Aim to drink at least 8 glasses of water a day, or more if you're physically active or in a dry climate.

  4. Avoid wearing shoes that are too tight or have thin soles, as they can cause pressure and friction on the heels, leading to dryness and cracking. Opt for supportive, well-fitting shoes with cushioned soles to reduce pressure on your feet.

  5. Wear socks or slippers at home to keep your feet warm on cold floors and rough surfaces. Avoid walking barefoot in public places because it increases the risk of injury or infection.

  6. If your job or lifestyle requires long periods of standing or walking, take regular breaks to rest your feet. Elevate your feet while sitting to reduce swelling and improve circulation.

  7. Eat a well-balanced diet rich in vitamins and nutrients, such as vitamins A, C, and E, zinc, and omega-3 fatty acids, all of which promote skin health. Include plenty of fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and healthy fats in your daily diet.

  8. Apply a thick layer of moisturiser or petroleum jelly to your heels before bedtime, then cover with clean cotton socks to keep moisture in. This softens the skin and keeps it from becoming too dry.

  9. Diabetes, eczema, psoriasis, and thyroid disorders are all medical conditions that can increase your risk of cracked heels. Manage these conditions effectively with the assistance of your healthcare provider to reduce the risk of complications.

By incorporating these preventive measures into your daily routine, you can keep your heels soft, smooth, and crack-free. If you notice any signs of cracked heels despite taking preventive measures, treat them right away to avoid further complications.

Proper foot care and hygiene practices

Proper foot care and hygiene are essential for keeping your feet healthy and happy. Here are some important practices to include in your daily routine:

  1. Keep Your Feet Clean: Wash your feet every day with warm water and a mild soap. Scrub dirt and dead skin cells away with a soft cloth or brush, paying special attention to the area between the toes, where bacteria and fungi thrive. Make sure to completely dry your feet, paying special attention to the spaces between your toes.

  2. Trim Toenails Regularly: To avoid ingrown toenails, trim your toenails straight across rather than too short. Use clean, sanitised nail clippers to smooth the edges of the nails. Trim your nails after bathing, when they are softer and easier to cut.

  3. Moisturise Daily: Apply a moisturising cream or lotion to your feet daily, paying special attention to dry areas like the heels and soles. Moisturising protects against dry, cracked skin and keeps your feet soft and supple. Avoid using moisturiser between your toes, as this can create a damp environment that encourages fungal growth. 

  4. Wear Clean Socks and Shoes: Change your socks every day, especially if your feet sweat excessively. Opt for socks made of moisture-wicking materials like cotton or bamboo to keep your feet dry. Rotate your shoes to allow them to air out and dry completely between wears. Consider using antifungal powders or sprays inside your shoes to prevent fungal infections.

  5. Choose Proper Footwear: Wear comfortable shoes and provide adequate support for your feet. Avoid shoes with too tight or narrow toe boxes, which can cause friction, pressure, and foot deformities. Choose breathable shoes made of natural materials that promote air circulation and reduce moisture buildup.

  6. Protect Your Feet: To reduce the risk of fungal infections like athlete's foot, wear protective footwear in public places such as swimming pools, gyms, and communal showers. Use flip-flops or shower shoes to protect your feet from potentially contaminated surfaces.

  7. Inspect Your Feet Daily: Examine your feet for signs of redness, swelling, cuts, blisters, or other abnormalities. Address any issues as soon as possible by cleaning and applying antiseptic ointment, or seek medical attention if necessary, especially if you have diabetes or other conditions that impair circulation or wound healing.

  8. Practice Good Foot Hygiene in Public Places: To reduce the risk of infection, avoid walking barefoot in public areas such as locker rooms, saunas or hotel rooms. Use your towel, and do not share personal items such as nail clippers or shoes with others.

By incorporating these foot care and hygiene practices into your daily routine, you can help keep your feet healthy and comfortable while avoiding common foot problems and infections. If you are concerned about your foot health or notice any persistent problems, see a healthcare professional for an evaluation and treatment.

Choosing the right footwear to prevent cracked heels

To avoid cracked heels, choosing the appropriate shoes is vital. The following advice will help you select shoes that will maintain the health and hydration of your heels:

  • Choose shoes that fit properly and give your feet enough support. Shoes that are excessively tight or loose can press and rub against the heels, causing dryness and cracking. Make sure the toe box has adequate room to avoid being crowded or rubbing against the heels.

  • Look for shoes that provide adequate cushioning and support, particularly in the heel area. Cushioned insoles and shock-absorbing materials can help reduce the impact on your heels and minimise the risk of developing cracks. Consider adding orthotic inserts if you need extra support or have specific foot conditions.

  • Choose shoes made of breathable materials such as leather or mesh, which promote air circulation and prevent moisture buildup. Breathable shoes help to keep your feet dry and reduce the likelihood of fungal infections and skin irritation.

  • While sandals and open-back shoes are fashionable, they typically provide little support and expose your heels to friction and dryness. Choose closed-back shoes or sandals with supportive straps to keep your feet securely in place.

  • High heels can put too much pressure on the heels, leading to dryness and cracking. Choose shoes with low or moderate heels to reduce foot strain and the risk of cracking.

  • Wear moisture-wicking socks made from cotton or bamboo, which absorb sweat and keep your feet dry. Moisture-wicking socks can help prevent excess moisture buildup, which can lead to cracked heels.

  • Avoid wearing the same shoes every day. Rotate your shoes regularly so they can air out and dry completely between wears. Moisture trapped inside shoes softens the skin, making it more susceptible to cracking.

  • Choose footwear that is appropriate for your planned activity. For exercise and activities that require prolonged standing or walking, wear supportive athletic shoes. Avoid going barefoot on hard or rough surfaces to prevent friction and injury to the heels.

By selecting footwear that fits well, provides adequate support and cushioning, and promotes proper airflow, you can help prevent cracked heels and maintain the overall health of your feet. The role of custom orthotics in managing foot health

Custom orthotics play an important role in foot health management by supporting, stabilising, and aligning the feet and lower limbs. Here are a few ways that custom orthotics help with overall foot health:

  • Custom orthotics are specifically designed to support the arches of the feet, thereby distributing body weight more evenly and reducing stress on the feet, ankles, knees, hips, and lower back. By supporting the arches, orthotics can help prevent overpronation (excessive inward rolling of the foot) or supination (excessive outward rolling), which can lead to various foot problems and injuries.

  • Custom orthotics assist in the proper alignment of the feet, ankles, and lower limbs while standing, walking, and running. Proper alignment lowers the risk of biomechanical issues like flat feet, high arches, and abnormal gait patterns, which can all lead to foot pain, instability, and injury.

  • Orthotics can redistribute pressure on the feet by cushioning and padding areas with high pressure or friction. This relieves the pain and discomfort caused by conditions such as plantar fasciitis, metatarsalgia, and heel spurs, allowing for greater comfort and mobility.

  • Custom orthotics can absorb shock and impact forces generated during weight-bearing activities like walking or running. By absorbing shock, orthotics help reduce strain on the muscles, tendons, and joints of the feet and lower limbs, which can help prevent overuse injuries and fatigue.

  • Orthotics can help correct or accommodate foot deformities like bunions, hammertoes, and claw toes by providing individualised support and realignment. By addressing underlying structural issues, orthotics can help improve foot function and reduce pain and discomfort associated with deformities.

  • Custom orthotics can help to prevent injuries by stabilising and supporting the feet and lower limbs during physical activities. By optimising biomechanics and reducing excessive motion, orthotics can help reduce the risk of common injuries such as stress fractures, tendonitis, and sprains.

  • Athletes and active individuals can benefit from custom orthotics by improving biomechanical efficiency, reducing fatigue, and enhancing performance during sports and exercise. Orthotics can improve foot function and mechanics, resulting in better balance, agility, and power transfer.

Long-Term Management and Care for Healthy Feet

Long-term management and care are critical for keeping feet healthy and avoiding common problems. Here are some key practices to include in your routine:

  1. Daily Foot Inspection: Examine your feet for signs of redness, swelling, cuts, blisters, and other abnormalities.   3. Regularly Moisturise: Treat your feet with a moisturising cream or lotion, paying special attention to the dry areas like the soles and heels. Moisturising keeps your feet supple and soft while preventing dry, cracked skin.

  4. Wear appropriate Footwear: Make sure your shoes fit properly and offer your feet enough support and cushioning. Shoes with narrow toe boxes or excessive tightness should be avoided, as they can lead to foot deformities, pressure sores, and friction. Rotate your shoes frequently to allow them to air out and reduce wear and tear.

  5. Wear appropriate socks: To keep your feet dry and prevent fungal infections, choose socks made of moisture-wicking materials such as cotton or bamboo. Change your socks daily, especially if your feet sweat excessively, and avoid wearing damp socks or shoes.

  6. Protect Your Feet: To reduce the risk of fungal infections like athlete's foot, wear protective footwear in public places such as swimming pools, gyms, and communal showers. Use flip-flops or shower shoes to protect your feet from potentially contaminated surfaces.

  7. Maintain a Healthy Weight: Excess weight can put extra strain on your feet, causing foot pain, arch collapse, and other issues. Maintain a healthy weight through regular exercise and a well-balanced diet to lower your risk of developing foot problems.

  10. Regular Foot Checkups: Schedule regular checkups with a podiatrist or healthcare provider to monitor your foot health and address any concerns or issues as soon as possible. Regular foot exams are especially important if you have diabetes or another condition that increases your risk of foot complications.

By incorporating these long-term management and care practices into your daily routine, you can help keep your feet healthy and reduce your chances of developing common foot problems or injuries. If you are experiencing persistent foot pain, discomfort, or other symptoms, see a doctor for an accurate diagnosis and treatment.
