Simple Yet Delicious Kiwano Recipe to Try at Home
The vibrant green colour and refreshing flavour of kiwano pair perfectly with the sweet and spicy elements of a traditional salsa, making it a perfect appetiser or condiment.
1. Ingredients:
1 ripe Kiwano
1 Roma tomato, seeded and diced
1/2 red onion, finely chopped
1 jalapeno pepper, seeded and finely chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
1/4 cup (60 ml) fresh cilantro, chopped
1 tablespoon (15 ml) lime juice
1 tablespoon (15 ml) olive oil
Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
2. Instructions:
Cut the kiwano in half lengthwise.
Using a spoon, gently scoop out the green flesh and black seeds.
Discard the seeds or save them for planting (optional).
Dice the kiwano flesh into small cubes.
In a mixing bowl, combine the diced kiwano, tomato, red onion, jalapeno pepper (if using), cilantro, lime juice, and olive oil.
Add salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste.
Serve the Kiwano Salsa with your favourite tortilla chips, grilled fish, or tacos.
3. Tips:
For a milder salsa, remove the seeds from the jalapeno pepper before chopping.
If you don't have fresh cilantro, you can substitute it with 1 tablespoon (15 ml) of chopped parsley.
Feel free to experiment with other ingredients like chopped avocado, mango, or pineapple.
Let the salsa sit for at least 15 minutes before serving to allow the flavours to meld.
This Kiwano Salsa with a Touch of Heat is a simple yet flavorful recipe that is sure to impress your guests. The unique combination of sweet, spicy, and refreshing flavours makes it a delightful addition to any meal. So ditch the ordinary salsa and give this kiwano twist a try!
Kiwano Smoothies: A Refreshing Way to Enjoy Its Health Benefits
Kiwano's vibrant green exterior and refreshing flavour make it a perfect candidate for a delicious and healthy smoothie. This Kiwano Smoothie recipe is a simple yet delightful way to experience the unique taste of kiwano and kickstart your day with a burst of vitamins and hydration.
1. Ingredients:
1 ripe Kiwano
1 cup (240 ml) Mango chunks (fresh or frozen)
1/2 cup (120 ml) Pineapple chunks (fresh or frozen)
1/2 banana, frozen
1 cup (240 ml) Plant-based milk (almond,
coconut
, oat, etc.) or yogurt (dairy or non-dairy)
1 tablespoon (15 ml) Honey or maple syrup (optional, to taste)
1/4 cup (60 ml) Water (optional, for desired consistency)
2. Instructions:
Cut the kiwano in half lengthwise.
Using a spoon, gently scoop out the green flesh and black seeds.
Discard the seeds or save them for planting (optional).
Add the kiwano flesh, mango chunks, pineapple chunks, frozen banana, plant-based milk or yogurt, and honey or maple syrup (if using) to a blender.
Blend until smooth and creamy.
If the smoothie is too thick, add water a little at a time until it reaches your desired consistency.
Pour the smoothie into glasses and garnish with a slice of fresh kiwano or a sprig of mint (optional).
3. Tips:
For a thicker smoothie, use frozen fruit instead of fresh.
If you prefer a sweeter smoothie, add more honey or maple syrup to taste.
Feel free to experiment with other fruits like oranges, peaches, or berries.
For a protein boost, add a scoop of your favourite protein powder.
