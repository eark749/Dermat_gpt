What is Saccharide Isomerate?
Saccharide isomerate, also known as Pentavitin or Saccharomyces cerevisiae extract, is a naturally derived carbohydrate complex that is commonly used in cosmetic and personal care products. It is derived from the carbohydrate fraction of the yeast Saccharomyces cerevisiae, which is the same species used in baking and brewing.
Saccharide isomerate is primarily used as a moisturizing and skin conditioning agent. It is known for its ability to provide long-lasting hydration to the skin. It works by binding to the skin and creating a moisture reservoir that can last up to 72 hours, even after washing. This property makes it particularly useful in skincare products, such as moisturizers, serums, lotions, and creams.
One of the key benefits of saccharide isomerate is its ability to mimic the natural moisturizing factor (NMF) found in the outermost layer of the skin. The NMF helps to regulate the skin's moisture content and maintain its barrier function. By resembling the NMF, saccharide isomerate enhances the skin's natural moisture retention ability, resulting in improved hydration and a smoother, more supple complexion.
Furthermore, saccharide isomerate has been shown to have soothing and anti-irritant properties. It helps to calm and reduce skin redness, making it suitable for sensitive or irritated skin types. Additionally, it is often used in hair care products to provide moisture and improve the overall condition of the hair.
It is important to note that saccharide isomerate is considered safe for cosmetic use and is well-tolerated by most individuals. However, as with any cosmetic ingredient, some people may have specific sensitivities or allergies, so it's always advisable to check the product's ingredient list and perform a patch test before using it extensively.
