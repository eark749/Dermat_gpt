Potential Side Effects and How to Manage Them
While Emface is generally a safe and non-invasive treatment, there are some potential adverse effects to consider. These side effects are usually minor and transitory. Here's an overview of probable side effects and how to properly handle them:
Potential Side Effects
Redness
: Mild redness in the treated areas, comparable to a mild sunburn. Usually goes away within a few hours to a few days.
Swelling
:
Slight edema around the treated regions. Typically fades after a few hours to a day or two.
Warmth or Heat Sensation
:
The radiofrequency energy causes a warming feeling in the treated areas. Usually, it subsides within a few hours of treatment.
Tingling or Mild Discomfort
:
A tingling feeling or minor discomfort during or soon following the treatment. Temporarily, generally resolves quickly after the session.
Dryness or Flaking
:
As the skin renews, it may become dry or flaky. Symptoms may appear a few days after treatment and normally fade as the skin heals.
When to Seek Medical Advice
Prolonged Redness or Swelling
: If redness or swelling lasts more than a few days or worsens,
consult
your doctor for guidance.
Unusual Symptoms
: If you have any unusual symptoms, such as extreme pain, blistering, or evidence of infection (such as increased redness, warmth, or pus), seek medical assistance right once.
Allergic Reactions
:
If you develop signs of an allergic reaction, such as itching, rash, or difficulty breathing, seek emergency medical care.
