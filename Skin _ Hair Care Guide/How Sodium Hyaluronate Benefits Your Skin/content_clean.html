Sodium Hyaluronate vs. Hyaluronic Acid: Deciphering the Differences

Sodium hyaluronate and hyaluronic acid have similar characteristics. Both attract and bind to water, making them ideal for moisturizing the skin. There are several distinctions. For example, hyaluronic acid hydrates the skin's surface more effectively than sodium hyaluronate, which can penetrate deeper layers due to its lower molecular weight. Sodium hyaluronate is also more stable than hyaluronic acid and less prone to oxidation, which is why it is widely used in skincare products. Companies typically do not distinguish between hyaluronic acid and sodium hyaluronate on product labels. Because "hyaluronic acid" is a more familiar term, it may be used on the label when a product contains sodium hyaluronate.
