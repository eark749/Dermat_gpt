Sodium Hyaluronate vs. Hyaluronic Acid: Deciphering the Differences
Sodium hyaluronate and
hyaluronic acid
have similar characteristics. Both attract and bind to water, making them ideal for moisturizing the skin. There are several distinctions. For example, hyaluronic acid hydrates the skin's surface more effectively than sodium hyaluronate, which can penetrate deeper layers due to its lower molecular weight. Sodium hyaluronate is also more stable than hyaluronic acid and less prone to oxidation, which is why it is widely used in skincare products. Companies typically do not distinguish between hyaluronic acid and sodium hyaluronate on product labels. Because "hyaluronic acid" is a more familiar term, it may be used on the label when a product contains sodium hyaluronate.

Dispelling Myths and Misconceptions About Sodium Hyaluronate
Sodium hyaluronate is frequently misinterpreted or misunderstood, with many myths and misconceptions surrounding it:
Myth
: Sodium hyaluronate is equivalent to hyaluronic acid.
Reality
: Sodium hyaluronate is a type of hyaluronic acid used in cosmetics and skincare.  It provides numerous advantages, including the ability to smooth wrinkles and reduce inflammation, among others. Hyaluronic acid (HA) is a chemical that occurs naturally in the body, including your skin and joint fluid.
Myth
: Oral sodium hyaluronate supplements are ineffective for skin care.
Reality
: Long-term application of hyaluronic acid serum to the skin or as an oral supplement can improve overall skin health. It is also great for increasing overall skin flexibility and elasticity (making it stretchier and softer).
Myth
: Supplements containing sodium hyaluronate are unsafe.
Reality
: Hyaluronic acid is unlikely to be harmful if used properly.  Allergic reactions are possible but exceedingly rare. When applied to the skin, hyaluronic acid is most likely safe when used properly.
Myth
: Sodium hyaluronate can cause allergic reactions.
Reality
: This medication rarely causes severe allergic reactions.  However, if you notice any of the following symptoms of a severe allergic reaction, seek medical attention right away: rash, itching/swelling (especially of the face, tongue, or throat), extreme dizziness, or difficulty breathing. This is not an exhaustive list of potential side effects.
Myth
: Sodium hyaluronate pills provide quick results.
Reality
: Although topical therapies may produce faster results, oral supplements frequently require long-term use to detect significant changes in skin moisture and suppleness.
