The Procedure: What to Expect During and After a Face Peel

A face peel, whether with glycolic acid, salicylic acid, lactic acid, or another type of chemical peel, requires a precise process and post-treatment maintenance to provide the best results with the fewest side effects. Here's what to expect during and after a facial peel:

During the Procedure:

  • Before the peel, your skin will be carefully washed to eliminate any makeup, debris, or oils. Some practitioners may use a pre-peel solution to degrease the skin and improve peel penetration.

  • The peel solution, which contains the chosen acid (e.g., glycolic, salicylic, lactic), will be delicately applied to your skin using a brush or cotton applicator. The solution is placed on the skin for a particular amount of time, which is dictated by the peel's kind and concentration.

  • During the peel application, you may experience a mild tingling or burning sensation, especially with stronger peels. Your practitioner may use a fan to provide cooling and comfort.

  • Some peels require neutralization with water or a neutralizing solution to stop the acid's activity and prevent over-exfoliation.

  • After the peel is completed, your skin may be soothed with a calming serum or moisturizer to reduce any immediate discomfort or redness.

After the Procedure:

  • Your skin may appear slightly red or flushed just after the peel. This is normal and should resolve within a few hours to a day, depending on the degree of the peel.

  • Your practitioner will provide you with precise recommendations for how to care for your skin after the peel. This may involve avoiding sun exposure, using gentle skincare products, and moisturizing regularly.

  • Depending on the depth of the peel, you may see minor flaking or peeling of the skin in the days following treatment. This is a natural aspect of the skin's renewal process and usually goes away within a few days or weeks.

  • It is essential to protect your skin from sun exposure following a peel because it will be more vulnerable to UV rays. Use sunscreen every day and avoid excessive sun exposure.

  • Depending on your skin's condition and treatment goals, you may need multiple peel sessions spaced several weeks apart to achieve optimal results.

The Peeling Process: Techniques and Care

Chemical peel treatments rely heavily on the peeling process and post-peel maintenance to provide the best results while minimizing potential negative effects. Here's all you need to know about the peeling procedure, the procedures utilized during the treatment, and how to care for your skin afterward:

  • Peel Care Techniques:

    1. Before applying the peel solution, your skin will be cleansed thoroughly to remove any dirt, oil, or makeup. Some practitioners may also use a degreasing solution to enhance peel penetration.

    2. The peel solution, which contains the desired acid (e.g., glycolic, salicylic, lactic), will be applied evenly to your skin with a brush or cotton applicator. The concentration and length of the application will be determined by the peel type and your skin's sensitivity level.

    3. Some peels, especially stronger ones, may require neutralization with water or a specific neutralizing solution to stop the acid's activity and prevent over-exfoliation.

    4. During the application, your practitioner will monitor your skin's response closely to ensure the peel is working effectively without causing excessive irritation.

  • Post-Peel Care:
    •

    1. After the peel solution is removed or neutralized, your skin may feel tight or dry. Apply a gentle, hydrating moisturizer to soothe and replenish moisture.

    2. Avoid sun exposure just after a peel because your skin will be more sensitive to UV radiation. To protect your skin from UV damage, apply a broad-spectrum sunscreen with an SPF of 30 or higher every day.

    3. Avoid using harsh skincare products, exfoliants, or makeup for several days after the peel. Use moderate, non-irritating skincare products prescribed by your practitioner.

    4. Drink plenty of water to keep your skin hydrated from within. Hydration supports skin healing and recovery post-peel.

    5. Do not pick at or scrub your skin as it begins to peel. Allow the peeling process to occur naturally to avoid irritation and potential scarring.

    6. Your practitioner will provide specific post-peel instructions tailored to your skin type and the type of peel performed. Follow these instructions carefully for the best results and to minimize side effects.

    Post-Peel Care: Ensuring the Best Results

    Post-peel care is critical for getting the best results and keeping your skin healthy after a chemical peel. Here's a complete guide to caring for your skin after a peel treatment:

    1. Follow your practitioner's instructions: Always follow the exact post-peel guidelines given by your skincare specialist. They will provide you personalized advise based on the sort of peel you underwent and your skin's specific requirements.

    2. Moisturize regularly: After a peel, apply a light, moisturizing moisturizer to soothe and nourish the skin. Choose formulations that are non-comedogenic and appropriate for your skin type.

    3. Avoid Sun Exposure: Protect your skin from direct sunlight and UV rays. Use a broad-spectrum sunscreen with an SPF of 30 or more every day, even on cloudy days. When spending time outside, reapply sunscreen every two hours and use protective clothing and accessories like hats and sunglasses.

    4. Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water to keep your skin hydrated from the inside. Hydration facilitates skin repair and improves overall skin health.

    5. Gentle Cleansing: Wash your skin with a moderate, non-foaming cleanser. Avoid strong cleansers or washes that may irritate the skin after peeling.

    6. Avoid Exfoliation and Harsh Products: For at least one week after the peel, do not use exfoliating scrubs, retinoids, or other active skincare products. Allow your skin to heal naturally and without further aggravation.

    7. Do Not Pick at Peeling Skin: If your skin begins to peel or flake, resist the temptation to pick at it. Allow the skin to shed normally to prevent injury or irritation.

    8. Patience During the Healing Process: Be patient during the peeling and recuperation phases. It is usual for the skin to go through a rejuvenation period following a peel, and the results will gradually become obvious over time.

    9. Avoid Sweating and Heat: Limit your exposure to heat sources, such as hot showers, saunas, and steam rooms, for a few days after peeling. Sweating excessively can irritate the skin and delay recovery.

    10. Maintain a Consistent Skincare Routine: Once your skin has completely recovered, return to the usual skincare routine prescribed by your practitioner. Use gentle cleansers, moisturizers, and sunscreens that are appropriate for your skin type to maintain and improve the results of your peel.

    11. Schedule Follow-Up Appointments: Schedule follow-up appointments with your skin care specialist for any extra treatments or evaluations that are recommended. Regular maintenance and follow-up treatments can help maximize and extend the advantages of your chemical peel.

    12. Monitor for Adverse Reactions: Keep an eye out for any signs of adverse reactions, such as persistent redness, swelling, or discomfort. Contact your practitioner immediately if you experience any concerning symptoms post-peel.
