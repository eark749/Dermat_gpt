Hair oiling is an excellent technique to nourish and preserve your hair, particularly during the monsoon season, when humidity and rain can create a variety of hair problems. This blog discusses some vital hair oiling suggestions during the rainy season.

Why Hair Oiling is Crucial During Monsoon

During the monsoon, the scalp accumulates dirt, sweat, and extra oil more quickly. To fight this, wash your hair more often than usual, especially if you have an oily scalp. However, avoid overwashing, since this can remove essential oils and produce dryness in the ends.

Benefits of Hair Oiling in Humid Weather

The monsoon season is characterized by high humidity levels, which can cause your hair to become frizzy and drab. Oiling your hair at this time might assist to nourish and protect it from the elements. Here are many benefits of oiling your hair during the monsoon season.

  1. Keeps your scalp healthy: Oiling your scalp during the monsoon season can help prevent dandruff and other scalp issues. It also keeps your scalp hydrated, preventing it from becoming dry and flaky.

  2. Nourishes your hair: Applying oil to your hair will nourish and condition it.  This is especially important during the monsoon season when humidity can cause hair damage.

  3. Prevents frizz: Monsoon season can be harsh on your hair. The humidity in the air can cause frizz, and prolonged exposure to water can strip your hair of its natural oils. This is why it is important to oil your hair during the monsoon. Hair oil can help to keep your hair hydrated and nourished while also taming frizz and creating a smooth and sleek appearance.

  4. Protects your hair: Applying oil to your hair can help it resist the damaging effects of humidity and rain.

  5. Makes your hair easier to manage: Oiling your hair can help you style and manage it more easily during the monsoon season.

Choosing the Right Hair Oil for Monsoon

We believe that monsoon hair care begins with smart oiling! Here's how to make it work for you.

  • Choose the Right Oil: During the monsoon season, use lighter oils such as coconut oil, jojoba oil, and almond oil. These oils penetrate the scalp easily and do not weigh down your hair.

  • Focus on Scalp Massage: Gently massage your scalp with oil to improve blood circulation and hair growth.

  • Leave-in Time: Unlike in the hot summer months, do not leave oil on your hair overnight during the monsoon. Apply the oil one to two hours before washing your hair.

  • Washing is Essential: During the monsoon season, regular washing is required to remove excess moisture and prevent fungal infections. Use a mild shampoo and conditioner that is appropriate for your hair type.

Natural vs. Commercial Oils

  1. They do not harm your health: The first and most important point is that pure oils are completely safe for consumption. The majority of them are edible, so you don't have to worry about using them on your skin or hair. Commercial oils, on the other hand, contain a diverse range of chemical components, some of which are known to be dangerous and others of which the effects are unknown. Fragrances, preservatives, and stabilizers are all examples of common additives. These increase the product's shelf life and are easily absorbed by the skin. These have a short-term effect on skin irritation, but they can also cause chronic disorders such as cancer. The more chemical compounds the product has, the more likely it is to cause an allergic reaction.

  2. They don’t have deceptive labels: Have you seen how beautiful the commercial oil bottles are? The images of diverse plants and flowers, as well as exotic names such as 'Ethiopian', 'Moroccan', and 'Jamaican', can captivate anyone. This is also the primary component of many hair serums. Conversely, silicones tend to build up in the hair, so the longer you use them, the more they accumulate. Because they act as a barrier on top of the hair, they can trap dirt. Silicones are also notoriously difficult to remove, so you should avoid them in the first place.

  4. Pure oils do not contain mineral oils: Pure oils are extracted directly from nuts and seeds, so you know they contain only the natural plant ingredients. Commercial hair oils, on the other hand, frequently contain mineral oil, which accounts for a significant portion of the oil content. Mineral oil provides a smoothing effect, which is why it is used in commercial hair products. Now imagine doing that to your hair!

Effective Hair Oiling Techniques

Massaging your scalp and hair improves blood circulation, which promotes hair growth. Follow the steps outlined below to properly oil your hair.

  1. Select the appropriate carrier oil:  Carrier oils may be used alone or in conjunction with essential oils. Some commonly used carrier oils include coconut oil, jojoba oil, grapeseed, olive, almond, and avocado oil. If you have a greasy scalp, use light oils such as grapeseed or almond.

  2. Select your essential oil: Choose an essential oil based on its properties, as well as your hair type and needs. Essential oils such as peppermint, lavender, and sandalwood must be diluted in carrier oils because they can be too potent and cause allergic reactions. To dilute any essential oil by 2.5%, use 15 drops per 6 teaspoons of carrier oil.

  3. Heat the oil: Heat the oils for a few seconds until they are warm. Warm oil allows for deeper penetration through your hair cuticles and seals them, keeping your scalp moisturized.

  4. Massage your scalp: Massage the oil into your scalp for a few minutes in circular motions. Work your way through the entire scalp for 10-15 minutes. After you have finished with the scalp, gently work your way to the ends of your hair.

  5. Wrap your hair in a warm cloth: Tie your hair into a bun and wrap a warm cloth around your brow. This won't clog pores and cuticles, allowing the oils to penetrate deeper into your scalp and hair follicles.

  6. Rinse it off thoroughly: After applying your oil, leave it on overnight before thoroughly washing it off with shampoo the following day. Rinse your hair with either regular or cold water.

How to Apply Hair Oil Correctly

Apply hair oil from the mid-lengths to the ends of your hair as a pre-shampoo treatment, leaving it on for 5-10 minutes before shampooing, conditioning, and styling as usual. You can also oil your hair the night before you wash it to let the oil work its magic overnight.
