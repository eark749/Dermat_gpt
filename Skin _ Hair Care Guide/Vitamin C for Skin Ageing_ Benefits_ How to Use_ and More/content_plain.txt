What is Vitamin C
A water-soluble vitamin that is vital for human health is
ascorbic acid
, also known as vitamin C. It is necessary for the production of
collagen
, the absorption of
iron
, the maintenance of strong bones, teeth, and skin, as well as the proper operation of the immune system.
Vitamin C
must be obtained through diet or supplements because the body does not produce it. Citrus fruits, berries, kiwifruit, tomatoes,
broccoli
, and leafy greens are high in vitamin C.
Scurvy is caused by a vitamin C deficiency, which causes fatigue, weakness, joint and muscle aches, and skin changes. A balanced and healthy diet rich in vitamin C rich foods, on the other hand, can help prevent deficiency and promote overall health.

Protecting Skin from Sun Damage with Vitamin C
Vitamin C is an excellent addition to your skincare routine for preventing sun damage. Here is how it works:
Antioxidant Protection
: Vitamin C is a powerful antioxidant that helps neutralise free radicals produced by UV radiation from the sun.  Free radicals can damage skin cells, causing premature ageing,
wrinkles
, and pigmentation. By scavenging these free radicals, vitamin C helps prevent oxidative stress and reduces the risk of sunburn and long-term sun damage.
Increasing Collagen Synthesis
: Vitamin C is essential for the synthesis of collagen, the protein that keeps skin elastic and firm.  UV rays can hasten the breakdown of collagen, causing wrinkles and sagging. By stimulating collagen production, vitamin C helps support skin structure and resilience, minimizing the visible effects of sun damage.
Brightening and Even Skin Tone
: Sun exposure can lead to uneven pigmentation, such as dark spots, sunspots, and hyperpigmentation.  Vitamin C brightens the skin and helps fade existing pigmentation while preventing the formation of new spots. Regular vitamin C intake can lead to a more even skin tone and a brighter complexion.
Sunscreen Efficacy Enhancement
: When combined with sunscreen, vitamin C can increase its effectiveness.  While sunscreen creates a physical barrier to protect the skin from UV rays, vitamin C adds antioxidant protection and aids in the repair of sun damage. Applying a vitamin C serum underneath sunscreen can increase its effectiveness and provide all-around sun protection.
Reduced Inflammation
: Sun exposure can cause inflammation in the skin, resulting in redness, irritation, and sensitivity.  Vitamin C has anti-inflammatory properties that soothe the skin and relieve sunburn symptoms. It can also help the skin's natural repair process, resulting in faster healing of sun-damaged skin.
To incorporate vitamin C into your sun protection skincare routine, look for a serum or moisturiser that contains stable and effective forms of vitamin C, such as ascorbic acid or sodium ascorbyl phosphate. Apply it in the morning before sunscreen to maximize its protective benefits. Furthermore, maintain sun-safe behaviours to further shield your skin from UV ray damage, like wearing protective clothes, looking for shade, and frequently reapplying sunscreen.

Vitamin C is a powerful antioxidant that aids in the neutralisation of free radicals produced by environmental factors such as pollution, UV radiation, and oxidative stress.  Free radicals can harm skin cells and cause dullness and uneven skin tone, as well as premature ageing. Optimizing Your Skincare Routine with Vitamin C
Optimizing your skincare routine with vitamin C entails incorporating this powerful antioxidant in a way that maximises its skin benefits. Here's how to do it successfully:
Choose the Right Product
: Look for a vitamin C serum or moisturiser that includes a stable and effective form of vitamin C, such as ascorbic acid, sodium ascorbyl phosphate, or tetrahexyldecyl ascorbate. Choose a product with a vitamin C concentration of 10% to 20% for maximum effectiveness without irritation.
Apply in the Morning
: Vitamin C is most effective when applied in the morning as part of your daily skincare routine.  Apply a few drops of vitamin C serum to clean, dry skin after cleansing and toning. Allow the serum to fully absorb before applying any additional skincare products, such as moisturiser or sunscreen.
Layering with Other Products
: Vitamin C can be combined with other skincare ingredients, but it must be applied correctly for maximum effectiveness.  After cleansing and toning, apply vitamin C serum first, followed by any lightweight serums (such as hyaluronic acid), and finally moisturiser. Finish with sunscreen during the day to protect your skin from UV rays.
Consistency is Key
: To see noticeable results, include vitamin C in your daily skincare routine. Incorporate it into your morning skincare routine and use it every day to keep the benefits for your skin. Over time, regular vitamin C use can help improve skin tone, texture, and radiance.
Be patient
: Using vitamin C in your skincare routine may take some time to yield visible results.  Expect to see significant changes in the appearance of your skin after a few weeks to several months of consistent use. Remember that skincare requires a long-term commitment, and patience is essential for achieving the desired results.
Store properly
: Vitamin C is light, air, and heat sensitive, which can cause it to degrade and lose effectiveness.  To avoid oxidation, store vitamin C products in a cool, dark place away from direct sunlight, and tightly seal the container after each use.
Pair with Sunscreen
: Vitamin C provides antioxidant protection against free radicals caused by UV radiation from the sun, but it is not a replacement for sunscreen.  Apply sunscreen as the last step in your morning skincare routine to protect your skin from sun damage and premature ageing.
By incorporating vitamin C into your skincare routine correctly and consistently, you can maximise its benefits for brighter, more radiant skin.

Combining Vitamin C with Other Skincare Ingredients
Combining vitamin C with other skincare products can increase its effectiveness and provide overall skin benefits. Here are some tips for including vitamin C in your skincare routine alongside other products:
When layering skincare products, apply your vitamin C serum first, following cleansing and toning.  Vitamin C serums are typically lightweight in texture, allowing them to easily penetrate the skin and deliver active ingredients to the skin's surface. Applying vitamin C serum first ensures the best absorption and effectiveness.
Allow your vitamin C serum to fully absorb into your skin before applying any additional products.  Give it a few minutes to dry and settle on your skin before proceeding with additional skincare steps. This allows the vitamin C serum to work its magic without interference from other products.
When layering multiple skincare products, begin with lightweight or watery-textured options before progressing to thicker or heavier formulations. For example, after applying your vitamin C serum, follow up with lightweight serums, essences, or treatments before applying heavier creams or moisturizers. This helps prevent product pilling and ensures optimal absorption of each product.
Vitamin C is most effective at slightly acidic pH levels, usually between 3 and 4. Avoid combining vitamin C with high-pH products like AHAs (alpha hydroxy acids) or BHAs (beta hydroxy acids), as these can neutralise vitamin C's acidity and reduce its effectiveness. To avoid potential interactions, use these products separately or on alternate days.
Vitamin C works in tandem with other antioxidants to provide superior protection against environmental damage and free radicals. Consider combining vitamin C with other antioxidant-rich ingredients, such as vitamin E, ferulic acid, green tea extract, or niacinamide, to maximise antioxidant support.
If you use vitamin C in the morning, always finish with a broad-spectrum sunscreen with an SPF of 30 or higher. Sunscreen protects against UV rays and helps to prevent sun damage, photoaging, and dark spots. Apply sunscreen as the final step in your skincare routine, after all other products have been absorbed by the skin.
By following these guidelines, you can effectively combine vitamin C with other skincare products to maximise its benefits and achieve healthier, more luminous skin. Pay attention to how your skin responds to different combinations, and adjust your routine as needed to address your specific skincare concerns and goals.

Addressing Common Questions About Vitamin C Usage
Here are some frequently asked questions about using vitamin C in skincare, along with their answers:
Can I combine vitamin C with other active ingredients, such as retinol or AHAs?
Yes, vitamin C can be combined with other active ingredients such as retinol (vitamin A) or alpha hydroxy acids (AHAs), but you must introduce them gradually and monitor how your skin reacts. Begin by using them on alternate days or at different times of day to avoid irritation, especially if you have sensitive skin. You can also layer them with hydrating products to reduce dryness and sensitivity.
When is the best time to use vitamin C in my skincare routine?
Vitamin C is most commonly used in the morning as part of a daily skincare routine. Apply after cleansing and toning, but before moisturiser and sunscreen. This allows vitamin C to provide antioxidant protection against environmental damage throughout the day, increasing the effectiveness of your sunscreen.
Can I use vitamin C if I have sensitive skin?
Yes, vitamin C can be used on sensitive skin, but it is critical to use gentle formulations with low concentrations and few additional ingredients to reduce the risk of irritation. Before applying vitamin C to your entire face, perform a patch test and gradually increase your frequency of use to allow your skin to adjust. If you experience irritation or sensitivity, stop using the product and consult a dermatologist.
How long does it take to see results from using vitamin C?
Vitamin C results can vary depending on the individual skin concerns and the product's specific formulation. Some people may notice improvements in skin brightness and clarity after a few weeks of consistent use, whereas areas such as hyperpigmentation or fine lines may take longer to show visible results. To maintain the benefits of vitamin C for your skin, you must incorporate it into your skincare routine regularly.
Can vitamin C be used on acne-prone skin?
Yes, vitamin C can help acne-prone skin because of its antioxidant and anti-inflammatory properties. It can reduce inflammation, speed up healing, and protect against post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation (PIH) caused by acne lesions.  Look for vitamin C serums or moisturisers that are made with lightweight, non-comedogenic ingredients to avoid clogging pores or exacerbating acne.
Does vitamin C increase my skin's sensitivity to the sun?
Vitamin C does not make your skin more sensitive to sunlight. It offers antioxidant protection against UV damage. However, it is critical to wear sunscreen during the day, especially when using vitamin C products, to protect your skin from sunburn and premature ageing caused by ultraviolet radiation. When exposed to the sun, apply a broad-spectrum sunscreen with an SPF of 30 or higher and reapply every two hours.

Can Vitamin C Irritate Sensitive Skin?
Yes, vitamin C can irritate sensitive skin, especially if used in high concentrations or if the formulation contains other irritants. Here are some of the reasons why vitamin C can irritate sensitive skin:
High Concentration
: Some vitamin C products contain high levels of ascorbic acid, which can be harsh on sensitive skin.  Higher vitamin C concentrations may cause stinging, redness, or irritation, particularly if your skin is not used to it.
Low pH Formulas
: Vitamin C serums with a low pH (3.5 or lower) can be more acidic and may irritate sensitive skin.  While a slightly acidic pH can improve vitamin C stability and efficacy, it can also increase the risk of irritation, particularly if your skin barrier is compromised.
Additional Ingredients
: Some vitamin C formulations include fragrances, preservatives, or botanical extracts, which may cause irritation or allergic reactions in sensitive individuals.  If you have sensitive skin, you should carefully read the ingredient list and avoid products that contain known irritants.
Overuse or Incorrect Application
: Using vitamin C too frequently or too generously can overwhelm sensitive skin and irritate it.  It is critical to begin slowly and gradually incorporating vitamin C into your skincare routine, using it every other day or a few times per week at first, and monitoring how your skin reacts.
Incompatibility with Other Products
: Vitamin C may react with certain skincare ingredients, such as retinoids or exfoliating acids, increasing sensitivity or irritation.  If you are incorporating other active ingredients into your skincare routine, start slowly and monitor how your skin reacts.
If you have sensitive skin and want to incorporate vitamin C into your skincare routine, follow these tips to reduce the risk of irritation:
Start with a low concentration of vitamin C (5% to 10%) and gradually increase it as your skin tolerates it.
Look for vitamin C formulations with a pH closer to neutral (5.5 to 6.5) to reduce acidity and irritation.
Before applying vitamin C to the entire face, perform a patch test to ensure there are no adverse reactions.
If you experience irritation or sensitivity, stop using the product and see a dermatologist for personalised recommendations. They may recommend alternative vitamin C formulations or skincare products that are more suitable for sensitive skin.
