Understanding Hair Fall in Women

Humans typically lose 50-100 hairs every day. Hair loss is a natural phenomenon in which some hair falls out, and some grows. If the balance is disrupted, hair loses due to hair falling out rather than increasing. The medical term for hair loss is "alopecia." Hair grows on practically all of your skin surfaces except the palms of your hands, soles of your feet, lips, and eyelids. Vellus hair is distinguished by its light, delicate texture and short length. Terminal hair grows thicker, darker, and longer.

Common Causes of Hair Fall in Women

Health issues, such as abnormal thyroid, anemia, vitamin insufficiency, hormonal changes, medications, supplements, and genetic susceptibility, are also responsible for the hair wall in women.

How Ingredients Help in Reducing Hair Fall

  • Biotin, also known as Vitamin B7, promotes keratin formation. It strengthens and improves hair elasticity, decreasing breakage and increasing hair health.

  • Keratin is a structural protein that is a vital component of hair. It can help repair and strengthen hair fibers, making them more resistant to damage and breakage.

  • Collagen promotes skin flexibility and moisture. Hair care products can use collagen to encourage hair structure and strength, minimize hair loss, and create healthier, more robust hair.

  • Minoxidil is a topical medicine that stimulates hair follicles, improves blood flow, and promotes hair regeneration. It is often used to treat androgenetic alopecia (pattern baldness).

  • Caffeine can stimulate hair follicles, increasing blood flow to the scalp and thus encouraging hair growth. It may also help reduce the effects of dihydrotestosterone (DHT), a hormone associated with hair loss.

  • Saw palmetto reduces the synthesis of DHT, a hormone that causes hair loss in men and women. It may aid in minimizing hair thinning and improving hair health.

  • Hyaluronic acid has moisturizing qualities. It helps to maintain the moisture balance in the scalp and hair, reducing dry scalp, dry hair and breakage.

  • Nettle extract contains vitamins and minerals that benefit the scalp. Its anti-inflammatory qualities may help prevent hair loss and enhance scalp health.

  • Argan hair oil contains fatty acids and vitamin E, which nourish and hydrate the hair and scalp. It reduces frizz, strengthens the hair, and prevents damage.

  • Zinc is an essential mineral for hair development because it promotes cell division and protein production. It also promotes scalp health and may reduce hair loss caused by zinc deficiency.

    Proper Application Techniques

    1. Wet your hair: We're all aware of this, but when it comes to a hair loss shampoo, it's best not to take chances. When you dampen your hair before applying shampoo, ensure all portions of your hair are thoroughly moist. When you skip a step and only use shampoo, the excess concentrated in one location might dry out your hair and scalp.

    2. Apply on your scalp and hair: Use your hair to apply it to your shafts in a circular motion and lather up the rest of your hair. However, do not rub it through your hair lengths, as this might cause tearing. After you've rinsed off the shampoo, add a conditioner to repair your hair length.

    3. Rinse thoroughly and ensure you’re squeaky clean: We all hurry into this stage and commit the cardinal sin of not knowing how to use shampoo. Apply shampoo to all sides of the hair shafts and rinse them. Make sure to cover every surface to avoid dryness. Rub your scalp with your fingers to get a thorough cleansing.
