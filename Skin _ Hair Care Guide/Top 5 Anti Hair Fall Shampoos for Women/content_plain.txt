Understanding Hair Fall in Women
Humans typically lose 50-100 hairs every day.
Hair loss
is a natural phenomenon in which some hair falls out, and some grows. If the balance is disrupted, hair loses due to hair falling out rather than increasing. The medical term for hair loss is "alopecia." Hair grows on practically all of your skin surfaces except the palms of your hands, soles of your feet, lips, and eyelids. Vellus hair is distinguished by its light, delicate texture and short length. Terminal hair grows thicker, darker, and longer.
Common Causes of Hair Fall in Women
Health issues, such as abnormal thyroid, anemia, vitamin insufficiency, hormonal changes, medications, supplements, and genetic susceptibility, are also responsible for the hair wall in women.
