Keratin treatment is a hair care solution that has been gaining popularity among people who want to achieve sleek, smooth, and frizz-free hair. The process involves applying a keratin-based product to the hair and using heat to activate it, which helps to smooth and straighten the hair. This treatment can last several weeks and provides a range of benefits, such as reducing frizz, improving hair texture, and making styling easier and faster. Whether you have curly, wavy, or straight hair, a keratin treatment can give you the smooth and healthy locks you've always wanted. In this blog, we'll explore the benefits of keratin treatment, side effects, if any, and how Keratin can help you achieve the hair of your dreams.

Keratin Treatment for Hair Growth

Keratin treatment is a hair smoothing and straightening treatment that uses keratin, a fibrous protein found naturally in the hair, skin, and nails, to improve the condition and appearance of hair. It works by penetrating the hair shaft and coating the hair strands with a layer of keratin protein, which helps to repair the damage, add shine, reduce frizz, and improve the overall health and strength of the hair. While some claim that keratin treatment can aid in hair growth, it’s more commonly associated with hair smoothing and straightening. If you are interested in promoting hair growth, it may be more effective to focus on maintaining a healthy diet, reducing stress, and using hair care products along with a Keratin treatment kit, which is specifically formulated to promote hair growth.

Benefits of Keratin Treatment

Some benefits of Keratin treatment hair are:

  • Improves hair texture: Keratin treatment can make hair smoother, shinier, and more manageable, making it easier to style and reduce frizz.

  • Repairs hair damage: Keratin treatment can help repair damaged hair by filling in gaps in the hair cuticle and providing a protective layer to the hair shaft.

  • Protects against environmental damage: The added layer of keratin protein can protect hair from environmental stressors such as heat styling and UV exposure.

  • Reduces styling time: Keratin-treated hair is typically easier to style and maintain, which can save time in the morning and reduce the need for frequent touch-ups.

  • Long-lasting results: Depending on the type of keratin treatment and individual hair type, the results can last anywhere from 3 to 6 months.

  • Safe for all hair types: Keratin treatments are suitable for all hair types, including colour-treated hair, and can help improve the overall health and appearance of the hair.

Keratin Treatment Process

The keratin treatment process typically consists of the following steps:

  1. Preparation: Before the treatment, hair should be cleaned and dried.  Some salons may recommend trimming the hair to remove split ends and damaged hair, while others may suggest leaving the hair at its natural length. 

  2. Application of Keratin Solution: The stylist will apply a keratin solution to the hair section by section, ensuring that each strand is thoroughly coated.  The solution usually contains a combination of keratin protein and other conditioning ingredients.

  3. Heat styling: After applying the solution, blow-dry the hair to remove excess moisture before flat-ironing to seal the keratin into the shaft.  This step is important because it activates the keratin and keeps the hair smooth, shiny, and frizz-free.

  4. Waiting period: Following the treatment, the stylist will typically advise against washing, styling, or exposing the hair to moisture for at least 24 to 48 hours to allow the keratin to fully absorb into the hair.

  5. After-care: Following the waiting period, it is critical to follow the recommended after-care instructions, which may include using sulphate-free shampoos, avoiding heat styling, and using protective products to help maintain the treatment's results and extend its lifespan.

It is important to note that the specific process and after-care instructions will differ depending on the type of keratin treatment used, as well as the individual hair type and desired results. It is critical to discuss the procedure and after-care instructions with your stylist to ensure that the treatment is carried out correctly and that you achieve the desired results.

How Long Does Keratin Treatment Last?

The longevity of a keratin treatment is determined by several factors, including:

  • Keratin treatments vary in their longevity and maintenance requirements, with some lasting longer than others.

  • The texture, porosity, and overall health of the hair can all have an impact on the treatment's longevity, with healthier hair usually retaining the results for longer.

  • Proper after-care, such as using sulphate-free shampoos, avoiding heat styling, and using protective products, can help the treatment last longer.

Most keratin treatments last between 3 and 6 months, with some lasting even longer with proper maintenance. However, the duration of a treatment is determined by the individual hair type and the type of treatment used. It is important to note that regular touch-ups and maintenance, as well as proper after-care, can help the treatment last longer and keep the hair smooth, shiny, and frizz-free for as long as possible.

Preparing Your Hair for Keratin Treatment

Preparing your hair properly before a keratin treatment can help ensure the treatment's effectiveness and longevity. Here are some steps for preparing your hair:

  1. Clarifying Shampoo: A few days before your keratin treatment appointment, wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup, oils, and impurities.  This will allow the keratin treatment to better adhere to your hair strands.

  2. Avoid Chemical Treatments: Do not colour, perm, or chemically treat your hair for at least two weeks before the keratin treatment.  Chemical treatments can weaken the hair and disrupt the keratin bonding process.

  3. Trim Split Ends: Before your keratin treatment appointment, get a trim or haircut to remove any split ends or damaged hair.  This will help to ensure that the keratin treatment is distributed evenly and effectively throughout your hair strands. 

  4. Moisturize: Deep condition your hair in the days preceding your keratin treatment to ensure that it is properly moisturized.  Healthy, moisturised hair will absorb the keratin treatment and retain its smoothness and shine.

  5. Avoid Heat Styling: In the days leading up to your keratin treatment, try to limit your use of hot tools.  Heat can damage the hair cuticle, reducing the effectiveness of the keratin treatment.

  6. Consult With Your Stylist: Before your keratin treatment appointment, speak with your stylist about any concerns or questions you may have.  They can offer personalised advice based on your hair type and condition.

  7. Prepare for Aftercare: Review the post-treatment care instructions provided by your stylist.  This could include not washing your hair or wearing hair ties for a while after the treatment to allow the keratin to fully bond to your hair.

By following these steps to prepare your hair for a keratin treatment, you can help ensure the best results and keep your hair smooth and manageable longer.

Aftercare Tips for Keratin Treatment

Aftercare is essential for maintaining the effectiveness of your keratin treatment and keeping your hair smooth, shiny, and healthy. Here are some essential after-care tips:

  1. Most keratin treatments require that you wait a certain amount of time before washing your hair. This waiting period allows the keratin to fully bond with your hair strands. Follow your stylist's instructions for when to wash your hair after the treatment.

  2. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner for keratin-treated hair. Sulphates can strip keratin from your hair, shortening the life of your treatment. Look for products marked "sulfate-free" or "safe for keratin-treated hair."

  3. Swimming pool chlorine and ocean saltwater can damage keratin-treated hair. If you swim frequently, wear a swim cap or rinse your hair with fresh water before and after swimming to reduce exposure to chlorine and saltwater.

  4. Limit the use of heat-styling tools like blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons. Excessive heat can cause keratin to degrade more quickly, resulting in dryness and damage. When heat styling is required, use a heat protectant spray to reduce damage.

  5. Avoid tying up your hair in tight hairstyles or using hair accessories like hair ties, clips, or headbands that can cause creases or indentations. These can impair the smoothness of your keratin-treated hair.

  6. Sleep with a satin or silk pillowcase, or wrap your hair in a silk scarf or bonnet. These materials reduce friction, preventing frizz and damage to your keratin-treated hair.

  7. Depending on the type of keratin treatment you receive and your hair's growth rate, you may require touch-up treatments every few months to keep the results. Consult your stylist to determine the best schedule for touch-ups.

  8. To keep your hair hydrated and nourished, incorporate deep conditioning treatments into your routine regularly. Look for deep conditioning masks designed for keratin-treated hair.

  9. Protect your hair from environmental stressors like sun exposure, pollution, and harsh weather. Wear a hat or use a UV protectant spray when spending time outdoors to prevent damage and color fading.

  10. Listen to your stylist's recommendations for aftercare products and maintenance routines based on your hair type and the type of keratin treatment you received.

By following these aftercare tips, you can extend the life of your keratin treatment and keep your hair smooth, shiny, and healthy. 

Potential Side Effects of Keratin Treatment

Keratin treatments are a popular way to smooth and straighten hair, but as with any chemical treatment, they can cause side effects. Some typical keratin treatment side effects include:

  1. Scalp irritation and itching: The chemicals used in the treatment may cause itching and redness on the scalp.

  2. Allergic reactions: Some people may have an allergic reaction to the ingredients in the keratin treatment, resulting in symptoms like hives, itching, and trouble breathing.

  3. Hair damage: Excessive use of keratin treatments or harsh chemical-based products can cause hair damage such as dryness, breakage, and split ends.

  4. Fumes: The chemicals used in keratin treatments can produce toxic fumes that can harm the eyes, skin, and respiratory system.  It is critical to administer the treatment in a well-ventilated area and to wear gloves and a mask.

  5. Unsatisfactory results: In some cases, the keratin treatment may not achieve the desired results, leaving hair frizzy or dull, or it may not last as long as expected.

Always perform a patch test before undergoing any chemical treatment, and carefully follow the instructions. If you have any keratin treatment side effects or concerns, consult a hair care professional.

Managing Irritation and Allergies

Managing irritation and allergies, particularly in the context of hair treatments such as keratin, necessitates careful consideration and proactive measures. Here's how you can manage them effectively:

  1. Identify Triggers: Determine which specific ingredients or factors cause irritation or allergic reactions in your hair and skin.  Fragrances, preservatives, and certain chemicals, such as formaldehyde, are common allergens found in hair products.

  2. Patch Test: Before beginning any hair treatment, including keratin treatments, conduct a patch test to check for allergic reactions.  Apply a small amount of the product to a discreet area of your skin, such as behind the ear or inside the elbow, and allow 24-48 hours to see if any adverse reactions occur.

  3. Choose Hypoallergenic Hair Products: Look for hypoallergenic or fragrance-free shampoos, conditioners, and styling products.  These formulations are less likely to cause irritation or allergies.

  4. Consult a Dermatologist or Allergist: If you have frequent irritation or allergic reactions to hair products, consider seeing a dermatologist or allergist for allergy testing and personalised recommendations.  They can help identify specific allergens and recommend appropriate products.

  5. Read Labels Carefully: Check the ingredient lists of hair products for potential allergens and irritants. Avoid products containing ingredients that have caused reactions in the past or that you know you are allergic to.

  6. Avoid Overlapping Treatments: Avoid getting multiple hair treatments, such as colouring or perming, in quick succession.  Overlapping treatments can cause irritation and damage to the hair and scalp.

  7. Use Protective Measures: If you are allergic to certain ingredients or chemicals found in hair treatments such as keratin, consider wearing gloves or a mask during application to reduce exposure.

  8. Rinse Thoroughly: Make sure to thoroughly rinse your hair products to remove any residue from your scalp and strands.  Residual product buildup can cause irritation and discomfort. 

  9. Seek Medical Attention: If you experience severe irritation, allergic reactions, or symptoms like itching, redness, swelling, or hives following a hair treatment, seek medical attention right away.  Your doctor can advise you on the best treatment options and help you manage your symptoms.

  10. Follow Aftercare Instructions: After a hair treatment, follow the aftercare instructions given by your stylist or healthcare provider.  This could include using specific products, avoiding certain activities, and making follow-up appointments.

By taking these proactive steps and being aware of potential triggers, you can effectively manage irritation and allergies caused by hair treatments such as keratin treatments while also maintaining healthy hair and scalp.

Understanding the Chemicals Used

Understanding the chemicals used in hair treatments, including keratin treatments, is critical for making informed decisions and reducing the likelihood of adverse reactions.  Here are some common chemicals used in keratin treatments and their roles:

  1. Keratin: Keratin is a protein naturally found in hair, skin, and nails. In keratin treatments, hydrolyzed keratin is typically used to replenish and strengthen the hair, smoothing the cuticle and reducing frizz.

  2. Formaldehyde or Formaldehyde-Releasing Agents: Formaldehyde or formaldehyde-releasing agents, such as methylene glycol or formalin, are occasionally used in keratin treatments to cross-link keratin proteins and bond them to the hair shaft. This process helps to make hair smoother and more manageable. However, formaldehyde can be an irritant and allergen, and high concentrations can cause respiratory problems and other health concerns.

  3. Glyoxylic Acid: Glyoxylic acid is a chemical compound that is commonly used as an alternative to formaldehyde in "formaldehyde-free" keratin treatments. It works by temporarily breaking and reforming the bonds in the hair, allowing keratin to penetrate and bond with the hair shaft. While glyoxylic acid is generally regarded as less irritating than formaldehyde, it can still cause sensitivity reactions in some people.

  4. Silicones: Silicones, like dimethicone or cyclopentasiloxane, are frequently used in keratin treatments to provide conditioning and smoothing effects. Silicones create a protective barrier around the hair shaft, which reduces frizz, increases shine, and improves manageability. However, some people may develop sensitivity to silicones over time.

  5. Surfactants: Surfactants, such as sodium lauryl sulfate (SLS) or sodium laureth sulfate (SLES), are detergents commonly found in shampoos and cleansers. They help cleanse the hair and scalp by removing dirt, oil, and product buildup. While surfactants are not typically a primary component of keratin treatments, they may be present in accompanying clarifying shampoos used before the treatment to prepare the hair.

  6. Other Ingredients: Keratin treatments may contain additional ingredients such as amino acids, vitamins, botanical extracts, and conditioning agents to enhance the overall performance and results of the treatment. These ingredients may provide moisturizing, strengthening, or antioxidant benefits for the hair.

It is important to carefully read the ingredients list of keratin treatments and consult with your stylist or a dermatologist if you have any concerns about specific chemicals or potential allergens. Patch testing can help identify potential sensitivities or allergic reactions before starting a full treatment. Furthermore, selecting reputable brands and following proper application procedures can help reduce the risk of side effects while achieving the desired results.

Minimizing Hair Damage

Minimizing hair damage is essential, especially when undergoing chemically intensive treatments such as keratin treatments. Here are some tips for minimizing hair damage.:

  • Choose a reputable salon with experienced stylists who are trained to perform keratin treatments safely and effectively. To ensure that you choose a reputable salon, conduct research and read reviews.

  • Before receiving a keratin treatment, schedule a consultation with your stylist. Discuss your hair type, concerns, and expectations, as well as any questions you may have about the treatment process and associated risks.

  • Before beginning treatment, perform a patch test to rule out any allergic reactions or sensitivities to the products being used. Apply a small amount of the treatment to a discreet area of your skin and wait 24-48 hours to see if there are any adverse reactions.

  • Choose formaldehyde-free keratin treatments or treatments that include alternative ingredients such as glyoxylic acid. Formaldehyde and formaldehyde-releasing agents can be harsh on the hair and scalp, causing irritation or allergy symptoms in some people.

  • Avoid using excessive keratin treatments and other chemical processes on your hair. Limit treatment frequency to avoid chemical overexposure and reduce the risk of damage and breakage.

  • Follow your stylist's aftercare instructions carefully. This could include waiting a certain amount of time before washing your hair, using sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners, and refraining from heat styling for a while after the treatment.

  • Reduce the use of heat styling tools such as flat irons, curling irons, and blow dryers, especially immediately following a keratin treatment. Excessive heat can cause keratin to degrade more quickly, resulting in dryness and damage.

  • Consider wearing protective hairstyles such as braids, buns, or ponytails to reduce exposure to environmental factors and manipulation of the hair, thereby preventing breakage and damage.

  • Schedule regular trims to remove split ends and prevent them from traveling up the hair shaft, leading to further damage. Trimming your hair every 6-8 weeks can help keep it healthy and attractive.

  • Maintain overall hair health by eating a well-balanced diet, staying hydrated, using gentle hair care products, and incorporating regular deep conditioning treatments into your routine. Healthy hair is more resilient and can withstand chemical treatments.

By following these tips and working with an experienced stylist, you can reduce the risk of hair damage while still getting the results you want from your keratin treatment.

Consider using volumizing products in your styling routine to give your hair more body and lift.

  • Thick or Coarse Hair: To properly smooth and manage the hair, thick or coarse hair usually needs a stronger keratin treatment formulation.  Look for keratin treatments designed for thick or coarse hair textures, which typically contain higher concentrations of smoothing agents. Allow sufficient application and processing time to fully penetrate the hair shaft and achieve the desired results.

  • Curly or frizzy hair: Keratin treatments can help relax curls, reduce frizz, and improve manageability.  Choose a keratin treatment tailored to curly or frizzy hair textures, which may include additional conditioning agents to nourish and soften the hair. Consider requesting a customized treatment process to preserve some curl or wave pattern while still achieving smoother, more manageable hair.

  • Color-Treated or Damaged Hair: Color-treated or damaged hair requires extra care during keratin treatments to prevent further damage and maintain hair health. Choose a keratin treatment formulated for color-treated or damaged hair, which often contains ingredients to protect and nourish the hair. Consider incorporating bonding treatments or strengthening masks into your hair care routine to repair and fortify damaged hair before and after the keratin treatment.

  • Natural or Virgin Hair: To reduce the risk of damage in natural or virgin hair that has not been chemically treated, choose a gentle keratin treatment formulation. Look for keratin treatments with lower levels of smoothing agents and fewer harsh chemicals. Consider incorporating protein-rich treatments into your hair care routine to help strengthen and fortify your hair before and after the keratin treatment.

  • Sensitive Scalp: If you have a sensitive scalp, use a keratin treatment with gentle, non-irritating ingredients and avoid formulations containing formaldehyde or other harsh chemicals. Before starting the treatment, consider performing a patch test to rule out any allergic reactions or sensitivities. Communicate any concerns or sensitivities to your stylist to ensure a comfortable and safe treatment.

  • Customised Application: Work with your stylist to tailor the application process to your hair type, texture, and desired outcome.  To achieve the best results for your hair, you may need to adjust the keratin solution concentration, application technique, and processing time.

By tailoring keratin treatments to different hair types, you can achieve smoother, more manageable hair while reducing the risk of damage or adverse reactions. Consult your stylist to determine the best keratin treatment for your hair and discuss any specific concerns or preferences you may have.

Keratin for Curly Hair

Keratin treatments can transform curly hair, providing smoother, more manageable locks while decreasing frizz and increasing shine. However, it is critical to approach keratin treatments for curly hair with caution to achieve the desired results without disrupting the natural curl pattern. Here's how to effectively use keratin treatments on curly hair:

  1. Choose a keratin treatment specifically designed for curly hair. Look for products labelled "curl-friendly" or "curly hair formula." These formulations aim to relax curls and reduce frizz while preserving the natural curl pattern.

  2. Schedule a consultation with a stylist who has worked with curly hair and keratin treatments. They can evaluate your hair's texture, curl pattern, and condition to determine the best keratin treatment and application method.

  3. Communicate your desire to maintain curl definition while reducing frizz and improving manageability. A skilled stylist can tailor the keratin treatment process to create smoother, more manageable hair without completely straightening the curls.

  4. Work with your stylist to tailor the keratin treatment to your hair's specific needs. This may entail adjusting the keratin solution concentration, application technique, and processing time to achieve the desired level of smoothness while maintaining curl integrity.

  5. Take precautions to keep your hair healthy and intact during the keratin treatment process. Ensure that your stylist applies the proper heat settings and techniques to seal the keratin into the hair without causing damage. Consider adding strengthening or conditioning treatments to your hair care routine before and after the keratin treatment to nourish and protect the hair.

  6. Follow your stylist's aftercare instructions to maintain the keratin treatment's results and promote hair health. This may include using sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners, avoiding heat styling, and limiting exposure to moisture immediately following the treatment.

  7. Depending on the type of keratin treatment used and your hair's natural growth cycle, you may need to have touch-up treatments every few months to maintain the results. Consult your stylist to determine the best schedule for touch-ups and maintenance. 

By working with a knowledgeable stylist and using keratin treatments specifically formulated for curly hair, you can achieve smoother, more manageable locks while preserving the natural beauty of your curls. Effective keratin treatments for curly hair require communication, customisation, and proper aftercare.

Adjustments for Fine Hair

When applying keratin treatments to fine hair, it is critical to make adjustments to ensure that the treatment provides the desired smoothing and strengthening effects without weighing down or overloading the hair. Here are some adjustments and considerations for keratin treatments for fine hair:

  1. Lighter Formulation: Choose a lighter keratin treatment that is specifically designed for fine hair.  These formulations typically contain lower concentrations of smoothing agents, making them less likely to weigh down the hair or cause buildup.

  2. Lower Heat Settings: Use lower heat settings during the treatment to avoid overheating and damage to fine hair.  High heat can cause keratin to break down more quickly, resulting in dryness and brittleness.

  3. Thorough Application: Apply the keratin treatment solution thoroughly to ensure that each hair strand is evenly coated.  Fine hair is more porous and prone to damage, so apply the treatment with caution to avoid chemical overexposure.

  4. Shorter Processing Time: To reduce the risk of over-processing and damage, keratin treatments on fine hair should be processed more quickly.  Fine hair absorbs products faster, so it may take less time to achieve the desired results.

  5. Avoid Overlapping Treatments: Avoid using keratin treatments or other chemical processes on fine hair.  Overlapping treatments can cause product buildup, overdrying, and damage to the hair shaft.

  6. Clarifying Shampoo: Before applying the keratin treatment, use a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup, oils, and impurities from the hair.  This allows the keratin treatment to penetrate more effectively and yield better results.

  7. Customised Aftercare: Follow the aftercare recommendations designed specifically for fine hair.  Use lightweight, sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners designed for fine or thin hair to keep the keratin treatment results without weighing down the hair.

  8. Limit Heat Styling: Avoid using heat styling tools like blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons on fine hair, especially right after a keratin treatment.  Excessive heat can harm the treatment and jeopardise its effectiveness.

  9. Regular Maintenance: Depending on the type of keratin treatment used and your hair's natural growth cycle, you may need to have touch-up treatments every few months to keep the results.  Consult your stylist to determine the best schedule for touch-ups and maintenance.

By making these changes and taking the necessary precautions, you can achieve smoother, more manageable hair with keratin treatments while preserving the health and integrity of fine hair.
