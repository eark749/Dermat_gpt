Step-by-Step Guide to Achieving Salon-Quality Blowouts at Home
A salon-quality blowout at home requires several steps and techniques. Here is a step-by-step guide to getting a professional-looking blowout:
Begin with Clean Hair
: Use freshly washed and towel-dried hair. To protect your hair from heat damage, use a heat protectant spray or
hair serum.
Sectioning
: Use clips or hair ties to divide your hair into sections. This makes the blow-drying process more manageable.
Rough Dry:
Use a blow dryer with a nozzle attachment and set it to medium heat. Roughly dry your hair until it's about 80% dry, using your fingers to move the airflow around and lifting the roots for added volume.
Start with the Crown:
Begin blow-drying the crown area. Use a round brush and lift the hair at the roots while directing the heat from the dryer downward to create volume.
Work in Sections:
Take small sections of hair and use a round brush to wrap the hair around it. Hold the brush vertically and aim the dryer from the roots to the ends, keeping tension on the hair for a smooth finish.
Focus on the Ends:
Concentrate on drying the ends last to avoid excess heat exposure, using a downward motion to smooth and shape them.
Cool Shot:
Finish each section with a blast of cool air from the blow dryer to set the style and add shine.
Add Volume:
For added volume, flip your head upside down and blow-dry the roots using a low heat setting. Use your fingers to lift the hair at the roots as you dry.
Set the Style
: Once your hair is dry, set the style by switching to a cool setting on your dryer and gently blowing cool air all over it.
Apply Finishing Products
: To set the style, tame flyaways, and add shine without weighing the hair down,
use a finishing serum
, oil, or hairspray.
By following these steps and practising the techniques, you can achieve a salon-quality blowout at home. Experiment with various brush sizes and techniques to discover what works best for your hair type and desired style.
Utilizing the Volume Spray for Maximum Effect
Follow these steps for added volume and lift to maximise the effect of a volume spray, such as Living Proof's Full Dry Volume Blast or a similar product:
Start with clean, dry hair. The spray works
best on dry hair
, so make sure it is completely dry before applying it.
Before using, thoroughly shake the volume spray to ensure that the ingredients are properly mixed.
Divide your hair into sections to ensure even application. Separate the hair with clips or hair ties, beginning at the bottom and working your way up.
Hold the spray can about 6-8 inches away from your head. Lift sections of hair and spray the product onto the roots, paying special attention to the areas where you want more volume. Keep the spray nozzle directed toward the roots for maximum lift.
After applying the spray, use your fingertips to gently massage or work the product into the roots. This promotes volume by distributing the product evenly.
For extra volume, flip your hair upside down and spray the product along the roots while your hair is flipped, then massage it in. This technique can add lift and fullness to the hair.
If you need more volume, layer the spray by repeating the application steps in targeted areas. Allow the product to dry before adding additional layers.
After applying the spray and achieving the desired volume, gently tousle or arrange your hair with your fingers to style it. Avoid combing or brushing too rigorously, as this can flatten the volume.
By following these steps and techniques, you can maximise the effectiveness of a volume spray to achieve fuller, more voluminous hair with lift and lasting body. The application should be tailored to your hair type and desired style.

Tailoring Use for Fine or Limp Hair
For fine or limp hair, achieving volume while maintaining a lightweight feel is crucial. Here are tailored tips to enhance volume and manageability for this hair type:
Choose the Right Products
:
Opt for products specifically designed to add volume and lift without weighing down the hair. Look for formulas that are lightweight and add body to fine hair. Use root-lifting sprays or mousses that target the roots for added lift. Apply these products at the roots before blow-drying for maximum effect.
Blow-Drying Techniques
:
When blow-drying, focus the airflow at the roots to lift them and create volume. Use a round brush while blow-drying to add lift and body to the roots. Finish blow-drying with a blast of cool air to set the style and add extra volume while minimizing heat damage.
Avoid Heavy Products
:
Use lightweight styling products like texturizing sprays or volumizing foams that provide hold and volume without weighing down fine hair. Steer clear of heavy oils or styling creams that can make fine hair look greasy and limp.
Minimal Use of Conditioning Products
:
Apply conditioner
mainly to the ends and mid-lengths of your hair to avoid weighing down the roots.
Dry Shampoo for Volume and Texture
: Use dry
shampoo at the roots
to absorb excess oil and add texture. It can provide extra volume and lift in between washes.
Haircut and Styling Techniques
:
Consider getting a layered haircut to add dimension and volume to fine hair. Layers can create the illusion of thicker hair. Lightly tease the roots to add volume at the crown, but do this sparingly to avoid damage.
You can add volume and manageability to fine or limp hair without weighing it down by using these tailored techniques and the right products. Experiment with various methods to find out what works best for your hair type and preferred style while keeping its health and natural texture.
Achieving Natural-Looking Volume for Various Hair Textures
Achieving natural-looking volume across various hair textures necessitates different approaches to enhancing each texture's natural characteristics. Here are some tips for different hair textures:
Straight Hair:
Consider a layered haircut to add dimension and movement to straight hair, promoting natural volume. Use root-lifting sprays or mousses at the roots before blow-drying. Focus on lifting the roots to create natural-looking volume. Blow-dry hair upside down or with a round brush to add lift to the roots while maintaining a natural appearance.
Wavy Hair:
Use a sea salt spray to enhance natural waves and create volume. Scrunch the hair while using the spray to amplify texture. Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to enhance waves while adding volume without disturbing the natural texture. Opt for layered haircuts to accentuate waves and add natural movement and volume.
Curly Hair:
Use lightweight leave-in conditioners or curl-enhancing creams to define curls while adding bounce and volume. Utilize diffusing techniques and plopping methods to enhance curls without sacrificing volume. Minimize combing or brushing to maintain natural curl patterns and prevent unnecessary flattening.
Coily or Kinky Hair:
Use moisturizing creams or leave-in conditioners to maintain hydration and prevent frizz, which can enhance the natural volume of coily hair. Steer clear of heavy styling products that can weigh down coily hair, opting for lighter formulas to enhance volume without sacrificing moisture. Embrace protective hairstyles or low-manipulation styling techniques to maintain natural volume and protect hair health.
Fine Hair:
Use volumizing powders or sprays sparingly at the roots to add natural-looking volume without adding weight. Lightly tease the roots or use backcombing techniques to add lift while avoiding damage to fine hair. Consider layered hairstyles to create the illusion of thicker hair and enhance natural volume.
You can achieve natural-looking volume while maintaining the health and integrity of your hair by tailoring your approach to your specific hair texture, embracing natural characteristics, and using appropriate products and techniques.
