How to Use Tranexamic Acid for Skin
Tranexamic acid is available in various forms, including serums, creams, and lotions. When using tranexamic acid for the skin, it's important to follow the instructions on the product label carefully. Generally, it's recommended to use a small amount of the product on clean,
dry skin
twice a day, preferably in the morning and evening.
To apply tranexamic acid (TXA) to your skin, follow these instructions:
Patch Test
: Before incorporating any new TXA-containing skincare product into your routine, conduct a patch test. Apply a small amount of the product to a discreet area of your skin (such as the inner forearm) and wait 24-48 hours to see if any adverse reactions occur, such as redness, itching, or irritation.
Choose the Right Product
: Choose a skin care product enriched with TXA that is appropriate for your skin type and concerns. TXA can be found in a variety of products, including serums, creams, masks, and spot treatments.
Cleanse Your Skin
: Start with clean skin by washing your face with a gentle
cleanser
suited to your skin type. Pat your skin dry with a clean towel.
Apply TXA Product
: Apply a pea-sized amount of the TXA product to your fingertips or directly to the areas of concern. Gently massage the product into your skin in upward, circular motions until completely absorbed.
Allow Absorption Time
: Let the TXA product absorb into your skin before applying any other skincare products. This could take several minutes.
Follow with Moisturizer (if needed)
: Depending on your skin type and the formulation of the TXA product, you may want to use a moisturiser to seal in hydration and nourish your skin.
Use Sunscreen
: If you use TXA during the day, apply a broad-spectrum
sunscreen
with SPF 30 or higher as the final step in your skincare routine. TXA may make your skin more sensitive to sunlight, so sunscreen protects your skin from harmful UV rays and prevents further hyperpigmentation.
Consistency is Key
: Incorporate TXA into your skincare routine consistently for best results. Use it as directed, once or twice a day, to see noticeable improvements in your skin's tone and texture over time.
Monitor Results
: Pay attention to how your skin responds to TXA. It may take several weeks to notice significant improvements in hyperpigmentation or other skin issues. If you experience irritation or adverse reactions, stop using the product and consult a
dermatologist
.
It's also important to note that tranexamic acid can make the skin more sensitive to the sun, so it's important to use a broad-spectrum
sunscreen
with an SPF of 30 or higher during the day.

Side Effects of Tranexamic Acid on Skin
While Tranexamic acid is generally considered safe for use on the skin, some people may experience side effects. These side effects can vary from person to person and can depend on the concentration and formulation of the Tranexamic acid product used. Some of the most common side effects of Tranexamic acid on the skin include:
Skin Irritation
- Tranexamic acid can cause skin irritation, redness, and itching, especially if you have sensitive skin or use a product with a high concentration of Tranexamic acid. If you experience any of these symptoms, it's best to discontinue the use of the product and consult with a dermatologist.
Sun Sensitivity
- Tranexamic acid can make your skin more sensitive to the sun, increasing your risk of sunburn and skin damage. It's essential to use a broad-spectrum
sunscreen
with an SPF of 30 or higher when using Tranexamic acid products. Additionally, it's best to avoid direct sun exposure and wear protective clothing such as hats and long-sleeved shirts.
Dryness
- Tranexamic acid can cause dryness and
flakiness
of the skin, especially if used in high concentrations or combined with other drying ingredients. It's important to maintain proper hydration levels by drinking plenty of water and using a moisturizer that suits your skin type. Avoid using products that contain alcohol, which can exacerbate dryness.
Acne
- In rare cases, Tranexamic acid can worsen
acne
and cause breakouts, especially if used in high concentrations or combined with other pore-clogging ingredients. If you have acne-prone skin, it's best to consult with a dermatologist before using any Tranexamic acid products.
Overall, Tranexamic acid is a powerful ingredient that can provide many benefits for the skin, but it's essential to use it safely and correctly. Always start with a low concentration and patch test before applying it to your entire face. If you experience any adverse reactions, discontinue use immediately and consult with a dermatologist.

Incorporating Sun Protection
Sun protection is essential for maintaining healthy, youthful-looking skin, particularly when using skincare products containing active ingredients such as tranexamic acid (TXA). Here's how to successfully incorporate sun protection into your routine:
Use a Broad-Spectrum Sunscreen
: Look for a sunscreen that protects your skin against both UVA and UVB rays. This protects against sunburn, premature ageing, and skin cancer.
Choose the Right SPF
: Use a sunscreen with a Sun Protection Factor (SPF) of 30 or higher. This provides adequate UVB protection. However, if you have fair skin, a history of skin cancer, or spend long periods in the sun, use SPF 50 or higher.
Apply Sunscreen Daily
: Sunscreen should be applied every morning as the final step in your skincare routine, after moisturizer and before makeup (if applicable). Even on cloudy days, UV rays can penetrate clouds and cause damage to your skin.
Reapply Throughout the Day
: If you will be spending a long time outside, reapply sunscreen every two hours, or more frequently if you sweat or swim. This provides continuous protection against sun damage.
Consider Sunscreen Ingredients
: Some sunscreen ingredients, like zinc oxide and
titanium dioxide
, offer physical (or mineral) protection by reflecting UV rays away from the skin. Chemical sunscreen ingredients such as avobenzone and octocrylene absorb ultraviolet radiation and convert it to heat. Choose the formulation that best suits your skin type and preferences.
Do not Forget Your Lips
: Lips are frequently overlooked when it comes to sun protection. Use a lip balm or lipstick with SPF to shield your lips from the sun's harmful rays.
Protect Exposed Areas
: Apply sunscreen to all exposed areas of your body, such as your neck, ears, hands, and any other areas not covered by clothing.
Combine with Protective Clothing
: In addition to sunscreen, wearing protective clothing such as wide-brimmed hats, sunglasses, and long-sleeved shirts can help shield your skin from UV damage.
Avoid Peak Sun Hours
: Whenever possible, limit your time outside between 10 a.m. and 4 p.m., when the sun's rays are at their most intense.
By incorporating these sun protection practices into your skincare routine, you can protect your skin from sun damage while also reaping the benefits of skincare products containing tranexamic acid and other active ingredients.
