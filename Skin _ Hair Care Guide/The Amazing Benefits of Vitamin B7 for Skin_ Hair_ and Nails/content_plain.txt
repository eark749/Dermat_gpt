What is Vitamin B7 (Biotin)?
Vitamin B7, or biotin, is a member of the B-vitamin family. It is a water-soluble vitamin, meaning it dissolves in water and is not stored in the body. Biotin is essential for the metabolism of carbohydrates, fats, and proteins, and plays a crucial role in cell growth and development. Let us understand the science behind this crucial vitamin and how it helps in skin, hair, and nail health.
The Science Behind Vitamin B7 and Its Role in Skin, Hair, and Nail Health
Biotin is an essential nutrient for maintaining healthy skin, hair, and nails. It helps to promote the production of keratin, a protein that forms the structural component of our skin, hair, and nails. Keratin is necessary for strong, healthy hair and nails and plays a vital role in skin health.
How Vitamin B7 Promotes Collagen Synthesis for Healthy Skin
Collagen is a protein that provides structure and support to our skin. As we age, our collagen levels decrease, leading to wrinkles and fine lines. Biotin plays a crucial role in collagen synthesis, helping to maintain healthy, youthful-looking skin.
