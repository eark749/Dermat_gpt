Understanding the Science Behind the Bloom

The butterfly pea flower's striking blue color is not simply for display; it is the consequence of interesting chemistry at work. Here's an overview of the science underlying the bloom:

  • Anthocyanins are the key components that give the butterfly pea bloom its bright blue color. These water-soluble pigments, known as flavonoids, can be found in a variety of fruits, vegetables, and flowers. Anthocyanins are pH-sensitive, which means they change color depending on the acidity or alkalinity of their surroundings.

  • Anthocyanins have a complicated chemical structure with alternating single and double carbon-carbon bonds. This structure enables them to absorb light in the visible spectrum at distinct wavelengths corresponding to different colors. The anthocyanins in butterfly pea blooms absorb largely in the blue-violet range, giving them their distinctive blue color.

  • The color of butterfly pea flower extracts varies according to the pH of the solution. Anthocyanins look reddish-purple in acidic conditions, but bright blue in alkaline settings. This pH-dependent color change is caused by the reversible conversion of anthocyanins into distinct chemical forms known as flavylium cations (acidic pH) and quinoidal bases (alkaline pH).

  • Temperature, light exposure, and the presence of metal ions can all affect the stability of anthocyanin pigments. Proper processing and storage processes are required to maintain the color intensity and quality of butterfly pea flower extracts in cosmetics compositions.

  • Beyond their visual attractiveness, anthocyanins have antioxidant qualities that serve to protect the flower from oxidative stress. In skincare, antioxidants can help neutralize free radicals and minimize oxidative damage to the skin, thereby improving its general health and vitality.

DIY Recipes: Butterfly Pea Flower Skincare at Home

Creating DIY skincare recipes using butterfly pea flowers is not only enjoyable, but it also allows you to make use of its inherent beauty and skincare properties. Here are some easy DIY recipes to include butterfly pea flowers into your skincare routine at home:

  • Butterfly Pea Flower Toner: Ingredients are 1 cup distilled water, 2-3 butterfly pea flowers. Optional: 1-2 drops of essential oil (such as lavender or rose). Boil the distilled water and add the butterfly pea flowers.  Let the mixture simmer for 5-10 minutes to extract the color and beneficial compounds from the flowers. Remove from heat and allow it to cool completely.  Strain out the flowers and pour the infused water into a clean spray bottle. Add a few drops of essential oil if desired and shake well. Use the toner after cleansing to hydrate and refresh your skin.

  • Butterfly Pea Flower Face Mask: Ingredients are 2 tablespoons plain yogurt, 1 tablespoon honey, and 1 tablespoon butterfly pea flower powder. In a small bowl, mix the yogurt, honey, and butterfly pea flower powder until well combined. Apply the mixture to clean, dry skin, avoiding the eye area. Leave the mask on for 15-20 minutes. Rinse off with lukewarm water and pat your skin dry. Follow up with your favorite moisturizer for hydrated and glowing skin.

  • Butterfly Pea Flower Infused Oil: Ingredients are 1/2 cup carrier oil (such as jojoba oil or sweet almond oil) and 2-3 tablespoons dried butterfly pea flowers. Place the dried butterfly pea flowers in a clean, dry glass jar. Heat the carrier oil in a double boiler until warm but not boiling. Pour the warm oil over the dried flowers in the jar, making sure they are completely submerged. Seal the jar tightly and let it sit in a cool, dark place for 1-2 weeks to allow the flowers to infuse into the oil. Strain out the flowers using a fine mesh strainer or cheesecloth.  Transfer the infused oil to a clean, dark glass bottle for storage. Use the infused oil as a facial oil or body oil to moisturize and nourish your skin.

These DIY recipes are simple to make and allow you to enjoy the benefits of butterfly pea flowers in your skincare routine right at home. Experiment with different combinations and find what works best for your skin type and preferences.
