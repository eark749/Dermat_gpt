: To prevent further harming your skin barrier, use a gentle, non-stripping cleanser. Avoid using abrasive scrubs or sulfate-containing cleansers because they can strip your skin of its natural oils.
Avoid over-exfoliating
: Over-exfoliation can harm your skin's protective layer, so pay attention to your skin's needs and adjust your application frequency as necessary. Take a break from using mandelic acid every night and cut back on how often you use it if you experience any redness or irritation.
Use a barrier-repairing serum
: Look for a serum that contains ingredients that can repair barriers, such as niacinamide, cholesterol, or fatty acids. These components can support a stronger skin barrier and shield your skin from aggressors in the outside environment.
Protect your skin
: Always apply a broad-spectrum SPF 30 or higher sunscreen during the day to protect your skin from further damage. Sun damage can weaken your skin barrier and exacerbate any existing concerns.
By using these recommendations, you can maintain hydrated, healthy skin while still enjoying the advantages of using mandelic acid every night.
