Unraveling the Origins and Composition of Abyssinian Oil
Abyssinian oil, also called Crambe abyssinica seed oil, is made from the seeds of the Mediterranean-native Crambe abyssinica plant. The Brassicaceae family includes the Crambe abyssinica plant, popularly known as Abyssinian cabbage or Ethiopian mustard. It is planted largely for the extraction of Abyssinian oil from its oil-rich seeds. The plant is renowned for being hardy and prospering in a variety of climates. The composition of abyssinian oil is rich in advantageous substances, which add to its efficiency for skincare and haircare. Its non-comedogenic properties, which make it suited for a variety of skin types, including those with oily or acne-prone skin, are especially valued.
