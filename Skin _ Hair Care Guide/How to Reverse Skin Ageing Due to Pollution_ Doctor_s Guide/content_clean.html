Does air pollution affect your skin?

Skin problems caused by air pollution can include:

  • Acne

  • Skin allergies

  • Psoriasis

Air pollution causes these skin issues because it can irritate the human skin, especially its upper layer, called epidermis. The human skin is constantly exposed to environmental factors such as UV light, dirt, and other microorganisms that may cause inflammation or infection. To avoid this happening, the human body has developed a barrier function that consists of a lipid layer called stratum corneum (SC) on top of SC cells. When pollutants enter our bodies through inhalation or ingestion, they reach this protective layer, where some may be absorbed into our bloodstream while others remain trapped underneath SC cells. Agents with a very irritating effect (e.g. sulphur dioxide SO2), once they get into our bodies, activate multiple pathways of inflammation within them (e.g., keratinocytes). Some pathways ignite melanocytes which produce too much pigment and end up giving you unwanted sun spots, while others increase production of mucus in your nose, causing runny noses, etc.

Understanding the impact of environmental pollutants

Environmental pollutants can have a significant impact on hair and scalp health due to exposure to various harmful substances found in the air, water, and surroundings. Here's a rundown of the effects of environmental pollutants on hair:

Air Pollution:

    • Particulate Matter (PM): Fine particles from vehicle exhaust, industrial emissions, and smoke can settle on the scalp and hair, causing dryness, irritation, and potential cuticle damage.

    • Chemical Compounds: Harmful chemicals in the air, such as sulphates, nitrogen dioxide, and volatile organic compounds (VOCs), can strip natural oils from the hair, resulting in dryness, frizziness, and increased breakage vulnerability.

    Water Quality:

    • Chlorine and Hard Water: Both chlorine in tap water and minerals in hard water can cause scalp irritation, and dryness, and strip the hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry, brittle, and prone to breakage.

    UV Radiation:

    • Sun Exposure: Prolonged exposure to UV radiation can damage the hair cuticle, resulting in dryness, discoloration, and weakened hair structure. UV rays can also degrade proteins in the hair, making it brittle.

    Climate Factors:

    • Humidity: High humidity levels can cause frizz and excessive moisture absorption by the hair, whereas low humidity levels can cause dryness and brittleness.

    • Extreme Temperatures: Cold weather can cause dry scalp and hair, whereas heat can cause dehydration and potential hair shaft damage.

    Impact on Hair Health:

    • Dryness and Brittleness: Environmental pollutants can strip natural oils from the scalp and hair, resulting in dry, brittle hair that is more prone to breakage.

    • Scalp Irritation: Pollutants can cause scalp irritation, itchiness, redness, and conditions like dandruff or eczema.

    • Hair Loss: Prolonged exposure to pollutants may contribute to hair thinning or exacerbate existing hair loss conditions by increasing oxidative stress and inflammation in the scalp.

    Protecting Hair from Environmental Damage:

    • Regular Cleaning: Use gentle shampoos to cleanse the hair and scalp to remove pollutants, dirt, and buildup.

    • Hydration and conditioning: Restore moisture and nourish the hair with conditioners and hydrating masks to prevent dryness and damage.

    • Protective measures: When exposed to sunlight for prolonged periods, wear hats or use UV-protecting hair products.

    • Filtered Water: To reduce the effects of hard minerals and chlorine, think about washing your hair with filtered or soft water.

    • Antioxidant Products: To counteract oxidative stress brought on by pollutants, think about utilising antioxidant-containing hair care products.

    • Reducing Exposure: To lessen direct contact with pollutants, wear protective hairstyles or limit your exposure to polluted environments.

    Protective measures against pollution-induced skin damage

    It is critical to protect your skin from pollution-induced damage to retain its health and attractiveness. Here are some precautions you may take to reduce the effects of environmental contaminants on your skin:

    • Cleansing Routine: Use a potent cleanser to get rid of any debris, pollutants, and makeup that have accumulated on your skin during the day. Consider starting with an oil-based cleanser and then switching to a gentle water-based cleanser.

    • Barrier Protection: Use a moisturiser to coat your skin in a shield that keeps impurities from getting through and irritating or drying it out. To protect your skin from UV rays and reduce the effects of pollution-induced oxidative stress, apply broad-spectrum sunscreen with an SPF of 30 or higher every day.

    • Avoid Touching Face: To stop bacteria or pollutants from spreading from your hands to your skin, try not to touch your face too often.

    • Protective Clothing: To reduce direct exposure to pollutants and UV radiation when you are outside, wear hats, scarves, or clothing that covers your skin.

    • Regular Skincare: To keep your skin clear, moisturised, and protected, create and stick to a regular skincare regimen that is appropriate for your skin type.

    • Hydration and Nutrition: To keep your skin hydrated from the inside out and flush out toxins, make sure you drink plenty of water. Omega-3 fatty acids, vitamins, and antioxidants are abundant in a balanced diet, which you should follow to maintain the general health of your skin.

    • Air Quality: To reduce exposure to indoor pollutants and enhance indoor air quality, think about installing air purifiers inside.

    • Post-Exposure Care: After heavy pollution exposure, consider using a detoxifying mask or a gentle exfoliant to remove impurities and rejuvenate your skin.

    • Regular Cleansing of Personal Items: To prevent bacterial buildup and pollution transfer, clean items that come into contact with your face regularly, such as phones, pillowcases, and makeup brushes.

    Adopting these protective measures and sticking to a consistent skincare routine can help reduce the impact of pollution on your skin, keeping it healthy, radiant, and less vulnerable to environmental pollutants.

    Anti-pollution skincare routines

    An anti-pollution skincare routine focuses on protecting your skin from environmental pollutants by using specific products and practices. Here's a comprehensive guide to an anti-pollution skincare routine:

    Morning Routine:

    • Gentle Cleanser: Use a mild, water-based cleanser to remove impurities and pollutants accumulated overnight. This helps prepare the skin for subsequent skincare steps.
