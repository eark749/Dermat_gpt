Exploring Potential Side Effects: Understanding and Managing Risks
Semaglutide, like any medicine, may cause negative effects. It's critical to be aware of these hazards and to address them with your doctor. Here are some of the potential adverse effects of semaglutide, as well as techniques for dealing with them:
Gastrointestinal Symptoms
:
The most common side effects are nausea, vomiting, diarrhoea, and abdominal discomfort.
These symptoms usually appear at the start of treatment and gradually fade away.
Taking semaglutide with food or starting with a lower dose and gradually increasing it can help reduce gastrointestinal symptoms.
If your symptoms persist or worsen, seek further advice from your healthcare provider.
Hypoglycemia
:
Semaglutide does not typically cause hypoglycemia (low blood sugar).
However, when combined with other blood sugar-lowering medications such as sulfonylureas or insulin, the risk of hypoglycemia may increase.
Your healthcare provider will monitor your blood sugar levels and may adjust the dosage of other medications to prevent hypoglycemia.
Injection Site Reactions
:
Some people may have injection site reactions, such as redness, swelling, or itching at the injection site.
To reduce the risk of these reactions, rotate injection sites and use proper injection techniques.
Inform your healthcare provider if you have persistent or severe reactions.
Pancreatitis
:
GLP-1 receptor agonists, including semaglutide, have been linked to rare cases of pancreatitis (pancreatic inflammation).
Seek medical attention right away if you have persistent, severe abdominal pain that is accompanied by vomiting.
Thyroid C-Cell Tumors
:
In animal studies, semaglutide was linked to an increased risk of thyroid C-cell tumors.
The significance of this discovery for humans is currently unknown.
If you have a personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC) or multiple endocrine neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2), tell your healthcare provider.
Other Adverse Effects
:
Other less common semaglutide side effects include dizziness, fatigue, constipation, and gallbladder-related events.
Any persistent or severe symptoms should be reported to your healthcare provider for evaluation.
