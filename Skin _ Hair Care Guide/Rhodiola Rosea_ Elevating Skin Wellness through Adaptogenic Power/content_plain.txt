DIY Skincare: Incorporating Rhodiola Rosea at Home
Incorporating Rhodiola Rosea into homemade skincare products can be a fun and satisfying method to take use of its skin-benefiting characteristics. Here are some unique and successful methods to use Rhodiola Rosea in homemade skincare recipes:
Rhodiola Rosea Facial Mask
: Ingredients are 1 tablespoon Rhodiola Rosea powder, 1 tablespoon honey (or yogurt for additional soothing) and 1 teaspoon aloe vera gel. Mix Rhodiola Rosea powder, honey (or yogurt), and aloe vera gel in a small bowl until well combined. Apply the mask evenly to clean, dry skin using clean fingertips or a brush. Leave the mask on for 15-20 minutes to allow the ingredients to work their magic. Rinse off with lukewarm water and pat dry. Follow up with your favorite moisturizer.
Rhodiola Rosea Infused Facial Toner
: Ingredients are 1 tablespoon dried Rhodiola Rosea root, 1 cup distilled water. Optional: 1-2 drops of essential oil (e.g., lavender, chamomile). Boil distilled water and add dried Rhodiola Rosea root to create a tea. Let the mixture steep for 15-20 minutes, then strain to remove the Rhodiola Rosea root. Allow the Rhodiola-infused tea to cool completely. Transfer the tea to a clean spray bottle and add optional essential oil drops. Use the toner after cleansing by spritzing onto the face or applying with a cotton pad.
Rhodiola Rosea Energizing Eye Serum
: Ingredients are 1 tablespoon sweet almond oil (or another carrier oil of choice), 3-4 drops Rhodiola Rosea extract or oil and 1-2 drops vitamin E oil (optional). In a small bowl, combine sweet almond oil, Rhodiola Rosea extract or oil, and vitamin E oil (if using). Mix well to ensure the ingredients are evenly blended. Transfer the serum to a clean dropper bottle for easy application. Gently dab a small amount of the serum around the eye area using your fingertips.
Rhodiola Rosea Relaxing Bath Soak
: Ingredients are
1 cup Epsom salts, 1/4 cup dried Rhodiola Rosea root (in a muslin bag or tea infuser). Optional: a few drops of lavender essential oil. Combine Epsom salts and dried Rhodiola Rosea root in a warm bath. Add a few drops of lavender essential oil (if desired) for a calming aroma. Soak in the bath for 20-30 minutes to relax and rejuvenate your body and skin.
