DIY Skincare Recipes: How to Include Isododecane
Incorporating isododecane into DIY skincare recipes can provide a variety of benefits, including better texture, lifespan, and product performance. Here are a few DIY skincare recipes using isododecane:
Mattifying Primer
:
Ingredients are 20% isododecane, 10% dimethicone, 5% silica powder, 62.5% cyclopentasiloxane, 2.5% zinc oxide (optional for added sun protection), and 0.5% vitamin E oil (as an
antioxidant
). Combine isododecane, dimethicone, and cyclopentasiloxane in a heat-resistant container. Heat the mixture gently using a double boiler until all ingredients are melted and well combined. Remove from heat and stir in silica powder until evenly dispersed. Add zinc oxide (if using) and vitamin E oil, stirring until fully incorporated. Pour the mixture into a clean, sterilized container and allow it to cool and solidify before use.
Long-Wear Foundation
: Ingredients are 30% isododecane, 20% cyclopentasiloxane, 15% dimethicone, 10% titanium dioxide (for SPF and coverage), 10% zinc oxide (for SPF and coverage), 10% pigments or iron oxides (for color), 3% silica microspheres (for a soft-focus effect) and 2% vitamin E oil (as an antioxidant). Combine isododecane, cyclopentasiloxane, and dimethicone in a heat-resistant container. Heat the mixture gently using a double boiler until all ingredients are melted and well combined. Stir in titanium dioxide and zinc oxide until fully dispersed. Add pigments or iron oxides, silica microspheres, and vitamin E oil, stirring until evenly distributed. Adjust the color and coverage as needed by adding more pigments or iron oxides. Pour the mixture into an airtight container and allow it to cool and set before use.
Hydrating Serum
: Ingredients are 50% water-based phase (distilled water or hydrosol), 25% humectants (glycerin, hyaluronic acid), 15% emollients (isododecane, squalane), 5% preservative (liquid germall plus), 3% silk peptides (optional for added hydration) and 2% panthenol (optional for soothing properties). Combine the water-based phase and humectants in a heat-resistant container. Heat the mixture gently using a double boiler until it reaches around 70°C (158°F). Add the emollients, preservative, silk peptides, and panthenol, stirring until fully incorporated. Allow the mixture to cool to room temperature before transferring it to a sterilized dropper bottle or pump dispenser. Use the serum daily after cleansing and before moisturizing to hydrate and nourish the skin.
