What are the drawbacks of using zinc oxide in sunscreen?

While zinc oxide is generally considered safe and effective for use in sunscreen, there are a few potential drawbacks to be aware of:

  1. White cast: Zinc oxide can leave a white cast on the skin, which can be particularly noticeable on people with darker skin tones. However, newer formulations of zinc oxide sunscreen are designed to minimize this effect.

  2. Thick texture: Zinc oxide can give sunscreen a thicker, more paste-like texture, which can be difficult to apply smoothly and evenly.

  3. Limited water resistance: Zinc oxide is not as water-resistant as some other sunscreen ingredients, so it may need to be reapplied more frequently if you're swimming or sweating.
