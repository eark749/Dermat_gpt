What are the drawbacks of using zinc oxide in sunscreen?
While zinc oxide is generally considered safe and effective for use in sunscreen, there are a few potential drawbacks to be aware of:
White cast
: Zinc oxide can leave a white cast on the skin, which can be particularly noticeable on people with darker skin tones. However, newer formulations of zinc oxide sunscreen are designed to minimize this effect.
Thick texture
: Zinc oxide can give sunscreen a thicker, more paste-like texture, which can be difficult to apply smoothly and evenly.
Limited water resistance
: Zinc oxide is not as water-resistant as some other sunscreen ingredients, so it may need to be reapplied more frequently if you're swimming or sweating.
Potential for inhalation
: When zinc oxide is in the form of fine particles, it can be inhaled and may cause respiratory irritation. However, this is primarily a concern for people who work in industries that involve handling zinc oxide powder, rather than for people using sunscreen.
Price
: Sunscreens containing zinc oxide may be more expensive than some other types of sunscreen.
It's important to note that the potential drawbacks of using zinc oxide in sunscreen are generally outweighed by its benefits. If you have concerns about using zinc oxide sunscreen, talk to your dermatologist or healthcare provider.
