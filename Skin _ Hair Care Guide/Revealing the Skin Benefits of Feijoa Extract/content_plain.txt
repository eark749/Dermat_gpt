DIY Skincare Recipes with Feijoa Extract
Creating DIY skincare formulations using feijoa extract can be a fun and effective method to take advantage of its skin-beneficial characteristics. Feijoa, which is high in vitamins, antioxidants, and moisturizing substances, can be utilized in a variety of DIY remedies. Here are some basic DIY skincare recipes using feijoa extract:
Feijoa Face Mask
: Ingredients are 1 ripe feijoa fruit, 1 tablespoon plain yogurt, and 1 teaspoon honey. Scoop out the flesh of the feijoa fruit into a bowl and mash it well with a fork. Add yogurt and honey to the mashed feijoa and mix thoroughly until you get a smooth paste. Apply the mask to
cleansed skin
, avoiding the
eye area
. Leave it on for 15-20 minutes. Rinse off with lukewarm water and pat your skin dry. Follow up with your favorite
moisturizer
.
Feijoa Sugar Scrub
: Ingredients are
1 ripe feijoa fruit, 2 tablespoons granulated sugar and 1 tablespoon coconut oil (or olive oil). Cut the feijoa in half and scoop out the flesh into a bowl. Mash the feijoa fruit well with a fork. Add granulated sugar and coconut oil to the mashed feijoa and mix until well combined. Gently massage the scrub onto damp skin in circular motions, focusing on rough areas like elbows and knees. Rinse off with warm water and pat your skin dry. Follow with moisturizer.
Feijoa and Aloe Vera Soothing Gel
: Ingredients are 1 ripe feijoa fruit, 2 tablespoons fresh aloe vera gel, and 1-2 drops of lavender essential oil (optional, for fragrance). Scoop out the flesh of the feijoa fruit into a blender or food processor. Add fresh aloe vera gel and blend until smooth. If desired, add a few drops of lavender essential oil for added soothing benefits and fragrance. Transfer the mixture into a clean container with a lid. Store in the refrigerator for a cooling effect. Apply a thin layer of this soothing gel to irritated or inflamed skin as needed. Leave it on for 10-15 minutes and rinse off with cool water.

Storing and Preserving Feijoa Extract
Storing and keeping feijoa extract properly is critical for maintaining its potency and preventing deterioration. Here are some suggestions for storing and preserving feijoa extract.
Storing Feijoa Extract
:
If you've just extracted juice or pulp from feijoa, keep it in an airtight container in the fridge. This preserves its freshness and inhibits bacterial growth. If you have a commercially made feijoa extract or serum, follow the manufacturer's directions. In general, these products should be kept cool and dry, away from direct sunlight.
Feijoa extract should be stored at temperatures ranging from 5°C to 15°C (41°F to 59°F). Avoid exposing it to severe temperatures, since this can impair its stability and efficacy.
Use clean, airtight glass containers or bottles to store feijoa extract. This prevents oxidation and contamination, ensuring the extract remains fresh for longer.
Store feijoa extract away from direct sunlight and in a dark environment. Light exposure can degrade the active compounds in the extract over time.
Preserving Feijoa Extract:
If preparing homemade feijoa extract, consider adding natural preservatives like vitamin E oil or grapefruit seed extract (GSE). These help extend the shelf life of the extract by inhibiting microbial growth and oxidation.
Adjusting the pH of the feijoa extract can also help in preserving it. Most microorganisms thrive in neutral pH conditions, so adjusting the pH to slightly acidic (around pH 4-5) can help prevent spoilage.
Feijoa extract itself contains antioxidants that can help preserve its freshness. However, adding additional antioxidants like vitamin C or rosemary extract can further extend its shelf life by scavenging free radicals that cause oxidation.
Check the expiration date if using commercially prepared feijoa extract. For homemade extracts, use them within a few weeks if refrigerated, or freeze them for longer-term storage.
Use clean utensils and containers when handling feijoa extract to prevent contamination. Ensure that hands are washed thoroughly before extraction or handling.
