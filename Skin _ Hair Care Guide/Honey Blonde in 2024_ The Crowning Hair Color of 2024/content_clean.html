DIY Tips for Dyeing Hair Honey Blonde at Home

Dying your hair Honey Blonde at home can be a fun and cost-effective option, but it is important to proceed with caution to achieve the desired result while minimising damage. Here are some do-it-yourself tips for dying your hair Honey Blonde at home:

  •  Choose the Right Shade: Choose a Honey Blonde hair dye that matches the tone you want. Select a shade based on your skin tone and natural hair colour; if a warm, golden tone suits your complexion, go for it.

  • Do a Strand Test: Do a strand test before putting the dye all over your head. This helps ascertain the color's appearance on your hair as well as the processing time required to achieve the desired outcome.

  • Prepare Your Hair: To preserve the natural oils that shield your scalp, begin with unwashed hair. To avoid staining, run a thin layer of petroleum jelly along your ears and hairline.

  • Observe the Guidelines: Read the directions carefully and adhere to them when using the hair dye. To prevent staining on your hands during the application process, wear gloves.

  • Make a section in your hair as follows: Using clips, divide your hair into manageable sections. This ensures that the dye is distributed evenly throughout your hair.  

  • Apply the Dye: Begin by applying the dye to the roots and working your way down to the ends. Apply with precision using an applicator bottle or tinting brush. Ensure thorough saturation of each section for even color.

  • Timing: Follow the recommended processing time provided with the hair dye. Check your hair periodically to monitor the color development.

  • Rinse and Condition: Once the processing time is complete, rinse your hair thoroughly with lukewarm water until the water runs clear. Apply the conditioner provided in the dye kit or a deep conditioning treatment to moisturize and nourish your hair.

  • Post-Color Care: Use sulfate-free, color-safe shampoos and conditioners formulated for blonde or color-treated hair to maintain the Honey Blonde color. Minimize heat styling and protect your hair from the sun to prevent color fading.

  • Maintenance: Plan for regular touch-ups as the roots grow out, maintaining the vibrancy of your Honey Blonde shade.

It's crucial to remember that to get the desired effect without harming your hair, major colour changes—especially going from a darker hue to Honey Blonde—may require expert assistance. Consider seeking advice or help from a hairstylist if you're unsure or have certain hair difficulties.

Protective Haircare Routines for Colored Hair

Maintaining the health and vibrancy of coloured hair, including Honey Blonde, necessitates a protective haircare routine. Here are some essential tips for protecting your coloured hair:

  1. Color-Safe Products: Select sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners designed specifically for colored or treated hair. These products help your hair retain colour while gently cleansing and moisturising it.

  2. Limit Washing: Avoid washing your hair on a daily basis, as this can strip away natural oils and cause colour to fade faster. Try to space out washes to preserve color, and use dry shampoo between washes if necessary. 

  3. Cold Water Rinse: After shampooing and conditioning, rinse your hair with cold water. Cold water seals the cuticle of the hair, locking in moisture and preventing colour from washing out

  4. Deep Conditioning: Incorporate deep conditioning treatments or hair masks into your routine on a regular basis to keep your hair hydrated, nourished, and resistant to damage.

  5. Heat Styling Protection: When using styling tools such as flat irons, curling irons, or blow dryers, use heat protectant products to minimise heat styling. Lower heat settings can also help to protect coloured hair.

  6. UV Protection: When spending time outside, protect your hair from the sun's harmful UV rays by using UV protection sprays, hats, or scarves.

  7. Avoid Chlorine and Saltwater: Before swimming in chlorinated pools or saltwater, wet your hair and apply a protective leave-in conditioner to create a barrier that helps minimise color damage.

  8. Gentle Detangling: Use wide-tooth combs or detangling brushes to remove knots and tangles gently, minimising breakage, especially when your hair is wet and more prone to damage.

  9. Trims: Schedule regular trims to remove split ends and prevent further damage. This contributes to the overall health and appearance of your coloured hair.

  10. Professional Care: Visit your hairstylist on a regular basis for touch-ups, toning treatments, or deep conditioning sessions to keep your coloured hair vibrant and healthy. 

  11. Hydration and a Well-balanced Diet: A well-balanced diet and staying hydrated both contribute to overall hair health. Nutrients such as vitamins A, C, E, and biotin can help promote healthy hair growth and strength.

Remember that each hair type and colour treatment have unique requirements. To keep your Honey Blonde colour looking vibrant and healthy for longer, tailor your haircare routine to your hair's unique characteristics and the specific needs of your colour.

Natural Remedies to Enhance Honey Blonde Tones

While natural remedies will not drastically alter your hair's colour, they will help to enhance and maintain Honey Blonde tones. Here are some natural ways to enhance the warmth and vibrancy of your Honey Blonde hair:

  • Chamomile Tea Rinse: Make chamomile tea, cool it, and use it as a final rinse after shampooing. Chamomile has mild lightening properties and can gradually add golden highlights to blonde hair.

  • Lemon Juice: Lemon juice can naturally lighten hair when exposed to sunlight. Mix fresh lemon juice with water and spritz it on your hair before going out in the sun. However, if used excessively, lemon juice can cause hair to dry out.

  • Honey and Olive Oil Mask: Combine honey and olive oil to make a hair mask. Apply an equal amount of honey and olive oil to damp hair. Leave it on for 20-30 minutes before rinsing. Honey can add shine and enhance golden tones.

  • Apple Cider Vinegar (ACV) Rinse: After shampooing, dilute apple cider vinegar with water and use it as a final rinse. ACV can help balance pH levels, remove buildup, and enhance hair shine.

  • Herbal Infusions: Make herbal infusions with calendula, marigold, or saffron. Steep these herbs in boiling water for 5 minutes, then strain and use as a rinse after shampooing to add subtle highlights.

  • Cinnamon Mask: Combine cinnamon powder and conditioner to make a paste. Apply the mixture to damp hair, let it sit for an hour or so (cover with a shower cap), and then rinse. Cinnamon may warm up your hair.

  • Rhubarb Root: Boil the rhubarb root in water, strain it, and use the liquid as a final rinse. This herb may help to bring out the golden tones in blonde hair.

Keep in mind that natural remedies may take time to work and may not produce immediate or dramatic results. Furthermore, these methods may not be suitable for all hair types, and excessive use or misuse may cause damage to your hair. Always perform a patch test and consult with a professional hairstylist before trying any new natural remedy to ensure it's suitable for your hair type and condition.
