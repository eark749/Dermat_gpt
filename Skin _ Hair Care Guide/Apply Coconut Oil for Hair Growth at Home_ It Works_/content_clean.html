How to apply coconut oil on hair and scalp

Coating the hair in coconut oil after washing helps make the hair smoother and softer. But for best results, experts recommend to apply coconut oil for hair fall in the following ways:

What You Will Need: Virgin or extra virgin coconut oil, A small bowl or container for warming the oil (optional), and a wide-tooth comb (optional)

Steps:

  1. Choose the Best Coconut Oil: Look for high-quality virgin or extra virgin coconut oil. These coconut oils have been minimally processed and thus retain more of their natural nutrients.

  2. Warm the Coconut Oil (Optional): Coconut oil is solid at normal temperature, but you can gradually warm it to a liquid form if you want. Place the container of coconut oil in a basin of warm water or microwave it for a few seconds. Take care not to overheat the oil, as this can ruin some of its therapeutic properties.

  3. Section Your Hair (Optional): If you have long or thick hair, sectioning it can help with even oil distribution.  Make manageable sections with clips or hair ties.

  4. Apply to Scalp: Apply the coconut oil to your scalp with your fingers.  Begin by parting your hair and applying the oil directly to your scalp, working it into the roots. You can also use a small brush or an applicator bottle for precise application.

  5. Massage the Scalp: Apply a small amount of coconut oil to your scalp by making circular movements with your fingertips.  Massage your scalp to increase blood circulation and hair growth. Massage for a further 5-10 minutes.

  6. Apply to Hair: After you have covered your scalp, work the remaining coconut oil through the length of your hair. If your hair is especially dry or damaged, concentrate on the ends.

  7. Comb Through (Optional): Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the oil evenly through your hair and detangle any knots.  This step is optional, but it can help ensure that the oil is distributed evenly.

  8. Wrap Your Hair (Optional): To keep the oil from dripping and to allow it to penetrate your hair and scalp, wear a shower cap or a warm, damp towel over your head.

  9. Leave it on: Leave the coconut oil on your hair and scalp for at least 30 minutes, but you can also leave it on overnight for a more intensive treatment.  Consider using a towel or a hair wrap to protect your pillow while sleeping.

  10. Shampoo and Rinse: After the specified time, thoroughly shampoo your hair to remove the oil.  You may need to shampoo more than once to remove all of the oil. If desired, apply conditioner after.

  11. Towel Dry: Gently towel dry your hair, then style as usual. After using coconut oil, you may notice that your hair feels softer and appears shinier.

You can use coconut oil as a hair treatment once a week or as needed, depending on the condition of your hair and your preferences. You may notice changes in the texture, strength, and overall health of your hair and scalp over time.

Addressing Common Misconceptions about Coconut Oil for Hair

Coconut oil has grown in popularity as a natural hair care solution; however, there are several common misconceptions regarding its use that must be addressed:

  1. Coconut Oil for All Hair Types: While coconut oil is beneficial to many hair types, it may not be appropriate for everyone. People with fine or thin hair may find that coconut oil weighs it down and makes it look greasy. It is important to evaluate your hair type and its requirements.

  2. Overnight Treatments Are Always Better: It's usual to leave coconut oil on your hair overnight, but it's not for everyone. Some people may find that applying the product for a shorter period of time, such as 30 minutes to a few hours, delivers the same advantages without leaving their hair overly oily.

  3. Coconut Oil is a Cure-All for Hair Loss: Coconut oil can help strengthen and repair hair, but it is not a cure for hair loss caused by genetics or medical conditions. If you are experiencing significant hair loss, you should see a doctor right away.

  4. Coconut Oil Can Repair Split Ends: Coconut oil can temporarily hide split ends by adding moisture and smoothing the hair. It cannot, however, repair or permanently repair split ends. Split ends are best addressed through regular haircuts.

  5. More is Better: Using too much coconut oil can make your hair look greasy and weighed down. Begin with a small amount and gradually increase based on your hair's needs and type.

  6. Coconut Oil Can Replace Conditioner: While coconut oil is a great natural conditioner, it may not be suitable for everyone as a sole replacement for traditional hair conditioner. Both are beneficial to many different hair types.

  7. Immediate Results: Some people may not see immediate results from coconut oil. The key is consistency, and it may take several applications to see improvements in hair health and appearance.

  8. One-Size-Fits-All: What works for one person may not work for another. The effectiveness of coconut oil for hair varies depending on individual factors such as hair type, condition, and specific needs.

  9. Addressing All Hair Issues: Coconut oil is not a panacea for all hair problems. While it can help with dryness, damage, and dandruff, it may not be the best solution for every hair problem.

Do's and Don’ts: Coconut Oil Hair Treatment

There are specific do's and don'ts to keep in mind when using coconut oil for hair care to achieve the greatest results and minimise any complications. Here are some pointers to keep in mind:

Do's:

  1. Start with Clean, Dry Hair: Make sure your hair is clean and dry before applying coconut oil. This allows the oil to enter deeper into the hair and scalp.

  2. Use Unrefined, Pure Coconut Oil: For the greatest benefits for your hair, use unrefined, pure coconut oil (extra virgin or virgin).  It is less processed and still has its original qualities.

  3. Warm the Coconut Oil: Before using, slowly reheat the coconut oil until it becomes liquid.  This guarantees even coverage and facilitates distribution.

  4. Split Your Hair: To ensure that you can apply the oil evenly and reach every part of your scalp and hair, split your long or thick hair into sections.

  5. Massage into Scalp: Apply the coconut oil to your scalp with circular motions using your fingertips.  This may aid in enhancing blood flow and encouraging hair development.

  6. Focus on the Ends: If your hair is dry and damaged, pay particular attention to the ends of it.  Breakage and split ends can both be avoided with the use of coconut oil.

  7. Cover Your Hair: To help the oil absorb more fully, you can cover your hair with a warm towel or shower cap after you have applied it.  If you want an intense treatment, you can leave it on overnight or for at least thirty minutes.

  8. Shampoo Completely: To completely remove the coconut oil, use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo.  It is possible that you will need to shampoo twice to remove all of the oil.

  9. Apply on a regular basis: For best results, use coconut oil as a hair treatment on a regular basis, ideally once a week or as needed based on the condition and preferences of your hair.

Don'ts:

  1. Don't Apply to Wet Hair: Coconut oil works best on dry hair. Wet hair can form a barrier that prevents the oil from penetrating efficiently.

  2. Don't Overheat the Oil: When warming coconut oil, take care not to overheat it, since it can become too hot to comfortably apply. Warm it gradually in short intervals over a double boiler or in the microwave.

  3. Don't Use Excessive Amounts: For hair treatment, you don't need to use a lot of coconut oil. A little goes a long way, and using too much might make it difficult to remove.

  4. Don't Leave It On Too Long: While overnight treatments might be useful, putting coconut oil on your hair for long periods of time, especially in hot conditions, can cause oiliness and make your hair seem greasy. Find a time frame that works for you.

  5. Do not forget to Shampoo: Shampooing your hair is essential for thoroughly removing the coconut oil. If you don't, you will look greasy and unwashed.

  6. Do not Ignore Allergies: If you have allergies or sensitivities to coconut or coconut oil, avoid using it on your hair or skin.  Perform a patch test if you're unsure about potential allergies.

  7. Do not Forget About Your Hair Care Routine: While coconut oil can be beneficial, it is not a replacement for your regular hair care routine.  Continue to use your regular shampoo and conditioner.

You can make the most of your coconut oil hair treatment and enjoy the benefits it provides for your hair and scalp by following these dos and don'ts.
