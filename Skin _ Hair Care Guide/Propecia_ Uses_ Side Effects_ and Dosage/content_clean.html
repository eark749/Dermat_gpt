Propecia (finasteride) is a medicine that primarily treats male pattern baldness. It acts by blocking the conversion of testosterone to dihydrotestosterone (DHT), a hormone associated with hair loss. This blog provides a detailed reference to Propecia's uses, side effects, and dosage.

Understanding Propecia 

Lowering DHT levels promotes regrowth. While it can cause adverse effects, they are usually controllable. Consistent use and regular medical monitoring are essential for attaining optimal effects while limiting dangers. 

What is Propecia?

Propecia inhibits the conversion of testosterone into dihydrotestosterone (DHT) in the body. Propecia is used to treat male pattern hair loss in the vertex and anterior mid-scalp area. Male pattern hair loss is a frequent condition in which men's hair thins on the scalp. Frequently, this leads to a receding hairline and/or balding on the crown of the head. Propecia is only for men, and it should not be used by women or children.

How Propecia Works

Propecia inhibits the conversion of testosterone to the androgen dihydrotestosterone (DHT) by targeting the enzyme 5-alpha reductase, which is involved in steroid metabolism. Testosterone is a hormone found in both men and women, with men typically having more testosterone in their systems. According to the Society for Endocrinology, around 10% of all adults' testosterone is transformed into DHT. Testosterone promotes the growth of body hair.

Conditions Treated by Propecia

Propecia is solely FDA-approved for the treatment of male hair loss. It is best suited for males with crown thinning or baldness. Propecia can—and should—be used in conjunction with other preventive hair loss therapies, such as shampoos and topical treatments. Because of the risk of fetal birth abnormalities, this medication is not indicated for women who are pregnant. It's worth noting that, according to limited data, women who experienced hair loss after menopause have had success with Propecia.

Benefits and Effectiveness of Propecia

  • Male pattern baldness, which affects about half of all men over 40, can be safely and effectively treated with propecia (finasteride).

  • Combining Propecia with other baldness treatments, like hair transplant surgery, can help treat hair loss.

  • Many of the most frequent side effects of Propecia are transient and go away as soon as the body gets used to the drug.

Propecia for Hair Loss

Propecia will not treat the underlying cause of hair loss in the long term. Although it temporarily treats the underlying problems by lowering DHT levels. It is not a cure for hair loss. Male pattern baldness is inherited. If you discontinue hair loss treatment, the results will wear off, and hair loss will resume within 6-12 months.

Clinical Studies and Research

Studies have demonstrated that finasteride can lower serum DHT levels by 70% and prostatic DHT levels by up to 90%. Nevertheless, a higher dose does not always translate into a lower level of serum DHT.

In contrast to the placebo-treated men, the majority of finasteride-treated men (65%) experienced increases in hair count at five years. However, even among the finasteride-treated men who experienced a decrease in hair count at five years relative to baseline, the average magnitude of loss was lower than that of the placebo group.

User Experiences and Reviews

Propecia user reviews are divided, with many reporting great outcomes in terms of hair regrowth and maintenance, while others complain about side effects, particularly sexual and mood concerns. Propecia's effectiveness varies, and continued use is required to sustain results. 

Potential Side Effects of Propecia

Finasteride, the main component of Propecia, effectively suppresses the hormone responsible for male pattern baldness, a common hereditary type of hair loss. However, multiple studies have found that hair-restoration medicine might harm a man's sexual health, including lower sex drive, erectile dysfunction, and a decrease in the volume of ejaculation.

Common Side Effects

The majority of side effects associated with finasteride, or Propecia, are related to men's sexual health. In clinical trials, ejaculatory disorders, decreased libido, and erectile dysfunction affected at least 1% of male participants taking Propecia. Additional research found that similar negative effects in men persisted even after they stopped taking the medication. Propecia side effects include the following:

  • Breast Tenderness or Swelling

  • Decreased libido

  • Dizziness

  • Ejaculatory disorder

  • Erectile dysfunction

  • Headache

  • Male breast cancer

  • Skin rash or hives

  • Swelling in the face, arms, hands, legs, or feet

  • Weakness

There are no clear links between using the hair-growth treatment and experiencing sexual side effects. However, finasteride reduces the hormone dihydrotestosterone, or DHT, which is important in sexuality and development, making the link more likely. Lowering DHT levels in the body can help with hair loss, but the sex hormone is also required for the structural integrity of nerves, smooth muscle, connective tissue, and signaling pathways in the penis.

Rare but Serious Side Effects

Serious side effects are extremely rare, occurring in less than one in every 1,000 people. Some people may experience these side effects after taking finasteride for a few months.

  • Any lumps, discharge from your nipples, or pain or swelling in the chest region could be indicators of a dangerous illness like breast cancer.

  • low mood (depression) - if you are taking it for hair loss and you get this side effect, stop taking the medication immediately.

It is important to inform your family and friends that you are taking finasteride and that it may occasionally affect your mood or behavior. This is so they can help you notice any changes.

Managing Side Effects  

Four approaches to reduce the negative effects of Finasteride are listed below.

  • Lowering estrogen levels: One strategy for mitigating the negative effects of finasteride is to reduce the level of estrogen, a female hormone. It was discovered that some patients taking finasteride had a 15% increase in estrogen levels.  In some cases, this can lead to reduced sexual drive, increased body weight, and sadness. Doctors will prescribe a medication to help these patients maintain their estrogen levels. Aside from pharmaceuticals, eating zinc or iron-rich foods and exercising regularly can help with hormonal rebalancing.

  • Overcoming erectile dysfunction and increasing sperm count: Regular exercise can significantly reduce the side effects of finasteride, which causes sexual dysfunction. Regular exercise not only strengthens your heart and increases blood flow, but it also lowers your stress level. However, some foods, such as red ginseng, honey, chocolate, red chillies, oysters, and avocado, can help with erectile dysfunction while also increasing sperm count.

  • Increasing nitric oxide levels in the body: Nitric oxide is a naturally occurring chemical that plays many important roles in our overall health, including erectile function. Nitric oxide is also an important component of Viagra. However, if you are not interested in taking drugs or supplements, eating foods high in nitric oxide, such as lettuce, spinach, carrots, dark chocolate, and pomegranate, is an excellent alternative.

  • Try the topical version: Finasteride is also available in a topical form that is designed to treat the affected area locally. Topical finasteride is less effective than oral finasteride, but it has fewer side effects. 

Proper Dosage and Administration

The 'normal' dosage of Finasteride is entirely dependent on what you're using it for - Finasteride is used to treat a variety of problems, so the exact dosage varies depending on which of these conditions you want to treat. Finasteride can be used to treat the following conditions:

  • Male hair loss

  • Male pattern baldness

  • Receding hairline

  • Hair thinning

  • Enlarged prostate

In most cases, the Finasteride dosage is 1mg, which is used to treat hair loss disorders. To learn more about the outcomes, visit our Finasteride results page. Finasteride is also used to treat the symptoms of an enlarged prostate. In this scenario, Finasteride is administered at a higher dose: 5mg to treat enlarged prostates. Finasteride is used in both circumstances because it inhibits the formation of dihydrotestosterone (DHT), a male hormone that causes hair loss and can enlarge the prostate gland.

Recommended Dosage for Hair Loss

Regardless of height, weight, or age, the dose of Finasteride used to treat hair loss is the same: 1mg. This is because Finasteride is effective at treating hair loss with a daily dose of merely 1mg. It is vital to remember that taking a higher dose will not increase the treatment's effectiveness for hair loss. The same is true for Finasteride taken in various quantities for other medical disorders, such as an enlarged prostate gland; if in doubt, consult your doctor, or a dermatologists. There are a few exceptions. Finasteride is only recommended for men over the age of 18, and it should not be used by children or adolescents. Women should also avoid taking Finasteride because of its interaction with female hormones and the potential risks during pregnancy and nursing. Women should also avoid touching Finasteride tablets (especially if they are crushed or broken) to prevent absorption into the body.

How to Take Propecia Correctly

Take this medication orally with or without food as advised by your doctor, generally once a day. Women who are pregnant or may become pregnant should avoid handling this medication or breathing the dust from the pills since it can be absorbed through the skin and lungs and harm an unborn child.

What to Do if You Miss a Dose

Propecia is intended to be used once daily. When you miss a dose, simply continue taking your tablet as usual - only one per day - the following day. missing one dose is unlikely to have a significant impact on your hair growth; however, if you begin to miss doses regularly, your progress will likely halt and your hair loss may resume.

Conclusion: Making an Informed Decision About Propecia  

When deciding whether to use Propecia (finasteride), you must measure the potential benefits against the potential adverse effects, as well as take into account your specific health needs and goals. By carefully weighing these variables and discussing them with your doctor, you can make an informed decision about whether Propecia is the best option for managing your hair loss.
