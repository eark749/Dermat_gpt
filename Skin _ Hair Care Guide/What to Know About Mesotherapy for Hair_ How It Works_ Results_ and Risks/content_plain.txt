Comparing Mesotherapy to Other Hair Loss Treatments
Mesotherapy is a minimally invasive treatment that focuses on administering nutrients and growth elements directly to the scalp. Topical therapies and oral drugs are less invasive, but they must be used regularly and may have varied degrees of success. Hair transplant surgery offers a permanent cure, but it is both invasive and expensive. Low-level laser therapy (LLLT) and PRP therapy are two different techniques with varying degrees of efficacy and expense. The kind and stage of hair loss, personal preferences, and budget all play a role in determining the best treatment.

Step-by-Step Guide to a Mesotherapy Session
Pre-session
: Meet with your physician to talk about hair loss problems and evaluate your scalp condition.
Cleansing the Scalp
: The physician will cleanse your scalp with an antiseptic solution to remove any dirt, oil, or residue, which helps prevent infection.
Preparing the Injection Solution
: The physician will prepare a solution containing a blend of amino acids, vitamins, minerals, and growth factors that is designed for the treatment.
Injection Process
: The physician will inject the solution into your scalp. The injections are conducted in a grid-like pattern on the targeted areas, and it takes 30–60 minutes.
Post-procedure
: The physician applies a cool compress to soothe the treated areas and reduce any initial redness.
Follow-up
: Schedule follow-up appointments as recommended by your physician. Additional sessions are required every 1-2 weeks for a few months. During follow-up visits, the physician will assess your progress and adjust the treatment plan.
Monitoring
: The physician should see gradual improvements in hair density and quality over several weeks or months.
Potential Side Effects
: Mild redness, swelling, or tenderness at the injection sites, which usually resolve within a few days.

Stage of Hair Loss

Frequency of Treatment

Lifestyle Factors
