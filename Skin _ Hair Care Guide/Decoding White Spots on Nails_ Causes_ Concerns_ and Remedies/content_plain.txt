What are White Nail Spots?
White nail spots, commonly known as leukonychia, are little white spots on the nails. These spots, which can occur on one or several nails, are usually innocuous. Leukonychia is classified into two types: punctate and longitudinal. Punctate leukonychia appears on the nail as minute, pinpoint-sized spots, whereas longitudinal leukonychia appears as long, narrow streaks going along the length of the nail. Minor trauma to the nail bed, which can occur through behaviors such as nail biting, finger sucking, or accidentally smacking your nail against a hard surface, is the most common cause of white nail patches. Fungal infections, psoriasis, eczema, and certain drugs are all probable causes. White nail patches, in most situations, do not require treatment and will fade as the nail grows. However, if you are concerned about the appearance of your nails or if you have other symptoms such as discomfort, swelling, or redness around the nail, you should seek the advice of a healthcare practitioner.

Manicure Safety and Hygiene

Choose a reputable salon
: Look for a salon that is well-known for its cleanliness and employs sterile tools and equipment.
Bring your own tools
: To reduce the risk of infection, bring your own nail clippers, files, and other tools to the salon.
Don't share tools
: Sharing nail clippers or other tools can increase infection risk. Make certain that the salon's tools are sterile for each client.
Avoid cutting your cuticles
: Cutting your cuticles can make you more susceptible to infection. Instead, use a cuticle pusher to gently push back your cuticles.
Never wax or shave before getting a manicure
: Small cuts or abrasions caused by waxing or shaving increase the risk of infection. Before getting a manicure, wait at least one day after shaving or waxing.
Wash your hands
: Before getting a manicure, make sure your hands are clean and dry.
Avoid soaking your nails for too long
: Soaking your nails for too long can weaken them and increase the risk of infection. Soak for a maximum of 10-15 minutes.
Keep an eye out for signs of infection
: If you experience pain, redness, swelling, or other signs of infection after a manicure, seek medical attention right away.
By following these guidelines, you can help reduce the risk of infection or injury during a manicure while also keeping your nails in good condition.
