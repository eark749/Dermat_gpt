What Exactly is Volcanic Ash?
Volcanic ash is a thin, dust-like particle produced by the ejection of magma, volcanic gases, and solid rock fragments into the atmosphere during volcanic eruptions.  It is made up of microscopic glass particles, mineral pieces, and rock dust that have been pulverised and fractured by the eruption's explosive energy. It can have serious consequences for human health, infrastructure, and the environment, but it also helps to create fertile soils and shape volcanic landscapes. To manage the dangers connected with volcanic ash, proper monitoring, and mitigation techniques are required.
