What is Physalis Angulata?

Physalis angulata, often known as ground cherry, wild tomato, cutleaf groundcherry, or simply physalis, is a plant species from the Solanaceae family. It is widespread in tropical and subtropical areas around the world. Here's a thorough description of Physalis angulata:

  • Physalis angulata is an annual herb that develops to be around 1 to 1.5 metres tall. It has an upright stem that frequently branches.

  • The leaves are simple, ovate to lanceolate, with serrated or wavy edges.

  • The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow with dark spots in the center.

  • The most distinctive feature is its fruit, which is enclosed in a papery husk, resembling a lantern. The fruit itself is a small, round berry that turns yellow or orange when ripe.

  • Physalis angulata thrives in a variety of habitats, including fields, gardens, roadsides, and waste areas. It prefers well-drained soils and can tolerate different soil types.

If you have any adverse reactions, stop using the product right once and rinse the affected area thoroughly.

  6. Store Properly: Store Physalis angulata extract or products containing this component by the manufacturer's directions. Keep the product out of direct sunlight, heat, and moisture. To avoid product contamination or deterioration, keep the container tightly closed while not in use.
