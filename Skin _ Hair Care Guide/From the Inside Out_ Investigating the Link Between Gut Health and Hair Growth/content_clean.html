Gut Health's Impact on Hair Quality

 

Gut health can have a big impact on hair quality. The relationship between the stomach and hair quality is complicated and involves a number of elements. Here are some examples of how gut health might affect hair quality:

  1. Nutrient Absorption: The gut is in charge of absorbing critical nutrients from the food you eat. Many of these nutrients are essential for good hair growth, including biotin, zinc, iron, and vitamins. A healthy gut ensures that these nutrients are adequately absorbed and utilised by the body to support hair quality.

  2. Inflammation Control: A microbial imbalance in the intestines can cause chronic inflammation throughout the body, including the scalp. Scalp inflammation can affect the growth cycle of hair follicles, resulting in hair loss or poor hair quality.

  3. Hormone Regulation: The gut microbiota is involved in hormone metabolism and regulation. Hormonal imbalances, such as high levels of dihydrotestosterone (DHT), are linked to male and female pattern baldness. A healthy stomach can help with hormone balance, which can affect the quality of your hair.

  4. Immune System Support: A healthy gut microbiome promotes a strong immune system, which helps protect against conditions that can harm hair quality.

  5. Stress and Mental Health: Gut health can have an impact on stress levels and mental health. High levels of stress and anxiety can have a negative impact on hair quality and may result in hair loss. The gut-brain axis, a communication system between the gut and the brain, facilitates this connection.

  7. Get Enough Sleep: Aim for at least seven hours of sleep per night. Poor sleep habits can have an impact on gut health and overall wellness.
