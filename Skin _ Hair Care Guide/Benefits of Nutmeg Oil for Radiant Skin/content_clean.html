  • Fact: people all around the world have utilised nutmeg in cooking and as a component of folk cures. Stomach problems, diarrhoea, and rheumatism may have all been traditionally treated in Asia with their anti-inflammatory and antioxidant characteristics.

  • Fact: It is deemed safe at low levels. However, persons with skin diseases and children should avoid it. In addition to anti-aging qualities, nutmeg is anti-inflammatory and antibacterial. The essential oil can be incorporated into skin care, massage, and aromatherapy products.

Integrating Nutmeg Oil into Your Skincare Routine: Tips for Best Results

Nutmeg oil may be an excellent complement to your beauty routine. Here are some recommendations for efficiently incorporating nutmeg oil to maximise its benefits:

  • Always Dilute: Nutmeg oil is quite powerful and should be diluted with a carrier oil to avoid skin irritation. Carrier oils include jojoba, almond, coconut, and olive oils. The dilution ratio is 1-2 drops of nutmeg oil per teaspoon (5 mL) of carrier oil.
  • Perform a Patch Test: Before using nutmeg oil on your face, perform a patch test to rule out any allergic reactions or irritation. Add a tiny amount of nutmeg oil to a carrier oil. Use a little quantity on a discrete area of skin, such as the inside of your wrist or elbow. Wait 24 hours to see if irritation, redness, or itching develops.
  • Start Slowly: Introduce nutmeg oil gradually into your daily regimen to watch how your skin reacts. Use it once or twice a week at first, and then more frequently if your skin tolerates it.
