Causes of Acne Breakouts in the Rainy Season
Acne outbreaks during the rainy season can be attributable to a variety of factors, including changes in environmental circumstances and personal habits. The following are some of the most common reasons for acne breakouts during the rainy season:
Increased humidity
: High humidity levels are common during the rainy season, which can lead to increased sweating. Excess sweat can clog pores and cause acne breakouts by combining with dirt, oil, and dead skin cells.
Excess sebum production
: Humidity can cause the skin's sebaceous glands to produce more oil. Excess sebum can mix with bacteria and dead skin cells, clogging pores and causing acne.
Bacterial growth
: The warm and humid conditions of the rainy season provide an ideal environment for bacteria to thrive on the skin. Propionibacterium acnes, a skin bacterium, can multiply rapidly, causing inflammation and acne breakouts.
Increased pollution exposure
: Pollutants and environmental toxins can settle on the skin and contribute to clogged pores and acne breakouts. Additionally, due to increased moisture in the air during the rainy season, pollution levels may be higher.
Improper skincare routine
: Weather changes may necessitate changes to your skincare routine. Failure to properly cleanse the skin, use harsh or drying products, or fail to moisturize can all disrupt the skin's balance and cause acne breakouts.
Fungal infections
: During the rainy season, fungi infections like pityrosporum folliculitis are more prevalent. The overgrowth of yeast on the skin causes the bumps and inflammation that resemble acne that characterize these infections.
Lifestyle factors
: Certain lifestyle practices, such as frequently touching your face with dirty hands, wearing damp or wet clothes for an extended period of time, or skipping a shower after getting wet in the rain, can cause bacteria, dirt, and sweat to build up on your skin, increasing your risk of developing acne.
Maintaining a consistent skincare routine is essential for preventing or managing acne breakouts throughout the rainy season. This involves cleaning gently to remove excess oil, dirt, and pollutants, using non-comedogenic products, appropriately moisturizing the skin, and avoiding touching the face with dirty hands. It is also advised to wear loose, breathable clothing and to change out of moist or wet clothes as soon as possible. A dermatologist can provide additional information and personalized treatment choices in cases of persistent or severe acne.

Over-the-counter Treatments for Monsoon Acne
There are various over-the-counter treatments for monsoon acne available. Here are some common substances to look for in over-the-counter acne treatments:
Benzoyl peroxide
: Benzoyl peroxide is a powerful ingredient that kills acne-causing bacteria and aids in the unclogging of pores.  It has anti-inflammatory properties as well. Look for cleansers, creams, or gels with benzoyl peroxide concentrations ranging from 2.5% to 10%. Begin with a lower concentration to reduce the risk of skin irritation and gradually increase as needed.
Salicylic acid
: Salicylic acid is a beta-hydroxy acid that exfoliates the skin and aids in the unclogging of pores. It has anti-inflammatory properties as well. Look for salicylic acid-containing cleansers, toners, or spot treatments.  Begin with a low concentration (around 0.5% to 2%), then gradually increase as needed.
Retinoids
: Vitamin A derivatives such as adapalene promote skin cell turnover, prevent clogged pores, and reduce inflammation. They are available in gel and cream forms. Begin with a low concentration and use only as directed, as they can cause skin dryness and irritation. It is best to consult a dermatologist before using retinoids.
Sulfur
: Sulfur has antibacterial and drying properties. It aids in the reduction of excess oil production and the unclogging of pores. To target specific areas of acne, look for sulfur-based spot treatments or masks.
Alpha-hydroxy acids (AHAs)
: AHAs exfoliate the skin and help to improve the appearance of acne. Examples include glycolic acid, lactic acid, and citric acid. They can also aid in the fading of acne scars and the promotion of a more even skin tone.  Look for cleansers or toners that contain AHAs in low concentrations.
Topical antibiotics
: Some over-the-counter acne treatments include topical antibiotics such as clindamycin or erythromycin.  These antibiotics aid in the killing of acne-causing bacteria as well as the reduction of inflammation.  However, long-term use of topical antibiotics can lead to antibiotic resistance, so it is best to seek advice from a dermatologist.

The function of nutrition in acne control is still being researched and debated. While there is no one-size-fits-all solution, some dietary considerations may help with monsoon acne. Here are a few things to think about:

: Probiotics are advantageous microorganisms that promote gut health. There may be a connection between gut health and acne, according to some studies.  Consuming foods high in probiotics, such as yogurt, kefir, sauerkraut, and kimchi, may help to support a balanced population of gut bacteria.
Hydration
: Staying hydrated is essential for overall skin health. Drinking enough water helps flush out toxins and keeps your skin hydrated. Drink at least eight glasses of water each day.
Dairy and acne
: Some studies have suggested a link between dairy consumption and acne, specifically skim milk. However, the evidence is inconclusive, and individual reactions may differ. If you suspect a link, try reducing or eliminating dairy from your diet to see if it affects your acne.
Food sensitivities
: Some people may experience acne flare-ups as a result of food sensitivities. Gluten, dairy, and certain high-allergen foods are common culprits. If you suspect a certain food is causing your acne, keep a food diary to track any patterns.
