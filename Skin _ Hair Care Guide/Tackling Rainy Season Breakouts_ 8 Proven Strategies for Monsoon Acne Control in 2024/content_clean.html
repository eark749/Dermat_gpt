Over-the-counter Treatments for Monsoon Acne

There are various over-the-counter treatments for monsoon acne available. Here are some common substances to look for in over-the-counter acne treatments:

  1. Benzoyl peroxide: Benzoyl peroxide is a powerful ingredient that kills acne-causing bacteria and aids in the unclogging of pores.  It has anti-inflammatory properties as well. Look for cleansers, creams, or gels with benzoyl peroxide concentrations ranging from 2.5% to 10%. Begin with a lower concentration to reduce the risk of skin irritation and gradually increase as needed.

  2. Salicylic acid: Salicylic acid is a beta-hydroxy acid that exfoliates the skin and aids in the unclogging of pores. It has anti-inflammatory properties as well. Look for salicylic acid-containing cleansers, toners, or spot treatments.  Begin with a low concentration (around 0.5% to 2%), then gradually increase as needed.

  3. Retinoids: Vitamin A derivatives such as adapalene promote skin cell turnover, prevent clogged pores, and reduce inflammation. They are available in gel and cream forms. Begin with a low concentration and use only as directed, as they can cause skin dryness and irritation. It is best to consult a dermatologist before using retinoids.

  4. Sulfur: Sulfur has antibacterial and drying properties. It aids in the reduction of excess oil production and the unclogging of pores. To target specific areas of acne, look for sulfur-based spot treatments or masks.

  5. Alpha-hydroxy acids (AHAs): AHAs exfoliate the skin and help to improve the appearance of acne. Examples include glycolic acid, lactic acid, and citric acid. They can also aid in the fading of acne scars and the promotion of a more even skin tone.  Look for cleansers or toners that contain AHAs in low concentrations.

  6. Topical antibiotics: Some over-the-counter acne treatments include topical antibiotics such as clindamycin or erythromycin.  These antibiotics aid in the killing of acne-causing bacteria as well as the reduction of inflammation.  However, long-term use of topical antibiotics can lead to antibiotic resistance, so it is best to seek advice from a dermatologist.
