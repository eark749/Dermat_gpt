Myths and Facts About Hair Dye: Debunking Misconceptions
Myth
: Does hair dye cause side effects?
Truth
: Hair dyes contain harsh chemicals that can irritate the scalp's fragile skin, causing itching, redness, and inflammation. Some people may develop more severe allergic responses, such as hives, swelling, or even blistering.
Myth
: Will hair dye permanently damage my hair?
Truth
: Some, particularly permanent hair dyes, change the structure and composition of the hair. Many hair colors can cause damage to both the inside and outside of the hair shaft. Both natural and synthetic colors can weaken hair, but permanent dyes cause more damage since they enter deeper into the hair shaft.
Myth
: Can organic dyes be used safely?
Truth
: Organic coloring includes botanical components such as plant juices and oils, which are gentler on the scalp and less prone to cause long-term damage. Organic color is also safer for your skin and eyes because it contains no ammonia or peroxide.
Myth
: What if I dyed my hair without realizing I was pregnant?
Truth
: Everyone we spoke with said that hair dye rarely comes into contact with the skin on your head for long enough to cause substantial damage or harm. It just does not stay in contact with your skin long enough to absorb into the bloodstream in a way that is hazardous to a developing fetus.
Myth
: Does coloring hair cause hair fall?
Truth
: The truth is that the more frequently you dye your hair, the higher the likelihood of hair shedding. Hair dye does not prevent or inhibit
hair growth
, but it can induce hair loss by harming the color-treated hair.
Myth
: Can dying your hair permanently change its color?
Truth
: Unfortunately, hair color can harm your hair. When you dye your hair, the cuticle is lifted, altering the hair in a way that it will never fully return to its natural virgin state. However, the hair that grows from your scalp will grow normally, leaving only the dyed hair impacted.
Myth
: Is every hair the same color?
Truth
: Hair color is fully determined by the
pigments
found in your body. There are two types of pigments: melanin and carotene. Melanin is dark brown and carotene is reddish-yellow, and the amount of each determines your hair color.

Pre-Dye Hair Preparation: Steps to Minimize Damage
Before diving into the fascinating world of semi-permanent hair color, you should prepare your hair for the dyeing process. Follow these steps to get a vibrant, eye-catching hair color change that will boost your personality and attitude.
Cleanse and clarify your hair
: The first step in hair preparation is cleansing and clarifying. Removing product buildup, debris, and muck is critical for revealing your vibrant hair color. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove buildup and pollutants from the strands. Cleaning your hair provides the ideal canvas for your semi-permanent hair color to work its magic, resulting in a stunning hair color transformation. Furthermore, clean hair reduces damage and helps your hair color last longer. Wash your hair between 24 and 48 hours before applying hair color. This allows the natural oil to protect your hair from damage.
Pre-lighten your hair to the desired level
: Coloring your hair is a fun way to express your personality. Pre-lightening or bleaching is used to achieve the desired shade of your preferred hair color. It is an important step in getting your hair ready for semi-permanent coloring. Pre-lightening creates an even canvas, ensuring that the final hue is exactly what you want. If you have naturally light brown hair, you can avoid the pre-lightening step; nonetheless, brunettes must swim against the tide.
Conduct a Strand Test
: A strand test is required to ensure a positive outcome and avoid any negative consequences. Take a small section of hair, preferably from an inconspicuous location, and apply a small amount of the semi-permanent hair color you intend to use. Keep track of the color development and the time required to achieve the desired effect. The strand test allows you to fine-tune your color processing time to achieve the sparkling, vibrant color of your dreams.
Perform a patch test
: A patch test helps to detect any harmful reactions on the skin. Apply a small amount of semi-permanent hair color behind the ears. Wait 24 hours to check for any allergic reactions. Our semi-permanent hair color for men and women is generally suitable for all skin types. It is still suggested to perform a patch test for safety.
Gather All of Your Hair Coloring Supplies
: Before diving into the magical world of hair color, make sure you have all of your supplies. Gather all of your hair coloring supplies, including Colorisma hair coloring tubes, hand gloves, a hair coloring brush, a bowl, clips (for tying the hair in sections), a comb (for even application), and an old towel. These are the basic hair coloring ingredients you should always have on hand for a simple, even, and precise application that saturates each strand and yields stunning results.

Natural Alternatives to Chemical Hair Dye
Carrot juice
: If you want your hair to be reddish-orange, try using carrot juice. Depending on your hair color, the tint can last for several weeks. To tint your hair using carrot juice: Combine carrot juice with a carrier oil, such as coconut or olive oil. Use the mixture liberally on your hair. Wrap your hair in plastic and let the mixture cure for at least an hour. Rinse with apple cider vinegar. If the color isn't strong enough, repeat the process the next day.
Beet juice
: If you want a deeper red tint with colder undertones, use beet juice instead of carrot juice. To use beet juice as a natural hair dye, use the same techniques as for carrot juice: Combine beet juice with a carrier oil. Apply the mixture liberally to your hair, then wrap it. Allow the mixture to sit for at least an hour before washing it out.
Henna
: Henna is a natural plant-based dye that has traditionally been used to apply temporary tattoos straight to the skin. It can also be used to color your hair red. Henna powder is perhaps the most durable and bright natural hair dye alternative. The color can last up to four or six weeks. To use henna to dye your hair, use about 1/2 cup henna and 1/4 cup water. Stir until the mixture is thoroughly blended and has the consistency of mashed potatoes, adding more water if needed. Cover the mixture with plastic wrap and set aside for about 12 hours. (You may need to add more water after 12 hours to allow it to be administered to your hair.).  Wash your hair without conditioning it. Wear a headband and apply an
hair oil
, such as coconut oil, to your hairline. The henna mustn't discolor your skin. Apply the paste mixture in small parts to your combed, damp hair until it is completely covered.
Lemon juice
: Lemon juice can gradually remove pigment from your hair, causing it to lighten over time. It works well for creating natural sun-kissed highlights. Unlike the other treatments listed here, which produce only transitory color changes, the consequences of lemon juice are permanent. The pigment in the area of hair you apply lemon juice to will be removed. You can remove this lightened hair by cutting it off. How to use lemon juice to color your hair: Pour lemon juice into a spray bottle. Spray the juice liberally over your hair. Use a comb to distribute and separate the juice evenly throughout your hair. Sit outside in the sun for the best results. Allow the lemon juice to soak for at least an hour before washing it out. If necessary, repeat the procedure several times to achieve the desired result. It works slowly and will most likely require several sessions.
Coffee
: A cup of brewed coffee provides more than simply a caffeine boost. It can also help you tint your hair a few shades deeper and cover up some gray hair. How to dye your hair with coffee: Prepare a robust cup of dark-roasted coffee. Combine around 1/2 cup coffee, 2 tablespoons coffee grounds, and 1 cup leave-in hair conditioner. Use the mixture on clean, damp hair. Allow the mixture to rest for at least an hour before washing it out. Repeat as necessary.
Sage
: If you want to deepen your dark brown or black hair, sage is a great option. It can enhance brunette hair colors and help cover gray hair. How to Use Sage to Dye Your Hair: Soak 1/2 to 1 cup of dried sage in a quart of boiling water for at least 30 minutes. (The longer you let it steep, the darker the tint may become.) Allow the water to cool, and then filter out the sage. After washing and towel-drying your hair, use the sage water for as long as possible. Allow the color to develop for at least 15 minutes before washing it off.
Chamomile tea
: Do you want to lighten your hair? Chamomile tea can help you do just that, and it is especially effective for people who already have blonde hair.  Chamomile tea can help lighten your hair:  Steep 1/2 cup of chamomile flowers in boiling water. Allow the mixture to simmer for half an hour and then cool before straining the flowers. After washing your hair, apply the brewed tea to your damp hair at least ten times. Wait 16 minutes before washing it.

Frequency of Hair Dyeing: How Often is Too Often?
The last thing you want to worry about when you have dyed your hair and it looks great is the color fading. There are a few ways to increase the life of your naturally dyed hair:
Limit your use of hot tools such as hair dryers, straighteners, and curling irons.
When using hot styling tools, use a thermal protectant on your hair.
Avoid taking hot showers and washing your hair whenever possible.
Use a water filter in your shower to remove color-draining chemicals such as chlorine and heavy metals.
