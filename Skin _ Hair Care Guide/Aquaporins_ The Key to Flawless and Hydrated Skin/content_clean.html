What Are Aquaporins and How Do They Work?

Aquaporins are proteins that are found in the cell membranes of many species, including humans. They function as channels, or pores, that allow water molecules to pass across cell membranes. These proteins serve an important role in regulating water balance within cells and tissues by selectively allowing water to pass through while blocking other molecules or ions from passing through. Aquaporins have a pore-forming region made up of particular amino acids that form a channel through the cell membrane. This channel is extremely selective for water molecules, allowing them to pass the membrane quickly and efficiently.
