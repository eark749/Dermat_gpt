Myrrh, a resin extracted from Commiphora myrrha trees and allied species, has a long history of conventional use.  The main source of myrrh is the resin of the Commiphora myrrha tree, which is found in sections of Yemen, Oman, and Somalia as well as dry and semi-arid regions of the Middle East. Additionally, it can be found in eastern Africa, particularly in Sudan and Ethiopia. The use of myrrh in traditional medicine has a long history. Precautions and Considerations While Using Myrrh Essential Oil

 

While there are numerous potential benefits to using myrrh essential oil, it is critical to be aware of precautions and considerations to ensure safe and effective use. Here are a few key points to remember:

  1. Skin Sensitivity: When properly diluted, myrrh essential oil is generally considered safe for topical use.  However, it can irritate some people, particularly those with sensitive skin. Always do a patch test before applying it to a larger area of your skin.

  2. Dilution: Myrrh essential oil is extremely concentrated.  Before using it on the skin, it must be diluted with a carrier oil, like coconut oil or jojoba oil. 2-3 drops of myrrh essential oil per 1 ounce (30 mL) carrier oil is a common dilution ratio.

  3. Children and Infants: Myrrh essential oil is not typically recommended for use on children, particularly infants.  Consult a healthcare professional or aromatherapist before using it on children to ensure it is safe and properly diluted.

  4. Pregnancy and Nursing: Pregnant or nursing women should seek the advice of a healthcare professional before using myrrh essential oil.  While it is generally regarded as safe, personalised guidance is essential.

  5. Internal Use: Myrrh essential oil should not be consumed internally.  Ingestion of large amounts can be toxic. It should not be used as a food flavoring or dietary supplement.

  6. Medication Interactions: If you are taking medications, talk to your doctor or pharmacist before using myrrh essential oil, as it may interact with some of them.

  7. Storage: Keep myrrh essential oil in a cool, dark place away from direct sunlight and heat.  To prevent oxidation, make sure the cap is tightly sealed.

  8. Quality Matters: Select high-quality, pure myrrh essential oil from reputable sources to ensure it is free of contaminants and impurities.

  9. Sun Sensitivity: Myrrh essential oil has the potential to increase photosensitivity.  Avoid direct sunlight or UV rays for several hours after applying it to your skin. 

  10. Skin Irritation: If you experience skin irritation or other negative reactions after using myrrh essential oil, stop using it right away.  Wash the affected area with carrier oil to dilute the oil on your skin. If the irritation persists, seek medical advice.

  11. Consult a Professional: If you have underlying health conditions, consult with a healthcare professional or aromatherapist before using myrrh essential oil to ensure it is appropriate for your specific situation. 

By following these precautions and considerations, you can incorporate myrrh essential oil into your self-care routine safely and effectively, reaping its potential benefits while minimising the risk of adverse reactions.
