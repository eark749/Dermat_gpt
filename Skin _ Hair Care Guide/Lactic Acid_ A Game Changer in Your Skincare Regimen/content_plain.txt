It originated from sour milk, although in the cosmetics sector, it is frequently synthesised. Lactic acid is well-known for its ability to improve skin texture, stimulate cell turnover, and address a variety of skin issues. Understanding the Role of Lactic Acid
Lactic acid, a kind of alpha hydroxy acid (AHA), is important in cosmetics because of its diverse qualities.  Understanding the significance of lactic acid in skincare entails recognising its many benefits to skin health, such as exfoliation, hydration, brightness, and anti-aging properties. However, because of the heightened sensitivity to sunlight, it is recommended to use lactic acid products with caution, taking into account different skin types and conditions, and to combine sun protection.
Benefits of Lactic Acid for Skin Exfoliation and Renewal
Lactic acid is a popular ingredient in skincare products because it has several benefits for skin exfoliation and renewal. Here are the key advantages:
Gentle Exfoliation
: Lactic acid is an alpha hydroxy acid (AHA) that exfoliates the skin by breaking down the bonds that hold dead skin cells together. It provides a gentle exfoliation, making it ideal for people with sensitive skin who cannot tolerate stronger exfoliants like glycolic acid.
Improved Skin Texture
: Lactic acid promotes cell turnover, which means that dead skin cells are shed more efficiently.  Regular use results in smoother and softer skin texture, which reduces the appearance of rough or uneven areas.
Hydration and Moisture Retention
: Lactic acid is hygroscopic, meaning it attracts and retains water molecules. It helps to hydrate the skin, making it a valuable ingredient for people who have dry or dehydrated skin.
Brightening and Even Skin Tone
: Lactic acid inhibits tyrosinase, an enzyme involved in the production of melanin. Because of this property, lactic acid is effective in
addressing hyperpigmentation, dark spots
, and promoting a more even skin tone.
Reduction of Fine Lines and Wrinkles
: Lactic acid stimulates the production of collagen. Increased collagen production promotes skin elasticity, potentially
reducing the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles.
Acne Prevention and Treatment
: Lactic acid unclogs pores, which aids in the prevention and treatment of acne by keeping pores free of dead skin cells and excess sebum. It can help acne-prone people have clearer, healthier-looking skin.
Improved Absorption of Other Ingredients
: Lactic acid can improve the penetration of other skincare ingredients. It improves the overall efficacy of a skincare routine when combined with other active ingredients.
Anti-Inflammatory Properties
: Lactic acid has moderate anti-inflammatory properties. It can help soothe and calm irritated or inflamed skin, making it suitable for sensitive people.
pH Regulation
: Lactic acid aids in the maintenance of the skin's natural acidic pH. This promotes a healthy skin barrier and fosters the growth of beneficial microorganisms.
Suitable for Various Skin Types
:  Lactic acid is generally thought to be milder than other AHAs. It is suitable for a variety of skin types, including sensitive skin, and provides a gentle yet effective exfoliation option.
Including lactic acid in your skincare routine, whether through cleansers, toners, serums, or masks, can help you achieve a brighter, smoother, and more youthful face. Start with lesser doses and patch tests, especially if you're new to using exfoliating acids, and use sunscreen during the day to protect your skin. If you have specific skin concerns, a dermatologist can help you modify your skincare programme for the best results.
Lactic Acid for Different Skin Types
Lactic acid is acceptable for a wide range of skin types; however, the concentration and formulation of lactic acid-containing products should be chosen depending on individual skin needs and sensitivities. Here's a breakdown of how lactic acid can benefit various skin types:
Normal Skin
: Normal skin types can generally tolerate a wide range of skincare ingredients, including lactic acid. To maintain skin texture and promote a healthy glow, use lactic acid products in moderate concentrations (5-10%).
Dry or Dehydrated Skin
: Lactic acid's hygroscopic nature attracts and retains moisture, making it beneficial for dry or dehydrated skin. Choose formulations that focus on both exfoliation and hydration. Lactic acid lotions or serums can be particularly helpful.
Oily and Acne-Prone Skin
: Lactic acid exfoliates the skin, which aids in the unclogging of pores and the prevention of acne breakouts. Choose lactic acid toners or spot treatments with concentrations of 5-10%. Combine with other acne-fighting ingredients, such as salicylic acid.
Sensitive Skin
: Lactic acid is generally milder than other AHAs, making it suitable for people with sensitive skin. Look for lower concentrations (around 5%) and formulations designed specifically for sensitive skin. To ensure tolerance, perform a patch test first.
Combination Skin
: Lactic acid can address both oily and dry skin types, providing balanced exfoliation. Consider including lactic acid in formulations such as serums or cleansers. Adjust the frequency to meet the needs of your skin in different areas.
Mature or Aging Skin
: Lactic acid promotes skin firmness and reduces the appearance of fine lines by stimulating collagen production. For greater anti-aging benefits, choose formulations with higher concentrations (10-15%). Include in a comprehensive skincare regimen for mature skin.
Uneven Skin Tone and Hyperpigmentation
: Lactic acid inhibits tyrosinase, reducing hyperpigmentation and promoting even skin tone. Depending on the severity of hyperpigmentation, choose products with concentrations ranging from 5-15%. Combine with other brightening agents for the best results.
Rosacea-Prone Skin
: Exfoliating acids should be used with caution by people who have rosacea. Use lower concentrations of lactic acid (around 5%) and formulations with soothing ingredients if tolerated.
Consult a dermatologist
after performing a patch test.
Teenage Skin
: Lactic acid can help unclog pores, which is beneficial for teens suffering from acne. Look for cleaners or toners with lower concentrations. Stress the significance of sun protection.
Pregnancy and Breastfeeding
: While lactic acid is generally considered safe, pregnant or breastfeeding women should seek the advice of a healthcare professional. Use lower concentrations and avoid more aggressive formulations if approved.
Remember to gently add lactic acid into your skincare routine, particularly if you are new to utilising exfoliating acids. Exfoliating acids might make you more sun sensitive, so use sunscreen during the day. If you have specific skin concerns or conditions, a dermatologist can assist you in developing a tailored skincare strategy.
Incorporating Lactic Acid into Your Routine
To achieve optimal results while avoiding irritation, incorporate lactic acid into your skincare routine. Here's a step-by-step guide to effectively introducing and using lactic acid:
Start with a Patch Test
: Perform a patch test on a small area of your skin before applying lactic acid to your face to check for any adverse reactions.
Choose the Right Product
: Select a lactic acid product with an appropriate concentration based on your skin type and concerns. The typical concentrations range from 5% to 15%.
Frequency
: To begin, use lactic acid once a week to allow your skin to adjust. Increase the frequency gradually as your skin develops tolerance. Depending on individual needs, some may eventually use it every other day or even daily.
Apply on Clean, Dry Skin
: Lactic acid should be applied to clean, dry skin. If you use a toner, apply it before the lactic acid.
Layering with Other Products
: Use lactic acid before applying heavier creams or oils. If you use other active ingredients, such as
vitamin C
or retinoids, layer them on alternate nights to avoid irritation.
Sunscreen is Non-Negotiable
:
Using a broad-spectrum sunscreen
with at least SPF 30 during the day is essential, even on overcast days, as lactic acid can exacerbate sun sensitivity.
Adjust Based on Skin Response
: Take note of your skin's reaction. If you notice irritation, redness, or excessive dryness, reduce the frequency or switch to a lower concentration.
Combining with Other Products
: Lactic acid can be combined with other skincare ingredients, but be careful not to overdo it. To avoid irritation, avoid using multiple exfoliating products at the same time.
Consider Formulation
: Lactic acid is available in a variety of forms, including cleansers, toners, serums, and masks. Choose a formulation that meets your needs and fits into your daily routine.
Consult a Professional
: If you have specific skin concerns or conditions, consult with a dermatologist or skincare professional. They can provide personalized advice and recommend the most suitable products.
Take Breaks
: Give your skin a break from exfoliating acids now and then. This prevents excessive exfoliation and allows your skin to recover.
Seasonal Adjustment
: Consider adjusting the frequency of lactic acid use according to the season. You might need more exfoliation in the winter and less in the summer.
Be Patient
: Exfoliating acids take time to produce results. It may take a few weeks to notice improvements in skin texture, brightness, and other concerns.
Take into account Professional Treatments
: Professional chemical peels containing lactic acid can be considered under the supervision of a skincare professional if you want more intensive results.
Remember that everyone's reactions to skincare products differ, and what works for one person may not work for another. You must be patient, pay attention to your skin, and adjust as necessary. If you are unsure whether to incorporate lactic acid into your routine, a dermatologist can provide personalised advice based on your skin's specific needs.

How to Safely Use Lactic Acid in Daily Care
Including lactic acid in your regular skincare routine can help promote skin renewal and address a variety of issues. To minimise irritation or over-exfoliation, it's critical to use a careful and moderate approach. Here's a step-by-step approach to safely incorporating lactic acid into your regular skincare routine:
Start with a Patch Test
: Perform a patch test on a small, inconspicuous area of your face before applying lactic acid to the entire face to check for any adverse reactions. Wait 24 hours to ensure there is no discomfort.
Choose the Right Product
: Choose a lactic acid product with a concentration appropriate for daily use. Generally speaking, a lower concentration (about 5-8%) is advised for daily exfoliation.
Consider Your Skin Type
: Adapt the formulation to your skin type. If you have sensitive skin, choose products that are designed for it or contain additional soothing ingredients.
Frequency of Use
:
To begin, use lactic acid once or twice a week to allow your skin to adjust. Gradually increase the frequency as your skin develops tolerance. Some people may eventually use lactic acid daily.
Cleanse Your Skin
:
Cleanse your skin to remove any makeup, dirt, or impurities before beginning your skincare routine. This ensures that the lactic acid can effectively penetrate the skin.
Apply Lactic Acid
: Apply the lactic acid product to dry skin. Spread a small amount evenly across your face, avoiding the eye area.
Layering with Other Products
: Consider layering other active ingredients, such as vitamin C or retinoids, on alternate nights to avoid irritation. Apply lactic acid before applying heavier creams or oils.
Sunscreen is Non-Negotiable
: Lactic acid increases sun sensitivity, so use a broad-spectrum sunscreen with at least SPF 30 during the day, even on cloudy days.
Monitor Your Skin
: Take note of how your skin reacts. If you experience irritation, redness, or excessive dryness, reduce your lactic acid use or switch to a lower concentration.
Consider Morning or Evening Use
: Some people prefer to use lactic acid in their evening routine for overnight exfoliation and skin renewal. It can, however, be used in the morning if sunscreen is applied afterwards.
Take Breaks
: Allow your skin to rest from daily exfoliation on occasion. This prevents excessive exfoliation and allows your skin to recover.
Adjust Based on Seasons
: Consider varying the frequency of lactic acid use according to the season. You may require more exfoliation in the winter and less in the summer.
Be Patient
: Exfoliating acids produce long-term results. It may take a few weeks to notice improvements in skin texture, brightness, and other issues.
Consult a Professional
: Consult a dermatologist or skincare professional if you have specific skin concerns or conditions. They can provide personalised advice and recommend the best products.
Keep in mind that everyone's skin is unique, and what works for one person might not work for another. Listen to your skin, be patient, and make any necessary adjustments. Consistency is essential for achieving the desired results while maintaining skin health.

DIY Approaches with Lactic Acid
While professional-grade lactic acid treatments are typically performed by skincare specialists, there are other ways to incorporate lactic acid into your home skincare routine. It's vital to remember that while using lactic acid at home, you should start with lower quantities and follow basic rules to avoid irritation. Here are some do-it-yourself lactic acid solutions:
Lactic Acid Toners
: Select a lactic acid toner with a lower concentration (around 5-8%). Toners are simple to add to your routine after cleansing and before applying other products. Gently swipe a toner-soaked cotton pad over your face, avoiding the
eye area.
Begin with 2-3 times per week and gradually increase based on your skin's response.
Lactic Acid Serums
: Lactic acid serums come in a variety of concentrations. Begin with a lower concentration (around 5-10%) for beginners. Apply a small amount of serum to clean, dry skin before
applying moisturiser.
Begin with 1-2 times per week and gradually increase as tolerated.
Lactic Acid Masks
: Lactic acid masks are intended for only occasional use. Select a mask with a moderate concentration (approximately 10-15%). Apply the mask to clean skin, leave it on for the recommended amount of time, and then rinse off. Masks should be used once a week or as directed by the manufacturer..
DIY Lactic Acid Peels
: DIY peels must be approached with caution, and the concentration should be lower than that used in professional settings.  You can make your lactic acid peel with yoghurt (which contains lactic acid), honey, and aloe vera. However, it is critical to conduct a patch test and begin with extremely low concentrations.
Lactic Acid Cleansers
: Lactic acid cleansers are a gentle way to introduce lactic acid into your routine. Choose cleaners with lower concentrations (less than 5%). Use the cleanser as part of your daily cleansing routine. Massage into damp skin, then thoroughly rinse.
Combination with Hydrating Ingredients
: To combat potential dryness, combine lactic acid with hydrating ingredients such as hyaluronic acid or glycerin. After applying lactic acid, apply a hydrating serum or moisturiser to lock in moisture.
Sunscreen is Non-Negotiable
: Apply a broad-spectrum sunscreen with at least SPF 30 every morning when using lactic acid. Lactic acid can make you more sensitive to the sun.
Patch Testing
: First and foremost, perform a patch test on a small area of your face to check for any adverse reactions before applying lactic acid to your entire face.
Gradual Introduction
: If you are new to lactic acid, start with lower concentrations and gradually increase frequency as your skin develops tolerance.
Listen to Your Skin
: As needed, reduce the frequency or switch to a lower concentration if you experience irritation, redness, or excessive dryness. Always put your skin's health and comfort first.
Consultation with Professionals
: If you have specific skin concerns or conditions, consult with a dermatologist before beginning any DIY lactic acid treatments.
It's critical to proceed with caution while performing DIY lactic acid treatments and to be conscious of your skin's reaction. Seek expert guidance if you have any worries or uncertainties.
