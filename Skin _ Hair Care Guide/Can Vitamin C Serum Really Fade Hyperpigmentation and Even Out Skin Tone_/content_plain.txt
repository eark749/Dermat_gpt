The Science Behind Vitamin C's Skin Benefits
Vitamin C's skin advantages are based on its molecular and biochemical features, as well as its different roles within the skin. A closer look at the research underlying Vitamin C's skin benefits follows:
Antioxidant Activity
:
Vitamin C is an extremely potent antioxidant
. Free radicals, which are highly reactive molecules produced by conditions like as UV radiation, pollution, and environmental stress, are neutralised by antioxidants. Free radicals can cause skin cell damage and accelerated ageing. The capacity of vitamin C to neutralise free radicals protects the skin from oxidative stress.
Collagen Synthesis
: Vitamin C is required for the manufacture of collagen, a structural protein that gives the skin strength and flexibility. Collagen is an important component of the extracellular matrix of the skin. Vitamin C functions as a cofactor for collagen production enzymes such as prolyl and lysyl hydroxylases. Vitamin C promotes collagen formation, which helps to keep skin tight and resilient.
Inhibition of Melanin Production:
Vitamin C inhibits the production of melanin, the pigment that gives skin its colour. It inhibits the enzyme tyrosinase, which is involved in melanin formation. Vitamin C prevents and reduces hyperpigmentation by controlling melanin production, resulting in more equal skin tone.
Wound Healing
: Vitamin C is essential for wound healing. It helps to produce a robust and functioning collagen matrix, which is essential for tissue repair. Vitamin C also promotes the migration and multiplication of skin cells that aid in wound healing.
Anti-Inflammatory Properties
:
Vitamin C has anti-inflammatory effects that can help reduce skin inflammation. This makes it useful for people who suffer from inflammatory skin disorders like acne or redness. The anti-inflammatory properties of vitamin C contribute to general skin health.
UV Protection
:
While it is not a replacement for sunscreen, Vitamin C does provide some UV protection. It can help reduce the consequences of UV-induced skin damage, including the generation of free radicals and collagen degradation.
Regeneration of Vitamin E
: Vitamin C promotes regeneration. Another key antioxidant is vitamin E. Vitamin E protects cell membranes from oxidative damage, and Vitamin C regenerates Vitamin E, which improves the skin's total antioxidant defence system.
It should be noted that the efficacy of vitamin C in skincare products can be affected by factors such as formulation, stability, and concentration.  Vitamin C is a water-soluble vitamin that degrades in the presence of light, air, or heat. Choosing a stable type of Vitamin C and storing skincare products properly is therefore critical for reaping its full advantages. Individual reactions to Vitamin C might vary, so it's best to patch-test new products before incorporating them into a beauty programme. A dermatologist can provide personalised advice if you have specific skin concerns or conditions.

Combining Vitamin C with Other Hyperpigmentation Treatments

Combination Tip

Retinoids (Retinol, Tretinoin)
:
How They Work
: Retinoids are known for promoting cell turnover, increasing collagen production, and fading hyperpigmentation. They have the potential to be effective in the treatment of sun damage and age-related hyperpigmentation.
Combination Tip
: Vitamin C and retinoids can be combined to provide a synergistic effect. However, some people may be sensitive to them, so introduce them gradually and watch your skin's reaction.
Niacinamide (Vitamin B3)
:
How It Works
: Niacinamide has anti-inflammatory properties and aids in the regulation of melanin production, making it effective in the treatment of hyperpigmentation and the improvement of skin tone.
Combination Tip

Licorice Extract

Combination Tip

Hydroquinone
:
How It Works

Combination Tip

Best Practices for Applying Vitamin C Serum
To maximise the benefits of Vitamin C serum and ensure its effectiveness, it is critical to apply it according to best practises. Here are some pointers for using Vitamin C serum:
Start with Clean Skin
: Begin your skincare routine by cleansing your skin. Remove any makeup, dirt, or impurities with a gentle cleanser. This enhances the penetration of the Vitamin C serum.
Toner (Optional)
:
If you use a toner in your routine, apply it after cleansing. Toners can help to balance the pH of the skin and create a smooth canvas for subsequent products.
Use Vitamin C Serum on Dry Skin
:
After cleansing, pat your face dry and apply the Vitamin C serum to dry skin. Water on the skin has the potential to dilute the serum and reduce its effectiveness.
Dispense the Appropriate Quantity
:
Follow the product instructions for how much serum to use. In general, a small amount (typically the size of a pea) is adequate for the face. Excessive application may not produce better results and may cause irritation.
Gently Apply and Pat
: Utilise your fingertips to apply the Vitamin C serum. Gently pat and press the serum into your skin, concentrating on areas of hyperpigmentation or uneven skin tone. Avoid vigorously massaging the serum.
Avoid the Eye Area
:
Vitamin C serums might cause eye irritation. Avoid putting the serum directly on your eyes. Choose a different
eye cream
or serum tailored for that area if you want to address issues around the eyes.
Wait for Absorption
:
Allow the Vitamin C serum to seep into your skin for a few minutes before applying any other products. This permits the active substances to permeate and function properly.
Layering with Other Products
:
Vitamin C can be used in conjunction with other skincare products. If you use additional serums or treatments, apply them after the Vitamin C serum. Follow up with your regular moisturiser to lock in hydration.
Morning Application (If Using Sunscreen)
:
Use a broad-spectrum sunscreen after applying Vitamin C serum in the morning.  Vitamin C boosts the effectiveness of sunscreen, providing additional UV protection.
Store Properly
:
Keep your Vitamin C serum in a cool, dark place away from direct sunlight. Light and air exposure can degrade Vitamin C, reducing its stability and efficacy.
Consistency is Key
:
Use Vitamin C serum on a regular basis as part of your daily skincare routine for the best results.  Visible improvements may take time, so be patient and persistent.
