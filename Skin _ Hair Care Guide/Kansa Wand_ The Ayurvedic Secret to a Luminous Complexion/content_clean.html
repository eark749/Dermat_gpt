Ayurvedic Philosophy: Balancing the Skin’s Doshas

In Ayurvedic philosophy, the concept of doshas is central to understanding and maintaining skin health. The doshas—Vata, Pitta, and Kapha—are three fundamental energies or principles that govern many physiological and psychological functions in the body, including those related to the skin. Each dosha has distinct qualities, and an imbalance between them can result in skin problems and conditions.

  • Vata is associated with the elements air and ether and governs movement, dryness, and lightness. Vata imbalance can cause dry, rough, and flaky skin, as well as conditions such as eczema and psoriasis. To balance the Vata dosha in the skin, nourishing and hydrating practices are recommended, such as the use of moisturising oils and gentle, grounding skincare routines.

  • Pitta dosha, associated with the elements fire and water, regulates metabolism, transformation, and heat in the body. Imbalanced Pitta can cause skin problems such as inflammation, sensitivity, acne, and rash. To balance Pitta dosha in the skin, use gentle, non-irritating skincare products, avoid too much sun exposure, and incorporate calming ingredients like aloe vera and cucumber.

  • Kapha is associated with the elements earth and water and governs stability, lubrication, and structure. When Kapha is imbalanced, it can cause oily, congested skin, cystic acne, and excess sebum production. To balance the Kapha dosha in the skin, cleansing and detoxifying practices such as exfoliation, clay masks to draw out impurities, and the use of astringent and stimulating ingredients such as lemon and ginger are recommended.
