: Vitamin F strengthens the lipid barrier, which improves overall skin barrier function. A strong skin barrier is necessary to protect against external aggressors and maintain healthy hydration levels. With a stronger barrier, the skin is better able to heal and recover from dryness and flakiness.
Promoting Skin Repair
: EFAs promote skin healing and renewal. They increase cell turnover, allowing damaged skin cells to be replaced by healthy ones. This speeds up the recovery of dry, inflamed skin and provides a smoother, more even complexion.
Anti-aging Benefits
: Vitamin F contains omega-3 fatty acids, which have
antioxidant

fine lines and wrinkles

Versatile Application

DIY Skincare: Crafting Your Own Vitamin F Infusions at Home
Making your DIY skincare products loaded with vitamin F can be a satisfying way to nurture and care for your skin using natural components. Here are some simple DIY recipes with vitamin F-rich oils:
Hydrating Vitamin F Face Serum
: Ingredients are 1 tablespoon hemp seed oil, 1 tablespoon rosehip seed oil, 1 tablespoon grapeseed oil, and 5-10 drops of lavender essential oil (optional, for fragrance). In a small glass dropper bottle, combine the hemp seed oil, rosehip seed oil, and grapeseed oil. Add 5-10 drops of lavender essential oil for a soothing aroma (if desired). Close the bottle and shake well to mix the oils. Use the serum in your morning and evening skincare routine. Apply a few drops to clean, damp skin and gently massage until absorbed. Follow up with your favorite moisturizer.
Soothing Vitamin F Body Butter
:  Ingredients are 1/2 cup shea butter, 2 tablespoons coconut oil, 1 tablespoon flaxseed oil, 1 tablespoon jojoba oil, and 10 drops of chamomile essential oil (optional, for calming effect). In a double boiler or microwave-safe bowl, melt the shea butter and coconut oil together until fully melted. Remove from heat and let it cool for a few minutes. Stir in the flaxseed oil and jojoba oil. Add 10 drops of chamomile essential oil for a soothing aroma (if using). Place the mixture in the refrigerator for about 20-30 minutes or until it starts to solidify. Wash the mixture with a hand or stand mixer until it becomes light and fluffy. Transfer the body butter to a clean, airtight container. Apply to dry skin as needed, focusing on areas prone to dryness like elbows, knees, and feet.
