Understanding Facial Hair Growth: The Basics

Understanding the foundations of facial hair growth will help you control and eliminate it more successfully. Here's a summary of hair growth stages, hormonal influences, genetics, age, and hair removal methods.

Why Does Facial Hair Grow?

Facial hair growth is primarily influenced by hormonal activity, particularly the presence of androgens such as testosterone. Here are the key factors influencing facial hair growth:

  • Hormones: The main hormones that stimulate facial hair growth are androgens, which include testosterone and dihydrotestosterone (DHT).  These hormones are more abundant in males, which is why men typically have more facial hair than women. But women also produce androgens, though not as much, and this can result in the development of vellus hair, which is a fine type of facial hair.

  • Genetics: Genetic factors influence the pattern, density, and thickness of facial hair.  Family history can influence the distribution and growth rate of facial hair, implying that individuals may inherit facial hair characteristics from their parents.

  • Puberty: Hormonal changes cause the development of secondary sexual characteristics, such as the growth of facial hair in males.  This is when androgen levels rise, resulting in thicker and darker facial hair. Hormonal changes associated with puberty in females can lead to the development of fine facial hair.

  • Age: Hormonal changes during adolescence and the early stages of adulthood tend to accelerate the growth of facial hair.  However, hormonal changes may cause a decrease in facial hair growth as people age, particularly after middle age. As they age, men may notice a thinning or patchy appearance of facial hair.

  • Medical Conditions: Several illnesses, including hormonal imbalances in both men and women and polycystic ovarian syndrome (PCOS) in women, can alter androgen levels, which in turn can affect the growth of facial hair.  Furthermore, the growth of facial hair may be influenced by specific medications, such as those used for hormone therapy or to treat particular medical conditions.

Types of Facial Hair: Vellus vs. Terminal Hair

There are two primary types of facial hair: vellus hair and terminal hair. The breakdown of each is as follows:

  • Vellus Hair: Vellus hair has a light colour, is fine, and is short.  The majority of the body is covered in it, with the face being the most affected, especially in women and children. Vellus hair, also known as "peach fuzz," is usually barely visible. This type of hair serves primarily to regulate body temperature and protect the skin.

  • Terminal Hair: Terminal hair is coarser, denser, and darker than vellus hair. It can be found on the scalp, brows, eyelashes, pubic region, and face in adult men. Terminal hair grows longer and is more noticeable. It typically replaces vellus hair in specific areas during puberty under the influence of androgens. This type of hair serves a variety of functions, including protection, sensation enhancement, and sexual dimorphism.

Men typically have more terminal hair on their faces, such as a beard, moustache, and sideburns, whereas women have finer vellus hair with fewer terminals. Individual differences do exist, however, due to genetic and hormonal influences, among other factors. Understanding the difference between vellus and terminal hair is critical when considering hair removal methods or addressing concerns about facial hair growth.

DIY Facial Hair Removal: Methods You Can Try

DIY facial hair removal methods are convenient and inexpensive. Here are a few techniques you can use at home:

  1. Shaving: Shaving is a quick and effective way to remove facial hair. Use a clean razor and shaving cream or gel to soften the hair and reduce irritation. Shave in the direction of the hair growth to avoid ingrown hairs and irritation.

  2. Waxing: Waxing removes hair from the root, providing longer-lasting smoothness. You can buy pre-made wax strips or make your own with ingredients like sugar, lemon juice, and water. Apply the wax in the direction of hair growth, then press down on a strip and quickly pull it off in the opposite direction.

  3. Threading: Threading is the process of twisting a cotton thread around each hair and pulling it out from the root. This method is precise and appropriate for small areas, such as the brows and upper lip. You can learn threading techniques online or through professional training.

  4. Sugaring: Sugaring is a natural hair removal technique similar to waxing that employs a sticky paste made of sugar, water, and lemon juice. Apply the sugaring paste to the skin, then press on a cloth strip and pull it off in the opposite direction of hair growth.

  5. Depilatory Creams: Depilatory creams contain chemicals that dissolve the protein structure of hair, allowing it to be wiped away. Apply the cream to the desired area, leave it on for the recommended time, and then remove it with the dissolved hair.

  6. Dermaplaning: Dermaplaning is a gentle exfoliation of the skin with a sterile scalpel that removes peach fuzz and dead skin cells. This method can improve skin texture and increase the efficacy of skin care products.

  7. Tweezing: Tweezing is a precise way to remove individual hairs. Using a clean pair of tweezers, grasp the hair near the root and pull it out in the direction of growth. This method is ideal for small areas and touch-up work.

Before attempting any DIY facial hair removal method, you should consider your skin type, sensitivity, and any existing skin conditions. First, test the method on a small area to ensure that there are no adverse reactions. If you have any concerns or are experiencing persistent irritation, see a dermatologist or professional esthetician for personalised advice and guidance.

Facial Hair Removal Creams: Pros and Cons

Facial hair removal creams, also known as depilatory creams, provide a simple and relatively painless way to remove unwanted facial hair. Here are the advantages and disadvantages of using these creams.

Pros:

  • Facial hair removal creams are simple to use and require little effort. Simply apply the cream to the desired area, wait the specified amount of time, and then remove it with the dissolved hair.

  • Unlike waxing or threading, which can be painful or uncomfortable, depilatory creams dissolve hair on the skin's surface without pulling or tugging, making them suitable for people with low pain tolerance.

  • Depilatory creams work quickly, usually within a few minutes. This makes them an appealing option for those seeking a quick hair removal solution, particularly when compared to methods such as shaving, which may require more time and precision.

  • Unlike shaving, which only removes hair on the surface, depilatory creams dissolve hair slightly beneath the skin's surface, resulting in longer-lasting smoothness.

  • Many facial hair removal creams are made with ingredients that are gentle on the skin, making them ideal for people who have sensitive skin and may experience irritation or razor burn when shaving.

Cons:

  • Depilatory creams contain chemicals like calcium thioglycolate or potassium thioglycolate, which degrade the protein structure of the hair. Some people may be sensitive or allergic to these ingredients, resulting in skin irritation or allergic reactions.

  • Facial hair removal creams frequently have a strong, chemical odour due to the ingredients used in their formulation. Depilatory creams are most effective on fine to medium hair types. However, some users may find the odour unpleasant. Coarse or thick hair may require a longer application time and may not be fully dissolved by the cream.

  • While depilatory creams are generally safe when used as directed, they can cause skin irritation, redness, or chemical burns if applied for an extended period or to sensitive areas.

  • The effects of depilatory creams are transient, just like those of most hair removal techniques; hair will eventually grow back. Each person experiences smoothness for a different amount of time, but it usually lasts a few days to a week or longer.

Before using a facial hair removal cream, make sure you take your skin sensitivity and hair type into consideration, carefully read and follow the instructions, and conduct a patch test to check for allergic reactions. Use caution and see a dermatologist if you have any worries or persistent irritation.

Facial Hair Waxing: What to Expect

Facial hair waxing is a popular method for removing unwanted hair from the upper lip, chin, and eyebrows. Here's what to expect when having your facial hair waxed:

  1. Preparation: Before your appointment, ensure that the hair in the treatment area is long enough for the wax to adhere to (usually about 1/4 inch). Cleanse your skin thoroughly to remove any makeup, oil, or debris that may interfere with the waxing procedure. If you are sensitive to pain, consider taking a mild pain reliever such as ibuprofen 30 minutes before your appointment to help reduce discomfort. 

  2. Consultation: Your esthetician will likely conduct a brief consultation to discuss your desired results, any concerns you may have, and any medications or skincare products you're using that could affect the waxing process.

  3. Application of Wax: The esthetician will apply warm wax to the treatment area with a disposable spatula or applicator, ensuring that the hair is covered in an even and thin layer of wax.

  4. Placement of Wax Strips: After the wax is applied, the esthetician will firmly press a cloth or paper strip onto the waxed area to ensure it adheres to the wax.

  5. Removal of Strips: After the wax has cooled and adhered to the hair, the esthetician will quickly remove the wax strips in the opposite direction of hair growth, removing the hair from the root.

  6. Post-Wax Care: Your esthetician may apply a soothing lotion or oil to the waxed area to relieve redness and inflammation. Avoid touching or scratching the waxed area, as this can cause irritation or ingrown hairs. To avoid irritation or sensitivity, keep the waxed area out of direct sunlight and heat for at least 24 hours.

  7. Results: Facial hair waxing usually results in smooth skin for several weeks because it removes hair from the root. Individual results may vary depending on hair growth rate and texture. Some redness or mild irritation is normal right after waxing, but it usually goes away within a few hours to a day.

  8. Maintenance: To keep the results smooth, schedule waxing appointments every 4-6 weeks or as needed, depending on your hair growth cycle.

  9. Potential Side Effects: While facial waxing is generally considered safe, it can cause temporary redness, irritation, or minor bruising. These typically resolve in a few hours to a day. In rare cases, facial waxing can result in more serious complications such as burns, allergic reactions, or infections. Be sure to follow all pre- and post-waxing care instructions provided by your esthetician to minimize the risk of complications.

Laser Facial Hair Removal: How Does It Work?

Laser facial hair removal is a popular method for removing unwanted hair from the face, particularly the upper lip, chin, cheeks, and jawline. This is how it works.

  1. Principle of Selective Photothermolysis: Laser hair removal works on the principle of selective photothermolysis, which involves using a specific wavelength of light to target the hair follicles and absorb the pigment (melanin) in the hair shaft and follicle.

  2. Melanin Absorption: The melanin in the hair follicle absorbs the laser light energy, which is then converted to heat. This heat harms the hair follicle, impairing its ability to produce new hair. 

  3. Cooling Mechanism: To protect the surrounding skin and reduce discomfort, modern laser hair removal devices frequently include a cooling mechanism, such as a built-in cooling tip or cryogen spray, to cool the skin before, during, and after each laser pulse.

  4. Phases of Hair Growth: Laser hair removal is most effective when the hair is in its active growth phase (anagen phase), which is when the hair follicle contains the most melanin and is most responsive to laser treatment. However, not all hairs are in the active growth phase at the same time, necessitating multiple treatment sessions to target hairs in various growth stages.

  5. Multiple Treatment Sessions: A series of laser hair removal sessions spaced several weeks apart is usually recommended to target hairs in various growth stages and achieve the best results. The number of sessions required varies according to hair colour, skin type, hair density, and personal response to treatment.

  6. Post-Treatment Care: Following each laser hair removal session, it is critical to avoid sun exposure and apply sunscreen to protect the treated area, as the skin may be more sensitive to sunlight. Some redness and swelling may occur immediately following treatment, but they usually resolve within a few hours to a day. Avoiding hot baths, saunas, and strenuous exercise can help to alleviate discomfort and the risk of complications.

  7. Long-Term Results: Laser hair removal can provide long-term reductions in hair growth, with many people experiencing significant hair loss after a series of treatments. While laser hair removal is not considered permanent, many people report that the regrowth is lighter, finer, and less noticeable than before the treatment.

Taking Care of Your Skin After Facial Hair Removal

Following facial hair removal, whether by waxing, threading, shaving, or laser treatment, it is critical to take care of your skin to avoid irritation, redness, and ingrown hairs. Here are some recommendations for post-facial hair removal skincare:

  • Gently cleanse the treated area with a fragrance-free cleanser to remove any remaining wax, residue, or sweat. Avoid using harsh or abrasive products that may irritate the skin further.

  • After cleansing, use a soothing product like aloe vera gel, witch hazel, or a fragrance-free moisturiser to calm the skin and reduce redness and inflammation. Look for products containing anti-inflammatory ingredients such as chamomile or green tea extract.

  • Protect the treated area from direct sunlight for at least 24 hours after facial hair removal, as the skin may be more sensitive to UV light. If you need to go outside, use a broad-spectrum sunscreen with SPF 30 or higher, and reapply every two hours.

  • Exfoliating the treated area should be avoided for at least 48 hours after facial hair removal because it can irritate the skin and increase the risk of ingrown hair. Instead, wait until the skin has completely healed before continuing exfoliation.

  • Avoid touching or rubbing the treated area unnecessarily, as this can introduce bacteria and cause skin irritation. If you need to touch the area, ensure that your hands are clean and dry.

  • Avoid using products with alcohol, fragrances, or other harsh ingredients that may irritate the skin further. Choose gentle, hypoallergenic products formulated for sensitive skin.

  • Drink plenty of water to keep your skin hydrated on the inside out. Hydrated skin is less susceptible to irritation and inflammation, so drink at least eight glasses of water per day.

  • Makeup, perfumes, and other cosmetic products should be avoided for at least 24 hours after facial hair removal because they can irritate the skin even more.

  • If you received a professional treatment, such as laser hair removal or electrolysis, follow any aftercare instructions given by your esthetician or dermatologist. This could include refraining from using certain skincare products or engaging in specific activities for a set period. 

By following these post-facial hair removal skincare tips, you can help soothe and protect your skin, reduce irritation, and speed up healing. If you have persistent redness, swelling, or other signs of infection, see a dermatologist for a thorough evaluation and treatment.

Permanent Facial Hair Removal: Is It Possible?

Yes, permanent facial hair removal is possible using techniques such as electrolysis and laser hair removal. Here's how each method operates:

  • Electrolysis: The FDA has approved only electrolysis as a permanent hair removal method. It works by inserting a tiny probe into each hair follicle and applying an electrical current to disable the follicle's ability to produce new hair. Electrolysis is effective for all hair colours and types because a licenced electrologist targets each hair follicle individually. To achieve optimal results, electrolysis must be repeated over several months to target hairs at various growth stages.

  • Laser Hair Removal: Laser hair removal uses intense beams of light that are absorbed by the pigment (melanin) in the hair shaft and follicle. The laser's heat causes damage to the hair follicle, preventing it from producing new hair. While laser hair removal is not considered permanent, many people notice significant hair reduction after a series of treatments. Laser hair removal works best on people with fair skin and dark hair because the contrast between the two allows the laser to target the hair follicles more precisely. Laser hair removal, like electrolysis, requires multiple sessions spaced several weeks apart for optimal results.

While both electrolysis and laser hair removal provide long-term reductions in hair growth, individual results may vary, and regrowth may occur over time. Hormone levels, genetics, and skin type can all have an impact on treatment efficacy. Consult with a licenced electrologist or dermatologist to determine the best method for your needs and receive personalised advice and guidance throughout the treatment process.

Natural Facial Hair Removal: Home Remedies and Tips

Natural facial hair removal methods can be gentle on the skin while providing temporary results. Here are a few home remedies and tips to try:

  1. Turmeric and Milk Paste: Make a thick paste by mixing turmeric powder and milk. Apply the paste to the areas of unwanted facial hair. Allow it to dry for 15-20 minutes, then rinse with warm water. Turmeric contains anti-inflammatory properties, and milk acts as a gentle exfoliant.

  2. Papaya and Turmeric Mask: Mash ripe papaya and combine it with a teaspoon of turmeric powder to make a paste. Apply the paste to your facial hair and leave on for 15-20 minutes. Rinse with lukewarm water. Papaya contains papain, an enzyme that can weaken hair follicles and prevent hair growth over time.

  3. Sugar and Lemon Juice Wax: In a saucepan, combine equal parts sugar, lemon juice, and water; heat until the sugar dissolves and turns golden brown. Allow the mixture to cool slightly before applying it to the areas of unwanted facial hair with a spatula or popsicle stick. Place a cloth strip over the wax, firmly press it down, and quickly remove it in the opposite direction of hair growth. This natural waxing method is effective at removing hair from the root, but it may cause discomfort.

  4. Oatmeal and Banana Scrub: Combine oats and ripe bananas to make a thick paste. Apply the paste to your facial hair and gently massage it in circular motions for a few minutes. Rinse with warm water. Muesli acts as a natural exfoliant, and banana moisturises the skin.

  5. Egg White Mask: Separate an egg white from its yolk and whisk until frothy. Apply egg white to the areas with unwanted facial hair and allow to dry for 15-20 minutes. Remove the mask gently, and rinse your face with water. Egg white contains proteins that help tighten the skin and adhere to fine facial hair, making it easier to remove.

  6. Gram Flour and Yogurt Mask: To make a thick paste, combine gramme flour (besan) and plain yoghurt. Apply the paste to your facial hair and leave it on until it dries.  To remove the hair, gently rub the dried paste in the opposite direction of its growth. Rinse off any remaining residue with water. Gram flour acts as a natural exfoliant, while yogurt moisturizes the skin.

While natural remedies can temporarily reduce facial hair, they may not be as effective as professional methods like waxing, threading, or laser hair removal.
