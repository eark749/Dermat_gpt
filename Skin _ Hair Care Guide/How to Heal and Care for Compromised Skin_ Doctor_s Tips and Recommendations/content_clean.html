 What is compromised skin and what causes it?

Skin that is weakened, injured, or dealing with numerous problems that interfere with its normal health and function is said to have compromised skin. Different symptoms, including dryness, redness, sensitivity, inflammation, and even open wounds, can be present. A variety of factors can contribute to compromised skin, including:

  1. Environmental Factors: Harsh weather conditions, such as extreme temperatures, low humidity, wind, or excessive sun exposure, can damage the skin's barrier function, resulting in dryness, redness, and sensitivity.

  2. Irritants and Allergens: Skin irritation, inflammation, and allergic reactions can occur when irritants and allergens, such as certain skincare products, cosmetics, fragrances, detergents, or fabrics, come into contact with the skin.

  3. Excessive Use of Harsh Products: The balance of the skin's protective barrier can be upset by using excessive amounts of harsh cleansers, exfoliants, or treatments that remove the skin's natural oils.

  4. Skin conditions: Skin problems like rosacea, eczema, dermatitis, and psoriasis can compromise the skin. These conditions frequently involve a damaged skin barrier, itchiness, and inflammation.

  5. Allergic Reactions: Allergic reactions to specific substances, such as foods, medications, and topical products, can compromise skin irritation and integrity.

  6. Internal Factors: Medical conditions, hormonal imbalances, or medications may also contribute to skin deterioration. For example, hormonal changes that occur during pregnancy or menopause can have an effect on the skin's condition.

  7. Improper Skincare Techniques: Using ineffective skin care methods, such as harsh exfoliation or scrubbing, can harm the skin and jeopardise its health.

To choose the best course of treatment and care for compromised skin, it's critical to determine the underlying cause. A dermatologist or other medical expert can help diagnose the particular condition and suggest a customised strategy for healing and regaining the health of the skin. 

Understanding the skin barrier

The outermost layer of skin, commonly referred to as the stratum corneum, serves as a barrier between the body and the outside world. It is known as the skin barrier. It is essential for preserving the integrity and health of the skin. Here are some essential ideas to comprehend regarding the skin barrier:

  1. Structure: Layers of dead skin cells called corneocytes embedded in a lipid matrix make up the skin barrier. The flat, compacted corneocytes on the surface of the skin create a barrier of protection.

  2. Function: The main job of the skin barrier is to shield the underlying tissues from outside influences like allergens, irritants, microbes, and moisture loss. It keeps the skin hydrated and stops dangerous substances from getting in.

  3. Lipid Barrier: Ceramides, cholesterol, and fatty acids make up the lipid matrix that makes up the skin barrier. These lipids support the integrity of the barrier and control the flow of water into and out of the skin. They are essential for halting moisture loss and preserving skin hydration.

  4. pH Balance: The skin's natural pH balance has an impact on how well it serves as a barrier. The pH of the skin's surface is slightly acidic (between 4.5 and 5.5), which aids in preventing the development of dangerous bacteria and fungi.

  5. Compromised Barrier: When the skin barrier is compromised or damaged, it can lead to various skin issues. A compromised barrier may result in dryness, redness, sensitivity, increased water loss, increased susceptibility to irritants and allergens, and a higher risk of infections.

  6. Factors Affecting the Barrier: Harsh cleansers, over-exfoliation, excessive sun exposure, harsh weather conditions, specific skin conditions, genetics, ageing, and specific skincare products with an irritant or stripping ingredients can all negatively impact the integrity of the skin barrier.

  7. Maintaining a Healthy Barrier: It's critical to use proper skin care techniques in order to maintain a healthy skin barrier. This includes maintaining a balanced diet, staying hydrated, and adopting healthy lifestyle practices. It also includes gentle cleansing, moisturising, avoiding harsh products, shielding the skin from environmental stressors, and promoting overall skin health.

Maintaining healthy skin and resolving various skin issues requires an understanding of the skin barrier. You can encourage optimum skin health and function by supporting and safeguarding the skin's natural defences.

Common triggers of skin sensitivity

Skin sensitivity can result from a multitude of causes, and every individual has a unique response to it. The following are some typical causes of sensitive skin:

  1. Environmental Factors: Exposure to unfavourable climatic conditions, such as hot or low temperatures, low humidity, wind, or extensive sun exposure, can cause skin sensitivity and irritation.

  2. Allergens: Some allergens, such as pollen, pet dander, mould, or particular foods, can make someone more sensitive to the sun and cause allergic reactions.

  3. Irritants: Skin sensitivity can develop as a result of exposure to irritating substances. These may include corrosive substances, strong scents, dyes, specific skincare products, detergents, or particular fabrics.

  4. Skincare Items: Some skincare items, especially those that contain fragrances, preservatives, or specific active ingredients, can irritate sensitive skin and cause sensitivity reactions.

  5. Over-exfoliation: Exfoliation that is excessive or aggressive can damage the skin barrier and make the skin more sensitive.

  6. Stress: Emotional stress and anxiety can aggravate pre-existing skin conditions and increase skin sensitivity.

  7. Hormonal Changes: The skin can become more sensitive and reactive when hormone levels fluctuate, such as during puberty, menstruation, pregnancy, or menopause.

  8. Existing Skin Conditions: People with eczema, dermatitis, rosacea, or psoriasis frequently have more sensitive skin and are more susceptible to sensitivity triggers.

  9. Several medications: Some medicines, including some retinoids, chemotherapeutic agents, and antibiotics, can increase the skin's sensitivity to outside influences.

  10. Mechanical Factors: Activities like vigorous towel drying, wearing tight clothing, or rough handling can cause friction, pressure, or rubbing on the skin, which can make it more sensitive.

Finding your specific skin sensitivity triggers and taking preventative measures to limit exposure to them is essential. Skin sensitivity can be controlled and lessened by avoiding known irritants, using gentle skincare products designed for sensitive skin, following a regular skincare routine, and shielding the skin from external stressors. A dermatologist should be seen if skin sensitivity persists or gets worse in order to receive an accurate diagnosis and recommendations.

How to Care for Compromised Skin: Doctor’s Tips

To help promote healing and restore the health of compromised skin, special attention and gentle care are needed. The following are some medical advice on how to treat compromised skin:

  1. Cleanse Gently: To gently cleanse compromised skin, use a mild, fragrance-free cleanser. To avoid further drying or irritation, choose lukewarm water instead of hot water. With a soft towel, pat the skin dry rather than rubbing it.

  2. Regularly moisturise: After cleansing skin that has been damaged, use a mild, hypoallergenic moisturiser. Look for moisturisers that contain soothing ingredients like ceramides, hyaluronic acid, or aloe vera. Moisturising helps replenish the skin's moisture barrier and soothes dryness or irritation.

  3. Avoid Harsh Ingredients: Avoid skincare products that may cause irritation because they contain scents, alcohol, dyes, or harsh chemicals. These may make already damaged skin worse. Choose items that are hypoallergenic or designed for sensitive skin instead.

  4. Sun Protection: Cover weakened skin with a broad-spectrum sunscreen with at least SPF 30 to protect it from the sun's damaging rays. Choose zinc oxide or titanium dioxide-based physical sunscreens instead, as these ingredients are typically well-tolerated by sensitive skin. Sunscreen should be reapplied every two hours or as directed.

  5. Reduce Irritation: Steer clear of activities or products that might irritate or cause friction to vulnerable skin. The use of rough towels, harsh exfoliation, and excessive scrubbing are all prohibited. To reduce friction, choose soft fabrics for your clothing and bedding.

  6. Hydrate from Within: Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water throughout the day. Proper hydration promotes overall skin health and can aid in wound healing.

  7. Avoid Potential Triggers: Identify and avoid potential triggers that can aggravate the condition of compromised skin.  This might involve particular foods, allergens, cosmetics, or environmental elements.

  8. Consult a Doctor: If compromised skin persists, worsens, or is accompanied by severe symptoms, it is imperative to consult a dermatologist or other healthcare provider. They can offer an accurate diagnosis, suggest suitable treatments, or, if required, prescribe medications.

Keep in mind that these pieces of advice are only general suggestions and that specific situations may differ. For individualised guidance and treatment choices depending on your unique requirements and the severity of your impaired skin, speak with a healthcare professional.

Gentle cleansing and moisturising tips

Skincare must include both gentle washing and moisturising, especially for those with sensitive or impaired skin. Here are some pointers to remember:

Gentle Cleansing:

  1. Use a Gentle Cleanser: Select a gentle, fragrance-free cleanser designed specifically for sensitive skin. Look for cleansers that are non-comedogenic and hypoallergenic.

  2. Avoid Scrubbing: When cleansing your face, be gentle. Avoid scrubbing with harsh brushes or sponges that can irritate the skin.

  3. Lukewarm Water: To cleanse your face, use lukewarm water. Hot water can remove natural oils, causing dryness or irritation.

  4. Pat Dry: After rinsing, use a soft towel to gently pat your skin dry. A rough drying technique should be avoided because it can irritate the skin even more. 

Moisturising:

  1. Choose an Appropriate Moisturiser: Look for moisturisers made especially for skin that is sensitive or compromised. Choose items devoid of fragrance, hypoallergenic, and harsh ingredients.

  2. Apply Moisturizer to Damp Skin: After cleansing, moisturise skin that is just a little bit damp. This promotes hydration and aids in retaining moisture.

  3. Gentle Application: When applying the moisturiser, make sure not to pull or rub the skin too hard. Instead, use small, upward strokes.

  4. Consider Emollients or Occlusives: If your skin is extremely dry or damaged, use emollients or occlusive products. Emollients soften and smooth the skin, while occlusives form a protective barrier to prevent moisture loss.

  5. Moisturise Twice Daily: To keep your skin hydrated, apply moisturiser in the morning and evening, as well as as needed throughout the day.

Additional Suggestions:

  1. Patch Test: Before attempting a new cleanser or moisturiser, test a small area of your skin for any adverse reactions.

  2. Be Consistent: Maintain skin health by sticking to a regular skincare routine and being consistent with cleansing and moisturising.

  3. Sun protection: As part of your daytime skincare routine, always apply sunscreen with at least SPF 30 to protect your skin from harmful UV rays.

Remember that everyone's skin is unique, so pay attention to your skin's needs and adjust your skincare routine as needed. If you have specific questions or medical conditions, you should consult with a dermatologist or skincare expert to receive personalised advice.

Recommended skincare ingredients

There are numerous beneficial ingredients commonly found in skincare products that can address a variety of skin concerns. Here are some skincare ingredients to consider:

  1. Hyaluronic Acid: Hyaluronic acid, known for its excellent hydrating properties, attracts and retains moisture in the skin, promoting hydration and plumpness.

  2. Retinol: A vitamin A derivative, retinol is a potent ingredient that can reduce the appearance of fine lines, wrinkles, and uneven skin tone. It aids in the turnover of skin cells and the production of collagen.

  3. Vitamin C: As an antioxidant, vitamin C protects the skin from free radicals and promotes collagen synthesis. It can brighten the skin, fade hyperpigmentation, and even out the skin tone.

  4. Niacinamide: Also known as vitamin B3, niacinamide is a multitasking ingredient. It can help to strengthen the skin barrier, reduce inflammation, regulate oil production, and reduce the visibility of pores.

  5. Peptides: Peptides are short chains of amino acids that have the ability to increase collagen production, and enhance skin elasticity, and firmness. They can support a smoother complexion and help to lessen the appearance of wrinkles.

  6. Ceramides: The skin's barrier is made up of ceramides, which are lipid molecules. They reinforce the skin's barrier of defence, aid in moisture retention, and stop moisture loss.

  7. AHA/BHA: Chemical exfoliants known as alpha hydroxy acids (AHAs) and beta hydroxy acids (BHAs) can help remove dead skin cells gently, clear clogged pores, and improve the texture of the skin. While BHAs are oil-soluble and can enter pores, AHAs are water-soluble and work on the skin's surface.

  8. Antioxidants: Antioxidants, such as coenzyme Q10, vitamin E, resveratrol, or green tea extract, assist in protecting the skin from environmental harm caused by free radicals, which can speed up the ageing process.

  9. Peppermint Oil: Peppermint oil has calming effects and can make the skin feel cool. It might assist in reducing inflammation and redness.

  10. Centella Asiatica (Cica): Centella Asiatica is a plant extract that has been shown to have calming and restorative effects. It can support the skin's natural barrier function, encourage wound healing, and soothe irritated skin.

Always remember to patch test and introduce new products gradually to your skincare regimen so you can watch how your skin reacts. To find the finest components and products for your particular skin conditions, it's also wise to speak with a dermatologist or skincare expert.
