The Safety Profile of Propanediol: Evaluating Its Use in Cosmetics
Propanediol (PDO) is typically considered harmless when absorbed via the skin in tiny doses via topical cosmetics. Although PDO is classified as a skin irritant, the Environmental Working Group (EWG) reports that the health concerns in cosmetics are minimal. After analyzing current evidence on propanediol, a panel of experts from the Cosmetic Ingredient Review determined that it is safe for use in cosmetics.
In a trial using topical propanediol on human skin, researchers discovered irritation in a very small percentage of participants. Another study found that administering high doses of propanediol orally can be lethal to lab rats. However, when rats inhaled propanediol vapor, they did not die or experience significant discomfort.
