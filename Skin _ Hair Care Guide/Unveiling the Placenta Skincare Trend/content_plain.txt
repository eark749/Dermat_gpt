What is Placenta Extract?
Placenta extract is a material obtained from the placenta, an organ that grows in the uterus during pregnancy to nourish and nurture the developing foetus. Placenta extract can come from humans, animals, or plants, and it is utilised in a variety of applications such as skincare, vitamins, and traditional medicine. In cosmetics, placenta extract is frequently advertised as an ingredient that can promote collagen formation, reduce the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles, improve skin moisture, and brighten the complexion. It is crucial to emphasise, however, that the scientific data supporting these statements is sometimes inadequate or inconclusive.
The use of human placenta extract raises ethical questions about placenta source and informed consent. Furthermore, the safety and performance of placenta extract products can vary greatly, and legislation controlling these products differs from nation to nation. Before utilising placenta extract-containing cosmetics or supplement items, contact a dermatologist or healthcare expert. They can advise you on product safety and suitability for your skin type, as well as address any concerns you may have about the origins and ethical implications of placental extracts.

Boosting Collagen for Youthful Skin
Collagen is a vital protein in the skin that provides structure, elasticity, and firmness. As we age, collagen production naturally decreases, leading to sagging skin, wrinkles, and fine lines. There are several ways to boost collagen production and promote youthful skin:
Topical Collagen Products
: In the skincare sector, collagen-infused creams, serums, and masks are available. Although these products may improve skin hydration and texture briefly, collagen molecules are too big to enter deeply into the skin. As a result, their ability to stimulate collagen formation is limited.
Retinoids (Vitamin A Derivatives
): Retinoids, including retinol and tretinoin, are well known for stimulating collagen formation. They act by increasing skin cell turnover and encouraging the creation of new collagen fibres.
Vitamin C (Ascorbic Acid)
: Vitamin C is an antioxidant that is essential for collagen formation. It can assist in protecting existing collagen from free radical damage and accelerate the synthesis of new collagen fibres.
Peptides
: Peptides are amino acid chains that can stimulate the skin's collagen production. Peptides are found in several skincare products and may help improve skin firmness and suppleness.
Sunscreen
: UV rays from the sun can cause collagen breakdown and hasten the aging process. Regular use of broad-spectrum sunscreen helps to retain current collagen and prevent future damage.
Microneedling
: Microneedling is a cosmetic technique in which small needles cause controlled micro-injuries in the skin. As the skin repairs itself, this process encourages collagen formation, resulting in better skin texture and firmness.
Dermal Fillers
: Injectable dermal fillers, such as hyaluronic acid or poly-L-lactic acid, can replace lost volume while also stimulating collagen formation in the treated areas. They are frequently used to firm and lift drooping skin.
Collagen Supplements
: Collagen supplements, which come in powders, capsules, and beverages, claim to boost collagen production when taken orally. While the efficacy of these supplements is debatable, some people claim that they improve skin moisture and texture.
Diet and Nutrition
: A healthy diet rich in vitamins, minerals, and amino acids is necessary for collagen formation. Skin health can be improved by eating foods high in vitamin C, zinc, and antioxidants. Collagen-rich meals, such as bone broth, may also be beneficial.
Hydration
: Proper hydration is essential for skin elasticity and plumpness. Drinking plenty of water keeps the skin moisturised and promotes overall skin health.
Lifestyle Factors
: Avoiding smoking and excessive alcohol consumption, controlling stress, and getting enough sleep are all lifestyle choices that can help you have healthier, younger-looking skin.
