What is Hormonal Acne and What Causes It?

Hormonal acne is commonly associated with puberty, but it can affect adults of any age and is more prevalent in women. Menstruation and menopause are two factors that may contribute to this. Acne is estimated to affect 50% of women aged 20 to 29. It affects approximately 25% of women aged 40 to 49. Hormonal acne can appear as pimples, blackheads, whiteheads, cysts, or nodules on the face, neck, chest, back, and shoulders.

The main cause of hormonal acne is an increase in androgens, which are male hormones found in both men and women. Androgens stimulate the skin's sebaceous glands to produce more oil, which can clog pores and cause acne. Androgens also cause skin thickening, which can contribute to clogged pores. Hormonal acne in women can be caused by changes in oestrogen and progesterone levels during the menstrual cycle. These hormone fluctuations can cause the sebaceous glands to produce more oil, resulting in acne.

Stress, certain medications (such as corticosteroids and hormonal birth control), and medical conditions such as polycystic ovary syndrome can all contribute to hormonal acne (PCOS). Topical retinoids, oral contraceptives, spironolactone, and antibiotics are all effective treatments for hormonal acne. Additionally, avoiding dairy products, high-glycaemic foods, processed foods, and fried foods can help improve acne-prone skin's appearance. A dermatologist can provide personalized recommendations for dealing with hormonal acne.

The Role of Androgens and Sebaceous Glands

Androgens, which are male hormones found in both males and females, are important in the development of acne, particularly hormonal acne. Androgens stimulate the skin's sebaceous glands to produce more oil, or sebum. Sebaceous glands are small glands located in the skin that produce oil, which aids in the lubrication and hydration of the skin. However, excessive oil production can result in clogged pores, which can lead to acne. When androgen levels rise, whether due to puberty or hormonal imbalances, the sebaceous glands are stimulated to produce more sebum. Excess sebum can then combine with dead skin cells and bacteria, causing pimples, blackheads, and whiteheads to form. Androgens also cause skin thickening, which can contribute to clogged pores. Hormonal acne is frequently linked to the menstrual cycle in women because of changes in estrogen and progesterone levels, which can result in an increase in androgens. Treatments for hormonal acne include topical retinoids, oral contraceptives, spironolactone, and antibiotics. These remedies function by either reducing sebum production, eliminating acne-causing bacteria, or controlling hormone levels. A dermatologist should be consulted for individualized recommendations on how to manage hormonal acne.

Acne Triggers and Hormonal Imbalances

Acne triggers differ from person to person, but hormonal imbalances, particularly hormonal acne, are a common cause of acne. Numerous life events, including puberty, menstruation, pregnancy, and menopause, as well as illnesses like polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) and disorders of the adrenal glands, can lead to hormonal imbalances.  When there is a hormonal imbalance, the body may produce an excess of androgens, which are male hormones found in both men and women. Androgens stimulate the skin's sebaceous glands to produce more oil or sebum. When bacteria, dead skin cells, and excessive oil production come together, pimples, blackheads, and whiteheads can develop.

Other causes of acne include:

  1. Genetics: There may be a genetic predisposition to acne in some people, and it can run in families.

  2. Stress: Stress can cause hormonal imbalances, and these imbalances can exacerbate acne.

  3. Diet: Some foods, including fried foods, dairy products, high-glycemic foods, processed foods, and foods, can cause acne.

  4. Skincare items: Some skincare items, especially those with comedogenic ingredients, can clog pores and exacerbate acne.

Effective Treatments for Hormonal Acne

Hormone fluctuations, particularly those caused by androgens like testosterone, which can increase sebum production and cause inflammation in the skin, are the main cause of hormonal acne. The following are some potent remedies for hormonal acne:

  1. Pills for birth control: Oral contraceptives can help regulate hormones and lessen androgen production. This can help prevent hormonal acne from occurring. There are some birth control pills that are more effective than others at treating acne, though, so it's important to keep that in mind.

  2. Spironolactone: This medication can help with hormonal acne because it blocks the effects of androgens on the skin. It usually only works for women, and it can take a few months to notice any real changes.

  3. Topical retinoids: These medications, which are derived from vitamin A, are used to treat various skin conditions, such as acne.   They function by opening up pores, calming down inflammation, and stopping the development of fresh acne lesions.

  4. Antibiotics: Antibiotics can treat acne by lowering the number of bacteria that cause it on the skin. They are typically prescribed in conjunction with other treatments for moderate to severe cases of acne.

  5. Isotretinoin: This medication, a potent oral retinoid, is used to treat severe acne that does not respond to other therapies. It is highly effective but can have significant side effects and should only be used under the supervision of a dermatologist.

It's significant to remember that the ideal course of action for hormonal acne depends on the patient and their unique situation.  It is always advised to speak with a dermatologist to figure out the best course of action for your skin.

Hormone Therapy: Birth Control Pills and Anti-Androgen Medications

Hormone therapy can be used to treat a variety of conditions, including polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS), hirsutism (excessive hair growth), and hormonal acne. These conditions frequently respond to two common types of hormone therapy: birth control pills and anti-androgen drugs.

Oral contraceptives, also known as birth control pills, contain synthetic hormones that control the menstrual cycle and prevent pregnancy. They can also aid in the treatment of hormonal acne by controlling the production of androgens like testosterone, which can result in excessive sebum production and skin inflammation. In addition to treating acne, birth control pills can help control menstrual cycles, lessen cramping and pain during periods, and lower the risk of ovarian and endometrial cancer.

Anti-androgen drugs function by limiting androgens' negative effects on the body.  They can effectively treat hirsutism and PCOS by reducing excessive hair growth and regulating menstrual cycles. Spironolactone is a common anti-androgen medication used to treat these conditions. It may take several months to see noticeable results, and it should only be used under the supervision of a healthcare provider. It should be noted that hormone therapy does carry some risks even though it can be helpful in treating some conditions. Birth control pills and anti-androgen drugs may have adverse effects such as blood clots, high blood pressure, and liver damage. Consult your doctor about the advantages and disadvantages of hormone therapy to decide if it is right for you.

Topical Treatments for Hormonal Acne

Topical treatments can be an effective option for treating hormonal acne. The following list of topical medications for hormonal acne is typical:

  1. Benzoyl peroxide: This topical treatment reduces inflammation and eliminates acne-causing bacteria.  It is available over-the-counter and in prescription strengths.

  2. Topical retinoids: These treatments, which are made from vitamin A, work by clearing out pores, calming down inflammation, and halting the growth of new acne lesions. Some examples include adapalene, tretinoin, and tazarotene.

  3. Topical antibiotics: Antibiotics can be applied topically to the skin to lessen the number of bacteria that cause acne.  Common examples include clindamycin and erythromycin.

  4. Azelaic acid: This topical medication works by reducing inflammation and killing acne-causing bacteria. It can also help to reduce hyperpigmentation caused by acne.

  5. Salicylic acid: This beta-hydroxy acid works by exfoliating the skin and unclogging pores. It can also help to reduce inflammation.

  6. Tea tree oil: This natural oil has antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular choice for treating acne. However, it should be used with caution as it can cause skin irritation in some people.

Natural Remedies and Lifestyle Changes to Improve Skin Health

There are a number of natural remedies and way-of-life adjustments that can help skin health in addition to medical treatments, such as:

  1. Eating a healthy diet: A diet full of fruits, vegetables, lean protein, and healthy fats can give you the nutrients your skin needs to be healthy.

  2. Keeping hydrated: Water is essential for maintaining healthy, hydrated skin.

  3. Sleeping enough: The skin can heal and regenerate when you get enough restful sleep.

  4. Managing stress: Stress can contribute to a variety of skin conditions, so it is important to find ways to manage stress, such as meditation, yoga, or exercise.

  5. Sun protection: Preventing skin damage and early aging can be accomplished by shielding the skin from the sun's damaging UV rays. This entails using sunscreen, donning protective clothing, and avoiding exposure to the sun directly during the hottest parts of the day. 

  6. Natural treatments: Aloe vera, tea tree oil, honey, and turmeric are a few natural treatments that can be used to improve the health of your skin.  These remedies have anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties that can help reduce inflammation and prevent acne.

  7. Avoiding harsh chemicals: Skin irritation from harsh chemicals in skincare products can lead to acne and other skin conditions. Choosing natural and gentle products can help improve skin health.

It is important to remember that these natural treatments and lifestyle modifications might not be sufficient to completely treat severe skin conditions, even though they can be helpful. It is always advised to speak with a dermatologist or other healthcare professional to figure out the best course of action for your skin.

Foods to Avoid for Clearer Skin

There is no one diet that will completely cure acne or other skin conditions, but there are some foods that are known to cause inflammation and lead to acne breakouts. The following foods are some to limit or avoid for clearer skin:

  1. High glycaemic index foods: Foods that are high in sugar and refined carbohydrates, such as white bread, pasta, and pastries, can cause blood sugar spikes and lead to inflammation in the body.

  2. Dairy products: Dairy products have hormones in them that can cause acne to flare up. This includes milk, cheese, and yogurt.

  3. Processed foods: Foods that have been through a processing process often contain additives and preservatives that can aggravate skin conditions and cause inflammation.

  4. Fried and greasy foods: Eating foods that are fried or high in unhealthy fats can cause acne and clogged pores.

  5. Alcohol and caffeine: These substances can dry out the skin and aggravate inflammation.

  6. Spicy foods: Some people experience acne breakouts as a result of spicy foods' ability to increase inflammation in the body.

  7. Gluten: Consuming gluten can cause skin rashes and breakouts in some people with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity.

The Link Between Diet and Hormonal Acne

Hormonal imbalances in the body, specifically an excess of androgens, which are male hormones produced by both men and women, are the root cause of hormonal acne. Androgens cause the sebaceous glands on the skin to produce more oil, which may clog pores and cause breakouts. 

Although diet does not directly cause hormonal acne, some foods can have an impact on hormone levels and cause acne flare-ups. Here are a few instances:

  1. White bread, pasta, and sweets are examples of foods high in sugar or refined carbohydrates that can spike blood sugar levels and increase insulin production. Acne may result from insulin's ability to stimulate the production of androgen.

  2. Hormones found in milk and other dairy products can alter the body's hormone levels. Milk in particular has a hormone called IGF-1 that can boost androgen production.

  3. Foods high in omega-6 fatty acids, such as vegetable oils, fried foods, and processed snacks, can lead to inflammation in the body, which can exacerbate acne.

  4. Both caffeine and alcohol can alter hormone levels in the body, which can cause flare-ups of acne.

However, some foods can also help to lessen inflammation and support healthy skin. These include:

  1. Antioxidants can help to lower body inflammation and shield the skin from harm. Fruits and vegetables, nuts and seeds, and green tea are a few examples.

  2. fatty fish, chia seeds, and walnuts are a few examples of foods high in omega-3 fatty acids that can help to reduce inflammation and support healthy skin.

  3. Probiotics are good bacteria that can balance the microbiome in the gut and lower inflammation in the body. Yogurt, kefir, and fermented vegetables are a few examples.

It's crucial to remember that diet is not the only factor in hormonal acne, even though it can play a part. Genetics, stress, and other environmental factors can also affect hormonal acne. It's a good idea to consult a dermatologist if you have persistent acne in order to identify the underlying cause and create an effective treatment regimen.

Why Dairy Products, Sugar, and Processed Foods Can Worsen Acne

Several factors, including dairy products, sugar, and processed foods, can make acne worse:

  1. Dairy products: Milk and other dairy products contain hormones that may raise androgen levels in the body and cause excess production of sebum, or skin oil. This may cause acne by clogging the pores. Additionally, some people's inflammatory reactions to dairy products can make acne worse.

  2. Sugar: Consuming foods high in sugar or refined carbohydrates can quickly raise blood sugar levels, which triggers an increase in the production of the hormone insulin. Acne may result from insulin's ability to stimulate the production of androgens. Furthermore, inflammation in the body brought on by high blood sugar levels can exacerbate acne by causing it. 

  3. Processed foods: Processed foods are often high in refined carbohydrates, sugar, and unhealthy fats, all of which can contribute to inflammation in the body. Inflammation both causes acne and exacerbates pre-existing acne.  Additionally, crucial nutrients like zinc and vitamins A, C, and E which are essential for healthy skin are frequently missing from processed foods.

Managing Inflammation and Stress for Clearer Skin

Stress and inflammation both have a role to play in skin conditions like acne. The following advice will help you control stress and inflammation to help your skin look clearer:

  1. Eat a diet low in inflammatory foods: Eating a diet low in inflammatory foods, such as trans fats, can help to reduce inflammation in the body. Inflammation can be decreased by avoiding foods high in sugar, refined carbohydrates, and unhealthy fats.

  2. Exercise frequently: Reducing stress and inflammation in the body is easy to do with exercise. Try to exercise for at least 30 minutes, most days of the week, at a moderate intensity.

  3. Use techniques to reduce stress: Stress can cause inflammation and acne, so finding ways to manage stress is important.  Deep breathing, yoga, meditation, and time spent in nature are a few effective stress-reduction methods.

  4. Get enough sleep: Lack of sleep can increase inflammation in the body and lead to acne breakouts. To keep your skin clear, aim for 7-9 hours of sleep every night.

  5. Use topical anti-inflammatory products: Topical anti-inflammatory products can help to reduce inflammation and encourage clearer skin by containing anti-inflammatory ingredients like niacinamide, green tea extract, and aloe vera.

  6. Consider supplements: Probiotics and omega-3 fatty acids are two supplements that can help to lower body inflammation and support healthy skin.  Before beginning any new supplements, consult your healthcare provider.

In order to encourage clearer, healthier skin, it's crucial to control inflammation and stress. You can reduce stress and inflammation in your body and get a more radiant complexion by implementing these suggestions.
