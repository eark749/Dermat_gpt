Exploring the World of Neuromodulators: Daxxify and Botox

Botox is the brand name for a toxin produced by the bacterium Clostridium botulinum. It is used for a variety of medical and cosmetic applications, most notably to temporarily paralyse muscle activity. Here's a quick overview of Botox.

  • Botox injections are commonly used in cosmetic procedures to reduce the appearance of wrinkles and fine lines. It works by inhibiting nerve signals in the muscles where it is injected, resulting in temporary muscle paralysis.

  • Botox has therapeutic applications and is used to treat a variety of medical conditions, including muscle spasms, chronic migraines, excessive sweating (hyperhidrosis), and specific neurological disorders.

  • Botox injections are given by a qualified healthcare professional. The procedure is relatively quick, with patients typically seeing results within a few days to a week.

  • Botox has only temporary effects, lasting three to six months on average. As the effects fade, muscle activity gradually returns, and additional injections may be required to maintain the desired results.

Daxxify has made headlines as the first significant competitor to Botox in decades. Here's why:

  • Daxxify has joined the ranks of Botox, Dysport, Xeomin, and Jeuveau.

  • Daxxify has been approved as a safe and effective wrinkle treatment following a thorough review process.

  • Daxxify, unlike Botox, contains no human or animal products derived from peptides or amino acids.

  • Clinical trials revealed that Daxxify lasts approximately six months, whereas most neuromodulators last only three months.

  • Daxxify can be stored at room temperature, unlike competitors that require refrigeration.

What Are Daxxify and Botox: Understanding the Basics

Botulinum toxin is the source of Daxxify, a neurotoxin that is similar to other well-known therapies like Dysport and Botox. Daxxify is unique because of its sophisticated composition, which is developed to specifically target and block nerve signals that result in muscular contractions. Daxxify momentarily paralyses the muscles that are being treated, which helps to reduce wrinkles and creases and provides the appearance of being more youthful and refreshed.

Comparing Efficacy: Daxxify vs Botox

While both products have similar features, there are some significant differences, such as:

  • Longevity: The Daxxify treatment process produces longer-lasting results than Botox. Botox results usually last four months. Meanwhile, Daxxify results last about six months on average. In the clinical trials submitted to the FDA for approval, 80% of people who used Daxxify had no or barely visible facial wrinkles after four months, and roughly half of the participants had little evidence of wrinkles even after six months. 

  • Treatment Areas: In this case, Botox is advantageous because it can address several issues, including hooded eyes, bunny lines, crow's feet, forehead lines, and frown lines. Daxxify, on the other hand, is limited to treating frown lines, which limits its versatility. It is expected to treat every other area soon.

  • Ingredients: Botulinum toxin type A is an ingredient in both Daxxify and Botox, but their compositions and other components are different.  Botox is based on human serum albumin, whereas Daxxify contains no human or animal products.  Instead, Daxxify employs peptides as a stabiliser. 

  • Storage: While Botox requires refrigeration, Daxxify can be stored at room temperature.

  • Frequency and Maximum Unit Allowed: For Botox, a maximum of 20 units may be administered per treatment; for Daxxify, this can be up to 40 units. Despite this, both neuromodulators deliver 0.18 nanograms of botulinum. 

  • Cost-effectiveness: At the moment, Daxxify costs more than Botox. However, to maintain their desired appearance, a person undergoing Botox must undergo three treatment sessions annually.  In contrast, Daxxify only requires two sessions per year. Daxxify is therefore an affordable option for treating facial wrinkles.

  • Possible Side Effects: Research has demonstrated that both treatment modalities are very successful and have minimal negative effects. Few people may experience rash, itching, redness, swelling, wheezing, dizziness, or fainting as side effects of Daxxify treatment.  Some of the side effects of Botox include infection at the injection site, droopy eyelids or crooked eyebrows, a crooked smile or drooling, headaches or flu-like symptoms, and pain, swelling, or bruising at the injection site.

Delving into the Results: Effectiveness and Longevity

Daxxify’s Effects

  • Daxxify distinguishes itself from Botox with a faster onset and longer efficacy. While Botox typically takes 5-7 days, Daxxify frequently results in a significant reduction in facial wrinkles in just two days.

Botox’s Effects

  • Botox's effects typically last three to four months. During this time, muscle contractions in the treated areas are reduced, which results in smoother skin and fewer wrinkles.

  • Several external factors can influence the long-term effectiveness of Botox. Facial expressions, metabolism, and the rate of toxin metabolism can all influence the duration. Sun exposure and lifestyle choices also help to maintain the benefits.

Safety and Side Effects: What You Need to Know

It is uncommon to have severe side effects from Daxxify or Botox treatment, but it is possible. Here are a few risks you should be aware of:

  • Severe allergic reactions can manifest as swelling of the face, throat, or tongue.

  • The spread of toxins can cause muscle weakness, double vision, and difficulty speaking. Although extremely rare, both Daxxify and Botox Cosmetic issued a black box warning for this.

  • Cardiac issues, such as an irregular heartbeat, are especially common in people who already have heart conditions.

  • People who already have muscle-related conditions will experience worsening symptoms. 

  • Trouble breathing can occur months after treatment. It is more common in people who already have breathing or swallowing problems.

  • If you experience any of the aforementioned side effects, please seek emergency medical attention.

Common Side Effects and How to Manage Them

Common Side Effects of Neuromodulators:

  • Localised Pain or Bruising: Applying a cold compress to the injection site can help reduce pain and bruising. Avoiding strenuous physical activity for the first 24 hours after the procedure may also be recommended.

  • Temporary Weakness or Drooping: This is usually a temporary effect, but if it occurs, inform your healthcare provider immediately. They can guide managing any asymmetry.

  • Headache: Over-the-counter pain relievers may be recommended by your healthcare provider if you experience a headache after the treatment. Staying hydrated and getting adequate rest may also help.

  • Flu-Like Symptoms (Less Common): Rest and staying well-hydrated are generally advised. If symptoms persist or worsen, consult your healthcare provider.

Important Considerations:

  • If you experience any unexpected or concerning side effects, contact your healthcare provider right away.

  • Certain medications and substances, such as blood thinners and alcohol, can increase the likelihood of bruising. Inform your healthcare provider of all medications you are taking.

  • Adhering to any post-treatment instructions provided by your healthcare provider is critical for achieving the best results while minimising side effects.

  • While serious reactions are rare, symptoms such as difficulty breathing or swallowing require immediate medical attention.

Individual Responses Vary: Remember that reactions to neuromodulator therapies can differ from person to person. Based on your medical history, the particular product utilised, and the area of treatment, your healthcare professional will offer tailored advise. Special Considerations for Different Skin Types

When it comes to neuromodulator treatments such as Botox or potential future products, a variety of factors, including skin type, can influence the results and side effects. Here are some specific considerations for various skin types:

  1.  Fair Skin: Fair skin may be more prone to bruising or redness at the injection site. Inform your healthcare provider about any tendency for easy bruising and discuss ways to minimise potential discoloration.

  2.  Dark Skin: Individuals with darker skin tones may be at a lower risk for visible bruising but might be more prone to hyperpigmentation. Discuss the potential for hyperpigmentation with your healthcare provider. They may adjust the injection technique to minimize this risk.

  3.  Sensitive Skin: Individuals with sensitive skin may be more prone to temporary redness or irritation at the injection site. If you have a history of skin sensitivity, inform your healthcare provider. They may recommend post-treatment skincare or avoid certain products.

  4. Oily or Acne-Prone Skin: Individuals with oily or acne-prone skin may have larger pores, which can affect how injections are administered. Discuss any skin concerns, such as acne, with your healthcare provider. They can tailor the treatment to avoid exacerbating skin issues.

  5. Aging or Mature Skin: Aging skin may have reduced elasticity and increased wrinkles. Your healthcare provider may adjust the treatment plan to address specific aging concerns. Combining neuromodulators with other cosmetic procedures may also be considered.

  6. Combination Skin: Combination skin may have both oily and dry areas, requiring a balanced approach. Discuss the specific characteristics of your combination skin with your healthcare provider. They can develop a treatment plan that addresses different areas appropriately.

General Recommendations:

  • During the consultation, your healthcare provider will assess your skin type and condition to tailor the treatment plan appropriately.

  • Some healthcare providers suggest specific skincare routines before and after treatment to improve outcomes and reduce potential side effects.

  • Regardless of skin type, sun protection is essential. Protect your skin from harmful UV rays to keep it healthy and avoid complications.

Always be open with your healthcare provider about your skin type, concerns, and expectations. They can provide personalized advice and address specific considerations related to your unique skin characteristics.
