Understanding the Role of Acid Mantle

The acid mantle is a thin, protective layer on your skin's surface, made up of sebum, sweat, and dead skin cells. It plays a vital role in shielding the skin from harmful bacteria, viruses, and other potential threats. The acid mantle's PH lies between 4.5 and 5.5, making it slightly acidic. This acidic nature helps neutralize any alkaline-based threats that might disturb the skin's natural balance.

How PH Levels Affect Your Skin's Health

If your skin's PH levels skew too much in either direction - becoming too acidic or too alkaline - it can lead to a host of skin issues. An overly acidic skin can cause inflammation, leading to skin conditions such as acne or rosacea. On the other hand, skin that's too alkaline tends to be dry and sensitive, leading to premature aging and wrinkles. Maintaining an optimal PH level ensures the skin remains healthy and vibrant.

The Link Between PH-Balance and Skin Aging

As we age, our skin naturally becomes more alkaline. This shift in PH balance can result in dryness and fine lines. Maintaining the skin's PH balance is crucial to delay the signs of aging and to keep the skin supple and youthful.
