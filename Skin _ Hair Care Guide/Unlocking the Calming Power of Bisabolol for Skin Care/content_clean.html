Bisabolol and Its Source: The Chamomile Plant

Bisabolol is a natural chemical found mostly in chamomile essential oil (Matricaria chamomilla or Chamaemelum nobile). Chamomile is a well-known herb with numerous medicinal characteristics that have been used for ages in traditional medicine and skincare. German chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) and Roman or English chamomile (Chamaemelum nobile) are the two primary varieties of chamomile commonly utilised for their essential oil content.

Source and Extraction:

  1. German chamomile: This variety, which is native to Western Europe, is well-known for its powerful medicinal qualities. The essential oil is extracted from the flowers of German chamomile using steam distillation. The oil contains a high concentration of alpha-bisabolol, the primary active form with anti-inflammatory and skin-soothing properties.

  2. Roman Chamomile: Roman chamomile is native to Western Europe and North Africa. It is thought to be a milder variety than German chamomile. The essential oil is extracted from the flowers using steam distillation and contains bisabolol in lower concentrations than German chamomile.
