Combination skin is a common skin type characterized by having both oily and dry areas on the face. The signs of combination skin can include:

What causes combination skin

Combination skin occurs when different parts of your face have different skin types. Typically, the T-zone (forehead, nose, and chin) is oilier, while the cheeks and other areas are drier. AHAs and BHAs

AHAs and BHAs are two types of exfoliating acids commonly used in skincare products. AHAs, or alpha hydroxy acids, are water-soluble acids derived from fruits and milk. The most common types of AHAs used in skincare products are glycolic acid (derived from sugar cane) and lactic acid (derived from milk). AHAs work by breaking down the bonds between dead skin cells, allowing them to be easily removed from the skin's surface. They are particularly effective at reducing the appearance of fine lines, wrinkles, and uneven skin tone.

BHAs, or beta hydroxy acids, are oil-soluble acids derived from salicylic acid. BHAs penetrate deep into the pores to dissolve the oil and debris that can cause acne and blackheads. They also have anti-inflammatory properties, making them useful for treating redness and inflammation associated with acne-prone skin.

Both AHAs and BHAs can be effective in improving the overall appearance of the skin, but they may be better suited for different skin types and concerns. AHAs are generally better for dry or mature skin types, while BHAs are more effective for oily or acne-prone skin. It's important to use these acids as directed, as overuse can lead to skin irritation and damage.
