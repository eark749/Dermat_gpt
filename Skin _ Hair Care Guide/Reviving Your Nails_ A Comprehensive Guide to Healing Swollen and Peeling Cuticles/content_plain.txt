Understanding Cuticle Health
Understanding cuticle health is critical for overall nail health and preventing problems such as dryness, peeling, irritation, and infections. This blog provides an in-depth look at cuticle health.
Causes of Swollen and Peeling Cuticles
External irritants, a lack of moisture, infections, or underlying health concerns can all contribute to swollen and peeling cuticles. Dryness and dehydration,
intensive nail care practises
, allergic reactions or irritants, infections, health issues, nutritional deficiencies, trauma or injury, environmental variables, hormonal shifts, and underlying medical conditions are some of the most common reasons. If swollen and peeling cuticles persist or become painful,
consult a dermatologist
or healthcare professional, especially if an infection or underlying health issue is suspected.

Nutrition and Vitamins for Cuticle Strength
The importance of nutrition in maintaining healthy cuticles and overall nail health cannot be overstated.Â  Including specific vitamins and nutrients in your diet can help you have stronger, healthier cuticles. The following nutrients and vitamins are important for cuticle strength:
Biotin (Vitamin B7):
Biotin is well-known for its ability to promote healthy hair, skin, and nails, including cuticles. Eggs, nuts (almonds, peanuts), seeds (sunflower seeds), whole grains, bananas, and leafy green vegetables are all good sources.
Vitamin E
: Vitamin E acts as an antioxidant, protecting cells, including those in cuticles, from free radical damage. Almonds, sunflower seeds, spinach, broccoli, avocados, and vegetable oils (sunflower, safflower) are good sources.
Vitamin C
:
Vitamin C is required for collagen synthesis
, which is necessary for healthy nail growth and cuticle strength. Citrus fruits (oranges, lemons), strawberries, bell peppers, kiwi, and leafy greens are good sources.
Omega-3 Fatty Acids
: Omega-3 fatty acids aid in the retention of moisture in the nails and cuticles, preventing dryness and brittleness. Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel), flaxseeds, chia seeds, walnuts, and soybeans are good sources.
Iron
: Iron promotes healthy blood flow, ensuring proper nutrient delivery to the nails and cuticles. Red meat, poultry, lentils, beans, tofu, spinach, and fortified cereals are all good sources.
Zinc
: Zinc promotes healthy nail growth and repair by supporting the immune system. Oysters, beef, poultry, pumpkin seeds, lentils, and chickpeas.
Protein
:
Protein is crucial for keratin production, the main structural protein in nails and cuticles. Lean meats, fish, poultry, eggs, dairy products, legumes, nuts, and seeds.

How to Use Natural Oils for Cuticles
:
Direct Application
: To help the oil penetrate and hydrate the skin, apply a small amount of the selected oil directly to the cuticles and massage gently.
Soak or Mask
: Either soak your fingertips in a bowl of warm water that has been diluted with a few drops of your favourite oil, or use your favourite oil to make a homemade cuticle mask.
Overnight Treatment
: To allow the oil to deeply moisturise cuticles overnight, apply it before going to bed and cover your hands with gloves or socks.
DIY Soothing Treatments for Swollen Cuticles
Soothing creams and lotions can help relieve irritation and swelling in the cuticles. Here are some do-it-yourself remedies for swollen cuticles:
Warm Water Soak with Epsom Salt
:
Ingredients
: Epsom salt and warm water.
Procedure
: Mix Epsom salt in warm water in a bowl. To reduce swelling and promote relaxation, soak your fingertips for 10-15 minutes.
Chamomile Tea Compress
:
Ingredients
: Warm water and bags of chamomile tea.
Procedure
:
Steep chamomile tea bags in warm water and let them cool slightly. To relieve inflammation, place warm tea bags on the swollen cuticles for a few minutes.
Aloe Vera Gel
:
Ingredients
: gel of fresh aloe vera
Procedure
: The swollen cuticles should be directly covered with fresh aloe vera gel. Leave it on for 10-15 minutes to help reduce swelling and soothe irritation.
Cucumber Slices
:
Ingredients
: Cucumber slices.
Procedure
: For a few minutes, apply slices of chilled cucumber to the afflicted area. Relief and a decrease in swelling may be obtained from the cooling effect.
Witch Hazel Soak
:
Ingredients
:
Witch hazel.
Procedure
: Dilute witch hazel with a bit of water. Soak a cotton ball in the mixture and apply it to the swollen cuticles for anti-inflammatory properties.
Oatmeal Paste
:
Ingredients
:
Water and ground oats.
Procedure
:
Mix ground oatmeal with water to create a paste. Apply the paste to the affected area to relieve pain.
Honey Wrap
:
Ingredients
:
Raw honey.
Procedure
:
Apply a thin layer of raw honey to the swollen cuticles. To help reduce swelling, wrap the area in gauze or a bandage for a while.
These soothing do-it-yourself remedies can help relieve swelling and discomfort caused by swollen cuticles. However, if the swelling is severe, painful, or accompanied by signs of infection, it is best to see a doctor for proper treatment.
