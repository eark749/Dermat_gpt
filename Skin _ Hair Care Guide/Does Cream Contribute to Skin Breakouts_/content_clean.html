When it comes to deciding between cream and powder blush for acne-prone skin, each has advantages and disadvantages. The decision is ultimately based on your skin type, preferences, and unique issues. Here's a comparison to help you decide which one is better for acne-prone skin.

How to Clean Your Makeup Tools to Reduce Skin Issues

Maintaining good skin and avoiding skin problems like acne, inflammation, and infections requires routinely cleaning your makeup equipment. Here's a detailed tutorial on properly cleaning your makeup tools:

  1. Gather Your Supplies: Warm water, rubbing alcohol or brush cleaner solution, dish soap with antibacterial properties, gentle liquid soap or baby shampoo, and clean towels or paper towels. Silicone brush cleaning mat or glove are optional.

  2. Cleanse Brushes: To moisten the bristles, rinse your makeup brushes in lukewarm water. Put a tiny bit of baby shampoo or mild liquid soap in the palm of your hand, or apply some on a silicone brush cleaning pad. Work up a lather with the damp brush in the cleanser to loosen and remove germs, oils, and makeup residue. After removing all soap residue, rinse the brush under running water until the water is clear. Using your fingers, gently reposition the bristles of the brushes, then place them flat on a fresh towel or paper towel to let them air dry. While brushes are drying, avoid standing them upright as this may allow water to enter the ferrule and loosen the bristles.

  3. Cleanse Sponges: Using lukewarm water, wet your makeup sponge until it swells and gets saturated. Use a silicone brush cleaning pad or a damp sponge to immediately apply a tiny bit of antibacterial dish soap. To dissolve microorganisms and makeup, gently rub the soap into the sponge and knead it through the material. Rinse the sponge under running water to get rid of any remaining soap residue. After removing any remaining water from the sponge, blot it dry with a fresh towel. Before putting the sponge in a dry, clean container, let it air dry fully.

  4. Disinfect Tools: After cleaning, you can spritz your brushes and sponges with rubbing alcohol or a brush cleaner solution for an additional layer of disinfection. Before using them again, let them dry thoroughly on the air.

  5. Cleaning Frequency: To avoid the accumulation of bacteria, oils, and makeup residue, clean your makeup brushes and sponges at least once a week. Compared to brushes used for powders, brushes used for liquid or cream cosmetics typically contain more bacteria, therefore wash them more often.
