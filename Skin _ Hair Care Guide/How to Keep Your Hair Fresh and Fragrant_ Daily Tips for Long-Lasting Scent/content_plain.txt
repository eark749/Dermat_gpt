Homemade Hair Sprays for a Fresh Scent
Creating homemade hair sprays is an excellent way to add a fresh scent to your hair without relying on commercial products that might contain harsh chemicals. Here are a few simple recipes using natural ingredients:
Floral Hair Mist
:
Ingredients
: Distilled water: 1 cup, Rosewater: 1-2 tablespoons, Essential oil (Lavender, Rose, Jasmine): 5-10 drops
Instructions
:
Mix distilled water and rosewater in a spray bottle. Add 5-10 drops of your preferred essential oil. Shake well before each use and mist lightly over your hair for a floral scent
Herbal Hair Refreshing Spray
:
Ingredients
: Distilled water: 1 cup Dried herbs (Rosemary, Mint, Lavender): 2-3 tablespoons (or 3-4 sprigs of fresh herbs), Apple cider vinegar: 1-2 tablespoons (optional) and Essential oil (optional): 5-10 drops
Instructions
: Boil the water and add the herbs. Let it steep for 15-20 minutes. Strain and allow it to cool. Add apple cider vinegar (if using) and essential oil to the herbal infusion. Pour into a spray bottle and use as a refreshing hair mist.
Citrus Hair Revitalizing Spray
:
Ingredients
:
Distilled water: 1 cup, Citrus fruit peels (Lemon, Orange, or Grapefruit): Peel of 1-2 fruits, Witch hazel: 1-2 tablespoons (optional) and Essential oil (Lemon, Orange, Grapefruit): 5-10 drops
Instructions
: Steep citrus peels in boiled water for 30 minutes. Let it cool and strain.  Add witch hazel (if using) and essential oil to the citrus-infused water. Transfer to a spray bottle and spritz onto hair for a citrusy scent.
These homemade hair sprays are natural, customizable, and can provide a refreshing fragrance to your hair while offering potential benefits from the herbs and essential oils used. Adjust the recipes as needed to create your preferred scents and enjoy the natural freshness they provide.
Tips for Using Natural Deodorizers
Natural deodorizers work well and provide a fresh, clean scent without the use of harsh chemicals. Here are some pointers on how to use them effectively:
Baking Soda
: Eliminate shoe odor by sprinkling a small amount of baking soda inside shoes. Before wearing, leave it overnight and shake out the excess. Sprinkle baking soda on carpets, upholstery, and fabric-covered furniture. Allow it to sit for a few hours before vacuuming.
Vinegar
: In a spray bottle, combine equal parts water and vinegar. It can be used as a room spray to eliminate odours. Refrigerate a bowl of vinegar to absorb unpleasant odours.
Essential Oils
: Combine a few drops of essential oils (such as lavender, lemon, or eucalyptus) with water in a spray bottle for a natural air freshener. To give your clothes a nice scent while doing laundry, add a few drops of essential oils to the washing machine.
Citrus Peels
:
Place citrus peels (from lemons, oranges, or grapefruits) in a bowl on countertops or in rooms for a refreshing scent. To make a natural air freshener, boil citrus peels in water with a cinnamon stick or herbs.
Activated Charcoa
l: Place activated charcoal in a bowl or mesh bag in areas prone to odours, such as closets, bathrooms, or near trash cans.
Herbal Sachets
: Fill sachets with dried herbs such as lavender, rosemary, or mint. Put them in drawers or closets to fill them with a natural, fragrant aroma.
Plants
: Indoor plants such as aloe vera, snake plants, and peace lilies can help purify the air and eliminate odours.
Open Windows and Ventilation

These eco-friendly, safe natural deodorising methods can effectively eliminate unwanted odours in your home while providing pleasant scents. Customise the combinations and application methods to your liking and enjoy the benefits of a naturally fresh environment.

Avoiding Products That Cause Odor Buildup
To avoid odour buildup in your hair, choose haircare products and practises that minimise residue accumulation and promote scalp health. Here are some suggestions for avoiding odor-causing products:
Choose Residue-Free Products
:
Use sulfate-free shampoos to avoid leaving behind excess residue that can trap odours in the hair. Silicone-based conditioners can cause buildup over time. Choose silicone-free conditioners that won't weigh down your hair or contribute to odor retention.
Use Clarifying Products Periodically
:
Use a clarifying shampoo
regularly to remove product buildup, oils, and impurities that can trap odours in your hair. Use a diluted apple cider vinegar rinse regularly to clarify the scalp and hair, removing buildup and neutralising odours.
Rinse Thoroughly
: After shampooing and conditioning, thoroughly rinse your hair to remove all product residue.
Limit Styling Products
:
Avoid using heavy styling products like gels, waxes, or pomades, which can cause buildup and trap odours. To avoid buildup, opt for lightweight styling products or those labelled as non-comedogenic and non-greasy.
Clean Hair Tools
: Regularly clean your hairbrushes and combs to prevent the transfer of odors or residue onto your hair.
Avoid Overwashing
:
Avoid overwashing your hair, as excessive cleansing can strip natural oils, leading to an overproduction of sebum and potential odor buildup.
Practice Scalp Care
: Ensure you wash your scalp regularly to prevent oil, sweat, and bacteria accumulation, which can contribute to odors. Consider using scalp exfoliating treatments to remove dead skin cells and keep your scalp healthy.
Pay Attention to Ingredients
: Be wary of hair products containing strong fragrances, which can temporarily mask odours but may contribute to buildup over time. Avoid products containing heavy silicones, parabens, and sulphates, which may contribute to residue buildup.
Maintain Overall Hygiene
:
Regularly wash pillowcases, hats, headbands, and any accessories that come in contact with your hair to prevent odor transfer.
By following these tips and being mindful of the products you use, you can reduce residue buildup and avoid unpleasant odours in your hair. Maintaining good scalp hygiene and using products appropriate for your hair type can also contribute to a fresher and cleaner hair environment.

:
After digestion, foods such as garlic, onions, and certain spices can impart their odours onto the scalp and hair, affecting its scent. Consuming foods high in antioxidants can help combat free radicals, which contribute to odour.

:
