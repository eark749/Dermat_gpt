Tencel and lyocell bedding are made from natural fibres derived from sustainable wood sources. Tencel has a smooth surface, similar to silk, satin, and bamboo, which prevents friction and tangling in your hair. It is also skin-friendly and moisture-wicking.
High Thread Count Cotton:
Choose high thread count cotton sheets if you want cotton bedding. A higher thread count indicates that the cloth is closely woven, resulting in a smoother surface that eliminates friction and hair damage.
Microfiber:
Microfiber bedding is a synthetic alternative that can be gentle on your hair. Microfiber sheets have a smooth texture that minimizes friction, making them a hair-friendly option.
You can promote healthier, smoother, and more manageable hair over time by using bedding materials that decrease friction and tension on your hair, as well as including silk or satin pillowcases or hair accessories into your evening regimen. For best hair health, match your bedding selections with the correct hair care routine and a healthy lifestyle.
