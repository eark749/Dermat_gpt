  6. AHAs and BHAs: Salicylic acid and other beta-hydroxy acids (BHAs) like glycolic acid are two common exfoliating ingredients. They assist in removing dead skin cells, clearing clogged pores, and enhancing skin texture and clarity.

Skincare Experts Weigh In: The Benefits and Drawbacks of Trending Ingredients

Trendy substances for skincare frequently cause enthusiasm, but it's crucial to comprehend both their advantages and disadvantages. The benefits and drawbacks of some common trending substances are shown below:

Hyaluronic Acid:

Pros:

  • Excellent moisturising qualities because it draws and holds moisture in the skin.

  • Skin is plumped and made smoother, which makes wrinkles and fine lines less noticeable.

  • Can be used in a variety of formulations and is appropriate for different skin types. 

Cons:

  • Hyaluronic acid can remove moisture from the skin in low-humidity or dry environments, which could result in dryness.

  • Hyaluronic acid with a high molecular weight may rest on the skin's surface, providing superficial hydration but not lasting hydration.

Niacinamide:

Pros:

  • It is suitable for oily and acne-prone skin because it aids in controlling sebum production.

  • Improves the skin's barrier function, resulting in greater moisture retention.

  • Reduces redness, evens out skin tone, and minimises the appearance of pores.

Cons:

  • Niacinamide is generally well tolerated, but some people may experience skin sensitivity or irritation. Patch testing is strongly advised.

  • Rare cases of flushing or a sensation of warmth may occur, especially at higher concentrations, but this usually goes away quickly.

Retinol:

Pros:

  • A gold standard ingredient for reducing the appearance of fine lines, wrinkles, and uneven skin texture.

  • Improves skin elasticity and firmness by stimulating collagen production.

  • Can help to reduce the appearance of hyperpigmentation and acne scars.

Cons:

  • Skin irritation is possible, especially when starting retinol or using higher concentrations. Starting with a low concentration and gradually increasing usage is critical.

  • It's important to use sunscreen and limit your exposure to the sun because retinol can make your skin more sensitive to sunlight.

Peptides:

Pros:

  • Aid in the production of collagen and the improvement of skin elasticity.

  • Can help to minimise the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles.

  • Possesses potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties. 

Cons:

  • The effectiveness of a given peptide can vary, and its advantages can sometimes be more apparent when combined with other active ingredients.

  • The importance of selecting products with carefully formulated peptide complexes cannot be overstated because some peptides may be more stable and effective in specific formulations.

Antioxidants (such as green tea extract, vitamin C, and vitamin E):

Pros:

  • Free radicals, environmental stressors, and UV ray damage should all be avoided.

  • Can aid in the brightening, evening, and fading of hyperpigmentation.

  • Anti-aging properties, such as antioxidants can help to reduce collagen and elastin breakdown.

Cons:

  • Vitamin C, in particular, is highly unstable and oxidises rapidly. To guarantee effectiveness, look for stable formulations and suitable packaging.

  • Some people may react to certain antioxidant ingredients with skin sensitivity or irritation. Patch testing is advised.
