Profhilo vs. Traditional Dermal Fillers
While both Profhilo and Dermal Fillers are non-surgical treatments that use hyaluronic acid, there are some significant differences between them.
To begin, dermal fillers are most commonly used to add volume to specific areas of the face, such as the cheeks, temples, and lips, as well as to soften deeper wrinkles and folds. Profhilo, on the other hand, is intended to improve overall skin quality by stimulating collagen production and attracting moisture. This produces a lovely natural appearance, with the skin appearing and feeling tighter with a healthy glow.
Second, dermal fillers typically have an immediate effect, with results visible immediately after injection. Profhilo, on the other hand, takes a little longer to show its effects, with optimal results typically appearing within two weeks of the second treatment. Both have a comparable downtime, with redness and inflammation taking 24-48 hours to resolve. This may influence the timing of your treatment and any plans you have. Third, the effects of dermal fillers are more localised and specific to the areas treated. Profhilo, on the other hand, spreads evenly beneath the skin, producing a more natural-looking result and a consistent improvement in skin texture and appearance. Finally, whereas dermal fillers typically last 8-18 months, Profhilo's effects can last up to six months, depending on the individual's metabolism, lifestyle, and maintenance regimen.
When deciding between Profhilo and dermal fillers, you should consider your goals and expectations. If you want to add volume to specific areas of your face, dermal fillers could be the best option. However, if you want to improve the overall quality of your skin and achieve a more natural-looking result, Profhilo may be the best option. If you enjoy the sound of both treatments, they complement each other well.

Understanding the Safety Profile of Profhilo
Profhilo has been generally regarded as safe when administered by trained and qualified medical professionals. It is a hyaluronic acid-based product designed to improve skin hydration, texture, and elasticity. Hyaluronic acid is a naturally occurring substance in the body, making Profhilo well-tolerated by most individuals. However, as with any medical procedure, there are safety concerns.
Profhilo injections should only be administered by qualified and experienced medical professionals, such as dermatologists, plastic surgeons, or trained aestheticians. The practitioner's knowledge of injection techniques is critical for achieving optimal results and reducing the risk of complications.
Hyaluronic acid is generally well tolerated, and allergic reactions are uncommon. Patients should, however, be tested for allergies and sensitivities during their pre-treatment consultation. Profhilo is highly purified, which lowers the risk of allergic reactions.
As with any injectable procedure, there is the possibility of infection. To reduce this risk, practitioners must maintain aseptic conditions, use sterile instruments, and adhere to proper hygiene protocols throughout the injection process.
Bruising and swelling are common side effects of Profhilo injections, but they are typically mild and temporary. Patients should be informed of these potential side effects during their consultation.
While serious complications are uncommon, they can occur after treatment, such as lumps, bumps, or nodules. These are typically injection-related issues that can be addressed by the treating practitioner.
Granulomas (small, localised nodules) are a rare reaction to hyaluronic acid injections. However, this risk is generally low when using high-quality, pure products such as Profhilo. Such complications can be managed and resolved with timely intervention from a medical professional.
Not all patients may be appropriate candidates for Profhilo. Individuals who are pregnant or breastfeeding, have a history of autoimmune diseases, or are prone to keloid formation may not be appropriate candidates.
Profhilo's long-term safety has been investigated in a number of clinical trials, and it has been found to have a favourable safety profile. However, as with any new aesthetic treatment, ongoing monitoring and research improve our understanding of long-term safety.
Both practitioners and patients must communicate openly, address any concerns, and adhere to appropriate pre- and post-treatment protocols. Patients should seek out a reputable and licenced professional to ensure a safe and effective treatment experience. Always consult with a qualified medical professional for personalized advice and information regarding the safety of Profhilo based on individual health and circumstances.
Post-Treatment Care and Tips
Proper post-treatment care is required to maximise the efficacy of Profhilo injections and reduce the risk of complications. Here are some post-treatment care tips to follow:
Refrain from touching, rubbing, or massaging the treated area immediately after the procedure. This helps prevent the unintended spread of the product and minimizes the risk of irritation.
Avoid strenuous exercise and activities that may cause excessive sweating for at least 24 to 48 hours post-treatment. This helps prevent increased blood flow to the treated area, reducing the risk of swelling and bruising.
If there is mild swelling or bruising, applying a cold compress to the treated area can help reduce inflammation. Use a clean cloth or a cold pack wrapped in a thin towel and apply it for short intervals.
Maintaining good hydration is important for overall skin health. Drink plenty of water to help support the body's natural healing processes and enhance the results of the treatment.
Protect the treated area from direct sunlight for a few days following the procedure. If exposure is unavoidable, use a broad-spectrum sunscreen with a high SPF to prevent sun damage.

gentle cleanser
