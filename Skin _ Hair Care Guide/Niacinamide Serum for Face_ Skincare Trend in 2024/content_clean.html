Addressing Common Myths about Niacinamide

Niacinamide is a well-studied skincare component with numerous advantages, but it is also the topic of some myths and misconceptions. In this section, we'll debunk some common niacinamide myths and present the correct information to help you make informed judgments about its use in your skincare routine:

  • Myth 1: Niacinamide can significantly lighten the skin.

  • Fact: Niacinamide is not a skin-whitening substance, but it can help lighten hyperpigmentation and balance out skin tone. It will not significantly lighten your skin tone above what it naturally is. Niacinamide produces a more even complexion by diminishing the appearance of redness, sun damage, and dark spots.

  • Myth 2: Niacinamide (Vitamin B3) Is the Same as Niacin.

  • Fact: Although they are related forms of vitamin B3, niacinamide and niacin (nicotinic acid) are not the same. Niacinamide does not cause the skin flushing that is commonly associated with niacin. Both forms have distinct properties and applications.

  • Myth 3: Niacinamide Causes Skin Irritation or Flushing.

  • Fact: Niacinamide is well tolerated by most skin types and is less prone to cause irritation than niacin. Skin flushing is not a typical side effect of niacinamide. Some people may be allergic to it, like with any skincare product. Start with a lower concentration and gradually increase your usage if you have sensitive skin.

  • Myth 4: Niacinamide cannot be used in conjunction with vitamin C or acidic products.

  • Fact: Niacinamide can be used in conjunction with other skincare ingredients such as vitamin C and acidic products such as alpha hydroxy acids (AHAs) and beta hydroxy acids (BHAs). Some early research suggested a possible interaction, but current scientific evidence indicates that they can be used together without causing significant harm. If you have any doubts, apply them at different times of the day or consult a dermatologist for personalised advice.

  • Myth 5: Niacinamide Is Only Used for Anti-Aging.

  • Fact: Niacinamide has many benefits than just anti-aging. It can be used to treat acne, oily skin, sensitivity, and hyperpigmentation, among other skin issues. Its adaptability makes it suited for a wide range of skincare requirements.

  • Myth 6: Niacinamide Should Be Used Moderately.

  • Fact: Niacinamide is safe to use in your everyday skincare routine. In most circumstances, it can be used both morning and evening, after cleaning and toning but before moisturising. It's a versatile and well-tolerated substance that may be used in your skincare routine on a regular basis.

  • Myth 7: Niacinamide Can Be Used Instead of Sunscreen.

  • Fact: Niacinamide has some UV protection properties, but it should not be used in place of sunscreen. Sunscreen is still the most effective way to shield your skin from the damaging effects of UV rays. When using niacinamide products during the day, it is critical to use a broad-spectrum sunscreen with an SPF of at least 30.

By busting these niacinamide myths, you will be able to better understand its role in skincare and make more informed decisions about incorporating it into your routine. Remember that individual skin reactions can vary, so tailor your skincare routine to your specific needs and seek personalised advice from a dermatologist if necessary.

Side effects and precautions using Niacinamide serum

Niacinamide, also known as vitamin B3 or nicotinamide, is well tolerated by the majority of people and has a low risk of causing side effects. However, as with any skincare product, some people may have sensitivities. The following are some of the potential negative effects and precautions to take when using a niacinamide serum:

Potential Side Effects:

  1. Irritation: Some people with sensitive skin may feel moderate irritation, redness, or a burning sensation when using niacinamide products. This is quite unusual, but it can happen, especially if you have never taken niacinamide before or are using a product with a high concentration.

  2. Allergic Reactions: Allergic reactions to niacinamide are uncommon, however, they can include itching, redness, or hives. If you suspect an allergic response, stop using the product and visit a dermatologist.

  3. Flushing (Skin Redness): Niacinamide, unlike niacin (nicotinic acid), is less prone to induce skin flushing or redness. Some people with extremely sensitive skin may still notice slight redness.

Precautions:

  1. Patch Test: Consider performing a patch test before applying a niacinamide serum to your full face. Apply a small amount of the product to a hidden part of your skin (such as behind your ear) and wait 24-48 hours to observe if any adverse reactions arise.

  2. Start with Lower Concentrations: If you have sensitive skin or have never used niacinamide previously, start with a lower dose (e.g., 2-5%), then gradually raise the concentration if your skin tolerates it well.

  3. Sun Protection: Niacinamide might make the skin more sensitive to UV rays. As a result, if you use niacinamide products throughout the day, you should use a broad-spectrum sunscreen with at least SPF 30 to protect your skin from UV damage.

  4. Avoid the Eye Area: Niacinamide serums should not be applied directly to the eyelids or the immediate eye area because they can cause mild irritation in this sensitive area.

  5. Compatibility with Other Ingredients: Use caution when combining niacinamide with highly acidic products such as vitamin C or alpha hydroxy acids. While studies show that such combinations are generally well tolerated, some people may experience mild irritation. To be safe, apply them at different times of the day or seek personalised advice from a dermatologist.

  6. Consult a Dermatologist: Before incorporating niacinamide into your skincare routine, consult a dermatologist if you have specific skin conditions, concerns, or a history of skin allergies. They can advise you on the best products and concentrations to use on your skin.

In most cases, niacinamide is a safe and well-tolerated skincare ingredient that can be used for a variety of skin types and concerns. By taking these precautions and monitoring your skin's response, you can use niacinamide serums effectively while minimising the risk of side effects.

The chemistry: How does Niacinamide work on the skin?

Niacinamide, commonly known as vitamin B3 or nicotinamide, works on the skin through a variety of methods due to its complex chemistry. It has various skin benefits due to its effect on cellular processes. Here's how niacinamide acts on the skin:

  1. Supports the Skin Barrier: Niacinamide is essential for improving the skin's natural barrier function. It assists in the preservation of the stratum corneum (the epidermis's outermost layer), which acts as a protective shield for the skin. A robust skin barrier is necessary for avoiding moisture loss and the entry of environmental irritants into the skin.

  2. Reduces Inflammation: Niacinamide has anti-inflammatory qualities that help to soothe and calm the skin. It can reduce redness and inflammation, making it useful for people who have sensitive or irritated skin.

  3. Addresses Hyperpigmentation: Niacinamide prevents melanin (skin pigment) from being transferred from melanocytes (skin cells responsible for pigment formation) to the epidermis (upper skin layer). It helps to diminish the appearance of hyperpigmentation, dark patches, and uneven skin tone by doing so.

  4. Minimises Pore Size and Oil Production: Niacinamide regulates sebum (skin oil) production and helps reduce excess oiliness, making it an excellent choice for people with oily or mixed skin. It can also aid in the reduction of the appearance of enlarged pores.

  5. Stimulates Collagen Production: Niacinamide contains antioxidants, which protect the skin from free radical damage, which can cause premature aging. It also stimulates collagen formation, which enhances skin texture and lowers the visibility of fine lines and wrinkles.

  6. Improves Skin Hydration: Niacinamide diminishes trans-epidermal water loss (TEWL), which aids in the skin's ability to hold onto moisture. This leads to improved skin hydration and a reduction in dryness and flakiness.

  7. Compatible with Other Ingredients: Niacinamide is versatile and can be combined with a variety of different skincare products, such as vitamin C, retinol, and hyaluronic acid. Because of its adaptability, it can successfully address a variety of skin issues.
