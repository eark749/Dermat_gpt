DIY Hair Treatments with Cordyceps Militaris
Making DIY hair treatments with Cordyceps militaris is a fun and effective way to incorporate this natural ingredient into your hair care regimen. Here are some DIY recipes you can try:
Cordyceps Infused Hair Oil
: Ingredients are Cordyceps militaris extract or powder, carrier oil (such as coconut oil, jojoba oil, or argan oil). Mix 1-2 teaspoons of Cordyceps militaris extract or powder with 1/4 cup of your chosen carrier oil. Heat the mixture gently in a double boiler or microwave until the oil is warm but not hot. Allow the mixture to cool slightly, then strain out any solid particles if using Cordyceps powder. Transfer the infused oil to a clean, glass bottle or jar for storage.
Cordyceps Hair Rinse
:
Ingredients are Cordyceps militaris tea (brewed from dried Cordyceps militaris mushrooms), apple cider vinegar (optional), and essential oils (optional). Brew a strong tea using dried Cordyceps militaris mushrooms. Let the tea cool completely before using it. Add 1-2 tablespoons of apple cider vinegar to the cooled tea for added scalp health benefits (optional). Optionally, add a few drops of essential oils such as lavender, rosemary, or tea tree oil for fragrance and additional scalp benefits.
Cordyceps Hair Mask
: Ingredients are Cordyceps militaris extract or powder, yogurt, honey, egg yolk. In a bowl, mix 1-2 tablespoons of Cordyceps militaris extract or powder with 1/2 cup of plain yogurt. Add 1 tablespoon of honey and 1 egg yolk to the mixture and stir until well combined. Apply the mask to clean, damp hair, focusing on the lengths and ends. Leave it on for 20-30 minutes. Rinse the mask out thoroughly with lukewarm water, followed by shampoo and conditioner as usual.
