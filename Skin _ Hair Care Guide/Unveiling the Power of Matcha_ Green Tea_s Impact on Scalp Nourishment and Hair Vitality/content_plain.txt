Tea is one of the most popular beverages in many cultures, coming in second to water. Its particular flavor, aroma, and health-promoting properties are greatly valued worldwide, as are its socio-cultural associations. Green tea is available in a variety of forms, including loose leaves, tea bags, and powdered. Matcha is a powdered form of Japanese green tea (
Camellia sinensis
) from the Tencha variety. The popular beverage has been spreading over the world. Because of the unique cultivation method, it contains a high concentration of antioxidants. The traditional approach involves covering the tea bushes with bamboo mats for the majority of the growth time to protect the leaves from direct sunlight. This blog discusses green tea's impact on scalp nutrition and hair vitality.

Stimulating Growth: The Role of Matcha in Enhancing Hair Vitality
Matcha, a finely mashed powder made from specially produced and processed green tea leaves, has been praised for its multiple health advantages, including the potential to boost hair vitality. While the scientific study on matcha's impact on hair growth is still in its early stages, its high nutrient profile suggests that it could help maintain healthy hair in various ways.
Antioxidant Properties
: Matcha contains a high concentration of antioxidants, mainly catechins, which have been demonstrated to counteract oxidative stress and protect cells. This can help prevent hair follicles from free radical damage, creating a more favorable environment for hair growth.
Caffeine Content
: Matcha contains caffeine but at lesser levels than coffee. Caffeine has been shown to activate hair follicles, potentially increasing hair growth. Caffeine is even included in some hair care products to achieve this.
Nutrient Density
: Matcha contains a high concentration of vitamins and minerals, including vitamin E,
vitamin C
, vitamin A, and trace minerals such as selenium and zinc. These nutrients play critical roles in scalp health and hair growth.
Anti-Inflammatory Effects
: Chronic scalp inflammation can impair hair development and cause
dandruff
and scalp discomfort. Matcha's anti-inflammatory qualities may help ease these symptoms, resulting in a healthier environment for hair follicles.
Stress Reduction
: Matcha includes L-theanine, an amino acid that has been demonstrated to enhance relaxation and lower stress. Stress is a recognized contributor to
hair loss
, thus ingesting matcha may indirectly benefit hair health by reducing stress levels.
Soothing Solutions: Matcha's Calming Effect on Unbalanced Scalps
Matcha's relaxing properties extend beyond its antioxidant and vitamin content; it may also provide comfort for an imbalanced scalp. Here's how matcha can assist with scalp issues:
Matcha contains polyphenols, including epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG), which are anti-inflammatory. Matcha, whether applied topically or consumed, may help reduce scalp irritation caused by disorders such as dandruff,
psoriasis
, or
dermatitis
.
An imbalanced scalp might result in excessively oily or dry skin. Matcha's capacity to regulate sebum production may help normalize scalp oil levels, providing comfort to those who suffer from oily or
dry scalp
.
Matcha exhibits antimicrobial properties, thanks to compounds like catechins. These antimicrobial properties may help combat scalp infections caused by fungi or bacteria, potentially reducing symptoms like itching and irritation.
The fine texture of matcha powder makes it an excellent ingredient for gentle exfoliation. When applied topically, matcha can help remove dead skin cells and product buildup from the scalp, promoting a healthier environment for hair growth.
Matcha contains natural humectants that help retain moisture. This can be particularly beneficial for individuals with dry scalp issues, as it helps prevent moisture loss and keeps the scalp hydrated and supple.
Crafting Your Matcha Hair Ritual: Incorporating Green Tea into Your Routine

Matcha Hair Mask
: Make a healthy
hair mask
by combining matcha powder with yoghurt, honey, and coconut oil. Apply the mask to damp hair, concentrating on the scalp and roots, and leave on for 20-30 minutes before completely rinsing out. This can help to calm the scalp, stimulate hair development, and give gloss to your hair.

: Prepare a strong cup of matcha tea and let it cool. After shampooing and conditioning, rinse with matcha tea. Pour it on your hair and scalp, massage gently, then rinse with cool water. This can help balance the pH of the scalp, reduce irritation, and give your hair a natural sheen.
DIY Matcha Hair Scrub

shampoo
and
conditioner
.
Matcha Infused Hair Oil
: Combine matcha powder with carrier oils such as coconut oil, argan oil, or jojoba oil to make healthy hair oil. Matcha Hair Mist

DIY Matcha Hair Masks: Recipes for Nourished Locks
Three DIY matcha hair mask recipes that can nourish your hair:
Moisturizing Matcha Hair Mask
: Ingredients are 1 tablespoon of matcha powder, 2 tablespoons of coconut oil (melted), and 1 tablespoon of honey. In a small bowl, mix the matcha powder, melted coconut oil, and honey until well combined. Apply the mask to damp hair, focusing on the scalp and roots, and working your way down to the ends. Gently massage the mask into your hair and scalp for a few minutes to ensure even distribution. Leave the mask on for 20-30 minutes. Rinse thoroughly with lukewarm water, followed by shampoo and conditioner as usual.
Strengthening Matcha Protein Hair Mask
: Ingredients are 1 tablespoon of matcha powder, 1 egg, and 2 tablespoons of plain yogurt. In a bowl, whisk together the matcha powder, egg, and yogurt until smooth. Apply the mixture to clean, damp hair, focusing on the scalp and roots. Cover your hair with a shower cap or towel and leave the mask on for 30-45 minutes. Rinse thoroughly with cool water to prevent the egg from cooking in your hair.  Follow up with shampoo and conditioner as usual.
Clarifying Matcha Scalp Scrub
: Ingredients are 1 tablespoon of matcha powder, 2 tablespoons of brown sugar, and 2 tablespoons of coconut oil (melted).  In a small bowl, combine the matcha powder, brown sugar, and melted coconut oil.  Mix until you have a gritty paste-like consistency. Divide your hair into sections and apply the scrub to your scalp, massaging gently in circular motions. Continue massaging for 5-10 minutes to exfoliate the scalp and remove buildup. Rinse thoroughly with lukewarm water, then shampoo and condition your hair as usual.
From Roots to Tips: Strengthening Hair with Matcha-Infused Treatments
Strengthening your hair from roots to tips with matcha-infused treatments can be a fun and practical method to improve its health. Here are some treatments you could try:
Matcha Scalp Massage Oil
: Ingredients are 2 tablespoons of coconut oil, 1 tablespoon of olive oil, and 1 tablespoon of matcha powder. Mix all the ingredients in a small bowl until well combined. Warm the mixture slightly to liquefy the coconut oil if necessary. Part your hair into sections and apply the oil to your scalp, massaging gently in circular motions. Continue massaging for 5-10 minutes to stimulate blood flow and promote absorption of nutrients. Leave the oil on for at least 30 minutes or overnight for deeper conditioning. Rinse thoroughly with shampoo and conditioner.
Matcha Hair Rinse
: Ingredients are 2 tablespoons of matcha powder and 2 cups of warm water. Brew a strong cup of matcha tea by whisking the matcha powder into warm water until dissolved. Allow the tea to cool to room temperature. After shampooing and conditioning your hair, pour the matcha tea over your hair and scalp as a final rinse. Massage gently and leave it on for a few minutes. Rinse thoroughly with cool water to seal the hair cuticles and enhance shine.
Matcha Hair Mask for Strength
: Ingredients are 2 tablespoons of matcha powder, 1 ripe avocado, 1 tablespoon of honey, and 1 tablespoon of olive oil. Mash the avocado in a bowl until smooth. Add matcha powder, honey, and olive oil to the mashed avocado and mix until well combined. Apply the mask to clean, damp hair, focusing on the lengths and ends. Cover your hair with a shower cap or towel and leave the mask on for 30-45 minutes. Rinse thoroughly with lukewarm water, followed by shampoo and conditioner.
Scalp Rejuvenation: Massaging with Matcha for Optimal Results
Matcha scalp massage can be a rejuvenating experience with several health advantages for your hair and scalp. Here's how to do a scalp massage with matcha for best results:
Ingredients: 1 tablespoon matcha powder, 2 tablespoons coconut oil or olive oil (optional), and a few drops of essential oil such as lavender or rosemary (optional for extra scent and scalp benefits).
If you're using oil in your scalp massage, begin by warming it slightly to liquefy it. You can accomplish this by placing the oil in a small bowl and microwaving it for a few seconds, or by immersing the bowl in a larger dish of warm water. In a small bowl, whisk together the matcha powder, warmed oil (if using), and essential oil. Mix thoroughly until you have a smooth paste-like consistency.
Divide your hair into portions using a comb or your fingers. This will make it simpler to distribute the matcha mixture evenly on your scalp.
Use your fingertips to apply the matcha mixture to your scalp, concentrating on one part at a time. Begin at the front of your scalp and work your way back. Massage the matcha mixture into your scalp in circular strokes. Massage with mild pressure to increase blood circulation and relaxation. Continue massaging each part of your scalp for 5-10 minutes, making sure the matcha mixture is well distributed throughout.
After massaging the matcha mixture into your entire scalp, keep it on for another 10-15 minutes to allow the nutrients to enter. If desired, cover your hair with a shower hat or towel to keep the mixture from dripping.
After the allotted time has gone, properly rinse the matcha mixture from your hair with lukewarm water. Use your regular shampoo and conditioner to cleanse your hair and scalp.
