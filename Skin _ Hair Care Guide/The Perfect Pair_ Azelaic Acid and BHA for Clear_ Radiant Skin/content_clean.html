Understanding Azelaic Acid and BHA: Benefits and Science

BHA and azelaic acid are two potent skincare components that support clear, bright skin. Here is a quick rundown of their advantages and how they function:

Azelaic acid: This is a naturally occurring acid that is present in grains like rye, barley, and wheat. Acne, rosacea, and hyperpigmentation can all be effectively treated thanks to their antibacterial and anti-inflammatory characteristics. Additionally, azelaic acid aids in pore-clearing and skin exfoliation for a smoother, more radiant complexion.

BHA (beta hydroxy acid): A specific kind of exfoliating acid, it penetrates the skin's pores and dissolves the "glue" holding dead skin cells together. BHA can aid in pore-clearing and irritation reduction, which makes it particularly helpful for treating acne and blackheads.

Azelaic acid and BHA can support clear, beautiful skin when used in combination. BHA aids in pore cleaning and irritation reduction, while azelaic acid helps exfoliate and brighten the skin. These two substances working together can be very beneficial for treating acne-prone or clogged skin.

What is Azelaic Acid and How it Benefits the Skin?

Azelaic acid is a dicarboxylic acid that occurs naturally and is formed from cereals like wheat, barley, and rye. It has been used for many years to treat hyperpigmentation, rosacea, and acne. There are various advantages to azelaic acid for the skin, including:

  1. Reducing inflammation: The anti-inflammatory properties of azelaic acid contribute to the reduction of skin redness and swelling.

  2. Fighting acne: The antibacterial properties of azelaic acid can aid in the destruction of the bacteria that cause acne.

  3. Exfoliating the skin: Azelaic acid can aid in exfoliating the skin by clearing clogged pores and removing dead skin cells.

  4. Reducing hyperpigmentation: Azelaic acid can help to reduce melanin production in the skin, resulting in less hyperpigmentation and dark spots. 

  5. Brightening the skin: Azelaic acid can help brighten the skin, making it appear more radiant and youthful.

What is BHA and How it Benefits the Skin?

The chemical exfoliant known as BHA, or beta hydroxy acid, is frequently used in skincare products. Salicylic acid, which comes from willow bark, is the BHA that is used in skincare the most frequently. In order to remove sebum and dead skin cells that might block pores and cause acne and blackheads, BHA penetrates deeply into the skin's pores. Additionally, BHA contains anti-inflammatory qualities that may lessen skin inflammation and redness.

Two active ingredients—azelaic acid and BHA—have been shown to improve the skin in complementary ways. It has been shown that azelaic acid has anti-inflammatory and antibacterial effects, making it useful for treating hyperpigmentation, rosacea, and acne. BHA, commonly referred to as salicylic acid, is a beta-hydroxy acid that effectively treats acne and blackheads by exfoliating the skin and clearing clogged pores.

Combining these components can help to cleanse and brighten the skin, lessen inflammation, and enhance the general texture of the skin. A variety of skin issues, including acne, rosacea, hyperpigmentation, and uneven skin texture, have been successfully treated using azelaic acid and BHA in combination.

How to Use Azelaic Acid and BHA Combination in Your Skincare Routine

If you want to incorporate azelaic acid and BHA into your skincare routine, here are some tips to follow:

  1. Start slowly: Start with a low concentration and only introduce one product at a time. Use it sometimes during the week to see how your skin responds.

  2. Cleanse your face: Begin by cleansing your face with a gentle cleanser to remove any dirt, oil, or makeup.

  3. Apply BHA: Apply your BHA product (such as a toner or serum) to your face and allow it to absorb for a few minutes.

  4. Apply azelaic acid to your skin: Apply a small amount of azelaic acid to your face and massage it in.

  5. Moisturize: After cleansing, apply a moisturizer to lock in moisture.

  6. Sun protection: Always use broad-spectrum sunscreen to shield your skin from UV rays.

  7. Consider using them at different times of the day: BHA should be used at night because it can make your skin more sensitive to sunlight. Azelaic acid can be used both day and night.

It's important to note that not everyone's skin reacts the same way, so patch-testing new products before using them all over your face is always a good idea. If you experience any irritation, redness, or discomfort, discontinue use and consult a dermatologist.

How to Incorporate Azelaic Acid and BHA Combination into Your Daily Skincare Routine

Precautions and Tips for Using Azelaic Acid and BHA Combination

Both azelaic acid and BHA (beta hydroxy acid) are widely used compounds in skincare products and are useful for treating a range of skin issues, including acne, hyperpigmentation, and uneven skin texture. They can offer even more advantages when combined, but it's crucial to utilize them correctly to prevent any negative effects. The followings are some warnings and suggestions for using azelaic acid and BHA together:

  1. If you're new to using azelaic acid or BHA, begin with a lower concentration and use it once a week to see how your skin reacts. As your skin adjusts, gradually increase the frequency and concentration.

  2. Azelaic acid and BHA work best when used at different times of the day. Azelaic acid is most effective in the morning, while BHA is most effective at night.

  3. Always patch-test new products before applying them to your entire face. Wait 24 hours after applying a small amount of the product to your inner wrist or behind your ear to see if any adverse reactions occur.

  4. Azelaic acid and BHA both have the potential to increase your skin's sensitivity to the sun. Wear sunscreen. During the day, be sure to use broad-spectrum sunscreen with at least SPF 30.

  5. Azelaic acid and BHA should not be combined with other active ingredients, such as retinoids or vitamin C, as this may result in sensitivity and rashes.

  6. Always adhere to the directions on the product's label and use the smallest amount suggested. Utilizing too many of these ingredients can result in dryness and irritation.

  7. Be patient and consistent with your use of azelaic acid and BHA because results might not be seen right away. To fully experience the advantages, it can take a few weeks or even months.

You can securely and effectively combine azelaic acid with BHA to enhance the general well-being and beauty of your skin by adhering to these precautions and suggestions.
