Understanding Your Hair: Texture, Porosity, and Type

In order to create a hair care routine that meets your hair's specific needs, it is essential to understand the texture, porosity, and type of your hair. Here's a breakdown of each factor:

  1. Hair Texture: The thickness or thinness of each individual strand of your hair is referred to as its texture. It may be coarse, medium, or fine. Fine hair is brittle and easily damaged, whereas coarse hair is more resilient but may be more difficult to style. You can select products and styling tools that are best for your hair by being aware of the texture of your hair.

  2. Hair porosity: The capacity of your hair to absorb and hold moisture is referred to as porosity. Hair can have low, normal, or high porosity. Low-porosity hair has tightly packed cuticles, making it difficult for moisture to penetrate the hair shaft.  Normal porosity hair has a balanced level of moisture absorption, and high porosity hair has open cuticles, which can make it challenging for hair to retain moisture. Understanding your hair's porosity can assist you in selecting the best products and ingredients for your hair.

  3. Hair Type: The shape of your hair strands and the pattern they form are referred to as your hair type. Straight, wavy, curly, and coily hair are the four main hair types. Each hair type presents its own set of challenges and necessitates a different approach to care. Understanding your hair type can assist you in selecting the appropriate products and styling tools to achieve the desired look.

You can perform a simple hair analysis to determine the texture, porosity, and type of your hair. Examine a single strand of hair to determine its texture: fine, medium, or coarse. A simple water test can be used to determine hair porosity by placing a strand of hair in a bowl of water. If it sinks quickly, your hair has high porosity. If it takes some time to sink, your hair has normal porosity. If it floats, your hair has low porosity. Examine the shape of your hair strands and the pattern they form when dry to determine your hair type. For a more precise analysis, you could also talk to a hairstylist.

You can choose the best products and ingredients to keep your hair moisturized and healthy by being aware of the porosity of your hair.

  3. The type of your hair determines how much moisture it requires, how much styling it can withstand, and how frequently you should wash it. Understanding your hair type can assist you in selecting the appropriate products and styling techniques to achieve the desired look.

  2. Blood circulation: A healthy scalp promotes blood circulation, which is necessary for delivering nutrients to the hair follicles.  Hair follicles can produce healthy hair if they receive enough nutrients.

  3. Sebum production: Sebum is a natural oil produced by the scalp that moisturizes and protects the hair. If the scalp produces too much or too little sebum, the result can be dry or oily hair, which can stifle hair growth.

  4. Scalp conditions: Scalp conditions like dandruff, psoriasis, or eczema can affect hair growth. These conditions can cause inflammation, itching, and flaking, which can damage the hair follicles and affect hair growth.

  5. Hair follicle health: Healthy hair growth starts with healthy hair follicles. Hair follicles can be damaged by factors such as stress, pollution, or chemical treatments. A healthy scalp can aid in the protection of hair follicles and the promotion of healthy hair growth.

Scalp health is critical for healthy hair growth because the scalp serves as the foundation for our hair.  A healthy scalp promotes blood circulation, sebum production, and protects hair follicles from damage.
