Occlusives are an important part of skincare, and anyone trying to create a successful skincare routine has to grasp their function and significance. Occlusives are a class of chemicals used in skincare products that form a barrier on the skin's surface, keeping moisture in and limiting water loss. This post will demystify occlusives by outlining their purpose and highlighting their benefits for skincare.
What Are Occlusives? An In-depth Look
Occlusives are a class of chemicals or products used in skincare that work by covering the skin's surface in a barrier of protection. This barrier protects the skin from outside irritants, retains hydration, and stops water loss. Let's examine the qualities, varieties, and applications of occlusives in skincare in this blog post to obtain a better grasp of them. You can choose from a range of occlusive products to include in your skincare routine, based on your skin type and preferences, to keep your skin moisturised and healthy.
Understanding the Basics of Occlusives
Developing a successful skincare regimen requires a solid understanding of occlusives. Occlusives are substances or goods that create a barrier of defence across the skin's surface to assist in keeping moisture in, stopping water loss, and shielding the skin from irritants outside.  You may design a skincare routine that encourages healthy, well-hydrated skin by learning the fundamentals of occlusives and choosing the best products for your skin type.

When to Use Occlusives: Timing Matters
The optimal timing for occlusives in your skincare regimen is important because it influences the products' efficacy and advantages. Here are some important factors to consider when incorporating occlusives into your skincare routine:
Routine for the Evening/Night
: Occlusives are commonly used in the evening or nighttime skincare routine for a variety of reasons, including improved moisture retention, a heavier texture, and protection while sleeping.
As the Final Step
: After cleansing,
using a moisturiser
, and applying treatment products (such as serums or treatments), occlusives should normally be the last step in your routine.
Occasional Use
: Although their weight may not be appropriate for all skin types or circumstances, occlusives are not always necessary for everyday use. Depending on your skin's needs and the climate, you may use occlusives more frequently during dry or cold seasons and less frequently during hot and humid weather.
Targeted Areas
: Occlusives can also be used on specific areas of concern. Using an occlusive lip balm, for example, is an effective way to prevent chapped lips. Similarly, if you have extremely dry patches on your face or body, you can apply occlusives only to those areas rather than all over.
Personal Preference
: In the end, personal preference can influence the timing of occlusive use. Some people's skin may benefit from nightly occlusive use, while others may need to use them less frequently. Experimenting with timing can assist you in determining what is best for your skin and lifestyle.
Recognizing the Need for Occlusives in Your Routine
Recognising the need for occlusives in your skincare routine is essential for maintaining skin health and addressing specific concerns. Here are some signs and situations that indicate you may benefit from incorporating occlusives into your skincare routine:
Dry or dehydrated skin
: You could require occlusives if you frequently experience dry, tight, or flaky skin, especially after cleansing. These items can aid in retaining moisture and halting water loss, keeping your skin hydrated.
Skin that is sensitive or irritated
: Let us say that redness, sensitivity, or irritation of the skin are common. In this case, occlusives can act as a
protective barrier
, reducing moisture loss and preventing external irritants from penetrating the skin.
Cold or dry climate
: Your skin is more prone to dehydration in harsh weather conditions, such as cold, dry winters or windy environments. Occlusives protect your skin by acting as a barrier against these external stressors.
Following the use of drying or exfoliating products
:
If your skincare routine includes products that can be drying or exfoliating, such as retinoids, alpha hydroxy acids (AHAs), or beta hydroxy acids (BHAs), occlusives can help mitigate dryness and peeling by sealing in moisture.
Lips chapped
: If your lips are frequently chapped or dry, a lip balm with occlusive properties can be extremely beneficial. It helps to keep your lips soft and supple by preventing moisture loss.
Eczema or psoriasis
: If you have skin conditions like eczema or psoriasis, which frequently involve a compromised skin barrier, occlusives can help repair and protect the skin by reducing water loss and preventing further irritation.
Healing tattoos or wounds
:
When you have a fresh tattoo, wound, or healing incision, using an occlusive product can promote healing by creating a protective barrier and keeping the area moist.
Preventing water loss at bedtime
: Transepidermal water loss (TEWL) is higher at night, making the skin more susceptible to dehydration. Including an occlusive in your evening routine can help keep your skin moist while you sleep.
Locking in the benefits of active ingredients
: If you use active ingredients like retinoids or antioxidants in your routine, occlusives can help these actives work more effectively by sealing them onto the skin.
Personal preference
:
Some people enjoy the sensation of occlusive products, while others discover that they improve the overall texture and appearance of their skin. If you enjoy the feel of occlusives and notice a difference in your skin, that is a good reason to incorporate them into your regimen.
It is important to note that, while occlusives can be beneficial for many people, they may not be appropriate for all skin types. Heavy occlusive products may make acne-prone or extremely oily skin types' problems worse. Thus, the secret to figuring out whether occlusives should be a part of your skincare routine is to understand your skin's unique needs and how it reacts to various products.
Best Times to Apply Occlusive Products
The best times to use occlusive products in your skincare routine are determined by your specific needs and preferences. However, there are a few common scenarios and recommendations for effective use of occlusives:
Nighttime Routine
: One of the most common and effective times to use occlusives is at night. Sleep increases the activity of your skin's natural repair and regeneration processes. Applying an occlusive at night helps to lock in moisture, prevent transepidermal water loss (TEWL), and promote hydration while you sleep.
Following hydrating serums and moisturisers
:
Occlusives should be used as the final step in your skincare routine. After cleansing, applying any treatment products (e.g., serums or retinoids), and moisturising, apply your occlusive product to lock in the benefits of the previous steps.
In dry or stormy weather
: In extremely dry, cold, or windy climates, applying an occlusive before going outside can provide a protective barrier against environmental stressors, assisting in moisture retention and skin comfort.
Following the use of exfoliating or drying products
: If you use products that are drying or exfoliating, such as retinoids, alpha hydroxy acids (AHAs), or beta hydroxy acids (BHAs), applying an occlusive afterward can help to reduce dryness and peeling. This is especially beneficial during the adjustment period when your skin is becoming accustomed to such products.
Concerns about specific issues
: If you have specific concerns, such as dry or chapped lips, eczema, or sensitive skin, you can apply occlusive products to the affected areas as needed, day or night.
Aftercare for Tattoos
:
Using an occlusive ointment can help protect, moisturise, and speed up the healing process when taking care of a newly applied tattoo. Before going to bed and as needed for dry or sensitive skin, adhere to the aftercare instructions that your tattoo artist has given you.
Keep in mind that your entire skincare regimen should be customised to your tastes and concerns, and that the application of occlusives should be dependent on the unique demands of your skin. The product formulation should also be taken into account. Certain occlusives are heavier and should be used at night, while others are lighter and can be used throughout the day. Always be aware of how different products affect your skin, and modify your routine accordingly.
Seasonal Skincare: When Occlusives Shine
During specific seasons or climatic conditions, occlusives might be especially beneficial, as your skin is more susceptible to dryness and dehydration. Occlusives can shine in various seasons, as follows:
Winter
:
Winter is a season that can deplete your skin of moisture due to its cold, dry air and interior heating. Occlusives are particularly useful in the winter months because they provide a barrier that keeps moisture in and keeps the skin hydrated. One way to mitigate the impact of the harsh winter climate is to apply occlusives at night.
Fall
: You might observe that your skin gets drier when the summer heat gives way to the crisp autumn air. During this seasonal transition, occlusives can help keep your skin protected and moisturised.
Dry Climates
: If you live in a dry region, your skin is always exposed to low humidity levels, which can lead to dehydration regardless of the season. In these types of situations, occlusives are a year-round solution for keeping moisture in and hydration out.
Indoor Heating
: When interior heating systems are operating in the winter, your home's air can get rather dry. By trapping moisture and maintaining the comfort of your skin, occlusives assist in counteracting the drying effects of artificial heating.
Following sun exposure
: Summer is synonymous with heat and humidity, but it can also cause sunburn and skin dehydration. Applying an occlusive product after sun exposure can soothe and heal the skin while also preventing further moisture loss.
High Altitudes
: When you are at a high altitude, the air is thinner and typically drier, so occlusives can help keep your skin hydrated, which may be compromised by the lower air pressure and humidity.
Extreme Weather
: Extreme weather conditions, whether hot and dry or cold and windy, can be harsh on your skin. Occlusives act as a barrier between your skin and the harsh outdoor elements, keeping it comfortable and hydrated.
Long-Haul Flights
: The air in airplane cabins is infamously dry, and prolonged flights can cause skin dehydration. Especially on long flights, applying an occlusive product before takeoff can help seal in moisture and avoid dry skin.
How to Incorporate Occlusives Effectively
By successfully incorporating occlusives into your skincare regimen, you can fully enjoy their advantages. When incorporating occlusives into your routine, take into account the following steps:
Understand Your Skin Type
: Recognise your skin type and any particular concerns first. Different occlusives might work better on skin types that are combination, oily, dry, or sensitive. It is helpful to know your skin type in order to select the appropriate occlusive products.
Choose the right occlusive product
: Choose an occlusive product that is appropriate for your skin type, issues, and environment. If you have sensitive skin, take into account aspects including the product's texture (such as cream, ointment, or balm), ingredients, and if it is hypoallergenic or fragrance-free.
Cleanse your skin
:
Cleanse your skin
to remove impurities, makeup, and sunscreen before beginning your skincare routine. A clean canvas allows the occlusive product to function properly.
Apply treatment products
: Apply any treatment products, such as serums,
retinoids
, or acne treatments, prior to your occlusive product. Allow these treatments to absorb and perform their functions before sealing them in with an occlusive.
Moisturise
: If necessary, use a moisturiser after applying your treatment products. This helps to hydrate your skin. Occlusives work best when applied on top of a moisturiser because they seal in the moisture provided by the moisturiser.
Use occlusives sparingly
:
When it comes to occlusives, a little goes a long way. You do not need to apply a thick layer to your face. A small amount, evenly distributed, is usually enough to form an effective barrier.
Apply as the final step
:
Occlusives should be applied as the final step in your skincare routine. After applying your treatment products and moisturiser, pat or smooth the occlusive over your face, paying special attention to areas prone to dryness or dehydration.
Using Occlusives at Night
: Occlusives are most effective when used at night. The natural repair processes of your skin are more active while you sleep, and using an occlusive can help lock in moisture and support these processes.
Adapt to Seasonal Variations
: Be adaptable in your skincare routine. Depending on the season and weather, you may require more or less of the occlusive product. In extremely dry or harsh conditions, you may want to use occlusives more frequently.
Treatment for spots
: Spot treatments with occlusives are useful for dry or troublesome regions.  To specifically hydrate and protect these regions, apply a tiny quantity directly on them.
Patch Test
: Conducting a patch test is a recommended procedure to make sure you don't experience any negative responses before introducing a new occlusive product into your regimen, particularly if you have sensitive skin.
Monitor Your Skin
: Keep an eye on how your skin reacts to occlusives. If you have any negative reactions or your skin feels too greasy, reduce the amount of product you use or the frequency with which you use it.
Selecting Suitable Occlusive for Your Skin Type
It's essential to choose an occlusive that suits your skin type if you want to ensure that you get the right amount of protection and hydration without suffering any negative side effects. The following methods can be used to select the best occlusive product for your skin type:
Skin that is dry or dehydrated
: Select heavier occlusive products, like those that contain genuine butter (cocoa, shea), petrolatum, or mineral oil. These provide effective hydration and seal in moisture. Products with additional moisturising ingredients, such as
hyaluronic acid
or glycerin should be considered.
Oily Skin
: Look for occlusive products that are lighter, including those made of silicone or those containing jojoba oil or another lightweight oil. They offer a barrier without becoming overly thick or oily. Gel creams that are water-based and have occlusive qualities may also be effective.
Combination Skin
: Look for occlusive products with a balanced texture, such as creams or lotions. Use a heavier occlusive in drier areas and a lighter one in oilier areas. Customising your application to meet the specific needs of your skin can be beneficial.
Sensitive Skin
:
Select occlusive products with mild chemicals, hypoallergenic formulations, and no scent. Look for occlusives that are calming and won't make sensitive areas worse.
Acne-Prone Skin
: People with acne-prone skin may prefer lighter, non-comedogenic occlusive products. Choose occlusives that will not clog pores or aggravate acne, and make sure to use acne-specific products.
Mature Skin
: Occlusives with hydration and anti-aging properties can benefit mature skin. Look for occlusive products that contain antioxidants, peptides, and other anti-aging ingredients, as well as barrier-forming properties.
Normal Skin
: If your skin is relatively balanced, you have more options when it comes to occlusive products. Creams or lightweight occlusives should work well to keep your skin hydrated.
Consider the Climate and Season
: Make adjustments to your occlusive product based on the climate and season. In drier, colder months or when traveling to arid areas, a heavier occlusive may be required. In humid or hot conditions, lighter occlusive options may be preferable.
Patch Testing
: Before introducing a new occlusive product into your routine, always perform a patch test, especially if you have sensitive or reactive skin. This allows you to ensure that the product does not cause any negative reactions.
Consult a Dermatologist
: If you are unsure about which occlusive product to use for your particular skin type and concerns,
consult a dermatologist
or skincare professional. They can make recommendations based on your specific requirements.
Combining Occlusives with Other Skincare Ingredients
Combining occlusives with other skincare ingredients is a common practise that can improve your skincare routine's overall effectiveness. Here's how to pair occlusives with various types of skincare ingredients:
Hydrating Ingredients (Humectants)
: Occlusives are effective when combined with hydrating ingredients such as hyaluronic acid and glycerin. First, apply a hydrating serum or moisturiser containing these ingredients. Then, using an occlusive product, seal in the moisture. The hydrating ingredients attract and retain water on the skin, while the occlusive seals it in for long-lasting hydration.
Retinoids, AHAs, BHAs, Vitamin C, and other active ingredients
: Before applying occlusives, apply your active treatment products. This enables the active ingredients to penetrate the skin and carry out their functions. After your active products have had a few minutes to absorb, apply your occlusive product. The occlusive will aid in the sealing of the active ingredients, potentially reducing irritation and maximising efficacy.
Antioxidants
: Occlusives can be combined with antioxidants such as
vitamin C
. Apply your antioxidant serum or product first, allowing it to absorb for a few minutes before applying your occlusive. The occlusive seals in hydration and the antioxidant's benefits, which can help preserve the antioxidant's stability and efficacy.
Niacinamide
: Niacinamide is a versatile ingredient that works well with occlusives. Depending on your preferences, you
can apply niacinamide
-containing products before or after your occlusive.  Both methods have the potential to be effective. If you find that applying niacinamide after your occlusive provides additional soothing and barrier-strengthening benefits, this is an appropriate approach.
Sunscreen
: Sunscreen should always be the last step in your morning routine. After applying your skincare products, including occlusives,
apply a broad-spectrum sunscreen
. Check that the sunscreen is suitable for your skin type and preferences.
Prescription Medications
: Consult your healthcare provider or dermatologist about the best order to apply prescription medications such as topical antibiotics, oral acne medications, or other medicated ointments. They can provide specific instructions to ensure that these medications work properly.
Layering with Other Moisturisers
: Some like to stack occlusives on top of other moisturisers to form a moisture sandwich. This method maximises hydration and protection by applying a lighter moisturiser first, then an occlusive.
Personal Preference:
Ultimately, the order in which you use occlusives with other skincare ingredients can depend on your personal preferences and how your skin responds. Pay attention to how your skin looks and feels to determine the most effective routine for you.
Always follow the product recommendations, and if in doubt, consult with a dermatologist or skincare professional. They can provide tailored advice based on your specific skin type and concerns, ensuring that your skincare regimen is both effective and safe.
Dos and Don'ts When Using Occlusive Products
Maintaining skin moisture and protection can be greatly enhanced by incorporating occlusive products into your skincare regimen. Nonetheless, there are a few dos and don'ts to remember to make sure you utilise them safely and effectively:
Dos
:
Patch Test
: Always perform a patch test before using a new occlusive product, especially if you have sensitive or reactive skin. Apply a small amount of the product to a discreet area and wait 24 to 48 hours to see if there are any negative reactions.
Cleanse Your Skin
: Begin your skincare routine by cleansing your skin to remove dirt, makeup, and sunscreen. Occlusives work better when the skin is clean.
Use on Sensitive or Dry Areas
: Apply occlusives to specific problem areas. If you have dry or sensitive skin, apply the occlusive product as needed.
Apply as the Final Step
: In most cases, occlusive products should be applied as the final step in your skincare routine. The occlusive should be the final layer applied after treatment products, moisturisers, and sunscreen to lock in moisture and protect your skin.
Seasonal Adaptation
: Match your occlusive use to the season and climate.  Use heavier occlusives during colder, drier months and lighter ones during warmer, humid months.
Combine with Hydrating Ingredients
: Combine occlusives with hydrating ingredients such as hyaluronic acid or glycerin. Apply hydrating products first to attract moisture, then the occlusive to seal it in.
Use at Night
: The main application of occlusives is in your evening skincare regimen. Their ability to prevent increased transepidermal water loss (TEWL) at night makes them especially useful for preserving your fluid intake while you sleep.
Customise Application
: Depending on the unique requirements of your skin, change the quantity and placement of occlusive application. More or less product may be needed in some areas.
Don'ts:
Do not Use on Unwashed Skin
: Steer clear of using occlusives on dirty or unclean skin. To ensure that the product works well and removes impurities, always wash your face first.
Avoid Using Excessively Heavy Occlusives
: Using too many heavy occlusives on oily or acne-prone skin can clog pores and exacerbate acne. When using these products, use them sparingly and opt for lighter occlusives.
Apply Now Under Sunscreen
: Avoid using occlusives during the daytime when wearing sunscreen. The last thing you should do in the morning is apply sunscreen.
Do not forget patch Testing
: When trying a new occlusive product, do not forget to perform a patch test. It assists you in avoiding adverse reactions and skin sensitivities.
Do not Overdo It
: Using too much occlusive product can make your skin feel greasy and provide no additional benefit. Begin with a small amount and gradually increase as needed.
Do not Rely on Occlusives
: While occlusives are excellent at locking in moisture, they should not be used exclusively for skincare. A well-rounded routine should include cleansing, treatment products, moisturisers, sunscreen, and, if necessary, occlusives.
Do not Share Occlusive Products
: Avoid sharing occlusive products with others to avoid cross-contamination and the spread of bacteria or germs.
Do not Overapply to Oily Areas
: If you have combination skin, avoid applying occlusives to oily areas unless absolutely necessary. Use them on the driest parts of your face.
