How Terpenes Contribute to Skin Health

Terpenes, which are found in many plants, can benefit skin health by having anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and antimicrobial properties. The following are some ways that terpenes can improve skin health: 

  1. Anti-Inflammatory Properties: Numerous terpenes have shown anti-inflammatory qualities, including limonene, beta-caryophyllene, and alpha-bisabolol. Prolonged inflammation is a typical component of many skin disorders, such as psoriasis, eczema, and acne. By means of topical application, these terpenes have the potential to mitigate skin redness, swelling, and irritation, thereby fostering a more tranquil and harmonious complexion.

  2. Antioxidant Effects: Terpenes with antioxidant qualities include pinene, limonene, and myrcene. Antioxidants protect the skin from free radical-induced oxidative damage.  Free radicals can cause skin damage, fine wrinkles, and early aging. By using skincare products containing terpenes, you may keep your skin appearing younger and healthier by counteracting these free radicals.

  3. Moisturising and Hydrating: Essential oils frequently contain terpenes like linalool and geraniol, which help skincare products retain their moisture and hydration. They keep the skin hydrated, preventing dryness and maintaining its suppleness and softness.

  4. Antibacterial and Antifungal Properties: Research has demonstrated the antibacterial and antifungal properties of terpenes, including pinene and terpinolene.  This makes them beneficial in skincare products meant to treat bacterial or fungal infections and other skin conditions like acne.

  5. Terpenes: Terpenes can have a calming and soothing effect on the skin. For example, linalool, which has calming qualities, is frequently found in skin care products intended for sensitive or irritated skin.  These terpenes have the ability to lessen skin irritation-related redness and discomfort.

  6. Product Efficacy: Terpenes can act as penetration enhancers, assisting other active ingredients in skincare products to penetrate the skin more effectively. This means that terpene-containing products can deliver their benefits more efficiently, increasing the product's overall efficacy.

  7. Aromatherapy Benefits: Aromatherapy benefits can be obtained by inhaling the aroma of terpenes found in skincare products. Scents like lavender (with linalool), for example, can have a calming effect, reducing stress and promoting relaxation.

While terpenes may have potential benefits for skin health, individual sensitivities and allergies may vary.  Before using terpene-containing skincare products, perform a patch test to ensure that your skin does not react negatively. Additionally, the concentration of terpenes in products and their overall formulation should be carefully considered to ensure safety and effectiveness.

Safety Considerations With Terpene Usage

Although terpenes have many potential uses in skincare and other fields, it's crucial to use them responsibly. Like any active element, terpenes can interact with other substances and cause negative effects. Individual sensitivity levels can also differ. When utilising terpenes, keep the following safety precautions in mind:

  1. Patch Testing: A patch test is required before applying a terpene-infused product to your face or body. Apply a small amount of the product to a small, inconspicuous area of skin and leave it on for 24-48 hours.  If you experience redness, itching, irritation, or any other adverse reaction, stop using the product.

  2. Terpene Allergies and Skin Sensitivities: Terpenes can cause allergies or skin sensitivities in some people. If you are allergic to a specific terpene or the plant from which it is derived, avoid products that contain that terpene. Take note of any previous reactions you have had to terpenes.

  3. Terpene Concentration: Terpene concentration varies between products.  High concentrations of certain terpenes can be more potent and potentially irritating to the skin. Start with products with moderate or lower terpene concentrations, especially if you have sensitive skin.

  4. Interactions with Terpenes: Terpenes may interact with other skincare products or medications you are using.  If you use prescription skincare products or medications, consult with a healthcare professional to ensure there are no potential interactions between terpenes and these treatments.

  5. Phototoxicity: Terpenes, particularly those found in citrus essential oils, can make your skin more sensitive to UV radiation, potentially resulting in sunburn. Apply terpene-infused products with caution, especially during sun exposure, and use sunscreen to protect your skin.

  6. Avoid Ingestion: Terpenes are meant for topical use. They should not be consumed because some of them are toxic or harmful if consumed orally. Keep terpene-infused products out of the reach of children and away from their eyes and mouths.

  7. Consult a Professional: Before incorporating terpene-infused products into your routine, it is best to consult with a dermatologist or skincare professional if you have specific skin concerns or conditions. They can offer advice and recommendations based on your specific requirements.

  8. Quality and Brand Selection: Select reputable brands that place a premium on safety and quality. Look for products that have been tested and meet industry standards. Check that the products do not contain any harmful additives or contaminants. 

  9. Pregnancy and Nursing: If you are pregnant or nursing, consult with a healthcare professional before using terpene-infused products, as some terpenes may be unsafe to use during pregnancy or breastfeeding.

  10. If Needed, Stop Using: If using terpene-infused products causes you to feel uncomfortable or have any negative reactions, stop using them right away. You should also get medical help if needed.

By being informed and cautious, you can enjoy the potential benefits of terpenes while minimising the risk of adverse reactions or unwanted side effects. Always prioritise safety and use these products according to your specific skin type and needs.

Unveiling Myths: Clearing Misconceptions About Terpenes

 

Terpenes are intriguing organic molecules that are present in a wide range of plants. They have a wide range of uses, including aromatherapy and cosmetics, and provide a number of advantages. Still, there are a number of false beliefs and misconceptions regarding terpenes. Let's dispel a few of these misunderstandings:

  • Myth: Terpenes are only concerned with aromas. Fact: Terpenes are the molecules that give plants their characteristic fragrances, but they also serve a variety of other purposes. In addition to being important for pollinator attraction and plant defence, terpenes also have therapeutic uses.

  • Myth: Terpenes are bad for you. Fact: When applied as directed in cosmetics, aromatherapy, and other uses, terpenes are generally harmless. However, since everyone has different allergies and sensitivities, it's crucial to do a patch test before using anything with terpenes.

  • Myth: Terpenes are all the same. Fact: Terpenes come in hundreds of varieties, each with special qualities and possible advantages. Some terpenes are anti-inflammatory, while others are soothing, moisturising, or stimulating.

  • Myth: Terpenes can only be found in cannabis. Fact: Terpenes are not exclusive to cannabis; they can be found in a large range of plants. They can be made from a variety of plants, each with a unique aroma and potential advantages, including lavender, citrus, eucalyptus, and many others.

  • Myth: Terpenes Are Safe for Every Type of Skin. Fact: Terpenes can have different effects on different skin types. Some may be better suited to sensitive skin, while others may be better suited to oily or acne-prone skin. Patch testing and consultation with a skincare professional are both recommended.

  • Myth: Terpenes are a good substitute for sunscreen.  Fact: Because of their antioxidant qualities, some terpenes may offer some protection from the sun, but they should not take the place of sunscreen. Sunscreen is necessary for UV protection that works.

  • Myth: Terpenes can treat any skin condition. Fact: Terpenes are not miraculous treatments, but they can offer helpful support for a number of skin conditions. In a holistic approach, skincare takes into account a number of variables, such as lifestyle, nutrition, and hydration.

  • Myth: It is always best to use terpenes in their purest form. Fact: When expertly formulated skincare products are used, terpenes can be particularly effective. Terpene formulation and concentration are important, and they often work better in synergy with other ingredients.

  • Myth: Terpenes have no side effects. Fact: Like any active component, terpenes may cause allergic reactions or cause side effects in certain people. It is essential to perform patch tests and be aware of potential sensitivities.

  • Myth: The discovery of terpenes is recent. Fact: For centuries, people have utilised terpenes for their aromatic and medicinal qualities. Terpene-containing essential oils have long been utilised in aromatherapy and conventional medicine.
