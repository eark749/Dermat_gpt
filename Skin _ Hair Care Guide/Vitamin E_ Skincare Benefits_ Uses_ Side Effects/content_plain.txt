Vitamin E Oral Supplements
Vitamin E is a fat-soluble vitamin that acts as an antioxidant and provides numerous health benefits. It can be taken as a dietary supplement or found in foods such as nuts, seeds, and vegetable oil.
Vitamin E capsules or tablets are commonly sold as oral supplements. The recommended daily intake of vitamin E for adults is 15 milligrammes (or 22.4 international units), with a maximum safe intake of 1,000 milligrammes.
Some possible advantages of taking vitamin E supplements include:
Vitamin E acts as an antioxidant, preventing free radical damage to cells.
Vitamin E may help to maintain a healthy immune system.
Vitamin E may benefit skin health by lowering oxidative stress and inflammation.
Vitamin E may lower the risk of heart disease by inhibiting the oxidation of LDL ("bad") cholesterol.
Vitamin E may help prevent age-related macular degeneration, one of the leading causes of vision loss.
It's important to note that, while vitamin E supplements are generally considered safe for the majority of people, high doses can cause nausea, diarrhoea, and an increased risk of bleeding. Before beginning any vitamin E supplementation, consult your doctor to determine the proper dosage and whether it is right for you.

Niacinamide
: Niacinamide, also known as vitamin B3, has numerous skin benefits, including improved skin barrier function, reduced inflammation, and regulation of oil production. When combined with vitamin E, niacinamide can enhance these effects while also promoting overall skin health and resilience.
Essential Fatty Acids
: Vitamin E is frequently combined with essential fatty acids, such as linoleic acid or omega-3 fatty acids, to provide complete nourishment and hydration to the skin. Sunscreen
: Vitamin E is frequently used in sunscreen formulations because of its ability to neutralise free radicals produced by UV radiation and protect the skin from sun damage. When combined with broad-spectrum UV filters like zinc oxide or titanium dioxide, vitamin E can improve sunscreen's photoprotective properties and reduce the risk of sunburn and premature ageing.

Vitamin E for Acne Scars
Vitamin E's antioxidant and skin-regenerative properties make it a popular treatment for acne scars. However, its efficacy in treating acne scars is debatable, with conflicting research findings. Here's everything you need to know about using vitamin E for acne scars:
Vitamin E has strong antioxidant properties that help neutralise free radicals, which can cause skin damage and inflammation. By protecting the skin from oxidative stress, vitamin E may help prevent further damage to the skin and support the healing process.
Vitamin E is also known for its moisturising properties, which can help improve the overall appearance of the skin and may soften scars. Keeping the skin hydrated can help reduce the appearance of scars and promote a smoother texture.
While some anecdotal reports and small-scale studies suggest that vitamin E may help reduce the appearance of acne scars, scientific evidence is inconclusive. Some studies have found that topically applied vitamin E may be ineffective and, in some cases, worsen scarring or cause skin irritation.
Pure vitamin E oil is thick and can clog pores, exacerbating or causing new breakouts. Furthermore, some people may be allergic to vitamin E or develop skin irritation when it is applied topically.
Vitamin E is frequently used in conjunction with other treatments to improve acne scar management, such as retinoids, alpha hydroxy acids (AHAs), or silicone gel sheets. These treatments can help stimulate collagen production, exfoliate dead skin cells, and improve skin texture, in addition to vitamin E's benefits.
Before using vitamin E to treat acne scars, consult with a dermatologist. They can evaluate your skin condition, discuss treatment options, and recommend the most appropriate approach based on your individual needs and skin type.
Vitamin E for Sun Protection
While vitamin E has antioxidant properties that can help protect the skin from sun damage, it should not be used as the only source of sun protection. Here's how vitamin E helps with sun protection and what to keep in mind:
Vitamin E is a powerful antioxidant that helps neutralise free radicals produced by the sun's UV radiation. These free radicals can cause oxidative stress, resulting in premature ageing, sunburn, and a higher risk of skin cancer. By scavenging free radicals, vitamin E can help minimize sun damage and its associated effects on the skin.
Some research suggests that combining vitamin E with sunscreen may improve its photoprotective properties. Vitamin E can help stabilise sunscreen ingredients and increase their effectiveness in protecting the skin from UV rays. Furthermore, vitamin E's antioxidant properties can enhance the UV-filtering action of sunscreens, providing additional protection against sun-induced skin damage.
Vitamin E has moisturising properties that can hydrate the skin and repair UV-induced damage. While it does not block UV rays like sunscreen, it does support the skin's natural barrier function and promotes skin repair processes, which helps to mitigate the effects of sun exposure.
Despite its benefits, vitamin E should not be used as an alternative to sunscreen. Sunscreen is the most effective form of UV radiation protection and should be used every day, regardless of weather or skin type. Look for broad-spectrum sunscreens that protect against both UVA and UVB rays, and apply liberally to all exposed skin areas, such as the face, neck, and hands.
To effectively protect your skin from sun damage, follow a comprehensive sun protection routine that includes sunscreen, protective clothing, sunglasses, and seeking shade during peak sunlight hours. While vitamin E can provide additional antioxidant protection, it should be combined with other sun protection measures for best results.
