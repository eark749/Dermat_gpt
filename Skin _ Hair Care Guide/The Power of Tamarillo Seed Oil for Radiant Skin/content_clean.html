The Science Behind Tamarillo Seed Oil

Tamarillo seed oil, obtained from the seeds of the tamarillo fruit (Solanum betaceum), is gaining popularity in skincare because of its outstanding nutrient profile and possible health advantages, which have been confirmed by scientific studies. Here's a more detailed look at the science behind tamarillo seed oil:

  • Tamarillo seed oil is high in important fatty acids, particularly linoleic (omega-6) and alpha-linolenic (omega-3). These fatty acids are essential for protecting the skin's lipid barrier, retaining moisture, and increasing skin suppleness and elasticity.

  • It includes high quantities of antioxidants, including vitamin E (tocopherols and tocotrienols). Antioxidants shield the skin from oxidative stress caused by free radicals produced by UV radiation, pollution, and other environmental factors. This protection helps to prevent premature aging symptoms like fine lines, wrinkles, and age spots.

  • Tamarillo seed oil contains phytosterols, which are plant-derived chemicals that have anti-inflammatory properties. Phytosterols soothe and relax irritated skin, making the oil suitable for eczema, dermatitis, and sensitive skin.

Comparing Tamarillo Seed Oil with Other Natural Oils

Comparing tamarillo seed oil to other natural oils might help you better grasp its unique qualities and benefits in skincare. Here's a comparison of some popular natural oils:
       1. Tamarillo Seed Oil vs. Argan Oil

  • Tamarillo seed oil is derived from the seeds of the tamarillo fruit (Solanum betaceum), whereas argan oil is obtained from the kernels of the argan tree (Argania spinosa).

  • Tamarillo seed oil is high in linoleic acid (omega-6) and alpha-linolenic acid (omega-3), which provide great moisture and skin barrier support. Argan oil is high in oleic acid (omega-9) and contains linoleic acid, making it nutritious and moisturizing to the skin.

  • Both oils include antioxidants, with argan oil particularly high in vitamin E. Tamarillo seed oil also contains vitamin E, which provides comparable antioxidant protection against free radicals and environmental damage.

  • Tamarillo seed oil contains phytosterols, which have anti-inflammatory qualities and are good for calming sensitive or irritated skin. Argan oil is valued for its ability to moisturize and soften the skin, making it ideal for dry or aging skin.

     2. Tamarillo Seed Oil vs. Rosehip Seed Oil

  • Tamarillo seed oil is derived from the tamarillo fruit, whereas rosehip seed oil is taken from rosehip fruit seeds (Rosa canina).

  • Tamarillo seed oil has a balanced ratio of omega-6 and omega-3 fatty acids, whereas rosehip seed oil is strong in both linoleic acid (omega-6) and linolenic acid (omega-3). Rosehip oil is known for its skin regeneration capabilities, which aid in improving skin tone and texture.

  • Both oils are abundant in antioxidants, but rosehip seed oil is especially high in vitamin C, which brightens the skin and promotes collagen synthesis. Tamarillo seed oil contains vitamin E, which has similar antioxidant advantages for protecting the skin from free radicals.

  • Tamarillo seed oil is good for moisturizing, reducing inflammation, and strengthening the skin barrier. Rosehip seed oil is widely utilized for its anti-aging properties, scar reduction, and general skin vibrancy.

     3. Tamarillo Seed Oil vs. Jojoba Oil

  • Tamarillo seed oil is obtained from tamarillo fruit seeds, whereas jojoba oil is taken from jojoba plant seeds (Simmondsia Chinensis).

  • Tamarillo seed oil contains more omega-3 fatty acids than jojoba oil, which is primarily constituted of monounsaturated fats (such as oleic acid) and is structurally comparable to human sebum.

  • Both oils include vitamin E, and jojoba oil has extra antioxidant benefits. Tamarillo seed oil's antioxidants help to prevent aging and protect the skin.

  • Jojoba oil is well-known for its moisturizing characteristics and ability to regulate oil production, making it ideal for all skin types, particularly oily and acne-prone skin. Tamarillo seed oil has a balanced fatty acid profile and soothing characteristics that help to keep sensitive skin hydrated and quiet.

DIY Skincare Recipes with Tamarillo Seed Oil

Using tamarillo seed oil in DIY skincare recipes can enhance skin health by utilizing its moisturizing, antioxidant, and calming characteristics. Here are some easy DIY recipes to try at home:

Mix tamarillo seed oil, rosehip seed oil, and argan oil in a dark glass dropper bottle. Add lavender essential oil for fragrance and additional skin benefits if desired.   • Overnight Facial Oil Blend: Ingredients are 1 tablespoon tamarillo seed oil, 1 tablespoon jojoba oil, and 3-4 drops chamomile essential oil (optional for extra soothing properties). Mix tamarillo seed oil and jojoba oil in a small dropper bottle. Add chamomile essential oil for its calming effects if desired. Close the bottle and shake gently to blend the oils. Best Practices for Storing Tamarillo Seed Oil

To keep tamarillo seed oil fresh, potent, and useful for skincare, it must be stored properly. Here are some good procedures for keeping tamarillo seed oil:

  1. Choose the Right Container: Tamarillo seed oil should be kept in a dark glass bottle, preferably amber or cobalt blue. This sort of bottle protects the oil from light, which can promote oxidation and reduce its quality. To avoid oxidation, ensure that the bottle has a tight-sealing top or dropper.

  2. Store in a Cool, Dark Place: Keep tamarillo seed oil in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and heat sources. Excessive heat can damage and shorten the oil's shelf life. Keep the storage space dry, as moisture can degrade the quality of the oil over time.

  3. Refrigeration (Optional): Although not required, refrigerating tamarillo seed oil can extend its shelf life. If you live in a warm environment or don't use the oil often, chilling can help keep it fresh. Allow chilled tamarillo seed oil to reach room temperature on its own. Avoid heating it quickly, as this can alter its qualities.

  4. Avoid Exposure to Air and Light: To avoid oxidation, keep the bottle well-packed when not in use. Exposure to air can cause the oil to become rancid more quickly.

  5. Check for Spoilage: Periodically inspect the oil for any changes in smell or color. If it develops a sour or unpleasant odor, or if the color darkens dramatically, it may be time to replace it.

  6. Shelf Life: Tamarillo seed oil has a shelf life of around 1-2 years when stored properly. Use it within this timeframe to ensure maximum freshness and effectiveness.
