  2. Antioxidant Defence: Vitamin C contributes to overall health and well-being by combating oxidative stress. It protects cells, tissues, and organs from free radical damage.

Hair Benefits:

Dietary Sources of Vitamin C

There are numerous vitamin C dietary sources that you can include in your daily diet. Here are some examples of vitamin C-rich foods:

  1. Citrus Fruits: Oranges, grapefruits, lemons, and limes are all high in vitamin C. Eat them whole or squeeze their juice into your meals and drinks to add flavour.

  2. Berries: Strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, and blackberries are high in vitamin C and delicious. They can be eaten raw or added to smoothies, yoghourt, or salads.

  3. Kiwi: Kiwi is a tropical fruit with a high vitamin C content. It can be eaten as a snack or added to fruit salads and smoothies.

  4. Pineapple: This tropical fruit is high in vitamin C and also contains bromelain, an enzyme that aids digestion. Pineapple chunks can be eaten as a snack or added to stir-fries and fruit salads.

  5. Papaya: Another vitamin C-rich tropical fruit is papaya. Eat it on its own, in smoothies, or in fruit salads and salsas.

  6. Mango: Mangoes are not only sweet and delicious, but they are also high in vitamin C. You can eat them as a snack, add them to smoothies, or use them in salads and desserts.

  7. Bell peppers: Red, yellow, and green bell peppers are high in vitamin C. Toss them into stir-fries, salads, and roasted vegetable dishes to add flavour and nutrients. 

  8. Broccoli: This cruciferous vegetable is high in fibre as well as vitamin C. It's delicious steamed, roasted, or in stir-fries and salads.

  9. Leafy Greens: Spinach, kale, and Swiss chard are all vitamin C-rich leafy greens. Serve as a side dish in salads or smoothies, or sauté them.

  10. Tomatoes: Tomatoes are not only delicious but also high in vitamin C. Fresh in salads and sandwiches, or cooked in sauces and soups.

Incorporating these vitamin C-rich foods into your diet can help ensure that you're getting an adequate intake of this essential nutrient. Remember that vitamin C is water-soluble, so it's best to consume these foods raw or lightly cooked to preserve their vitamin C content.

Choosing the Right Vitamin C Product for Your Needs

Each form has advantages and levels of stability. L-ascorbic acid is the most potent but also the least stable, whereas sodium ascorbyl phosphate is more stable but has a slightly lower potency. When selecting a vitamin C form, keep your skin or hair concerns and sensitivities in mind.

  2. Concentration: Look for vitamin C products with a high concentration. Skincare products typically contain 10% to 20% vitamin C, whereas haircare products may contain varying amounts. Higher concentrations may be better suited to specific concerns, such as hyperpigmentation, whereas lower concentrations are generally well tolerated for daily use.

  3. Stability: Vitamin C is light, heat, and air-sensitive, which can reduce its efficacy. To ensure maximum potency and effectiveness, look for products with stable formulations, such as airless pumps, opaque packaging, or stabilised vitamin C derivatives.

  4. Additional Ingredients: Take into account the product's additional ingredients. Some products combine vitamin C with additional beneficial components to increase the effects on your skin or hair, such as hyaluronic acid, antioxidants, or plant extracts.

Tips for Incorporating Vitamin C Products into Your Routine

Incorporating vitamin C products into your skincare routine can help you get the most out of them. Here are some pointers for incorporating vitamin C products effectively:

  1. Cleanse and tone: Begin by washing your face with a gentle cleanser appropriate for your skin type. Use a toner to balance the pH of your skin and prepare it for better product absorption.

  2. Patch Test: Perform a patch test on a small area of your skin before applying a vitamin C product all over your face. This guarantees that the product has no negative effects and is well tolerated by your skin.

  3. Apply to Freshly Cleansed Skin: Apply your vitamin C product to freshly cleansed and dried skin for maximum absorption. This allows the product to penetrate more effectively, resulting in better results.

  4. Start with Lower Concentrations: If you're new to using vitamin C, start with lower concentrations and gradually increase as your skin adjusts. Higher concentrations can be more effective, but they can also be more irritating, especially on sensitive skin. 

  5. Use in the Morning: Vitamin C is well-known for its antioxidant properties and ability to protect the skin from free radicals and environmental damage. It is recommended that vitamin C products be taken in the morning to reap the benefits of this protective effect throughout the day.

  6. Layering Order: If you use multiple skin care products, layer your vitamin C product correctly.  Vitamin C should be applied after cleansing and toning but before moisturisers, sunscreens, or other treatments.

  7. Apply SPF after:  It's essential to apply a broad-spectrum sunscreen with an SPF of 30 or higher after vitamin C because it may make your skin more sensitive to the sun. This protects your skin from damaging UV rays and lessens the risk of sunburn.

  8. Be Consistent: Consistency is key when using products containing vitamin C. To get the best results, include them in your daily skincare routine and use them frequently. The tone, texture, and general appearance of the skin can all be seen to improve over time with continued use.

  9. Store correctly: Light, air, and heat can cause vitamin C to lose some of its effectiveness. Keep your vitamin C products out of direct sunlight in a cool, dark location. Observe the product manufacturer's recommended storage guidelines.

  10. Follow Up on Results: After incorporating vitamin C products into your routine, keep an eye out for any changes in your skin. Keep track of any improvements in skin tone, texture, brightness, and any targeted concerns. Adjust the frequency or concentration of the product based on your skin's response.

Keep in mind that individual results may vary, and pay attention to your skin's demands. If you encounter any discomfort or itchiness, stop using it and seek the counsel of a specialist.
