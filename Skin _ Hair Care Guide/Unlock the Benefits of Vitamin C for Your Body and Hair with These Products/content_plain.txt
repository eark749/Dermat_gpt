What is Vitamin C and How Does It Benefit the Skin?
Ascorbic acid, another name for vitamin C, is a water-soluble vitamin that is necessary for many biological processes. Numerous fruits and vegetables naturally contain it, with citrus fruits like oranges and lemons being well-known examples. Vitamin C has numerous skin-care advantages, including the following:
Strong Antioxidant
: Vitamin C is a powerful antioxidant that works to shield the skin from oxidative stress, UV radiation, pollution, and other environmental factors that can cause free radical damage. Vitamin C prevents premature ageing, including fine lines, wrinkles, and discoloration by scavenging free radicals.
Collagen Production
: Collagen, a protein that gives skin its firmness and elasticity, is produced with the help of vitamin C. Aging causes a decrease in collagen synthesis, which causes sagging and wrinkles. Vitamin C supports collagen production, which keeps skin looking young and supple.
Evening out Skin Tone and Brightening
: Vitamin C prevents the production of melanin, the pigment that causes hyperpigmentation and dark spots. A brighter and more even complexion can be achieved by regularly using vitamin C to help fade pre-existing dark spots and discoloration.
Protection from UV damage
: Vitamin C can add to the sun protection provided by sunscreen, but it shouldn't take its place of it. It lessens the damaging effects of sun exposure, such as sunburn and photoaging, by assisting in the neutralisation of free radicals produced by UV exposure.
Skin Healing and Repair
: Vitamin C is essential for the skin's healing and repair processes. It aids in wound healing, reduces inflammation, and supports the formation of scar tissue. Additionally, vitamin C can help improve the appearance of acne scars and promote a more even skin texture.
Antioxidant Regeneration
: Other antioxidants in the skin, such as vitamin E, can replenish thanks to vitamin C's special properties. By maximising defence against free radicals and oxidative stress, this combination strengthens the body's total antioxidant defence mechanism.

Dietary Sources of Vitamin C
There are numerous vitamin C dietary sources that you can include in your daily diet. Here are some examples of vitamin C-rich foods:
Citrus Fruits
: Oranges, grapefruits, lemons, and limes are all high in vitamin C. Eat them whole or squeeze their juice into your meals and drinks to add flavour.
Berries
: Strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, and blackberries are high in vitamin C and delicious. They can be eaten raw or added to smoothies, yoghourt, or salads.
Kiwi
: Kiwi is a tropical fruit with a high vitamin C content. It can be eaten as a snack or added to fruit salads and smoothies.
Pineapple
: This tropical fruit is high in vitamin C and also contains bromelain, an enzyme that aids digestion. Pineapple chunks can be eaten as a snack or added to stir-fries and fruit salads.
Papaya
: Another vitamin C-rich tropical fruit is papaya. Eat it on its own, in smoothies, or in fruit salads and salsas.
Mango
: Mangoes are not only sweet and delicious, but they are also high in vitamin C. You can eat them as a snack, add them to smoothies, or use them in salads and desserts.
Bell peppers
: Red, yellow, and green bell peppers are high in vitamin C. Toss them into stir-fries, salads, and roasted vegetable dishes to add flavour and nutrients.
Broccoli
: This cruciferous vegetable is high in fibre as well as vitamin C. It's delicious steamed, roasted, or in stir-fries and salads.
Leafy Greens
: Spinach, kale, and Swiss chard are all vitamin C-rich leafy greens. Serve as a side dish in salads or smoothies, or sauté them.
Tomatoes
: Tomatoes are not only delicious but also high in vitamin C. Fresh in salads and sandwiches, or cooked in sauces and soups.
Incorporating these vitamin C-rich foods into your diet can help ensure that you're getting an adequate intake of this essential nutrient. Remember that vitamin C is water-soluble, so it's best to consume these foods raw or lightly cooked to preserve their vitamin C content.

Choosing the Right Vitamin C Product for Your Needs

The vitamin C form
: Vitamin C is found in a variety of forms in skincare and haircare products, including L-ascorbic acid, ascorbyl palmitate, ascorbic acid, and sodium ascorbyl phosphate. Each form has advantages and levels of stability. L-ascorbic acid is the most potent but also the least stable, whereas sodium ascorbyl phosphate is more stable but has a slightly lower potency. When selecting a vitamin C form, keep your skin or hair concerns and sensitivities in mind.
Concentration
: Look for vitamin C products with a high concentration. Skincare products typically contain 10% to 20% vitamin C, whereas haircare products may contain varying amounts. Stability
: Vitamin C is light, heat, and air-sensitive, which can reduce its efficacy. To ensure maximum potency and effectiveness, look for products with stable formulations, such as airless pumps, opaque packaging, or stabilised vitamin C derivatives.
Additional Ingredients
: Take into account the product's additional ingredients. Some products combine vitamin C with additional beneficial components to increase the effects on your skin or hair, such as hyaluronic acid, antioxidants, or plant extracts.

Brand Reputation

: Apply your vitamin C product to freshly cleansed and dried skin for maximum absorption. This allows the product to penetrate more effectively, resulting in better results.

: If you're new to using vitamin C, start with lower concentrations and gradually increase as your skin adjusts. Higher concentrations can be more effective, but they can also be more irritating, especially on sensitive skin.

: Vitamin C is well-known for its antioxidant properties and ability to protect the skin from free radicals and environmental damage. It is recommended that vitamin C products be taken in the morning to reap the benefits of this protective effect throughout the day.

: If you use multiple skin care products, layer your vitamin C product correctly.  Vitamin C should be applied after cleansing and toning but before moisturisers, sunscreens, or other treatments.
