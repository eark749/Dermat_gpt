#13 Stress and its impact on hair fall

Stress has been shown to have a major impact on hair loss and hair health. Stress-related hair loss is a well-known phenomena that can appear in a variety of ways, including:

  1. Telogen Effluvium: Stress is a common cause of this type of hair loss.  During times of high stress, a greater proportion of hair follicles can be forced into the telogen (resting) phase. This causes significant hair loss after a few months. When the underlying stress is handled, this sort of hair loss is frequently brief and reversible.

  2. Alopecia Areata: Stress can cause or worsen autoimmune disorders such as alopecia areata, which causes hair loss in localised regions on the scalp or elsewhere in the body.

  3. Trichotillomania: Stress and anxiety can cause habits such as hair tugging (trichotillomania), which causes hair loss due to repeated mechanical stress.

The complex hormonal, immune, and physiological reactions that stress triggers include the following:

  1. Hormonal Changes: Stress causes the body to release stress hormones like cortisol by triggering the "fight or flight" response. Hair loss can occur as a result of elevated cortisol levels disrupting the hair growth cycle.

  2. Inflammation: Prolonged stress can cause the body to experience chronic inflammation, which can damage hair follicles and cause hair loss.

  3. Immune System: Stress can deplete the immune system, making the body more vulnerable to autoimmune diseases that target hair follicles.

  4. Blood Flow: Stress can cause blood vessels to tighten, decreasing the delivery of oxygen and nutrients to hair follicles and inhibiting hair growth.

The prevention and mitigation of stress-induced hair loss rely heavily on stress management strategies, which include regular exercise, a balanced diet, regular stress reduction sessions, getting support, and hair care routines. It is important to note that dealing with stress-related hair loss can take some time. After stress management is achieved, it might take several months to observe a discernible improvement in hair growth. It is advisable to see a dermatologist or other healthcare provider for an accurate diagnosis and treatment plan if you are suffering from significant and ongoing hair loss as a result of stress.  They can offer advice on how to handle the tension and the ensuing hair loss.
