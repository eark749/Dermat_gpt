What is Elderberry?
The fruit of the elder tree (Sambucus nigra), a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, is elderberry. The Adoxaceae family includes the elder tree. Elderberries are small, dark purple to blackish berries with a sweet-tart flavor that grows in clusters. The elderberry plant is also known by other names such as elder, elderflower tree, and European elder. Elderberries have a centuries-long history of traditional medical usage. They have been used for their potential health advantages and nutritional worth in numerous civilizations. The berries are high in nutrients such as vitamins (including vitamins C, A, and B6), minerals (such as potassium and iron), and dietary fibre. In addition to being consumed as food in the form of jams, jellies, and syrups, elderberries have been used in herbal remedies for their medicinal properties. They are often processed into elderberry extracts, syrups, tinctures, and supplements.

Precautions and Potential Side Effects of Elderberry Use
There are some precautions and potential side effects to be aware of, especially when using elderberry supplements or extracts, even though elderberry is typically thought to be safe for most people when consumed in moderation as part of a balanced diet. It is important to use caution and adhere to these rules:
Elderberries, raw or unripe
: Elderberries, raw or unripe, contain cyanogenic glycosides, which can cause cyanide to be released when consumed. As a result, it is critical to thoroughly cook elderberries before eating them to ensure their safety.
Allergic reactions
: Some people are allergic to elderberries or plants related to them, such as those in the Sambucus family. If you are allergic to certain foods or plants, it is best to conduct a patch test before using elderberry products topically and to consume elderberry products with caution.
Gastrointestinal issues
: Elderberry consumption may cause mild gastrointestinal discomforts, such as nausea, vomiting, or diarrhoea, in some people. If you experience these symptoms, stop using the product and seek medical attention if necessary.
Medication interactions
: Certain medications, particularly those that affect the immune system or are metabolised by the liver, may interact with elderberry. If you are taking any medications or have underlying health conditions, consult your healthcare provider before using elderberry supplements.
Pregnancy and breastfeeding
: Pregnant or nursing women should use any herbal supplement, including elderberry, with caution.  Before using elderberry products during pregnancy or while breastfeeding, it is critical to consult with a healthcare professional.
Autoimmune conditions
: If you have an autoimmune condition, such as rheumatoid arthritis or multiple sclerosis, you should consult with your doctor before using elderberry products, as they may stimulate the immune system.
Quality and sourcing
: Make certain that the elderberry products you use come from reputable sources and are manufactured to high standards. To ensure products are safe and effective, look for those that have undergone third-party testing and are certified organic.
Avoid overconsumption
: While elderberries have potential health benefits, eating too many of them can be harmful. Follow the dosage instructions and use elderberry products as directed.
Individual variability
: Keep in mind that individual responses to elderberry may vary, and what works well for one person may not work well for another. Pay attention to how your body reacts to elderberry products and adjust your usage accordingly.
Known Side Effects of Elderberry
While most people are safe to consume elderberry in moderation, there are some known side effects and precautions to be aware of:
Gastrointestinal issues
: Elderberry consumption can cause mild gastrointestinal discomfort in some people, such as nausea, vomiting, or diarrhoea. These effects are typically transient and resolve on their own.  If you have persistent or severe gastrointestinal symptoms, stop using elderberry and seek medical attention.
Allergic reactions
: Some people may be allergic to elderberries or other plants in the Sambucus family. Allergic reactions to elderberry can cause skin rashes, itching, swelling, and difficulty breathing. Seek immediate medical attention if you experience any symptoms of an allergic reaction after consuming elderberry.
Cyanide toxicity
: Elderberries, whether raw or unripe, contain cyanogenic glycosides, which can release cyanide when consumed. Cooking elderberries thoroughly ensures their safety and eliminates any risk of cyanide toxicity.
Interactions with medications
: Certain medications, particularly those that affect the immune system or are metabolised by the liver, may interact with elderberry supplements. If you are taking any medications, especially immunosuppressants or drugs that require liver metabolism, talk to your doctor before taking elderberry supplements.
Autoimmune stimulation
: Elderberry's immune-stimulating properties may aggravate symptoms in people with autoimmune diseases.  If you have an autoimmune disorder, you should talk to your doctor before using elderberry products.
Pregnancy and breastfeeding
: Pregnant or nursing women should use herbal supplements, including elderberry, with caution.  Before using elderberry products during pregnancy or while breastfeeding, it is critical to consult with a healthcare professional.
Excessive consumption
: Excessive consumption of elderberries may have negative consequences. Stick to the recommended dosages and avoid overdosing.
