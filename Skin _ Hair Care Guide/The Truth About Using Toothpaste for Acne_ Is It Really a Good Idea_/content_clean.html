Why Do People Believe Toothpaste Can Help Acne?

There are a few reasons why people might think toothpaste can treat acne, despite the lack of any supporting scientific data. Here are some explanations that might apply:

Before turning to specialized acne treatments, people may turn to readily available home remedies when they experience an acne breakout. In these situations, toothpaste might seem like a practical solution. 

  4. Confirmation bias: When people interpret and recall information in a way that supports their preexisting beliefs or expectations, confirmation bias takes place. If someone thinks toothpaste can treat acne, they might selectively recall the times it seemed to work and ignore or forget the times it didn't.

  5. Lack of knowledge or alternatives: People may turn to trying unconventional remedies like toothpaste if there isn't readily available and trustworthy information about how to treat acne.  Before accurate skincare information was widely available, home remedies and folk remedies were frequently used because there were few other options.

Understanding the Ingredients of Toothpaste

Knowing the typical ingredients used in toothpaste formulations will help you better understand the possible effects of toothpaste on the skin. Despite being primarily intended for oral hygiene, toothpaste may contain the following ingredients:

  1. Abrasives: Materials like hydrated silica, calcium carbonate, or baking soda are frequently found in toothpaste. These components aid in cleaning teeth of plaque, stains, and food residue. They might irritate or even cause microtears on the face because they are too harsh for delicate skin there.

  2. Fluoride: By fortifying tooth enamel, fluoride, a vital component of toothpaste, helps guard teeth against decay.  Although fluoride is good for oral health, it can be toxic if ingested or absorbed through the skin in toothpaste at high concentrations.

  3. Surfactants: To create foam and help the product spread, toothpaste contains surfactants like sodium lauryl sulfate (SLS) or sodium laureth sulfate (SLES). Particularly for people with sensitive skin or skin that is prone to acne, these surfactants may be drying and potentially irritating to the skin.

  4. Flavoring agents: To provide a pleasant taste and fresh breath, toothpaste contains various flavoring agents such as spearmint, peppermint, or fruit extracts. While these additives are safe to consume, they may not be suitable for use on the skin because they can cause irritation or allergic reactions.

  5. Humectants and binders: Humectants, such as glycerin or sorbitol, are commonly used in toothpaste formulations to help retain moisture and keep the product from drying out. Binders such as carrageenan and cellulose gum are also used to give toothpaste its distinctive texture. While these ingredients are generally safe for oral use, they are not intended for skin application.

Tips for Preventing Acne Breakouts

Acne prevention entails following a consistent skincare routine and making certain lifestyle changes. Here are some pointers to help you avoid acne breakouts:

  1. Cleanse your face twice a day: Wash your face in the morning and evening with a gentle, non-comedogenic cleanser. Scrubbing too hard can irritate the skin and cause breakouts.

  2. Avoid touching your face: Touching your face could spread bacteria and oils from your hands to your skin, clogging pores and possibly causing breakouts. Throughout the day, try to avoid touching your face.

  3. Keep your hair clean: Oily hair, particularly along the hairline, can contribute to acne breakouts. Wash your hair on a regular basis, especially if it is greasy, and keep it away from your face.

  4. Use non-comedogenic products: Look for skincare and cosmetics that are non-comedogenic, which means they won't clog pores. To help prevent breakouts, look for oil-free or water-based formulas.

  5. Remove your makeup before going to bed: Sleeping with makeup on can clog pores and cause breakouts. Use a gentle cleanser or makeup remover to thoroughly remove all makeup before going to bed.

  6. Maintain a healthy diet: While the relationship between diet and acne is complex and varies from person to person, some studies suggest that certain foods, such as high glycemic index foods and dairy products, may contribute to acne breakouts. Keep an eye on how your skin reacts to different foods and consider making dietary changes if you notice a link.

  7. Manage your stress levels: Stress can aggravate acne breakouts.  To help reduce stress, use stress-management techniques such as exercise, meditation, deep breathing, or engaging in activities you enjoy. 

  8. Avoid overexposure to the sun: While sun exposure can temporarily improve acne, prolonged exposure can cause skin damage and increased oil production. Wear sunscreen and avoid excessive sun exposure to protect your skin.

  9. Do not pick or squeeze acne lesions: Picking or squeezing acne lesions can cause inflammation, scarring, and the spread of bacteria. It is best to leave them alone and allow them to heal naturally, or to seek professional extraction assistance.

  10. Consult a dermatologist: If you have persistent or severe acne breakouts, you should see a dermatologist. They can make personalized recommendations, prescribe medication if necessary, and recommend professional acne treatments.

Keep in mind that preventing acne outbreaks requires perseverance and consistency. It might take some time to see results, so be patient and make the necessary lifestyle changes.
