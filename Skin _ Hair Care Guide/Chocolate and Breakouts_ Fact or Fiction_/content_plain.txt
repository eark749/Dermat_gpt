: When pores clog, a type of bacteria called Propionibacterium acnes, which is present on the skin naturally, can grow and cause inflammation. Notably, this bacterium is not the only cause of acne and is also found on the skin of those who do not have the condition.

Skincare and cosmetics
: Some skincare and cosmetics items, particularly those that contain comedogenic ingredients (substances that can clog pores), can cause acne outbreaks.  It is crucial to select non-comedogenic products and create a skincare routine that works for your skin type.
Diet
: Although research on the effects of diet on acne is still ongoing, some studies have suggested that foods with a high glycemic index, dairy products, and diets high in processed or sugary foods may be responsible for the development of acne in some people. Environmental Factors
