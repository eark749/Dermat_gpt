Understanding the Difference between Chemical and Physical Sunscreen Filters
The
two types of sunscreens,
chemical and physical, use various types of filters to shield the skin from the sun's damaging UV rays.
UV rays are absorbed by chemical sunscreens before they can harm the skin. Chemical sunscreens contain active ingredients like avobenzone,
oxybenzone
, and octinoxate that chemically bond with UV rays, turning them into heat and releasing them from the skin. On the other hand, UV rays are reflected and dispersed away from the skin by physical sunscreens. Physical sunscreens' active components, like zinc oxide and titanium dioxide, create a shield on the skin that reflects UV rays away from the skin.
The way they function and the types of protection they offer are the main distinctions between chemical and physical sunscreens. The fact that chemical sunscreens are frequently thinner and easier to apply makes them more popular for daily use. They also frequently offer greater defence against UVA and UVB rays. The active ingredients in chemical sunscreens can, however, cause skin rashes or allergic reactions in some people.
Physical sunscreens, on the other hand, are often thicker and can leave a white cast on the skin. However, they are typically regarded as being kinder to the skin and less likely to trigger allergic reactions or irritate it. Since they function by reflecting UV rays away from the skin, they also offer more immediate UV protection.
To summarise, physical sunscreens reflect and scatter UV rays away from the skin, whereas chemical sunscreens work by absorbing UV rays. It is crucial to select the sunscreen that is best for your skin type and lifestyle because each type has benefits and drawbacks.
