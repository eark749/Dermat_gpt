Understanding Betulinic Acid: A Powerful Skincare Ingredient

Betulinic acid is a naturally occurring pentacyclic triterpenoid found in the bark of several trees, most notably the white birch (Betula alba). It has gained popularity in skincare due to its powerful beneficial effects. This blog provides a complete explanation of betulinic acid, a strong skincare component.

What is Betulinic Acid?

Betulinic acid is a naturally occurring triterpenoid chemical found in the bark of various plants, including the white birch tree (Betula alba). It is also found in other plants, including the plane tree (Platanus spp.), the Chaga mushroom (Inonotus obliquus), and some fruits, such as berries. Betulinic acid is generated from betulin, a precursor found abundantly in the outer bark of birches.
