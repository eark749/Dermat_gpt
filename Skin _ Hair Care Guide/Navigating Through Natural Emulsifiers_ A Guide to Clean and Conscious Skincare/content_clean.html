Definition: What Are Natural Emulsifiers?

The compounds known as natural emulsifiers, which are frequently sourced from plants, allow water- and oil-based materials to be blended and stabilised for use in a variety of goods, such as food, skincare, and cosmetics. They are essential in keeping these two immiscible ingredients from separating and preserving the end product's uniform, harmonious texture. Those who value mindful and clean beauty prefer natural emulsifiers in skincare and cosmetic products. These emulsifiers are a more environmentally responsible option because they are usually sourced from sustainable and renewable resources. They are used in the manufacturing of stable formulations such as creams, lotions, and serums, where the proper blending of water and oil constituents is required to provide the desired texture and functionality. Natural emulsifiers are frequently preferred due to their perceived safety, lower risk of skin sensitivities, and compatibility with environmentally conscious values.

Understanding Different Types of Natural Emulsifiers

To choose the best natural emulsifiers for your skincare or cosmetic formulations, it is imperative to understand the various varieties available. As opposed to their synthetic counterparts, natural emulsifiers—which are sourced from plants—are usually thought to be safer and kinder to the skin. Typical natural emulsifier types include the following:

  1. Cetearyl Alcohol: A fatty alcohol derived from coconut or palm oil, cetearyl alcohol. It is frequently used in skincare products as both an emulsifier and a thickening agent. It helps stabilise emulsions and create a creamy texture.

  2. Cetearyl Glucoside: Cetearyl glucoside is a natural emulsifier made by combining cetearyl alcohol with glucose derived from corn or other plants. Its gentle and skin-friendly properties make it popular in natural and organic skincare products.

  3. Sorbitan Olivate: Derived from olive oil, sorbitan olivate is known for its skin-friendly properties. It aids in the combination of oil and water-based ingredients, while also providing hydration and a soft texture to the skin. 

  4. Xanthan Gum: Xanthan gum is made by fermenting sugars and is used as a natural thickening and stabilising agent. While it is not a traditional emulsifier, it can help improve emulsion texture and stability. 

  5. Lecithin: Lecithin is commonly derived from soybeans, but it can also be derived from sunflower seeds, and it is used in skincare products as an emulsifier and stabiliser. It is high in fatty acids and can aid in the creation of smooth textures.

  6. Beeswax: Beeswax is a natural wax that honeybees produce. It is used in skincare products as an emulsifier and thickener. It works well for a variety of skin types and is frequently used in natural and organic formulations.

  7. Sunflower Lecithin: Sunflower lecithin, like soy lecithin, is derived from sunflower seeds. It is a natural emulsifier and stabiliser that is suitable for people who are allergic to soy.

  8. Soy Lecithin: Derived from soybeans, soy lecithin is commonly used in skincare products as an emulsifier and texture enhancer. It is adaptable and useful for combining oil and water-based ingredients.

By understanding the different types of natural emulsifiers and their properties, you can make informed choices when selecting products and formulating your own skincare or cosmetic products.

Reading Labels: Identifying Natural Emulsifiers in Products

Reading and comprehending the ingredient labels is necessary to locate natural emulsifiers in skincare and cosmetic products. Natural emulsifiers are often listed as components of compound names or as an ingredient on their own. The following describes how to spot natural emulsifiers on product labels:

  • Read the Ingredient List: The product packaging usually has an ingredient list that is arranged in decreasing order of concentration. Look for words that say "natural emulsifiers" in the product.

  • Identify Common Natural Emulsifiers: Natural emulsifiers have distinct names and are frequently sourced from plants. Common natural emulsifiers include sorbitan olivate, beeswax, sunflower lecithin, xanthan gum, cetearyl alcohol, and cetearyl glucoside. These names indicate their natural origins.

  • Look for Plant-Based Sources: Take note of the references listed in the component list. Plant sources, including coconut, palm oil, corn, olive oil, soybeans, sunflower seeds, and beeswax, are frequently used to make natural emulsifiers. Recognising natural emulsifiers can be made easier by identifying these sources.

  • Look for Labels Saying "Natural" or "Organic": Certain products may state clearly on their labels that they are "natural" or "organic." This may be a sign of purer, more natural formulations, but it does not imply the absence of artificial emulsifiers. 

  • Avoid Synthetic Emulsifiers: To ensure that you are using natural emulsifiers, avoid synthetic emulsifiers such as polysorbates, PEG compounds, and other chemical-sounding ingredients. These are rarely derived from natural sources. 

  • Investigate Specific Products and Brands: If you have questions about the ingredients in a particular product, visit the brand's website or contact customer service. Many natural ingredient-focused brands provide detailed information on their formulations.

  • Seek Third-Party Certifications: Some products have third-party certifications such as "Certified Organic," "Cruelty-Free," or "Vegan."  These certifications can provide additional assurance that the product's ingredients are clean and natural.

  • Consider All Ingredients: In addition to the emulsifier, consider the entire ingredient list to ensure that the product matches your preferences and skin type. Looking for other natural and beneficial ingredients, as well as avoiding potential allergens, are all part of this process.

  • Perform a Patch Test: If you have sensitive skin or allergies, consider performing a patch test on a small area of your skin before using a product containing natural emulsifiers.

By learning the names and sources of common natural emulsifiers and reading product labels critically, you can identify products that align with your preferences for clean and natural skincare. Additionally, looking for products from reputable and transparent brands that emphasise clean beauty practises can help you make informed decisions.
