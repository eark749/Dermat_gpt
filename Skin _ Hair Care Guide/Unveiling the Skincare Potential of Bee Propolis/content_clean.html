An Overview of Bee Propolis

Bee propolis is a natural resinous substance that honeybees (Apis mellifera) produce from botanical sources such as tree buds, sap flows, and pollen. The bees collect this sticky material and bring it back to their hives, where it is used as a building material to seal and reinforce the hive's structure. The term "propolis" comes from the Greek words "pro," which means "in defence of," and "polis," which means "the city" or "the community," and refers to the beehive's role in defending itself from external threats. Bee propolis is an incredible natural material that may have health benefits.  More research is needed, however, to fully understand its mechanisms of action and demonstrate its efficacy for a variety of health and skincare applications.
