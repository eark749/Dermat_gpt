Hair loss affects millions of men and women throughout the world. Minoxidil is a well-known topical medication used to promote hair growth. We will examine the uses, advantages, and safety of minoxidil for women in this blog.

Understanding Minoxidil and Its Uses

More than 10% of women experience female-pattern hair loss by the age of 50, and more than 30% by the age of 70. Minoxidil for women is a popular drug for treating female hair loss. Minoxidil can be used to stimulate hair growth or prevent hair loss. It can be used to treat both progressive hair loss, such as female pattern baldness, and transient hair loss caused by conditions like telogen effluvium. Women who are very stressed are 11 times more likely to develop telogen effluvium. A common issue is whether minoxidil is suitable for women.

What is Minoxidil?

Minoxidil was developed in the 1980s. It has proven to be an effective solution to hair loss caused by certain conditions. Male and female pattern hair loss rank first on the list. Initially, it was used to reduce high blood pressure. However, its hair growth-promoting properties were quickly recognized.

How Minoxidil Works for Hair Growth

Minoxidil is applied straight to the head as a solution or foam. According to the review, when compared to minoxidil solution, minoxidil foam allows for better medication penetration, improved active ingredient delivery, and less scalp irritation. A scalp enzyme called sulfotransferase transforms minoxidil into minoxidil sulfate. It works by shortening the hair's telogen, or resting, phase, causing it to enter the anagen or growing stage. There is some evidence that minoxidil helps thicken the hair.

Learn more about the different stages of hair growth. Minoxidil does not pass the blood-brain barrier, and the kidneys filter 95% of the medicine after 4 days. People may notice a difference in their hair once they have used minoxidil for at least eight weeks.

Differences Between Minoxidil for Men and Women

Women and men can use the same minoxidil products. The only variation is how frequently the product is supposed to be applied. The suggested dosage of minoxidil foam for women is 5%, with half a capful used once a day. Men are instructed to apply the same dose, twice a day.

Safety and Side Effects of Minoxidil for Women

Some Minoxidil side effects are unique to women, while others can affect people of any gender. Side effects unique to women include:

  • Potential foetal deformities in pregnant women.

  • Minoxidil is excreted through breast milk and may harm a breastfeeding baby.

  • Hypertrichosis, whether generalized or localized is hypothesized to occur due to the lengthening of the anagen phase and can cause facial hair development.

General side effects (affecting men and women):

  • Inflammation of the skin or scales

  • Allergenic contact dermatitis

  • Temporary Minoxidil hair loss (this is thought to occur due to the shortening of the telogen phase)

  • Hair color changes

For more serious side effects, such as blurred vision, chest pain, and facial swelling, see a doctor or seek medical attention from your dermatologists right away.

Common Side Effects

The application site may burn, sting, or become red. If any of these symptoms persist or worsen, notify your doctor or pharmacist right away. If your doctor has advised you to take this medicine, remember that the benefit to you outweighs the danger of adverse effects.

How to Minimize Side Effects

To reduce these adverse effects, don't use minoxidil more than recommended, and wash your hands after applying it to avoid transferring it to other parts of your body. It's also worth noting that patients may notice increased hair loss when they initially begin therapy, which is termed minoxidil shedding.

Long-term Safety Considerations

Oral minoxidil is also associated with serious side effects, as detailed below. Pericarditis, pericardial effusion, cardiac tamponade, exacerbation of congestive heart failure, and worsening angina are all rare but serious reactions.

How to Use Minoxidil for Best Results

To use a topical solution with minoxidil:

  • Before applying this medicine, ensure that your hair and scalp are completely dry.

  • Apply the prescribed amount to the treated area of the scalp, starting in the center. Follow your doctor's instructions for applying the solution, using the applicator provided.

  • After applying minoxidil to your hair, wait 4 hours before shampooing it.

  • Wash your hands immediately after using this medicine to remove any remaining medicine.

  • Do not use a hairdryer to dry your scalp after applying the minoxidil solution. Blowing with a hairdryer on the scalp may reduce the treatment's effectiveness.

  • Allow the minoxidil to dry completely for 2 to 4 hours after application, including before bedtime. If your hair or scalp is not completely dry after using minoxidil, it may stain clothing, hats, or bed linens.

  • Avoid transferring wet medicine to other areas of the body. This can happen if the medicine gets on your pillowcase or bed linens, or if you do not wash your hands after using minoxidil.

To use topical minoxidil foam, follow these instructions:

  • Open the container by matching the arrow on the can ring to the arrow on the cap. Take off the cap.

  • Part the hair into one or more rows to reveal the scalp's thinning area.

  • To apply foam to your fingers, hold the can upside down and press the nozzle.

  • Spread the foam over the area where you are losing hair and gently massage it into your scalp.

  • Wash your hands immediately after using this medicine to remove any remaining medicine.

Step-by-Step Application Guide

  1. Fill the spoon with the appropriate amount of minoxidil cream (usually 1ml). One level spoonful is the full dose of 1ml, and we have included a spatula to level the amount in the spoon. 

  2. Using your fingertips, gently rub dabs of cream into the scalp in the direction of the hair. Begin with the areas most affected by hair loss or thinning, and make sure the cream does not absorb into the hair.

  3. Continue in this manner for every section of the scalp until the required measurement is reached.

  4. There should not be any traces of the cream left on the skin or hair.

  5. After using the product, wash your hands immediately.

  6. Wash any remaining cream from the spoon. Each tub of minoxidil cream is slightly overfilled to compensate for any waste.

Foam vs. Liquid: Which is Better?

Minoxidil foam and liquid are both effective at treating hair loss and stimulating hair growth. Because the foam version of minoxidil does not contain propylene glycol, it may be a better option if you frequently experience irritation, redness, or other side effects with the liquid form.

Combining Minoxidil with Other Treatments

  1. Minoxidil and Finasteride: Oral medication that lowers levels of dihydrotestosterone (DHT), a hormone associated with hair loss. Combining Minoxidil and Finasteride can address hair loss in various aspects, including topically encouraging hair growth and systemically lowering DHT levels.

  2. Minoxidil and Low-Level Laser Therapy (LLLT): Treatment that employs red light to stimulate hair follicles and increase hair growth. Combining Minoxidil and LLLT can increase hair density and thickness more efficiently than either treatment alone.

  3. Minoxidil and Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP) Therapy: This procedure includes injecting platelet-rich plasma from the patient's blood into the scalp to encourage hair growth. PRP can increase the effects of Minoxidil by supplying a high concentration of growth factors that stimulate hair follicle health and regeneration.

  4. Minoxidil and Microneedling: Fine needles are used to produce tiny punctures in the scalp, which promote healing and increase topical therapy absorption. Allows minoxidil to penetrate deeper into the scalp, increasing its effectiveness.

  5. Minoxidil and Hair Growth Supplements: Vitamins and minerals that promote hair health include biotin, zinc, iron, and vitamin D. Combining Minoxidil with a well-balanced diet and supplements can supply the nutrients required for healthy hair development.

  6. Minoxidil and Anti-Dandruff Shampoos: Products with ketoconazole, selenium sulfide, or zinc pyrithione. Using these shampoos can reduce scalp inflammation and dandruff, resulting in a more favorable environment for hair development.
