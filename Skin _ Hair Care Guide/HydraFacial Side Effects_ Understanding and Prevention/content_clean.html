Debunking Myths: Common Misconceptions About HydraFacial Treatments

A guide to debunking common myths and clarifying HydraFacial treatments:

  1. Myth: HydraFacial treatments are painful. 

  2. Truth: HydraFacials should not be painful.  Essentially, the machine's handpiece has spiralized therapy tips that use vortex technology to create a small suction on your skin.  The exfoliation in segment one is without a doubt the most unpleasant part of the treatment.

  3. Myth: HydraFacials are only for those with acne prone skin.

  4. Truth: While HydraFacial is safe for all skin types and can be used to treat a variety of facial care issues such as hyperpigmentation, fine lines and wrinkles, elasticity, firmness, skin texture, and tone, it is especially beneficial for people with acne-prone skin and acne scars.

  5. Myth: The HydraFacial procedure produces only temporary results.

  6. Truth: The effects of a Hydrafacial are only temporary, lasting about one to two weeks. While the treatment can improve skin hydration, tone, and texture right away, the results are not long-lasting.

  7. Myth: HydraFacials are the same as microdermabrasion.

  8. Truth: The hydro-peel wand will work repeatedly in the T-zone area to extract blackheads from pores without over-peeling the skin. It then adds peptides and serum to help the skin produce more collagen. Microdermabrasion, on the other hand, uses microcrystals to exfoliate the skin.

  9. Myth: Hydrofacials are effective for acne.

  10. Truth: Exfoliation and extraction can help clear out clogged pores, which are a major cause of acne. Removing these blockages has the potential to reduce breakouts.

  11. Myth: HydraFacials are only for women.

  12. Truth: Everyone wants to look good. As a result, it is a misconception that hydrafacial treatment is only for women.  Men can also benefit from this unique skin care treatment. The effectiveness and benefits of HydraFacial treatment are intended to beautify, cleanse, and improve skin health.

  13. Myth: HydraFacials are too expensive.

  14. Truth: This type of marketing is costly, so the equipment is prohibitively expensive to purchase, and the cost is passed on to the consumer. Professional HydraFacial: What to Expect from Expert Skin Care

A licensed aesthetician or dermatologist administers a HydraFacial. The process is finished in three sections:

  • Your provider applies a vortex-like wand to your skin to help remove dirt and oil from deep within your pores. They also use a peel to exfoliate and resurface the skin.

  • To extract debris from your pores, your provider will use a wand with a vacuum-like attachment.

  • Your provider applies antioxidant-rich moisturizing serum with a pen-like attachment.

Following a HydraFacial, you may have further skin care treatments such as dermal fillers or light therapy. These are considered add-ons and are not included with the HydraFacial.
