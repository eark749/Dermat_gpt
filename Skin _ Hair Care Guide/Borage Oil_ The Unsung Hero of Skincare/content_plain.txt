The Power of Gamma-Linolenic Acid (GLA) for Skin Health
Gamma-linolenic acid (GLA) is a strong molecule that promotes skin health. Here's why:
GLA, an omega-6 fatty acid, can help reduce inflammation throughout the body, including the skin. Many skin disorders, including
acne
,
eczema
, and
psoriasis
, are characterised by inflammation. GLA, by modifying inflammatory pathways, can help calm inflamed skin and reduce symptoms of inflammatory skin diseases.
GLA is an essential component of the skin's lipid barrier, which helps to retain moisture and prevent water loss. When the skin lacks sufficient GLA, it can become dry, rough, and irritated. Supplementing with GLA-rich oils, such as borage oil, can aid in moisture balance and keep the skin moisturised and supple.
GLA is involved in the synthesis of
ceramides
, which are required to maintain the structure and integrity of the skin barrier. A strong skin barrier is essential for defending against environmental stresses, minimising moisture loss, and promoting general skin health. GLA supports ceramide synthesis, which strengthens and improves the skin barrier.
GLA has been found to stimulate cell regeneration and
collagen
formation in the skin. Collagen is a protein that helps the skin maintain its structural integrity and flexibility. Collagen production naturally reduces with age, resulting in wrinkles, drooping, and a loss of firmness. GLA can increase collagen formation, resulting in smoother, firmer, and younger-looking skin.
GLA has antioxidant qualities, which help to neutralise free radicals that can harm skin cells and lead to premature ageing. GLA protects the skin from environmental contaminants, UV radiation, and other causes of oxidative damage by lowering oxidative stress.
GLA has been shown to promote wound healing by increasing cell proliferation, angiogenesis (the development of new blood vessels), and collagen deposition. This can help to cure cuts, abrasions, and other types of skin lesions more rapidly and efficiently.

Tailoring Your Skincare: How to Use Borage Oil Effectively
Understanding the qualities of borage oil and how it can address your unique skincare concerns is essential for effectively incorporating it into your skincare routine. Here's how to utilise borage oil effectively:
Identify Your Skincare Needs
: Determine your unique skincare concerns, such as dryness, irritation, symptoms of ageing, or a mix of these. Understanding your skin's needs will allow you to adjust your skincare programme accordingly.
Choose High-Quality Borage Oil
: To assure quality and purity, choose cold-pressed organic borage oil from reputed brands. Cold-pressed oils retain more of their natural nutrients and are less likely to contain contaminants.
Patch Test
: Before introducing borage oil into your skincare routine, perform a patch test to ensure that there are no adverse reactions. Apply a little amount of borage oil to a discreet part of your skin, such as the inner forearm, and wait 24-48 hours to check for irritation or sensitivity.
Determine Application Method
: Borage oil can be used topically on the skin or eaten as a supplement. Determine which approach best suits your skincare goals and tastes. Topical treatment is best for addressing specific areas of concern, but oral supplementation delivers total skincare benefits from inside.
Incorporate into Your Routine
: In topical application, mix a few drops of borage oil into your moisturiser, serum, or face oil and apply to clean skin. Massage lightly till absorbed. Borage oil can also be used as a spot therapy for dry skin, eczema, and other inflammatory diseases. On the other hand, for oral supplementation, you can take borage oil orally, following the dosage instructions on the product label or consult with a healthcare practitioner for personalised advice. Oral supplementation can improve overall skin health and may be useful for treating systemic inflammatory diseases.
Combine with Other Skincare Ingredients:
Borage oil can be combined with other skincare components to increase its efficacy. Consider combining it with
hyaluronic acid
for more hydration,
vitamin C
for brightening and antioxidant protection, or
retinol
for anti-aging properties. Be aware of potential interactions and begin with lesser dosages to avoid irritation.
Monitor Results
: Pay attention to how your skin reacts to borage oil and modify your application accordingly. It may take some time to see obvious results, so be patient and consistent with your skincare programme. If you suffer any bad effects, stop using it and see a
dermatologist
.
Protect from Oxidation
: Borage oil should be stored in a cool, dark place away from direct sunlight and heat to prevent oxidation and maintain its efficacy. Consider using opaque or tinted glass bottles to protect the oil from light exposure.
DIY Skincare: Creating Your Own Borage Oil Blends
Using borage oil to make your skincare mixtures can be a lucrative and enjoyable way to personalise your beauty regimen. Here are some homemade beauty recipes that call for borage oil:
Hydrating Facial Serum
: Ingredients are 1 tablespoon borage oil, 1 tablespoon rosehip oil, 1 tablespoon argan oil and 5-10 drops of lavender essential oil (optional for fragrance). In a small glass dropper bottle, combine the borage oil, rosehip oil, and argan oil. Add the lavender essential oil for a calming scent (optional). Close the bottle and shake well to mix the oils. Apply a few drops of the serum to clean, damp skin and massage gently until absorbed. Use morning and night for best results.
Soothing Body Oil
: Ingredients are 2 tablespoons borage oil, 2 tablespoons jojoba oil and 5-10 drops of chamomile essential oil. Combine the borage oil and jojoba oil in a small bowl. Add the chamomile essential oil for its soothing properties and gentle fragrance. Mix well to combine all the ingredients. After showering or bathing, apply the oil to damp skin and massage in circular motions until absorbed. Focus on dry or irritated areas for extra hydration and relief.
Anti-Aging Eye Cream
: Ingredients are 1 tablespoon borage oil, 1 tablespoon shea butter, 1 tablespoon coconut oil and 5-10 drops of rosehip seed oil. In a double boiler or microwave-safe bowl, melt the shea butter and coconut oil until liquid. Remove from heat and stir in the borage oil and rosehip seed oil. Allow the mixture to cool slightly, then transfer it to a small jar or container. Let the cream solidify at room temperature or in the refrigerator before use. Apply a small amount of the cream to the
under eye
area, using gentle tapping motions to avoid pulling or stretching the delicate skin.
Healing Lip Balm
: Ingredients are 1 tablespoon borage oil, 1 tablespoon beeswax pellets, 1 tablespoon coconut oil and 5-10 drops of peppermint essential oil (optional for a cooling sensation). In a double boiler or microwave-safe bowl, melt the beeswax pellets and coconut oil until liquid. Remove from heat and stir in the borage oil and peppermint essential oil (if using). Pour the mixture into lip balm containers or small jars. Let the lip balm solidify at room temperature or in the refrigerator before use. Apply as needed to dry or chapped lips for instant hydration and protection.
