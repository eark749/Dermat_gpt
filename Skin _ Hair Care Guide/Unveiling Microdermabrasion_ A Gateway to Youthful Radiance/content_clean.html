Preparing for Microdermabrasion

Preparing for Microdermabrasion starts outside the office. It involves the following:

#1 Understanding your skin needs and type:

If your skin shows signs of ageing such as wrinkles and dark spots, and scars, and you want to minimise it; or if you want to improve the appearance of skin and get rid of dullness, Microdermabrasion can be used to achieve the desired results. However, your skin may not support microdermabrasion if you have various skin conditions like

  • Eczema

  • Rosacea

  • Active Acne

  • Dermatitis

  • Psoriasis

  • Warts

  • Lupus

  • Uncontrolled Diabetes

  • Compromised Skin Barrier


#2 Understanding the Financial Factor:

Understand that certain dermatological procedures bear high costs. We, at Clinikally also provide the mentioned treatment. Book an appointment with our top dermatologists to know more.

#3 Providing a Comprehensive Medical History to the Doctor:

Inform your doctor about all the medicines you are taking/using or have taken/used at any point in the past. Mention about any skin problem that you are going through.

#4 Following all Instructions Given to You by the Dermatologist:

Doctors may ask you to indulge in special skin care practices so as to prepare your skin for the actual treatment. These products are usually available at the clinic or on prescription from pharmacies. Make sure to follow the instructions.

#5 Avoiding Salon Treatments:

Avoid indulging in beauty routines at the salon such as threading, waxing, facials etc. It is to be avoided as these may result in an increased sensitivity and dryness, that may lead to further complications.

#6 Avoiding Other Skin Treatments:

Receiving a BOTOX injection or doing chemical peels is contraindicated as it compromises the skin barrier, that may lead to complications during and after the procedure of microdermabrasion. 

#7 Minimising Sun Exposure:

Exposure to harsh sun rays causes damage to the sun and an increased sensitivity. Hence, avoid going out in the sun at least a week prior to the scheduled appointment.

#8 Discontinuing Other Topical Actives:

Discontinue using various topical active medications to decrease the skin sensitivity. Avoid using the following: 

  • Retinoids
  • Salicylic Acid Products
  • Benzoyl Peroxide
  • Alpha Hydroxy Acids
  • Beta Hydroxy Acids

#9 Discontinuing taking NSAIDs and Minimise Smoking:

Consult a doctor and stop taking Non-Steroidal Anti-Inflammatory Drugs (NSAIDs) as these increase the risk of bleeding and facial rashes. Common NSAIDs include:

  • Aspirin

  • Ibuprofen

  • Mefenamic Acid

  • Diclofenac

  • Naproxen

  • Ketorolac

  • Aceclofenac

  • Celecoxib

  • Oxaprozin

  • Piroxicam

  • Etoricoxib etc.

Also, minimise smoking at least a week prior to your appointment, as it inhibits blood circulation in the skin. To achieve a long term result of your treatment, minimise smoking. 

#10 Cleaning Face Before the Appointment:

On the day of your appointment, clean the face using a mild, oil free, hydrating cleanser. Also, avoid applying makeup on the scheduled appointment. 

Take a note to reschedule your appointment, if any blister, boil or active acne develops.

The Science of Skin Rejuvenation: How Microdermabrasion Works

Microdermabrasion is an exfoliation process that involves the following steps:

  1. Cleaning- It involves using a deep cleansing gel or foam to remove dust, makeup, or natural oils from the skin.

  2. Toning- A toner is further used to remove any remaining impurities.

  3. Exfoliating- A special diamond-coated tipped wand is used to thoroughly exfoliate the skin.

  4. Hydrating- After removing the dead skin cells from the top most layer of the skin, a face mask and serum are used to hydrate and nourish the skin.

  5. Moisturising- A moisturiser is used to deeply moisturise and nourish the skin.

  6. Applying Sunscreen- As the exposed underneath skin layer is weak and extremely soft, using a good sunscreen is an absolute necessity as it protects the fragile skin from any sun damage.

Post-Treatment Care: Maximising the Benefits of Microdermabrasion

The risks associated with microdermabrasion, while typically mild, include:

  • Short-Term Discomfort: Common immediate aftereffects like redness and swelling are temporary and usually resolve within a few hours.

  • Skin Dryness and Flaking: It's normal for the skin to experience dryness and flakiness for several days post-treatment as it heals and regenerates.

  • Bruising: The suction technique used in microdermabrasion can lead to bruising, which might last for a few days.

  • Sun Sensitivity: Post-treatment, the skin becomes more vulnerable to sun damage. Hence, it's crucial to apply sunscreen diligently, particularly right after a session, to protect the newly exposed skin layers. Clinikally SunProtect Sunscreen SPF 50/PA+++ is highly recommended.

These risks highlight the importance of proper aftercare following a microdermabrasion treatment to ensure safe and effective healing.

 The After-Care

To maintain the benefits of microdermabrasion treatment, follow these essential aftercare tips:

  • Stay Cool and Calm: For the first 24 hours, strictly avoid those activities that raise body temperature like hot showers and strenuous exercise. Keep your skin cool with dermatologist recommended creams.

  • Gentle Skincare Routine: Use the hydrating and gentle products advised by your dermatologist, such as specific cleansers, moisturisers, and serums.

  • Hydration is Key: Drink plenty of water. Also, use a moisturising spray throughout the day to maintain your skin's hydration.

  • Avoid Picking: Post-treatment peeling is normal; resist picking or peeling at your skin for optimal healing.

  • Sun Protection: Your new skin is sun-sensitive hence, appling a high SPF sunscreen every four hours, especially when outdoors is a necessity.

DIY Microdermabrasion: A Comprehensive Guide

Well, if you don’t want to go to a derma clinic, and don’t want to spend bucks on at-home microdermabrasion kits, then also we have got you covered. To avail maximum benefits, by using the ingredients from your kitchen, keep reading below. However, let us warn you beforehand, that it may irritate your skin. So do a patch test before using it on face.

  1. Gentle Cleansing: Start with a preferred facial cleanser. Cleanse to remove all the dirt, grime, sweat and makeup.

  2. Natural Ingredients: Use 2 teaspoons of aloe vera gel and half a teaspoon of baking soda to create a paste. Mix both the ingredients together.

  3. Careful Application: Apply the paste gently with light, circular motions, avoiding the eye area and lip area.

  4. Brief Massage: Limit the massage to about 60 seconds only. Don’t over-massage.

  5. Rinse Thoroughly: Rinse off the paste by using only plain water. Do not use a cleanser.

  6. Moisturising Routine: Follow up with a generous mist of hydrosol, a moisturising sheet mask or serum, and face oil.

  7. Regular Application: Perform every 6-8 weeks, up to three sessions, with a break before restarting.

Essential Tips for Safe and Effective Home Microdermabrasion

While indulging in microdermabrasion practice at home, using any kit or tool or diy paste, make sure to keep the below mentioned 10 points in mind:

  1. Always perform microabrasion on a cleansed face.

  2. While using any tool, always start with a low and a gentle setting. 

  3. Slowly increase the settings of the tool.

  4. Don’t be aggressive while using the tool/ kit as it can cause damage to the smallest capillaries. 

  5. If you are using vibrating tools that have bristles on it, make sure they are clean, so as to avoid infection.

  6. Do not use the tools when your acne, rosacea etc. is active.

  7. Always do a patch test before using the DIY Paste as it is harsh.

  8. Don’t forget to moisturise your skin after doing the procedure.

  9. Always apply sunscreen, as microdermabrasion may cause increased sensitivity.

  10. Consult a doctor immediately, if you experience any problem.

Personalised Skincare Strategies Post-Microdermabrasion

Microdermabrasion treatment is a non-invasive, gentle procedure, but it may result in some temporary side effects such as:

  • Redness or a pink hue to the face

  • Feeling of skin tightness and burning

  • Sensitivity

Hence, it becomes essential to follow a gentle and protective skincare routine to achieve maximum benefits along with healing:

  • Immediate Care: Cleanse your face gently with a wet cloth and a rehydrating toner to remove residual dead skin cells.

  • Moisturising: Apply a rich and a heavy moisturiser. Use it for 4-6 days to avoid excessive peeling. Clinikally HydraSoothe Moisturiser is your best bet.

  • Sun Protection: Use a broad spectrum sunscreen daily, as the treatment drastically increases sun sensitivity. Using sunscreen will aid in preventing sun damage or hyperpigmentation.

  • Sun Exposure: Limit direct sun exposure for at least a week post-treatment due to increased photo-sensitivity.

  • Hydration: Keep hydrated by drinking water and consuming water-rich fruits and vegetables.

  • Avoid Touching: Refrain from scratching or picking at the treated area to prevent bacterial infection, as the skin barrier is compromised.

  • Stay Away from Harsh Treatments: Avoid using harsh chemicals, facial waxing, depilatory creams, and other intensive skin procedures for at least a week.

These steps help ensure optimal healing and maintain the benefits of your microdermabrasion treatment. Do these to achieve the best and long lasting results.
