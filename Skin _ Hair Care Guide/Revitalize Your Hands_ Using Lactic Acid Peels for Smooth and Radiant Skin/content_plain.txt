Understanding Lactic Acid and Its Properties
Alpha-hydroxy acids (AHAs) called lactic acids are organic compounds that are naturally present in dairy products like milk and yoghurt. The human body also produces it when engaging in vigorous exercise because the muscles are working hard and the oxygen levels are low. Lactic acid is a water-soluble molecule that dissolves the bonds between dead skin cells, allowing them to be easily removed from the skin's surface. This exfoliating action promotes cell turnover and the growth of new, healthy skin cells.
Lactic acid is a common ingredient in skincare products because it has hydrating qualities in addition to exfoliating ones. Lactic acid can help to increase the skin's natural moisture levels, making it feel softer, smoother, and more supple when applied to the skin. Finally, lactic acid has been shown to have anti-bacterial and anti-inflammatory properties, which may be beneficial for acne-prone or sensitive skin.
