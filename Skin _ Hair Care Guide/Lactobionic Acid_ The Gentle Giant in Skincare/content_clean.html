Key Properties: What Makes Lactobionic Acid Unique

Several essential characteristics set lactobionic acid (LBA) apart from other skincare components and contribute to its special uses and advantages.

  • Gentle Exfoliation: LBA is a polyhydroxy acid (PHA), which means it helps the skin shed dead skin cells. PHAs, like LBA, are larger in molecular size than alpha hydroxy acids (AHAs) like glycolic acid. Because of its bigger size, LBA exfoliates more gently, making it ideal for sensitive skin types that are prone to discomfort from stronger exfoliants.

  • Antioxidant Activity: LBA has antioxidant capabilities, which assist in neutralising free radicals that cause oxidative stress and accelerate skin ageing. This antioxidant activity promotes skin health by minimising damage from environmental causes such as UV radiation and pollution.

  • Hydrating and moisturising: LBA is hygroscopic, which means it draws and holds moisture from the environment before binding it to the skin. This feature improves skin hydration, suppleness, and elasticity while decreasing the appearance of fine lines and wrinkles.

  • Skin Barrier Support: Research suggests that LBA can improve the skin barrier function. LBA improves the barrier, which helps to maintain healthy skin moisture and protects against external aggressors. This promotes healthier, more resilient skin.

  • Anti-inflammatory Properties: LBA has been shown to have anti-inflammatory properties, which can aid to soothe and calm irritated skin. This makes it ideal for people with sensitive skin or those who experience redness and inflammation.

  • Compatibility and Stability: LBA is stable across a wide range of formulas and pH levels, making it suitable with other skincare products. This enables formulators to include LBA in a wide range of goods, increasing their potency while maintaining stability.

  • Biocompatibility and Safety: LBA is deemed safe for topical use and has demonstrated strong biocompatibility with skin cells. Its moderate nature and low risk of irritation make it acceptable for long-term usage in skincare products.

This size is useful since it exfoliates without going too deeply, lowering the risk of irritation. The mild nature of PHAs such as LBA ensures that the skin's surface is exfoliated gently, eliminating dead skin cells while not damaging the skin barrier.

  2. Mild Exfoliation Mechanism: LBA primarily works on the skin's surface, breaking the connections that hold dead skin cells together. This procedure helps to expose younger, smoother skin underneath. Unlike more potent acids, LBA does not induce redness, dryness, or irritation.   3. Hydrating Exfoliation: LBA's hygroscopic characteristics exfoliate while also attracting and retaining moisture. This dual action keeps the skin moisturised and supple even after dead skin cells are gone. LBA improves skin texture by encouraging improved moisture retention and moderate exfoliation, resulting in a smoother, more even tone.

  4. Supporting the Skin Barrier: LBA helps to strengthen the skin's natural barrier. A robust skin barrier is essential for limiting moisture loss and protecting against external irritants, ultimately improving the skin's general health and resilience.   5. Antioxidant and Anti-inflammatory Benefits: LBA has antioxidant properties, which protect the skin from oxidative stress and free radical damage. This protection preserves youthful skin by preventing premature ageing.
