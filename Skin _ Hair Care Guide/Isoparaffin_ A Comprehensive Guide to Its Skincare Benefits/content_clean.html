What is Isoparaffin?

Chemical Nature:

  • Isoparaffin is a category of branched-chain hydrocarbons produced from petroleum. These chemicals are purified and refined so that they can be used in cosmetics and skincare.

  • There are several forms of isoparaffin, which are commonly designated after the number of carbon atoms they contain, such as C13-14 isoparaffin, C12-15 isoparaffin, etc.

Function:

  • Isoparaffin functions as a solvent, aiding in the dissolution of other ingredients and the formation of a homogeneous mixture.

  • It provides a smooth, soft texture to the skin, enhancing the overall feel and application of skincare products.
