Discovering the Power of Lingonberries for Your Skin
Lingonberries have developed to include a variety of biologically active phytochemicals to protect themselves from the extremely cold climate near the arctic region where they grow. Lingonberries have the most antioxidant activity when compared to their other cousins, such as blueberries, cranberries, and bilberries. The anthocyanins found in lingonberries, which give them their distinctive red colour, are not only powerful antioxidants but also efficient anti-inflammatory agents. Thus, lingonberries not only protect but also soothe the skin. These berries also include phytochemicals such as tocotrienols, flavonoids, and phenolic acids, which boost their antioxidant activity.
Lingonberries are high in omega fatty acids, particularly GLA (gamma linoleic acid), which helps to retain moisture and maintain a healthy skin barrier. These berries are also high in skin-friendly vitamins A, C, and E, as well as minerals like calcium, phosphorus, magnesium, and potassium, making them a superfood for your skin.

Lingonberry DIY Skincare: Nature's Gift to Your Skin
Harnessing the power of lingonberries in DIY skincare can be an excellent way to pamper your skin with natural ingredients. Here are a few simple yet effective DIY recipes to help you get started:
Lingonberry Face Mask
: Ingredients are 1/4 cup fresh or frozen lingonberries, 1 tablespoon plain yogurt and 1 teaspoon honey. Blend the lingonberries until you have a smooth puree.  Mix in the yogurt and honey until well combined.  Apply the mixture to clean skin and leave it on for 15-20 minutes.  Rinse off with lukewarm water and pat dry.
Lingonberry Sugar Scrub
: Ingredients are 1/2 cup lingonberries (fresh or frozen), 1/2 cup granulated sugar and 2 tablespoons coconut oil (melted). Mash the lingonberries into a pulp using a fork or blender. Mix in the sugar and melted coconut oil until well combined. Gently massage the scrub onto damp skin in circular motions for a few minutes.  Rinse off with warm water and pat dry.
Lingonberry Facial Toner
: Ingredients are 1/2 cup distilled water, 1/4 cup fresh or frozen lingonberries and 1 tablespoon witch hazel. Boil the distilled water and add the lingonberries.  Simmer for 10-15 minutes, then remove from heat and let cool. Strain the mixture to remove the berries, then stir in the witch hazel.  Transfer the toner to a clean spray bottle or glass jar. Use the toner after cleansing to balance your skin's pH and tighten pores.
Lingonberry Lip Balm
: Ingredients are 2 tablespoons lingonberry seed oil, 1 tablespoon beeswax pellets and 1 tablespoon shea butter. Melt the lingonberry seed oil, beeswax pellets, and shea butter in a double boiler or microwave.  Once melted, stir well to combine.  Pour the mixture into lip balm containers and let it cool and solidify. Apply the lingonberry lip balm whenever your lips need some extra hydration and protection.
Cleanse and Revitalize: Lingonberry Facial Cleanser
This gentle but effective facial cleanser uses the antioxidant-rich properties of lingonberries to cleanse and revitalise your skin. Here's how you make it:
Ingredients include 1/4 cup fresh or frozen lingonberries, 1/4 cup distilled water, 1 tablespoon honey, and 1 tablespoon coconut oil.
Instructions are to start by thoroughly washing the lingonberries to remove any dirt or impurities. In a small saucepan, combine the lingonberries and distilled water. Bring the mixture to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 5-10 minutes until the berries have softened and released their juices. Remove the saucepan from the heat and allow the mixture to cool slightly. Once cooled, strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth to separate the liquid from the solids. Press down on the berries to extract as much juice as possible. Discard the leftover berry pulp and transfer the lingonberry juice to a clean container.
To use, dampen your face with lukewarm water. Dispense a small amount of the lingonberry facial cleanser into your palm. Gently massage the cleanser onto your face using circular motions, paying extra attention to areas with makeup or impurities. Rinse thoroughly with lukewarm water and pat your skin dry with a clean towel. Follow up with your favorite toner, serum, and moisturizer for a complete skincare routine.
The benefits are that lingonberries are rich in antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals that help protect the skin from environmental damage and promote a healthy, radiant complexion. Honey is a natural humectant, attracting moisture to the skin and leaving it hydrated and supple. Coconut oil provides additional moisture and nourishment, making this cleanser suitable for dry or dehydrated skin types.
Lingonberries and Hydration: A Fresh Start for Your Skin
The natural goodness of lingonberries will revitalise and hydrate your skin. This DIY facial mist is a refreshing and nourishing way to give your skin the hydration it needs. Here's how you make it:
Ingredients are 1/2 cup fresh or frozen lingonberries, 1 cup distilled water, 1 tablespoon rose water (optional for added fragrance), and a few drops of vitamin E oil (optional for extra nourishment).
The instructions are to rinse the lingonberries thoroughly to remove any dirt or impurities. In a small saucepan, combine the lingonberries and distilled water. Bring the mixture to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 10-15 minutes until the berries have softened and released their juices. Remove the saucepan from the heat and allow the mixture to cool slightly. Once cooled, strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth to separate the liquid from the solids. Press down on the berries to extract as much juice as possible. Discard the leftover berry pulp and transfer the lingonberry-infused water to a clean spray bottle. If using, add the rose water and vitamin E oil to the spray bottle and shake well to combine.
To use, shake the bottle well to ensure all ingredients are thoroughly mixed. Close your eyes and hold the bottle about 6-8 inches away from your face. Spritz the lingonberry facial mist onto your skin, covering your face and neck with a light, even mist. Allow the mist to air dry, or gently pat it on your skin with clean fingertips. Use the facial mist as a refreshing pick-me-up throughout the day whenever your skin needs a boost of hydration.
The benefits are that lingonberries are rich in antioxidants and vitamins that help protect the skin from environmental damage and promote a healthy, radiant complexion. Rose water adds a delicate fragrance while helping to balance the skin's pH and soothe irritation. Vitamin E oil provides additional nourishment and hydration, leaving your skin feeling soft, smooth, and supple.
