How Fig Extract Elevates Skin Moisture Levels
Fig extract's composition and characteristics are well known for their capacity to raise skin moisture levels:
Natural Humectant
: The natural humectants found in fig extract draw and hold moisture from the surrounding air to keep the skin hydrated.  These humectants, which include sugars and polysaccharides, attract water molecules to the surface of the skin to keep the skin's moisture balance and prevent dehydration.
Hydration Retention
: The extract contains a lot of molecules that bind water, which helps to keep moisture in the skin.  Keeping the skin hydrated and averting dryness, aids in the prevention of transepidermal water loss (TEWL).
Moisturising Components
: Figs contain fatty acids, including omega-3 and omega-6, which nourish the skin and help to maintain the lipid barrier. A healthy lipid barrier helps trap moisture within the skin, preventing it from becoming dry or flaky.
Antioxidants
: Antioxidants found in fig extract, such as vitamins A, C, and E, protect the skin from free radical oxidative stress.  This protection helps to keep the skin's moisture barrier intact and prevents moisture loss.
Soothing Properties
: Fig extract has anti-inflammatory and soothing properties that can soothe irritated skin. It helps the skin retain moisture and prevents further damage from external aggressors by reducing inflammation.
When added to skincare products such as moisturisers, serums, or masks, fig extract works to hydrate and maintain the skin's moisture levels.  Patch testing is crucial for new products containing fig extract, especially if you have sensitive or reactive skin, as different skin types may react differently. Using products containing fig extract regularly as part of a skincare routine, along with other hydrating ingredients, can help achieve and maintain well-hydrated, supple, and healthy-looking skin. This will provide the best results in improving skin moisture.

The Antioxidant Powerhouse: Fig's Role in Skin Protection
Figs have strong antioxidant properties that help protect the skin from a variety of environmental aggressors.  Here's how figs help to protect your skin by acting as an antioxidant powerhouse:
Neutralising Free Radicals
: Figs contain antioxidants such as vitamins A, C, and E, as well as polyphenols.  These antioxidants aid in the elimination of free radicals produced by environmental factors such as UV radiation, pollution, and stress.  Free radicals can cause skin cells to age prematurely, causing wrinkles and dullness. Antioxidants in figs protect the skin from oxidative stress, which mitigates these effects.
Preventing Collagen Breakdown
: Free radicals can degrade collagen, the protein responsible for skin strength and elasticity.  The antioxidants in figs aid in inhibiting this process, preserving collagen integrity, and supporting firmer, more resilient skin.
UV Protection
: While not a substitute for sunscreen, the antioxidants found in fig extract, particularly vitamin C, have photoprotective properties that help to protect the skin from some of the harmful effects of UV rays.  They help to protect against UV damage by reducing oxidative damage produced by sun exposure.
Inflammation Reduction
: Antioxidants in figs have anti-inflammatory properties that help calm skin inflammation.  They contribute to skin health by reducing inflammation and the risk of various skin issues caused by inflammation, such as redness and irritation.
Enhancing Skin Repair and Renewal
: The antioxidants found in figs help the skin's natural repair processes.  They promote cell turnover and skin renewal, resulting in a younger, fresher appearance.
In skincare formulations, fig extract is often included because of its antioxidant-rich composition, which helps protect the skin against oxidative stress and environmental damage. These qualities make it a valuable element in products aimed at battling the indications of ageing, promoting skin health, and retaining a youthful complexion. However, while the antioxidants in fig extract offer skin protection benefits, they work best when combined with a comprehensive skincare programme that includes sun protection and a number of therapeutic components to address diverse skin conditions.
