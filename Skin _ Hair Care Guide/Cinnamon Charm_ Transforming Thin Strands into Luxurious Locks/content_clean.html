Cinnamon Infused Hair Serums: Amplifying Volume with Natural Solutions

Making hair serums with cinnamon extract is a great method to take advantage of cinnamon's potential advantages for thick hair. To encourage thicker and fuller-looking hair, these serums can help stimulate the scalp, enhance circulation, and nourish the hair follicles. Here's how to create a homemade hair serum with cinnamon:

Cinnamon Infused Hair Serum Recipe:

  • Ingredients: 1 tablespoon cinnamon powder or 2-3 cinnamon sticks, 1/2 cup carrier oil (such as coconut oil, jojoba oil, or sweet almond oil). Optional: 5-10 drops of essential oils for added benefits (e.g., rosemary oil, peppermint oil)

  • Instructions: Place the cinnamon powder or cinnamon sticks in a clean, dry glass jar. Heat the carrier oil in a small saucepan over low heat until warm but not boiling. Pour the warm carrier oil over the cinnamon in the glass jar. Stir the mixture gently to ensure the cinnamon is fully submerged in the oil. Let the mixture cool to room temperature, then cover the jar with a lid. Allow the cinnamon to infuse into the oil for at least 24 hours, preferably up to one week, in a cool, dark place. After the infusion period, strain the oil through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth to remove the cinnamon particles. If desired, add 5-10 drops of essential oils to the infused oil for additional benefits and fragrance. Transfer the infused oil to a clean, airtight glass bottle for storage.

  • How to Use: Apply a small amount of the cinnamon-infused hair serum to your scalp and massage gently with your fingertips. Work the serum through the lengths of your hair, focusing on areas where you want to add volume. Leave the serum on for at least 30 minutes, or overnight for a deeper treatment. Wash and condition your hair as usual to remove any residue.

  5. Massage for 5-10 Minutes: Continue massaging your scalp for five to 10 minutes to make sure the oil is absorbed and dispersed uniformly.

  6. Keep the Oil: To help the cinnamon oil feed and permeate the hair follicles, leave it on your scalp for at least half an hour.

  7. Rinse and Shampoo: Use lukewarm water to rinse the cinnamon oil from your hair when the allotted amount of time has elapsed. Use a mild shampoo afterward to get rid of any last bits of oil residue.

  8. Repeat Frequently: As part of your hair care regimen, massage your scalp with cinnamon two to three times a week for optimal effects.
