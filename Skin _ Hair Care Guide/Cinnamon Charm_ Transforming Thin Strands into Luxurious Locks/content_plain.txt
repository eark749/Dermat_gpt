DIY Cinnamon Hair Masks: Recipes for Thicker Tresses
Making homemade cinnamon hair masks is a fun and natural approach to encourage thicker, healthier hair. While scientific information on cinnamon's precise benefits on hair thickness is sparse, its qualities indicate that it may promote scalp health and circulation, which can indirectly contribute to thicker hair development. Here are some easy and excellent DIY cinnamon hair mask recipes to try:
Cinnamon and Honey Hair Mask
: Ingredients are 2 tablespoons cinnamon powder, 2 tablespoons honey, and 2 tablespoons coconut oil (or olive oil). In a bowl, mix the cinnamon powder, honey, and coconut oil until well combined. Apply the mask to damp hair, starting from the roots and working your way to the tips. Gently massage the mask into your scalp for a few minutes to promote circulation. Leave the mask on for 30-60 minutes to allow the ingredients to nourish your hair and scalp. Rinse thoroughly with lukewarm water, followed by shampoo and conditioner as usual.
Cinnamon and Yogurt Hair Mask
: Ingredients are 2 tablespoons of cinnamon powder and 1/2 cup plain yogurt. Mix the cinnamon powder and plain yogurt in a bowl until smooth and well combined. Apply the mask evenly to damp hair, focusing on the scalp and roots. Leave the mask on for 30-45 minutes to allow the ingredients to penetrate the hair shaft. Rinse thoroughly with lukewarm water, followed by shampoo and conditioner as usual.
Cinnamon and Avocado Hair Mask
: Ingredients are 2 tablespoons cinnamon powder, 1 ripe avocado, and 1 tablespoon honey (optional). Mash the ripe avocado in a bowl until smooth. Add the cinnamon powder (and honey, if using) to the mashed avocado and mix well. Apply the mask to clean, damp hair, focusing on the scalp and roots. Cover your hair with a shower cap or towel and leave the mask on for 30-60 minutes. Rinse thoroughly with lukewarm water, followed by shampoo and conditioner as usual.

Cinnamon Infused Hair Serums: Amplifying Volume with Natural Solutions
Making hair serums with cinnamon extract is a great method to take advantage of cinnamon's potential advantages for thick hair. To encourage thicker and fuller-looking hair, these serums can help stimulate the scalp, enhance circulation, and nourish the hair follicles. Here's how to create a homemade hair serum with cinnamon:
Cinnamon Infused Hair Serum Recipe
:
Ingredients
:
1 tablespoon cinnamon powder or 2-3 cinnamon sticks, 1/2 cup carrier oil (such as coconut oil, jojoba oil, or sweet almond oil). Optional: 5-10 drops of essential oils for added benefits (e.g., rosemary oil, peppermint oil)
Instructions
:
Place the cinnamon powder or cinnamon sticks in a clean, dry glass jar. Heat the carrier oil in a small saucepan over low heat until warm but not boiling. Pour the warm carrier oil over the cinnamon in the glass jar. Stir the mixture gently to ensure the cinnamon is fully submerged in the oil. Let the mixture cool to room temperature, then cover the jar with a lid. Allow the cinnamon to infuse into the oil for at least 24 hours, preferably up to one week, in a cool, dark place. After the infusion period, strain the oil through a fine-mesh sieve or cheesecloth to remove the cinnamon particles. If desired, add 5-10 drops of essential oils to the infused oil for additional benefits and fragrance. Transfer the infused oil to a clean, airtight glass bottle for storage.
How to Use
:
Apply a small amount of the cinnamon-infused hair serum to your scalp and massage gently with your fingertips. Work the serum through the lengths of your hair, focusing on areas where you want to add volume. Leave the serum on for at least 30 minutes, or overnight for a deeper treatment. Wash and condition your hair as usual to remove any residue.
