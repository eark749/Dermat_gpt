How Minoxidil Works

A common medication for hair loss is minoxidil, especially for pattern baldness. This is a thorough examination of how minoxidil promotes hair growth:

  • Minoxidil is a vasodilator, it dilates blood vessels. It increases blood flow to the hair follicle when applied topically to the scalp. Increased oxygen and nutrition to the follicles through improved circulation encourage the growth of hair.

  • Hair develops in three stages telogen, catagen, and anagen. By extending the anagen phase, minoxidil gives hair follicles more time to enter the active growth phase. Thick, longer hair is the result of this.

  • Minoxidil stimulates hair follicle cell growth. Thickner hair strands may be the outcome of their ability to amplify the size of miniaturized hair follicles. This effect is particularly potent in areas where hair thinning occurs.

  • Minoxidil may assist in stimulating dormant hair follicles. Follicles that have been inactive or miniaturized due to androgenetic alopecia may be induced to revert to an active condition, potentially resulting in new hair growth.
