How to Use Retinol and Ferulic Acid Combination in Your Skincare Routine
To prevent sensitivity or irritation, it's crucial to start slowly and build up the amount of retinol and ferulic acid you use in your skincare routine. To incorporate retinol and ferulic acid into your beauty routine, follow this general guide:
Face cleaning
: To remove any dirt, oil, or makeup, start by washing your face with a mild cleanser.
Apply toner
: Use a toner to bring your skin's pH into balance and prepare it for the treatment products.
Apply retinol
: If your skin accepts it well, apply a pea-sized amount of retinol to your face and neck every other night for the first week. If your skin tolerates it well, you can gradually increase this to nightly use.
Ferulic acid application
: After giving the retinol some time to absorb, dab your face and neck with a few drops of ferulic acid serum.
Moisturise
: Apply a moisturiser to your skin to help hydrate it and reduce any potential dryness or irritation caused by the retinol and ferulic acid.
Use sunscreen
: During the day, always wear broad-spectrum sunscreen with an SPF of 30 or higher, as retinol and ferulic acid can make you more sensitive to the sun.
It's crucial to be aware that retinol and ferulic acid, especially when first starting to use them, might irritate, dry up, and peel some people's skin. Precautions and Tips for Using Retinol and Ferulic Acid Combination
While retinol and ferulic acid can improve the skin in many ways, they can also irritate and dry up some people's skin. When using retinol and ferulic acid together, it's crucial to take the following measures and follow this advice in order to prevent potential negative effects:
Start with a low concentration
: As your skin adjusts, gradually increase the frequency of use while continuing to use a low concentration of retinol and ferulic acid.
Use at night
: It's best to use retinol at night and wear sunscreen during the day because retinol can increase your sensitivity to sunlight.
Avoid using other harsh products
: Retinol and ferulic acid shouldn't be combined with other abrasive or exfoliating skincare products because doing so increases the likelihood of irritation.
Use with caution if pregnant or breastfeeding
: Retinol should not be used during pregnancy or breastfeeding. If you are pregnant or breastfeeding, talk to your doctor before using retinol and ferulic acid together.
Apply to dry skin
: To avoid dilution of the product, apply the retinol and ferulic acid combination to dry skin after cleansing and toning.
Protect your eyes and lips
: Avoid applying retinol and ferulic acid combination near your eyes and lips as they are more sensitive areas of the skin.
Moisturise
: After applying the retinol and ferulic acid combination, apply a moisturiser to help prevent dryness and irritation.
Patch test
: Before applying retinol and ferulic acid to your face, perform a patch test on a small area of your skin to check for any adverse reactions.
If you experience persistent irritation or other side effects while using the retinol and ferulic acid combination, discontinue use and seek advice from a dermatologist or skin care professional.
