What is Royal Jelly?

Royal jelly is a nutrient-dense fluid made by worker bees. It is used to feed queen bee larvae and keep the queen alive throughout her life. This milky, gelatinous material is essential in the growth of a queen bee and distinguishes her from the other bees in the colony.

Royal jelly is famous for its diverse composition, which includes:

  • Vitamins include B-complex vitamins (B1, B2, B3, B5, B6, B7, B9), as well as vitamins A, C, D, and E.

  • Minerals include calcium, magnesium, potassium, zinc, iron, and phosphorus.

  • It contains all the essential amino acids necessary for human health.

  • Among the fatty acids, 10-Hydroxy-2-decenoic acid (10-HDA) is noteworthy for its antimicrobial qualities.

  • Antioxidants are substances that shield cells from the harm that free radicals can cause.

DIY Royal Jelly Face Masks and Treatments

Making your royal jelly face masks and treatments at home is an excellent method to harness the nourishing and renewing effects of this natural substance. Here are some basic but powerful recipes to try:

  1. Royal Jelly Honey Mask: Ingredients are a teaspoon of fresh royal jelly and 1 tablespoon of raw honey. Mix the fresh royal jelly and raw honey in a small bowl until well combined. Apply the mixture to clean, dry skin, avoiding the eye area. Leave the mask on for 15-20 minutes. Rinse off with lukewarm water and pat your skin dry.

  2. Royal Jelly Yogurt Mask: Ingredients are 1 teaspoon fresh royal jelly and 1 tablespoon plain yogurt. Combine the fresh royal jelly and plain yogurt in a bowl. Mix well until you achieve a smooth consistency. Apply the mixture evenly to your face and neck. Leave the mask on for 15-20 minutes. Rinse off with cool water and pat your skin dry.

  3. Royal Jelly Avocado Mask: Ingredients are 1 teaspoon of fresh royal jelly and 1/4 ripe avocado, mashed. Mash the ripe avocado in a bowl until smooth. Add the fresh royal jelly to the mashed avocado and mix well. Apply the mixture to your face and neck, avoiding the eye area. Leave the mask on for 15-20 minutes. Rinse off with lukewarm water and pat your skin dry.

  4. Royal Jelly Egg White Mask: Ingredients are 1 teaspoon of fresh royal jelly and 1 egg white. Separate the egg white from the yolk and place it in a bowl. Add the fresh royal jelly to the egg white and whisk until frothy. Apply the mixture to your face and neck in a thin layer. Allow the mask to dry for 15-20 minutes. Rinse off with lukewarm water and pat your skin dry.

Potential Side Effects and Precautions

While royal jelly has many benefits for skin health, it is important to be aware of any adverse effects and take the required precautions before taking it. Here are some considerations.

Potential Side Effects:

  • Royal jelly can cause allergic reactions in some individuals, especially those allergic to bee products. Symptoms may include itching, redness, swelling, rash, or hives. Perform a patch test before using royal jelly products on your face or body to check for any adverse reactions.

  • In rare cases, royal jelly may cause skin irritation or sensitivity, particularly if used in high concentrations or on sensitive skin. Discontinue use if you experience any discomfort or adverse effects and consult a dermatologist if symptoms persist.

  • Oral consumption of royal jelly supplements or products may cause digestive upset, such as diarrhea, stomach cramps, or nausea, in some individuals. Start with a low dose and gradually increase if tolerated, or consult a healthcare professional for guidance.

Precautions:

  • If you have known allergies to bee products or bee stings, consult a healthcare provider before using royal jelly products, especially orally. They can help determine whether royal jelly is safe for you and recommend appropriate precautions.

  • Perform a patch test before using any new royal jelly product on your skin, even if you don't have known allergies. Apply a small amount to a small area of your skin and wait 24-48 hours to check for any adverse reactions before using it more widely.

  • There is limited research on the safety of royal jelly during pregnancy and breastfeeding. Consult a healthcare provider before using royal jelly products if you are pregnant, nursing, or planning to become pregnant.

  • Royal jelly products should be kept away from the eyes and mucous membranes to prevent irritation or discomfort. If accidental contact occurs, rinse thoroughly with water and seek medical advice if necessary.

  • Choose royal jelly products from reputable brands known for their quality and purity. Ensure that the royal jelly is sourced from reliable and ethical sources and processed using safe and hygienic methods.

  • To retain the strength and efficiency of royal jelly products, store them as directed by the manufacturer. Keep them in a cool, dark place out of direct sunshine, heat, and moisture.

  • If you develop any allergic reactions, skin irritation, or other side effects after using royal jelly products, stop using them immediately and seek medical attention if necessary.

By following these precautions and being aware of potential side effects, you may safely enjoy the benefits of royal jelly for your skin while avoiding additional dangers. If you have any concerns or questions about taking royal jelly, speak with a healthcare practitioner or dermatologist for tailored advice and guidance.
