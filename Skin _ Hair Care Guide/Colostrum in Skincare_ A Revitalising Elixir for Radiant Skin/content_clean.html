What is Colostrum?

Colostrum is regarded as nature's first superfood. It's a creamy, nutrient-dense elixir that is produced by the mother during the early days of postpartum life. This 'liquid gold' is packed with an impressive array of nutrients that are tailored perfectly to meet the initial needs of a newborn.

  1. It is rich in high-quality protein, and low in fat and sugar. Colostrum is easily digestible, hence making it ideal for a baby's immature digestive system.

  2. The presence of Lactoferrin which is a potent protein, further enhances its infection-fighting prowess, thus making colostrum a natural defence mechanism against infections.

  3. It is extremely rich in Immunoglobulin A. It is a crucial antibody that lines the newly formed gastrointestinal tract of the baby, to guard against pathogens, thus acting as a natural shield.

  4. There is an abundance of Leukocytes, or white blood cells,that further fortify the baby's immune system.
  5. Colostrum also contains a high concentration of epidermal growth factor. It stimulates the development of the vital organs and skin of the baby.

  6. Since it is extremely rich in carotenoids and vitamin A, it has a distinctive yellow hue. Carotenoids and vitamin A are extremely essential for healthy vision, skin, and immune function.
  7. It is a storehouse of various minerals such as magnesium, copper, and zinc that play crucial roles too. Magnesium supports the development of strong bones and a healthy heart, while copper and zinc are pivotal for a robust immune system.
