The Science Behind Skincare Synergy

While combining the products, always keep in mind the following tips:

  • Determine your skin type, whether it’s dry, oily, normal, combination, dry and sensitive, or oily and sensitive.

  • Understand the issue that you want to address.

  • Go by resolving one issue at a time. For example, if you want to get rid of pimples and target signs of ageing, choose what you want to address first.

  • Don’t shock your skin with a sudden gush off too many products.

  • Incorporate skincare products into your routine slowly and steadily.

  • Always do a patch test first, before using the products together.

  • If you experience any reaction while using the products, consult a dermatologist.

Benefits of Pairing Skincare Products

Pairing skincare products offer many benefits to the skin such as:

  • Combining products can boost their individual benefits, leading to more effective skincare results.

  • Certain combinations improve the skin's texture and enhance the absorption of key ingredients.
  • Some ingredients work better together, enhancing each other’s properties for optimal skin health.

  • Efficient use of complementary products can be more economical than relying on single, multi-purpose products.

How to Incorporate Skincare Combinations into Your Routine?

While incorporating skincare combinations into your routine, start with understanding your skin type and do extensive research on what ingredients suit you and do not suit you. Then, take it slow. Don’t just jump into the well. Instead, take baby steps by incorporating less quantity for fewer days, and then gradually increasing the amount and frequency. Don’t expect to see the results in the initial weeks only. Be consistent and regular in your skincare practices to see the result.

 Creating Your Personalised Skincare Cocktail

Always keep in mind the below mentioned points while creating your unique skincare cocktail.

  1. Understand your skin type.

  2. Analyse your skin concern.

  3. Avoid starting with higher concentrations. Use a lower one initially and then gradually increase it.

  4. Do not use harsh products if you have sensitive skin that is prone to dryness.

  5. Make sure to not mix day care skin products and night care skin products together.

  6. Avoid using 2 products having the same action.

  7. Patch test before use.

  8. Consult a dermatologist for the best guidance.

Dos and Don'ts of Mixing Skincare Products

Avoid the following skincare duos to prevent any skin disaster:

  1. Do not mix 2 exfoliators. Avoid using retinoids, if you use salicylic acid serum. It can cause your skin to peel. Instead use a gentle cleanser such as Cetaphil Gentle Skin Cleanser before using retinols.

  2. Avoid mixing Vitamin C products with harsh acids as it can cause burning sensation along with redness.

  3. Avoid using niacinamide or harsh acid with benzoyl peroxide and retinoids.

  4. If you are pregnant or nursing or are taking some medication, always consult a dermatologist before using any skincare product. 

How to Monitor Skin's Response to Combinations

Lastly, but not the least, always monitor the response of your skin to the skincare products. If any redness or peeling is noticed, immediately stop using it and consult a doctor. However, if you notice a positive response, and your concern vanishes, do not stop using the product suddenly. Taper down its application gradually.
