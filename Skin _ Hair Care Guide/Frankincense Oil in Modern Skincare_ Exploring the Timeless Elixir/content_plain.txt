Before using any new product on your skin, perform a patch test by applying a small amount of the diluted oil to your inner forearm and waiting 24 hours. If you experience any redness, itching, or burning, discontinue use.

1. Olfactory System and Relaxation:
Scent as a Signal: When you inhale frankincense's calming aroma, the scent travels up the olfactory nerve (sensory nerve of nose) to the limbic system, the part of the brain associated with emotions and memory.
Promoting Relaxation: The limbic system, upon receiving the calming signals from the frankincense scent, is thought to trigger the release of neurotransmitters like dopamine and serotonin. These neurotransmitters are associated with feelings of well-being and relaxation, effectively countering the effects of stress hormones.
2. Potential Impact on Stress Hormones:
Studies Suggesting a Link: Some research suggests that inhaling frankincense oil may influence the body's production of cortisol, the primary stress hormone. Studies on this are ongoing, but some evidence shows a decrease in cortisol levels after exposure to frankincense aromatherapy.
3. Overall Effect:
By influencing the olfactory system and potentially impacting stress hormones, frankincense oil's calming aroma can create a more relaxed state, promoting feelings of peace and reducing stress.
