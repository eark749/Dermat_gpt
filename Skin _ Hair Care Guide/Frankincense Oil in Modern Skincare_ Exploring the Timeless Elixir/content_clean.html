Incorporating Frankincense into Hair and Body Care 

Frankincense oil's unique properties can be a valuable addition to your hair and body care routine. Here's how to incorporate Frankincense for hair growth safely and effectively:

1. Hair Care:

  • Scalp Massage: Dilute 2-3 drops of frankincense oil with a carrier oil like jojoba or coconut oil. Massage the mixture into your scalp to stimulate circulation, potentially promote hair growth, and soothe dryness. Scalp health with frankincense oil is a growing niche. Hair rejuvenation is one of the Frankincense essential oil uses.

  • Hair Mask: Add a few drops of frankincense oil to your regular conditioner or hair mask for a boost of shine and nourishment. This can be beneficial for dry, brittle hair.

  • Hair Rinse: After shampooing, dilute 1-2 drops of frankincense oil in a cup of cool water and use it as a final rinse. This can add a subtle fragrance and potentially reduce dandruff.

    2. Body Care:

  • Bath Soak: Add 5-7 drops of frankincense oil to your bathwater for a relaxing and potentially muscle-soothing soak.

  • Body Lotion: Mix a few drops of frankincense oil with your unscented body lotion to create a custom moisturiser with a calming scent. Patch test first to avoid irritation.

  • Body Balm: Mix few drops of Frankincense oil to shea butter and whip it up thoroughly. Indulge in Soothing frankincense balms whenever required.

  • Soothing Massage Oil: Dilute frankincense oil with a carrier oil like sweet almond oil to create a massage oil that may help ease muscle tension and promote relaxation.

    3. Important Considerations:

  • Always dilute: Frankincense oil is a potent essential oil and should never be applied directly to the skin. Always dilute it with a carrier oil like jojoba, coconut, sweet almond, or avocado oil before use. A common dilution ratio is 2-3 drops of essential oil per 1 tablespoon of carrier oil.

  • Patch test: Before using any new product on your skin, perform a patch test by applying a small amount of the diluted oil to your inner forearm and waiting 24 hours. If you experience any redness, itching, or burning, discontinue use. 

  • Consult a doctor: If you have any pre-existing skin conditions or concerns, consult a dermatologist before using frankincense oil.

  • Quality matters: Ensure you're using high-quality, therapeutic-grade frankincense oil for the best results.

By incorporating frankincense oil into your routine thoughtfully, you can potentially experience its benefits for both your hair and body.
