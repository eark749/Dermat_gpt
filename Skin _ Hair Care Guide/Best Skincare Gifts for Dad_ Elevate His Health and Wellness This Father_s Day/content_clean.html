Daily Skincare Routine for Men 

To start with, incorporating skin care into your daily routine is essential. Daily Skincare routine can be followed both at day and night time. The morning skincare ritual that can be followed is:

  1. Cleansing: Starting the day with washing the face not only refreshes you but also removes the oil produced by the skin during night. Use a gentle, refreshing facial cleanser to wash your face.

  2. Toning: After cleansing, it is recommended to incorporate a toner, as it not only removes the leftover oil but also hydrates the skin as the skin tends to become dry after thorough cleanser. In addition, it also leads to diminished appearance of the pores for a smoother appearance. Toning the face after cleansing restores the natural pH of the skin.

  3. Moisturising: Contrary to the usual belief that an oily skin does not require a moisturiser, in reality, it is of utmost necessity that a moisturiser is applied on to the skin. It is so because, if the skin is not moisturised, the skin produces more oil leading to acne. Hence, it is recommended to incorporate non-comedogenic moisturiser into your skincare ritual to make the skin well hydrated and moisturised.

  4. Protecting: No matter how flawless the skin looks right now, always apply sunscreen before stepping out in the sun. As we age, melasma and skin pigmentation that occurs due to UV damage becomes prominent. Hence, using a sunscreen of at least SPF 30 is extremely crucial for the skin wellness in the long run. Indulge in a waterproof and sweat resistant sunscreen to ensure optimal protection. Also, don’t forget to re-apply it after every 2 hours.

  5. Lip Care: Last, but not the least, indulge in nourishing and hydrating lip care. Use a SPF based lip balm that not only hydrates but also protects the lips from harmful effects of UV rays. 

At night time, indulging in a few additional steps can make the skin care game top notch.  Certain steps that can be added in night care skin regimen are: 

  1. Double Cleansing: It implies cleaning the face using 2 cleansers. Step one is usually using an oil based cleanser, followed with a water-based one as Step two. This ensures effective removal of sebum, sunscreen, pollution and other oil based impurities. This not only cleanses the skin, but also revitalises the skin and ensures effective penetration of other skincare products for optimal results.

  2. Exfoliating: Using certain exfoliants like Salicylic Acid or Glycolic Acid, twice or thrice daily, ensures optimal cell turnover. This makes the skin visibly brighter and radiant, as it ensures proper removal of dead skin cells and debris.

  3. Correcting: Recognise your main skin concern and use a serum specifically targeted to that concern. Remember, to pat and not rub it into the skin. For example, using a niacinamide serum like Clinikally The Ultimate NIA (10% Niacinamide) Serum to target scars and pigmentation, or a Retinol serum like EVE LOM Radiance Repair Retinol Serum during the night to prevent wrinkles and fine lines can be incorporated into the night time skin regimen. Always remember to follow the guidelines on the packaging for best results.

  4. Treating Under Eye: Incorporating an effective under eye cream like Clinikally UltraHeal Under Eye Cream and Epique Intensive Revitalising Eye Formula into the night time routine is also very beneficial as it minimises the swelling and fine lines that occur under the eye area. 

  5. Using a Mask: Incorporating a night time revival mask for skin offers a plethora of benefits as it provides ample hydration. It also works better because, at night time, during deep sleep, the skin heals maximally. This results in improved results. Essential Cleansers for Men  

Cleansing is just as important for men's skin as it is for anyone else's, but there are some specific reasons why it's particularly beneficial for men:

  1. Men tend to have thicker skin with larger pores that produce more sebum (oil). Regular cleansing removes excess oil, reducing shine and preventing clogged pores that can lead to breakouts.

  2. Shaving can irritate the skin and lead to ingrown hairs and razor bumps. Cleansing helps prevent these by removing dead skin cells and keeping pores clear.

  3. Cleansing removes dirt and pollutants that can contribute to wrinkles and fine lines. It also allows for better absorption of moisturisers which help keep skin hydrated and plump.

  4. Men's skin benefits from the general cleansing benefits - removing impurities, promoting circulation, and prepping the skin for other products.

  5. Cleansing removes sweat, dirt, and grime leaving skin feeling refreshed and invigorated, especially after a workout or a long day.

Here are some additional things to keep in mind for men's cleansing:

  1. Choose the right cleanser:  Men's skin tends to be oilier, so look for a gentle cleanser formulated for your skin type (oily, normal, etc.). Harsh cleansers can strip the skin of natural oils, leading to dryness and irritation.

  3. Cleanse twice a day: Morning and night is ideal, but if that's not possible, at least cleanse before bed to remove sweat, dirt, and oil accumulated throughout the day.

Hydrating Moisturizers to Nourish Skin

Contrary to popular belief, moisturising is just as important for men's skin as it is for women's. Here's why men should moisturise daily:

  • Men's skin, though it is thicker, is equally prone to dryness. Shaving, weather exposure, and even hot showers can strip away moisture, making the skin tight and uncomfortable. Moisturizing replenishes lost moisture, keeping skin feeling soft and supple.
  • A healthy, moisturised skin barrier acts as a defence system against environmental aggressors like pollution, sun damage, and harsh soaps. This can help prevent irritation, redness, and discomfort.
  • Even men want to maintain a youthful appearance. Moisturising helps keep skin hydrated and plump, reducing the appearance of wrinkles and fine lines.
  • Soothes After Shave: Shaving irritates the skin. A good moisturiser, especially one formulated for post-shave use, can soothe irritation, razor burn, and ingrown hairs. Look for ingredients like aloe vera or oatmeal for added calming benefits.
  • Prevents Breakouts: Believe it or not, even oily skin needs moisture.  When skin gets stripped of its natural oils, it can go into overdrive to produce more, leading to breakouts. Moisturising helps regulate oil production and prevent clogged pores that can cause pimples.
  • Improves Overall Skin Health: Moisturizing isn't just about aesthetics; it's about promoting healthy skin function. Proper hydration allows the skin to function optimally, shedding dead skin cells more effectively and maintaining a healthy glow.

Here are some additional things to keep in mind for men's moisturising:

  1. Choose the right moisturiser: Look for a lightweight, oil-free formula that absorbs quickly and won't leave a greasy residue. Gels or lotions are often good options for men's skin.

  2. Moisturise daily: Apply moisturiser after cleansing, both in the morning and at night.

  3. Don't forget the neck and eye area: The delicate skin around the eyes and neck needs moisture too. Look for a specific product for these areas or use a small amount of your regular moisturiser.

By incorporating a daily moisturising routine, men can experience a variety of benefits for their skin, leaving it feeling healthy, comfortable, and looking its best.

Daily sunscreen application offers a well-established defence mechanism, reducing UV-induced cellular damage and safeguarding against these malignancies. Exfoliation for Healthy Skin 

Exfoliation is a key player in maintaining healthy skin as it effectively removes the top layer of dead skin cells, dirt, and oil buildup. This process offers a range of benefits for your skin's health and appearance:

  1. By increased epithelial cell turnover, new skin cells are revealed, resulting in a smoother and more radiant complexion.

  2. Deeper penetration of the products occurs into the skin, which maximises the effectiveness of your moisturiser, serums, and other treatments.

  3. Regular exfoliation removes the dead skin cells and dirt effectively, leading to lesser appearance of pores, whiteheads and blackheads, thus revealing a smoother and more even skin tone.

  4. Gentle exfoliation stimulates blood circulation, thus improving overall skin health, as nutrient and oxygen delivery to the skin surface is enhanced.

  5. Due to proper and effective dead cell removal and unclogging of pores, acne breakouts are prevented with ease.

However, it's important to exfoliate with the right frequency and pressure to avoid irritation. Consider your skin type and choose an exfoliating method that suits your needs. Remember, consistency is key! Regular exfoliation, along with a proper skincare routine, can significantly contribute to healthy, glowing skin.

Essential Vitamins and Minerals for Men  

While a balanced diet is the best way to get the vitamins and minerals your body needs, some men may benefit from taking supplements to fill nutrient gaps. Here are some essential vitamins and minerals for men:

  • Vitamin D: Crucial for bone health and immune function, men are more likely than women to be deficient. Sunlight helps your body produce vitamin D, but supplementation might also be needed.

  • Vitamin B Complex: Important for energy production, cell metabolism, and nervous system function, there are eight B vitamins, each with a specific role. Men may need higher doses of certain B vitamins like B12 and folate.

  • Vitamin C: An antioxidant that protects cells from damage, vitamin C also plays a role in immune function and collagen production.

  • Magnesium: Involved in many bodily functions like muscle and nerve function, blood sugar control, and blood pressure regulation, men tend to lose magnesium as they age and may need supplementation.

  • Zinc: Important for immune function, wound healing, and testosterone production, zinc deficiency can lead to low testosterone levels, fatigue, and impaired wound healing in men.

  • Omega-3 Fatty Acids: Essential for heart health, brain function, and inflammation, men who don't eat much fish may benefit from omega-3 supplements.

  • Calcium: Essential for strong bones and teeth, men need just as much calcium as women, but are more likely to be deficient.

  • Selenium: An antioxidant that protects cells from damage and plays a role in thyroid function and reproduction.

If you're concerned about your nutrient intake, talk to your doctor before taking any supplement. They can assess your individual needs and recommend the most appropriate course of action.
