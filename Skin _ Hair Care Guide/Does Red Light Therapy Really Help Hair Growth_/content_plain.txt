The Science Behind Red Light Therapy: Mechanisms and Benefits
Red light treatment is an example of LLLT, also known as photobiomodulation. It employs light with precise wavelengths to encourage hair growth. Although "lasers" is in the name, LLLT presently mostly employs non-coherent light-emitting diodes (LEDs), which may create light of various wavelengths with distinct effects on the human body. Red light treatment, as the name implies, uses red or near-infrared light with wavelengths ranging from 630 to 700 nm (660 nm is commonly chosen). They permeate the scalp and improve cell function, which promotes hair development.

Common Misconceptions and Clarifications
Some common misconceptions and clarifications are given below:
How long does it take for red light therapy to promote hair growth?
Clarification
: Red light therapy promotes the body's natural growth processes at the cellular level. In the best situations, consumers have noticed improvements in their hair condition in as little as 16 weeks.
RLT can treat all types of hair loss.
Clarification
: Red light treatment for hair growth is a safe and effective low-light laser therapy (LLLT) that can help increase hair density and health in a variety of alopecia conditions.
All red light devices are the same.
Clarification
: Not all RLT devices are made equally. The efficacy of a device is determined by parameters such as wavelength (630-670 nm), power output, and the quality of the LEDs or laser diodes.
RLT alone is sufficient for complete hair restoration.
Clarification
: In most cases, hair regrowth has been documented after 12 to 26 weeks of red light treatment, including decreased hair loss each day and a considerable improvement in hair growth on the head. With the addition of some hair serums, results can be obtained in just a few weeks, especially when combined with red light therapy.
Hair transplants can be replaced with RLT.
Clarification
: RLT cannot replace the necessity for hair transplants in cases of severe hair loss where hair follicles are no longer viable. However, RLT is frequently used as a pre-and post-operative treatment to enhance healing and promote transplanted hair growth.
RLT is unsafe and can cause cancer.
Clarification
: RLT is considered extremely safe when taken as indicated. No scientific data indicates that RLT promotes cancer or other major health concerns. The light used in RLT is non-ionizing, which means it does not harm DNA or cause cell mutation.
Results are permanent without continued use.
Clarification
: The results from RLT are not necessarily permanent if treatment is discontinued. Like many hair loss treatments, ongoing use is often required to maintain results. Specialists usually recommend continued use or a maintenance schedule to sustain hair growth.
RLT can be uncomfortable or painful.
Clarification
: RLT is usually painless and noninvasive. Most users feel mild warmth during treatment, but this should not be bothersome. If the user experiences discomfort, it could be because the device is not being utilized properly or is too powerful for the user's skin type.
