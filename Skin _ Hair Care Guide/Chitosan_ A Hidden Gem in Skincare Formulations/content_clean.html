Delving into Chitosan’s Origin and Properties


Chitin, a material present in the exoskeletons of crustaceans including prawns, crabs, and lobsters, is the source of the natural polymer chitosan. Deacetylation is the process of removing acetyl groups from chitin to create chitosan, a more soluble and bioactive molecule.  Crustacean shells, which are often a byproduct of the seafood industry, are the source of chitosan. It is therefore a renewable and sustainable resource. Given that chitosan is derived from crab shells, it is crucial to think about the quality and purity of the ingredients when selecting skincare products that contain it, especially for people who have shellfish allergies. Additionally, when introducing new products to your skincare routine, a patch test and consultation with a dermatologist are recommended, especially if you have specific skin concerns or sensitivities.

Understanding Chitosan’s Chemical Structure

Chitosan is a linear polymer that differs from chitin due to its chemical composition. The method used to create it from chitin is deacetylation, which is the removal of acetyl groups from the chitin molecule to produce a more water-soluble and biologically active chemical. Chitosan's chemical structure includes the polymer backbone, monomer units, level of deacetylation, molecular weight, and chemical formula. Chitosan's chemical composition makes it biodegradable, biocompatible, and appropriate for use in a variety of fields, including agriculture, food processing, medicine, and skincare. Due to its adaptability and capacity for creating films, retaining moisture, and interacting with other molecules, it is a valuable and long-lasting component in a variety of goods.

Exploring the Skincare Benefits of Chitosan

 

Chitosan has several skincare benefits due to its natural and biocompatible properties. The following are some of the most important skincare benefits of chitosan when used in skincare products:

Chitosan’s Hydrating and Moisturising Effects

Chitosan is well-known for its excellent hydrating and moisturising properties when used in skincare products. This is how it helps with skin hydration and moisture retention:

  1. Hydrating Barrier Formation: Chitosan forms a thin, protective barrier on the skin's surface when applied topically. This barrier prevents water loss by forming a shield that keeps moisture in the skin. As a result, it can assist the skin in remaining hydrated for longer periods of time. 

  2. Improved Water Retention: Chitosan's hydrophilic (water-attracting) nature allows it to attract and retain moisture. This property is especially beneficial in skincare products that are intended to provide long-lasting hydration. It functions as a natural humectant, absorbing moisture from the environment and storing it in the skin.

  3. Dry Skin Relief: Chitosan's ability to retain water aids in the relief of dry, dehydrated skin. It can improve skin texture significantly, making it smoother and more supple.

  4. Skin Barrier Reinforcement: Chitosan strengthens the skin's natural barrier, allowing it to function more effectively. A strong, healthy skin barrier is critical for retaining moisture and preventing water loss, both of which are necessary for skin hydration.

  5. Reducing Transepidermal Water Loss (TEWL): Chitosan has been found to reduce transepidermal water loss, which is the process of water evaporating through the skin's outermost layer. By reducing TEWL, chitosan helps the skin maintain its moisture balance.

  6. Skin Softening: Chitosan's moisturising properties can aid in reducing the roughness of dry skin. The skin feels softer and more comfortable afterward.

  7. Compatibility: Chitosan is compatible with a variety of skincare ingredients, allowing formulators to create hydrating products that also contain beneficial compounds.

The ability of chitosan to lock in moisture, improve water retention, and strengthen the skin's barrier makes it a valuable ingredient in skincare products designed to provide long-lasting hydration and combat dryness.
