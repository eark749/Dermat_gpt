Integrating Glycol Soap into Your Skincare Routine: Tips for Optimal Results

Integrating glycol soap into your skincare routine can result in smoother, more vibrant skin if done correctly. Here's a step-by-step guide for incorporating glycol soap:

  1. Begin slowly: Use glycol soap 2-3 times per week to help your skin acclimatize to the glycolic acid. If your skin tolerates it well, gradually increase the frequency to daily or as prescribed by your dermatologist.

  2. Cleansing Method: Wet your face with lukewarm water to open your pores. Lather the glycol soap in your hands and massage it into your face in gentle, circular motions, avoiding the eyes. Rinse completely with lukewarm water to remove any soap residue.

  3. Follow with a Hydrating Toner: A hydrating toner restores the skin's pH balance and provides hydration.

  4. Moisturise: Glycolic acid can be drying, so use a good moisturizer afterward to keep your skin hydrated. Use a moisturizer with moisturizing components such as hyaluronic acid, glycerin, or ceramides.

  5. Sun Protection: Glycolic acid makes your skin more sensitive to the sun, therefore sunscreen is recommended. Every morning, even on cloudy days, use a broad-spectrum sunscreen with an SPF of at least 30.

  6. Complementary Products: To maximize the benefits of glycol soap, use serums with moisturizing, soothing, or brightening characteristics, such as hyaluronic acid or vitamin C. To avoid irritation and over-exfoliation, do not use other exfoliating products (like scrubs or AHAs/BHAs) on the same days as glycol soap.

DIY Glycol Soap Tips: Navigating the Path to Healthy, Glowing Skin

Making your glycol soap can be a satisfying and cost-effective approach to obtaining healthy, bright skin. Here are some instructions for making basic DIY glycol soap.

  • Ingredients include 1 lb melt-and-pour soap base (glycerin or shea butter), 1-2 tablespoons glycolic acid (adjust to desired concentration), essential oils (optional for aroma), and natural colorants.

  • Cut the melt-and-pour soap base into small bits and melt it in a double boiler over low heat. Once melted, remove from heat and allow it to cool somewhat before adding the glycolic acid. Mix thoroughly to ensure a uniform distribution. If desired, add a few drops of essential oils and natural colorants and combine thoroughly. Pour the mixture into soap molds and let it set until completely firm. Remove the soap from the molds and allow it to cure for a few days before using it to ensure that the ingredients are properly set.
