What is Skin Flooding?
The term may sound like a passing fad, but the idea is straightforward. To deliver deeper, longer-lasting hydration, skin flooding requires layering moisturising solutions in a precise order. It consists of four easy steps: a gentle cleanse, a mist application, the use of one or more serums, and finishing with moisturising. The main component of this regimen,
hyaluronic acid
, draws and hangs on to water molecules.
Niacinamide serum
can be added to skin flooding treatments for additional calming and anti-inflammatory effects. Your products will successfully supply hydration if you layer them carefully, starting with thinner, water-based products and moving on to thicker, oilier ones.
Understanding the Science behind Skin Flooding
It's actually quite easy, and it doesn't differ all that much from how many of us have been layering our skincare products for years. The distinction is that you must be precise about what you apply and how you layer it if you want to fully benefit from the moisturising effects of skin flooding. Layering water-based skincare products is said to increase the amount of moisture you apply to the skin because they penetrate deeper and provide longer-lasting hydration. Another crucial point to remember when it comes to successful skin flooding is that you apply each product while the face is still moist from the previous one, rather than letting them all soak in. Why? Keeping your face damp throughout the process stimulates increased moisture retention since water draws more moisture.

Improved Skin Barrier Function
Improving the skin barrier function is important for maintaining healthy skin as it acts as a protective shield against environmental aggressors and prevents water loss from the skin. The following are some techniques for enhancing skin barrier performance:
Use pH-balanced, gentle skincare products
: The skin's natural barrier function can be harmed by products that are too harsh or have a high pH. Look for mild cleansers, toners, and moisturisers that are pH-balanced and appropriate for your skin type.
Avoid excessive exfoliation
: Excessive exfoliation can harm the skin's protective barrier, causing dryness and irritability. Choose gentle, non-abrasive formulas and use exfoliating products sparingly.
Use products with barrier-restoring ingredients
: Look for skincare products that have ceramides, cholesterol, and fatty acids as they are barrier-restoring components. These substances aid in enhancing the skin's natural moisture barrier and halting moisture loss.
Avoid using hot water
: Hot water can dry out the skin and weaken the barrier function of the skin. When washing your face, use lukewarm water and stay away from taking long, hot showers.
Sun protection
: UV rays can damage the skin's barrier function, causing dryness and premature ageing. When going outside, always wear sunscreen and seek shade during peak sun hours.
By following these tips, you can help improve the barrier function of your skin, resulting in healthier, more resilient skin.

The Correct Order of Layering Skincare Products
The correct order of layering skincare products is critical to ensure that each product is absorbed properly and effectively, as well as to avoid any potential interactions or conflicts between the ingredients. The right order to layer skincare products is as follows:
Start by cleansing your skin with a cleanser to get rid of any dirt, oil, or makeup.
To balance the pH levels of your skin and get ready for the next steps, use a toner.
Any serums, treatments, or spot treatments that address particular skin issues like acne, dark spots, or wrinkles should be applied. The thinnest to thickest of these products should be applied in order of consistency.
Apply eye cream to the sensitive skin surrounding your eyes.
Apply a moisturiser to your skin to hydrate and shield it. Choose a moisturiser that is appropriate for your skin type, and apply it in an upward motion.
Apply sunscreen last to shield your skin from UV ray damage. Apply broad-spectrum sunscreen liberally to all exposed skin with an SPF of at least 30.
Applying a skin-flooding therapy, such as a hyaluronic acid serum or face mist, should come before any treatment products or moisturisers but after cleaning and toning. It's crucial to keep in mind that not all skincare products need to be used daily, and how often you use them can vary depending on your specific skin needs and issues.

Pairing Skin Flooding with Other Skincare Treatments
Skin flooding is a fantastic addition to your skincare regimen, but it's crucial to pay attention to any other skincare products you're using concurrently. The following advice can help you combine skin flooding with other skincare procedures:
Exfoliation is an important step in any skincare routine, but over-exfoliating can damage your skin's barrier, causing dryness and irritation. It's best to keep exfoliation to once or twice a week if skin flooding is a part of your routine.
Skin flooding can provide deep hydration, so you might not need as heavy a moisturiser as you normally do. Look for lighter, gel-like moisturisers that won't clog your pores or make your skin feel heavy.
Active ingredients like retinol and acids can be harsh on the skin, so use them with caution when paired with skin flooding. Begin with a low concentration and gradually increase it to avoid over-exfoliating or irritating your skin.
After skin flooding, a light facial oil can help lock in moisture and give your skin more nourishment.
When using multiple skincare treatments, it's critical to apply them in the correct order. As a general rule, apply your products from thinnest to thickest consistency. For example, after cleansing, apply toners, then serums or active treatments, moisturisers, and sunscreen.
You can combine skin flooding with other treatments to obtain healthy, glowing skin safely and efficiently by being aware of the other skincare products you're using and using them in the right order.
